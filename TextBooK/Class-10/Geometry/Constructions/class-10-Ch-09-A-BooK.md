## DIVISION OF A LINE SEGMENT IN A GIVEN RATIO

EXAMPLE 1 Divide a line segment 6 cm long in the ratio 4:3. Prove your assertion.

## STEPS OF CONSTRUCTION

Step 1. Draw a line segment $A B=6 \mathrm{~cm}$.
Step 2. Draw a ray $A X$, making an acute angle $\angle B A X$.
Step 3. Along $A X$, mark $(4+3)=7$ points $A_{1}, A_{2}, A_{3}, A_{4}, A_{5}, A_{6}$ and $A_{7}$ such that

$$
A A_{1}=A_{1} A_{2}=A_{2} A_{3}=A_{3} A_{4}=A_{4} A_{5}=A_{5} A_{6}=A_{6} A_{7} .
$$

Step 4. Join $A_{7} B$.
Step 5. From $A_{4}$, draw $A_{4} C$ parallel to $A_{7} B$ (by making an angle equal to $\angle A A_{7} B$ ), meeting $A B$ at $C$.

Then, $C$ is the point on $A B$, which divides it in the ratio $4: 3$.
![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-10/Geometry/Constructions/class-10-Ch-09-A-BooK-001.jpg)

Thus, $A C: C B=4: 3$.
PROOF Let $A A_{1}=A_{1} A_{2}=\ldots=A_{6} A_{7}=x$.
In $\triangle A B A_{7}$, we have $A_{4} C \| A_{7} B$.
$\therefore \quad \frac{A C}{C B}=\frac{A A_{4}}{A_{4} A_{7}}=\frac{4 x}{3 x}=\frac{4}{3} \quad$ [by Thales' theorem].
Hence, $A C: C B=4: 3$.

## ALTERNATIVE METHOD

## STEPS OF CONSTRUCTION

Step 1. Draw a line segment $A B=6 \mathrm{~cm}$.
Step 2. Draw a ray $A X$, making an acute $\angle B A X$.
Step 3. Draw a ray $B Y$ parallel to $A X$ by making $\angle A B Y=\angle B A X$.
Step 4. Locate the points $A_{1}, A_{2}, A_{3}, A_{4}$ on $A X$ and $B_{1}, B_{2}, B_{3}$ on $B Y$ such that

$$
A A_{1}=A_{1} A_{2}=A_{2} A_{3}=A_{3} A_{4}=B B_{1}=B_{1} B_{2}=B_{2} B_{3} .
$$

Step 5. Join $A_{4} B_{3}$, intersecting $A B$ at a point $C$.
Then, $A C: C B=4: 3$.
![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-10/Geometry/Constructions/class-10-Ch-09-A-BooK-002.jpg)
proof Here, $A A_{4} \| B B_{3}$.
$\therefore \quad \angle C A A_{4}=\angle C B B_{3} \quad$ (alt. int. $\angle$ s).
Also, $\angle A C A_{4}=\angle B C B_{3} \quad$ (vert. opp. $\angle$ s).
$\therefore \quad \triangle C A A_{4} \sim \triangle C B B_{3} \quad$ [by AA-similarity].
And so, $\frac{A C}{C B}=\frac{A A_{4}}{B B_{3}}=\frac{4}{3}$.
Hence, $A C: C B=4: 3$.

## TO CONSTRUCT A TRIANGLE SIMILAR TO A GIVEN TRIANGLE AS PER GIVEN SCALE FACTOR

EXAMPLE 2 Construct a triangle with sides $5 \mathrm{~cm}, 5.5 \mathrm{~cm}$ and 6.5 cm . Then construct another triangle whose sides are $\frac{3}{5}$ times the corresponding sides of the first triangle.
[CBSE 2014]

## STEPS OF CONSTRUCTION

Step 1. Draw a line segment $B C=5.5 \mathrm{~cm}$.
Step 2. With $B$ as centre and radius equal to 5 cm , draw an arc.

Step 3. With $C$ as centre and radius equal to 6.5 cm , draw another arc cutting the previous arc at $A$.
Step 4. Join $A B$ and $A C$.
Thus, $\triangle A B C$ with given lengths of
![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-10/Geometry/Constructions/class-10-Ch-09-A-BooK-003.jpg)
sides is obtained.

Step 5. Below $B C$, make an acute angle $\angle C B X$.
Step 6. Along $B X$, mark off five points $B_{1}, B_{2}, B_{3}, B_{4}$ and $B_{5}$ such that $B B_{1}=B_{1} B_{2}=B_{2} B_{3}=B_{3} B_{4}=B_{4} B_{5}$.

Step 7. Join $B_{5} C$.
Step 8. From $B_{3}$, draw $B_{3} D \| B_{5} C$ (by making an angle $\angle B B_{3} D=\angle B B_{5} C$ ), meeting $B C$ at $D$.
Step 9. From $D$, draw $D E \| C A$ (by making an angle $\angle B D E=\angle B C A$ ), meeting $A B$ at $E$.
Then, $\triangle E B D$ is the required triangle, each of whose sides is $\frac{3}{5}$ of the corresponding side of $\triangle A B C$.
PROOF Since $D E \| C A$, we have $\triangle A B C \sim \triangle E B D$.

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{E B}{A B}=\frac{D E}{C A}=\frac{B D}{B C}=\frac{3}{5} .
$$

EXAMPLE 3 Draw a triangle $A B C$ in which $A B=5 \mathrm{~cm}, B C=6 \mathrm{~cm}$ and $\angle A B C=60^{\circ}$. Then, construct a triangle whose sides are $\frac{5}{7}$ times the corresponding sides of $\triangle A B C$.
[CBSE 2011, '15]

## STEPS OF CONSTRUCTION

Step 1. Draw a line segment $B C=6 \mathrm{~cm}$.
Step 2. At $B$, construct $\angle C B X=60^{\circ}$.
Step 3. With $B$ as centre and radius 5 cm , draw an arc cutting ray $B X$ at $A$.

Step 4. Join $A C$.
Thus, $\triangle A B C$ is obtained.
![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-10/Geometry/Constructions/class-10-Ch-09-A-BooK-004.jpg)

Step 5. Below $B C$, make an acute angle $\angle C B Y$.
Step 6. Along $B Y$, mark off seven points $B_{1}, B_{2}, B_{3}, B_{4}, B_{5}, B_{6}$ and $B_{7}$ such that $B B_{1}=B_{1} B_{2}=B_{2} B_{3}=B_{3} B_{4}=B_{4} B_{5}=B_{5} B_{6}=B_{6} B_{7}$.

Step 7. Join $B_{7} C$.
Step 8. From $B_{5}$, draw $B_{5} D \| B_{7} C$, meeting $B C$ at $D$.
Step 9. From $D$, draw $D E \| C A$, meeting $A B$ at $E$.
Then, $\triangle E B D$ is the required triangle, each of whose sides is $\frac{5}{7}$ of the corresponding side of $\triangle A B C$.
proof Since $D E \| C A$, we have $\triangle A B C \sim \triangle E B D$.

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{E B}{A B}=\frac{D E}{C A}=\frac{B D}{B C}=\frac{5}{7} .
$$

EXAMPLE 4 Draw a triangle $A B C$ with side $B C=7 \mathrm{~cm}, \angle B=45^{\circ}$, and $\angle A=105^{\circ}$. Then, construct a triangle whose sides are $\frac{4}{3}$ times the corresponding sides of $\triangle A B C$.

## STEPS OF CONSTRUCTION

Step 1. Draw a line segment $B C=7 \mathrm{~cm}$.
Step 2. At $B$, construct $\angle C B X=45^{\circ}$ and at $C$, construct

$$
\angle B C Y=180^{\circ}-(\angle B+\angle A)=180^{\circ}-\left(45^{\circ}+105^{\circ}\right)=30^{\circ} .
$$

Suppose $B X$ and $C Y$ intersect at $A$.
$\triangle A B C$ is thus obtained.
![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-10/Geometry/Constructions/class-10-Ch-09-A-BooK-005.jpg)

Step 3. Below $B C$, make an acute angle $\angle C B Z$.
Step 4. Along $B Z$, mark off four points $B_{1}, B_{2}, B_{3}$ and $B_{4}$ such that $B B_{1}=B_{1} B_{2}=B_{2} B_{3}=B_{3} B_{4}$.

Step 5. Join $B_{3} C$.
Step 6. From $B_{4}$, draw $B_{4} D \| B_{3} C$, meeting $B C$ (produced) at $D$.
Step 7. From $D$, draw $D E \| C A$, meeting ray $B X$ at $E$.
Then, $\triangle E B D$ is the required triangle, each of whose sides is $\frac{4}{3}$ times the corresponding side of $\triangle A B C$.
proof Since $C A \| D E$, we have $\triangle A B C \sim \triangle E B D$.

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{E B}{A B}=\frac{D E}{C A}=\frac{B D}{B C}=\frac{4}{3} .
$$

EXAMPLE 5 Construct an isosceles triangle whose base is 6 cm and altitude 4 cm . Then, construct another triangle whose sides are $\frac{3}{4}$ times the corresponding sides of the first triangle.
[CBSE 2010, '15]

## STEPS OF CONSTRUCTION

Step 1. Draw a line segment $B C=6 \mathrm{~cm}$.
Step 2. Draw the perpendicular bisector $X Y$ of $B C$, cutting $B C$ at $D$.
Step 3. With $D$ as centre and radius 4 cm , draw an arc cutting $X Y$ at $A$.

Step 4. Join $A B$ and $A C$.
Thus, isosceles $\triangle A B C$ having base 6 cm and altitude 4 cm is obtained.
![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-10/Geometry/Constructions/class-10-Ch-09-A-BooK-006.jpg)

Step 5. Below $B C$, make an acute angle $\angle C B Z$.
Step 6. Along $B Z$, mark off four points $B_{1}, B_{2}, B_{3}$ and $B_{4}$ such that $B B_{1}=B_{1} B_{2}=B_{2} B_{3}=B_{3} B_{4}$.

Step 7. Join $B_{4} C$.
Step 8. From $B_{3}$, draw $B_{3} E \| B_{4} C$, meeting $B C$ at $E$.
Step 9. From $E$, draw $E F \| C A$, meeting $B A$ at $F$.
Then, $\triangle F B E$ is the required triangle, each of whose sides is $\frac{3}{4}$ times the corresponding side of $\triangle A B C$.
proof Since $E F \| C A$, we have $\triangle F B E \sim \triangle A B C$.

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{F B}{A B}=\frac{E F}{C A}=\frac{B E}{B C}=\frac{3}{4} .
$$

EXAMPLE 6 Construct a $\triangle A B C$ in which $A B=4 \mathrm{~cm}, \angle B=60^{\circ}$ and altitude $C L =3 \mathrm{~cm}$. Construct a $\triangle A D E$ similar to $\triangle A B C$ such that each side of $\triangle A D E$ is $\frac{3}{2}$ times that of the corresponding side of $\triangle A B C$.

## STEPS OF CONSTRUCTION

Step 1. Draw a line segment $A B=4 \mathrm{~cm}$.
Step 2. Construct $\angle A B P=60^{\circ}$.
Step 3. Draw a line $G H \| A B$ at a distance of 3 cm from $A B$, intersecting $B P$ at $C$.

Step 4. Join $C A$.
Thus, $\triangle A B C$ is obtained.
Step 5. Extend $A B$ to $D$ such that $A D=\frac{3}{2} A B=\left(\frac{3}{2} \times 4\right) \mathrm{cm}=6 \mathrm{~cm}$.
![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-10/Geometry/Constructions/class-10-Ch-09-A-BooK-007.jpg)

Step 6. Draw $D E \| B C$, cutting $A C$ produced at $E$.
Then, $\triangle A D E$ is the required triangle similar to $\triangle A B C$ such that each side of $\triangle A D E$ is $\frac{3}{2}$ times the corresponding side of $\triangle A B C$.

PROOF Since $D E \| B C$, we have $\triangle A D E \sim \triangle A B C$.

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{A D}{A B}=\frac{D E}{B C}=\frac{A E}{A C}=\frac{3}{2} .
$$
