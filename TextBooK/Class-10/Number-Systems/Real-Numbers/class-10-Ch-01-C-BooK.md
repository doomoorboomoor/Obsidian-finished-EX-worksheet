## Rational Numbers

- **Natural Numbers:** Counting numbers $1, 2, 3, 4, \dots$, etc., are known as natural numbers.
- **Whole Numbers:** All counting numbers together with $0$ form the collection of whole numbers. Thus, $0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, \dots$, etc., are whole numbers.
- **Integers:** All counting numbers, negatives of counting numbers and $0$ form the collection of all integers. Thus, $\dots, -4, -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, \dots$, etc., are integers.
- **Rational Numbers:** The numbers of the form $\frac{p}{q}$, where $p$ and $q$ are integers, and $q \neq 0$ are called rational numbers.
- **Rational Numbers in Decimal Form:** Every rational number when expressed in decimal form is expressible either in **terminating** or in **nonterminating repeating** decimal form.

---

## An Important Observation

**To Test Whether a Given Rational Number is a Terminating or Repeating Decimal**

Let $x$ be a rational number whose simplest form is $\frac{p}{q}$, where $p$ and $q$ are integers and $q \neq 0$. Then,
- (i) $x$ is a **terminating decimal** only when $q$ is of the form $(2^{m} \times 5^{n})$ for some non-negative integers $m$ and $n$.
- (ii) $x$ is a **nonterminating repeating decimal**, if $q \neq (2^{m} \times 5^{n})$.

---

## An Important Test

Let $\frac{p}{q}$ be the simplest form of a given rational number.
- (i) If $q=(2^{m} \times 5^{n})$ for some non-negative integers $m$ and $n$ then $\frac{p}{q}$ is a **terminating decimal**.
- (ii) If $q \neq (2^{m} \times 5^{n})$ then $\frac{p}{q}$ is a **nonterminating repeating decimal**.

## Solved Examples

### Example: 1

Without actual division, show that each of the following rational numbers is a terminating decimal. Express each in decimal form.
1. $\frac{31}{\left(2^{2} \times 5^{3}\right)}$
2. $\frac{33}{50}$
3. $\frac{41}{1000}$
4. $\frac{17}{625}$

#### Solution:

1. The given number is $\frac{31}{\left(2^{2} \times 5^{3}\right)}$.
   Clearly, none of 2 and 5 is a factor of 31. So, the given rational is in its simplest form.
   The denominator $(2^{2} \times 5^{3})$ is of the form $(2^{m} \times 5^{n})$. So, the given number is a terminating decimal.
   $$
   \frac{31}{\left(2^{2} \times 5^{3}\right)}=\frac{31 \times 2}{\left(2^{3} \times 5^{3}\right)}=\frac{62}{(2 \times 5)^{3}}=\frac{62}{(10)^{3}}=\frac{62}{1000} =0.062
   $$

2. The given number is $\frac{33}{50}$.
   Now, $50=(2 \times 5^{2})$ and none of 2 and 5 is a factor of 33. So, the given rational number is in its simplest form.
   The denominator $50=(2 \times 5^{2})=(2^{m} \times 5^{n})$, where $m=1$ and $n=2$. So, the given number is a terminating decimal.
   $$
   \frac{33}{50}=\frac{33}{\left(2 \times 5^{2}\right)}=\frac{33 \times 2}{\left(2^{2} \times 5^{2}\right)}=\frac{66}{(2 \times 5)^{2}} = \frac{66}{(10)^{2}}=\frac{66}{100}=0.66
   $$

3. The given number is $\frac{41}{1000}$.
   Now, $1000=(8 \times 125)=(2^{3} \times 5^{3})$. Clearly, none of 2 and 5 is a factor of 41. So, the given number is in its simplest form.
   The denominator $1000=(2^{3} \times 5^{3})$ which is of the form $(2^{m} \times 5^{n})$, where $m=3$ and $n=3$. So, the given number is a terminating decimal.
   And, $\frac{41}{1000}=0.041$.

4. The given number is $\frac{17}{625}$.
   And, $625=5^{4}$ and 5 is not a factor of 17. So, the given number is in its simplest form.
   The denominator $625=5^{4}$ is of the form $(2^{m} \times 5^{n})$, where $m=0, n=4$. So, the given number is a terminating decimal.
   $$
   \frac{17}{625}=\frac{17}{5^{4}}=\frac{17 \times 2^{4}}{5^{4} \times 2^{4}}=\frac{17 \times 16}{(5 \times 2)^{4}}=\frac{272}{(10)^{4}} =\frac{272}{10000}=0.0272
   $$

### Example: 2

Without actual division, show that each of the following rational numbers is a nonterminating repeating decimal.
1. $\frac{121}{\left(2^{3} \times 3^{2} \times 7^{5}\right)}$
2. $\frac{17}{90}$
3. $\frac{53}{343}$
4. $\frac{66}{180}$

#### Solution:

1. Given number is $\frac{121}{\left(2^{3} \times 3^{2} \times 7^{5}\right)}$.
   Clearly, none of 2, 3 and 7 is a factor of 121. So, the given rational number is in its simplest form. And, $(2^{3} \times 3^{2} \times 7^{5}) \neq (2^{m} \times 5^{n})$.
   $\therefore \frac{121}{\left(2^{3} \times 3^{2} \times 7^{5}\right)}$ is a nonterminating repeating decimal.

2. Given number is $\frac{17}{90}$.
   And, $90=(2 \times 3^{2} \times 5)$. Clearly, none of 2, 3 and 5 is a factor of 17.
   $\therefore \frac{17}{90}$ is in its simplest form. Also, $90=(2 \times 3^{2} \times 5) \neq (2^{m} \times 5^{n})$.
   $\therefore \frac{17}{90}$ is a nonterminating repeating decimal.

3. Given number is $\frac{53}{343}$.
   Now, $343=7^{3}$ and 7 is not a factor of 53.
   $\therefore \frac{53}{343}$ is in its simplest form. Also, $343=7^{3} \neq (2^{m} \times 5^{n})$.
   $\therefore \frac{53}{343}$ is a nonterminating repeating decimal.

4. Given number is $\frac{66}{180}$ and $\text{HCF}(66, 180)=6$.
   $\therefore \frac{66}{180}=\frac{66 \div 6}{180 \div 6}=\frac{11}{30}$.
   Now, $30=(2 \times 3 \times 5)$ and none of $2, 3, 5$ is a factor of 11.
   $\therefore \frac{11}{30}$ is in its simplest form. Also, $30=(2 \times 3 \times 5) \neq (2^{m} \times 5^{n})$.
   $\therefore \frac{11}{30}$ and hence $\frac{66}{180}$ is a nonterminating repeating decimal.

### Example: 3

The decimal expansion of the rational number $\frac{43}{2^{4} \cdot 5^{3}}$, will terminate after how many places of decimals? [CBSE 2009]

#### Solution:

We have
$$
\frac{43}{2^{4} \cdot 5^{3}}=\frac{43 \times 5}{2^{4} \times 5^{4}}=\frac{215}{(2 \times 5)^{4}}=\frac{215}{10^{4}}=\frac{215}{10000}=0.0215
$$
So, it will terminate after 4 places of decimals.

### Example: 4

Express each of the following as a rational number in simplest form.
1. $0 . \overline{6}$
2. $1 . \overline{8}$
3. $0.1 \overline{6}$

#### Solution:

1. Let $x=0 . \overline{6}$. Then,
   $$
   \begin{align*}
   & x=0.666 \ldots \tag{i}\\
   \therefore \quad & 10 x=6.666 \ldots \tag{ii}
   \end{align*}
   $$
   On subtracting (i) from (ii), we get
   $$
   9 x=6 \Rightarrow x=\frac{6}{9}=\frac{2}{3}
   $$
   Hence, $0 . \overline{6}=\frac{2}{3}$.

2. Let $x=1 . \overline{8}$. Then,
   $$
   \begin{align*}
   & x=1.888 \ldots \tag{i}\\
   \therefore \quad & 10 x=18.888 \ldots \tag{ii}
   \end{align*}
   $$
   On subtracting (i) from (ii), we get
   $$
   9 x=17 \Rightarrow x=\frac{17}{9}=1 \frac{8}{9}
   $$
   Hence, $1 . \overline{8}=1 \frac{8}{9}$.

3. Let $x=0.1 \overline{6}$. Then,
   $$
   \begin{equation*}
   x=0.1666 \ldots \tag{i}
   \end{equation*}
   $$
   $$
   \begin{equation*}
   \therefore \quad 10 x=1.6666 \ldots \tag{ii}
   \end{equation*}
   $$
   And, $100 x=16.6666 \ldots$ (iii)
   
   On subtracting (ii) from (iii), we get
   $$
   90 x=15 \Rightarrow x=\frac{15}{90}=\frac{1}{6}
   $$
   $\therefore 0.1 \overline{6}=\frac{1}{6}$.

### Example: 5

Express $0 . \overline{32}$ as a fraction in simplest form.

#### Solution:

Let $x=0 . \overline{32}$. Then,
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
& x=0.3232 \ldots \tag{i} \\
\therefore \quad & 100 x=32.3232 \ldots \tag{ii}
\end{array}
$$
On subtracting (i) from (ii), we get
$$
99 x=32 \Rightarrow x=\frac{32}{99}
$$
Hence, $0 . \overline{32}=\frac{32}{99}$.

### Example: 6

Express $0.2 \overline{54}$ as a fraction in simplest form.

#### Solution:

Let $x=0.2 \overline{54}$. Then,
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
& x=0.2545454 \ldots \tag{i} \\
\therefore \quad & 10 x=2.545454 \ldots \tag{ii} \\
\text{and} & 1000 x=254.545454 \ldots \tag{iii}
\end{array}
$$
On subtracting (ii) from (iii), we get
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 990 x=252 \Rightarrow x=\frac{252}{990}=\frac{126}{495}=\frac{42}{165}=\frac{14}{55} \\
\therefore \quad & 0.2 \overline{54}=\frac{14}{55}
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example: 7

Show that each of the following numbers is rational. What can you say about the prime factors of their denominators?
1. $23.123456789$
2. $32 . \overline{123456789}$

#### Solution:

1. Clearly, the given number $23.123456789$ is a **terminating decimal**. So, it is rational and the prime factors of its denominator are 2 or 5 or both.
2. Clearly, the given number $32 . \overline{123456789}$ is a **nonterminating repeating decimal**. So, it is rational and the prime factors of its denominator are other than 2 or 5 also.
