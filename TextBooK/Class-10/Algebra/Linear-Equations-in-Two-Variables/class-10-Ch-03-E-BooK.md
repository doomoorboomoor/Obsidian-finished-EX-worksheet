### Problems on Money Matters

#### Example 1
**8 chairs and 5 tables for a classroom cost $₹ 10500$, while 5 chairs and 3 tables cost $₹ 6450$. Find the cost of each chair and that of each table.**

**Solution:**
Let the cost of each chair be $₹ x$ and that of each table be $₹ y$.

Then, $8 x+5 y=10500$
and $5 x+3 y=6450$.

On multiplying (ii) by 5 and (i) by 3 and subtracting the results, we get

$$
25 x-24 x=32250-31500 \Rightarrow x=750
$$

Putting $x=750$ in (i), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& 8 \times 750+5 y=10500 \Rightarrow 6000+5 y=10500 \\
\Rightarrow & 5 y=(10500-6000)=4500 \\
\Rightarrow & y=900
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad$ cost of each chair $=₹ 750$ and cost of each table $=₹ 900$.

#### Example 2
**The coach of a cricket team buys 7 bats and 6 balls for ₹ 13200. Later, he buys 3 bats and 5 balls for ₹ 5900. Find the cost of each bat and each ball.**

**Solution:**
Let the cost of each bat be $₹ x$ and the cost of each ball be $₹ y$.
Then, $7 x+6 y=13200$.

And, $3 x+5 y=5900$.

On multiplying (i) by 5, (ii) by 6 and subtracting the results, we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& 35 x-18 x=66000-35400 \Rightarrow 17 x=30600 \\
\Rightarrow & x=\frac{30600}{17}=1800
\end{aligned}
$$

Putting $x=1800$ in (ii), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& 5400+5 y=5900 \Rightarrow 5 y=5900-5400 \\
\Rightarrow & 5 y=500 \\
\Rightarrow & y=100
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad$ cost of each bat $=₹ 1800$ and cost of each ball $=₹ 100$.

#### Example 3
**37 pens and 53 pencils together cost ₹ 955, while 53 pens and 37 pencils together cost $₹ 1115$. Find the cost of a pen and that of a pencil.**

**Solution:**
Let the cost of each pen be $₹ x$ and that of a pencil be $₹ y$.
Then, $37 x+53 y=955$.

And, $53 x+37 y=1115$.

Adding (i) and (ii), we get

$$
\begin{align*}
& 90 x+90 y=2070 \Rightarrow 90(x+y)=2070 \\
\Rightarrow & x+y=\frac{2070}{90} \\
\Rightarrow & x+y=23 \tag{iii}
\end{align*}
$$

On subtracting (i) from (ii), we get

$$
\begin{align*}
& 16 x-16 y=160 \Rightarrow 16(x-y)=160 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & (x-y)=10 . \tag{iv}
\end{align*}
$$

Adding (iii) and (iv), we get $2 x=33 \Rightarrow x=\frac{33}{2}=16.50$.
Subtracting (iv) from (iii), we get $2 y=13 \Rightarrow y=\frac{13}{2}=6.50$.
$\therefore \quad$ cost of each pen $=₹ 16.50$ and cost of each pencil $=₹ 6.50$.

#### Example 4
**Taxi charges in a city consist of fixed charges and the remaining depending upon the distance travelled in kilometres. If a person travels 60 km , he pays ₹ 960, and for travelling 80 km , he pays $₹ 1260$. Find the fixed charges and the rate per kilometre.**

**Solution:**

Then, $x+60 y=960$.

And, $x+80 y=1260$.

On subtracting (i) from (ii), we get

$$
20 y=300 \Rightarrow y=\frac{300}{20} \Rightarrow y=15
$$

Putting $y=15$ in (i), we get

$$
x+(60 \times 15)=960 \Rightarrow x=960-900 \Rightarrow x=60 .
$$

$\therefore \quad$ fixed charges $=₹ 60$ and the rate per $\mathrm{km}=₹ 15$ per km.

#### Example 6
**The monthly incomes of $A$ and $B$ are in the ratio $8: 7$ and their expenditures are in the ratio $19: 16$. If each saves $₹ 5000$ per month, find the monthly income of each.**

**Solution:**
Let the monthly incomes of A and B be $₹ 8 x$ and $₹ 7 x$ respectively, and let their expenditures be $₹ 19 y$ and $₹ 16 y$ respectively.

Then, A's monthly savings $=₹(8 x-19 y)$.
And, B's monthly savings $=₹(7 x-16 y)$.
But, the monthly saving of each is ₹ 5000 .

$$
\begin{equation*}
\therefore \quad 8 x-19 y=5000 \tag{i}
\end{equation*}
$$

and $7 x-16 y=5000$.

Multiplying (ii) by 19, (i) by 16 and subtracting the results, we get
$(19 \times 7-16 \times 8) x=(19 \times 5000-16 \times 5000)$
$\Rightarrow \quad(133-128) x=5000 \times(19-16)$
$\Rightarrow 5 x=15000 \Rightarrow x=3000$.
$\therefore \quad$ A's monthly income $=₹(8 x)=₹(8 \times 3000)=₹ 24000$.
And, B's monthly income $=₹(7 x)=₹(7 \times 3000)=₹ 21000$.

#### Example 7
**On selling a TV at 5\% gain and a fridge at $10 \%$ gain, a shopkeeper gains ₹ 3250. But, if he sells the TV at $10 \%$ gain and the fridge at $5 \%$ loss, he gains $₹ 1500$. Find the actual cost price of $T V$ and that of the fridge.**

**Solution:**
Let the cost price of the TV set be $₹ x$ and that of the fridge be ₹ $y$.

Then, total CP of TV and fridge $=₹(x+y)$.
SP in first case $=₹\left(\frac{105 x}{100}+\frac{110 y}{100}\right)=₹\left(\frac{21 x}{20}+\frac{11 y}{10}\right)$.
$\therefore \quad$ gain in this case $=₹\left\{\left(\frac{21 x}{20}+\frac{11 y}{10}\right)-(x+y)\right\}=₹\left(\frac{x}{20}+\frac{y}{10}\right)$.

So, $\frac{x}{20}+\frac{y}{10}=3250 \Rightarrow x+2 y=65000$.

SP in second case $=₹\left(\frac{110 x}{100}+\frac{95 y}{100}\right)=₹\left(\frac{11 x}{10}+\frac{19 y}{20}\right)$.
$\therefore \quad$ gain in this case $=₹\left\{\left(\frac{11 x}{10}+\frac{19 y}{20}\right)-(x+y)\right\}=₹\left(\frac{x}{10}-\frac{y}{20}\right)$.
So, $\frac{x}{10}-\frac{y}{20}=1500 \Rightarrow 2 x-y=30000$.

Multiplying (ii) by 2 and adding the result with (i), we get

$$
5 x=60000+65000 \Rightarrow 5 x=125000 \Rightarrow x=25000 .
$$

Putting $x=25000$ in (i), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& 25000+2 y=65000 \Rightarrow 2 y=65000-25000=40000 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & y=20000 . \\
\therefore \quad & x=25000 \text { and } y=20000 .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, the CP of the TV set is ₹ 25000 and that of the fridge is ₹ 20000 .

#### Example 8
**A man invested an amount at $12 \%$ per annum simple interest and another amount at $10 \%$ per annum simple interest. He received an annual interest of ₹ 2600. But, if he had interchanged the amounts invested, he would have received ₹ 140 less. What amounts did he invest at the different rates?**

**Solution:**
Let the amount invested at $12 \%$ be $₹ x$ and that invested at $10 \%$ be ₹ $y$. Then,
total annual interest

$$
\begin{align*}
& =₹\left(\frac{x \times 12 \times 1}{100}+\frac{y \times 10 \times 1}{100}\right)=₹\left(\frac{6 x+5 y}{50}\right) . \\
\therefore \quad \frac{6 x+5 y}{50} & =2600 \Rightarrow 6 x+5 y=130000 . \tag{i}
\end{align*}
$$

Again, the amount invested at $12 \%$ is $₹ y$ and that invested at $10 \%$ is $₹ x$.

Total annual interest at the new rates

$$
=₹\left(\frac{y \times 12 \times 1}{100}+\frac{x \times 10 \times 1}{100}\right)=₹\left(\frac{6 y+5 x}{50}\right) .
$$

But, interest received at the new rates $=₹(2600-140)=₹ 2460$.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\therefore \quad \frac{6 y+5 x}{50}=2460 \Rightarrow 5 x+6 y=123000 . \tag{ii}
\end{equation*}
$$

Adding (i) and (ii), we get

$$
\begin{align*}
& 11 x+11 y=253000 \Rightarrow 11(x+y)=253000 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & x+y=23000 \tag{iii}
\end{align*}
$$

Subtracting (ii) from (i), we get

$$
\begin{equation*}
x-y=7000 \tag{iv}
\end{equation*}
$$

Adding (iii) and (iv), we get $2 x=30000 \Rightarrow x=15000$.
Putting $x=15000$ in (i), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& 15000+y=23000 \Rightarrow y=23000-15000=8000 . \\
\therefore \quad & x=15000 \text { and } y=8000 .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, the amount at $12 \%$ is $₹ 15000$ and that at $10 \%$ is $₹ 8000$.

#### Example 9
**Each one of $A$ and $B$ has some money. If $A$ gives $₹ 30$ to $B$ then $B$ will have twice the money left with $A$. But, if $B$ gives $₹ 10$ to $A$ then $A$ will have thrice as much as is left with $B$. How much money does each have?**

**Solution:**
Suppose A and B have $₹ x$ and $₹ y$ respectively.

**Case I:** When $A$ gives $₹ 30$ to $B$.
Then, money with $\mathrm{A}=₹(x-30)$
and money with $B=₹(y+30)$.
$\therefore \quad(y+30)=2(x-30) \Rightarrow 2 x-y=90$.

**Case II:** When $B$ gives $₹ 10$ to $A$.
Then, money with $\mathrm{A}=₹(x+10)$
and money with $\mathrm{B}=₹(y-10)$.
$\therefore \quad(x+10)=3(y-10) \Rightarrow x-3 y=-40$.

On multiplying (ii) by 2 and subtracting the result from (i), we get

$$
5 y=170 \Rightarrow y=\frac{170}{5}=34
$$

Putting $y=34$ in (i), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& 2 x-34=90 \Rightarrow 2 x=(90+34)=124 \Rightarrow x=\frac{124}{2}=62 . \\
\therefore \quad & x=62 \text { and } y=34 .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, A has $₹ 62$ and $B$ has $₹ 34$.

### Problems on Numbers

#### Example 10
**The students of a class are made to stand in rows. If 4 students are extra in each row, there would be 2 rows less. If 4 students are less in each row, there would be 4 rows more. Find the number of students in the class.**

**Solution:**
Let the number of rows be $x$ and the number of students in each row be $y$.

Then, the total number of students $=x y$.

**Case I:** When there are 4 more students in each row.
Then, number of students in each row $=(y+4)$.
And, number of rows $=(x-2)$.
Total number of students $=(x-2)(y+4)$.

$$
\begin{align*}
\therefore \quad(x-2)(y+4)=x y & \Rightarrow 4 x-2 y=8 \\
& \Rightarrow 2 x-y=4 \tag{i}
\end{align*}
$$

**Case II:** When 4 students are removed from each row.
Then, number of students in each row $=(y-4)$.
And, number of rows $=(x+4)$.
Total number of students $=(x+4)(y-4)$.

$$
\begin{align*}
\therefore \quad(x+4)(y-4)=x y & \Rightarrow 4 y-4 x=16 \\
& \Rightarrow 4(y-x)=16 \\
& \Rightarrow(y-x)=4 . \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$

Adding (i) and (ii), we get $x=8$.
Putting $x=8$ in (ii), we get

$$
y-8=4 \Rightarrow y=12
$$

Thus, $x=8$ and $y=12$.
This shows that there are 8 rows and there are 12 students in each row.
Hence, the number of students in the class $=x y=8 \times 12=96$.

**Solution:**

and $x^{2}-y^{2}=256000$.

$$
\begin{equation*}

\end{equation*}
$$

Adding (i) and (iii), we get

$$
2 x=1256 \Rightarrow x=628
$$

Substituting $x=628$ in (i), we get $y=372$.
Hence, the required numbers are 628 and 372.

#### Example 12
**If three times the larger of two numbers is divided by the smaller one, we get 4 as the quotient and 3 as the remainder. Also, if seven times the smaller number is divided by the larger one, we get 5 as the quotient and 1 as the remainder. Find the numbers.**

**Solution:**
Let the larger number be $x$ and the smaller one be $y$.
We know that

$$
\text { dividend }=(\text { divisor } \times \text { quotient })+\text { remainder } .
$$

Using the above result and the given conditions, we have

$$
\begin{align*}
3 x & =4 y+3 \Rightarrow 3 x-4 y=3  \tag{i}\\
\text { and } 7 y=5 x+1 & \Rightarrow 5 x-7 y=-1 . \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$

Multiplying (i) by 5, (ii) by 3 and subtracting, we get $y=18$.
Putting $y=18$ in (i), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& 3 x-(4 \times 18)=3 \Rightarrow 3 x-72=3 \\
\Rightarrow & 3 x=75 \Rightarrow x=25 .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, the required numbers are 25 and 18.

#### Example 13
**The sum of two numbers is 8 and the sum of their reciprocals is $\frac{8}{15}$. Find the numbers.**
[CBSE 2009]

**Solution:**
Let the required numbers be $x$ and $y$.
Then, $x+y=8$.

And, $\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{y}=\frac{8}{15} \Rightarrow \frac{x+y}{x y}=\frac{8}{15}$

$$
\begin{align*}
& \Rightarrow \frac{8}{x y}=\frac{8}{15}  \tag{i}\\
& \Rightarrow x y=15
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad(x-y) & =\sqrt{(x+y)^{2}-4 x y} \\
& =\sqrt{8^{2}-4 \times 15}=\sqrt{64-60}=\sqrt{4}= \pm 2
\end{aligned}
$$

Thus, we have

$$
\left.\begin{array}{l}
x+y=8 \\
x-y=2
\end{array} \quad \begin{array}{l}
\ldots \text { (i) }  \tag{iv}\\
\ldots \text { (ii) }
\end{array}\right\} \text { or }\left\{\begin{array}{l}
x+y=8 \\
x-y=-2
\end{array}\right.
$$

On solving (i) and (ii), we get $x=5$ and $y=3$.
On solving (iii) and (iv), we get $x=3$ and $y=5$.
Hence, the required numbers are 5 and 3 .

### Problems on Ages

#### Example 23
**Five years ago, $A$ was thrice as old as $B$ and ten years later $A$ shall be twice as old as $B$. What are the present ages of $A$ and $B$ ?** [CBSE 2002C]

**Solution:**
Let the present ages of B and A be $x$ years and $y$ years respectively. Then,

B's age 5 years ago $=(x-5)$ years
and A's age 5 years ago $=(y-5)$ years.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\therefore \quad(y-5)=3(x-5) \Rightarrow 3 x-y=10 . \tag{i}
\end{equation*}
$$

B's age 10 years hence $=(x+10)$ years.
A's age 10 years hence $=(y+10)$ years.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\therefore \quad(y+10)=2(x+10) \Rightarrow 2 x-y=-10 . \tag{ii}
\end{equation*}
$$

On subtracting (ii) from (i), we get $x=20$.
Putting $x=20$ in (i), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& (3 \times 20)-y=10 \Rightarrow y=60-10=50 . \\
\therefore & x=20 \text { and } y=50 .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, B's present age = 20 years
and A's present age $=50$ years.

### Problems on Time and Distance

#### Example 25
**A man travels 370 km , partly by train and partly by car. If he covers 250 km by train and the rest by car, it takes him 4 hours. But, if he travels 130 km by train and the rest by car, he takes 18 minutes longer. Find the speed of the train and that of the car.**

**Solution:**
Let the speed of the train be $x \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$ and that of the car be $y \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$.

**Case I:** Distance covered by train $=250 \mathrm{~km}$.
Distance covered by car $=(370-250) \mathrm{km}=120 \mathrm{~km}$.
Time taken to cover 250 km by train $=\frac{250}{x}$ hours.
Time taken to cover 120 km by car $=\frac{120}{y}$ hours.

Total time taken $=4$ hours.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\therefore \quad \frac{250}{x}+\frac{120}{y}=4 \Rightarrow \frac{125}{x}+\frac{60}{y}=2 . \tag{i}
\end{equation*}
$$

**Case II:** Distance covered by train $=130 \mathrm{~km}$.
Distance covered by car $=(370-130) \mathrm{km}=240 \mathrm{~km}$.
Time taken to cover 130 km by train $=\frac{130}{x}$ hours.
Time taken to cover 240 km by car $=\frac{240}{y}$ hours.
Total time taken $=4 \frac{18}{60}$ hours $=4 \frac{3}{10}$ hours $=\frac{43}{10}$ hours.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\therefore \quad \frac{130}{x}+\frac{240}{y}=\frac{43}{10} \Rightarrow \frac{1300}{x}+\frac{2400}{y}=43 . \tag{ii}
\end{equation*}
$$

Putting $\frac{1}{x}=u$ and $\frac{1}{y}=v$, equations (i) and (ii) become

$$
\begin{equation*}
125 u+60 v=2 \quad \ldots \text { (iii) and } 1300 u+2400 v=43 \text {. } \tag{iv}
\end{equation*}
$$

On multiplying (iii) by 40 and subtracting (iv) from the result, we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& 5000 u-1300 u=80-43 \Rightarrow 3700 u=37 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & u=\frac{37}{3700}=\frac{1}{100} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{x}=\frac{1}{100} \Rightarrow x=100 .
\end{aligned}
$$

Putting $u=\frac{1}{100}$ in (iv), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \left(1300 \times \frac{1}{100}\right)+2400 v=43 \Rightarrow 2400 v=43-13=30 \\
\Rightarrow & v=\frac{30}{2400}=\frac{1}{80} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{y}=\frac{1}{80} \Rightarrow y=80 . \\
\therefore & x=100 \text { and } y=80 .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, the speed of the train is $100 \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$ and that of the car is $80 \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$.

### Problems on Boats and Stream

#### Example 28
**A man can row downstream 20 km in 2 hours, and upstream 4 km in 2 hours. Find his speed of rowing in still water. Also, find the speed of the current.**

**Solution:**

But, speed downstream $=\frac{\text { distance }}{\text { time }}=\frac{20}{2} \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}=10 \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$.

$\therefore x+y=10 \quad \ldots$ (i) and $x-y=2$

Adding (i) and (ii), we get $2 x=12 \Rightarrow x=6$.
Putting $x=6$ in (i), we get $6+y=10 \Rightarrow y=4$.
Thus, $x=6$ and $y=4$.
Hence, the speed of the man in still water is $6 \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$ and the speed of the current is $4 \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$.

#### Example 29
**A boat goes 16 km upstream and 24 km downstream in 6 hours. Also, it covers 12 km upstream and 36 km downstream in the same time. Find the speed of the boat in still water and that of the stream.**

**Solution:**
Let the speed of the boat in still water be $x \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$ and the speed of the stream be $y \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$. Then,
speed upstream $=(x-y) \mathrm{km} / \mathrm{hr}$
and speed downstream $=(x+y) \mathrm{km} / \mathrm{hr}$.
Time taken to cover 16 km upstream $=\frac{16}{(x-y)}$ hours.
Time taken to cover 24 km downstream $=\frac{24}{(x+y)}$ hours.
Total time taken $=6$ hours.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\therefore \quad \frac{16}{x-y}+\frac{24}{x+y}=6 \tag{i}
\end{equation*}
$$

Again, time taken to cover 12 km upstream $=\frac{12}{(x-y)}$ hours.
Time taken to cover 36 km downstream $=\frac{36}{(x+y)}$ hours.
Total time taken $=6$ hours.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\therefore \quad \frac{12}{x-y}+\frac{36}{x+y}=6 \tag{ii}
\end{equation*}
$$

Putting $\frac{1}{(x-y)}=u$ and $\frac{1}{(x+y)}=v$ in (i) and (ii), we get

$$
\begin{align*}
& 16 u+24 v=6 \Rightarrow 8 u+12 v=3  \tag{iii}\\
& 12 u+36 v=6 \Rightarrow 2 u+6 v=1 \tag{iv}
\end{align*}
$$

On multiplying (iv) by 4 and subtracting (iii) from the result, we get

$$
\begin{align*}
& 12 v=1 \Rightarrow v=\frac{1}{12} \\
\Rightarrow & \frac{1}{x+y}=\frac{1}{12} \Rightarrow x+y=12 \tag{v}
\end{align*}
$$

On multiplying (iv) by 2 and subtracting the result from (iii), we get

$$
\begin{align*}
& 4 u=1 \Rightarrow u=\frac{1}{4} \\
\Rightarrow & \frac{1}{x-y}=\frac{1}{4} \Rightarrow x-y=4 \tag{vi}
\end{align*}
$$

On adding (v) and (vi), we get $2 x=16 \Rightarrow x=8$.
On subtracting (vi) from (v), we get $2 y=8 \Rightarrow y=4$.
$\therefore$ speed of the boat in still water $=8 \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$, and speed of the stream $=4 \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$.

### Problems on Time and Work

#### Example 30
**8 men and 12 boys can finish a piece of work in 5 days, while 6 men and 8 boys can finish it in 7 days. Find the time taken by 1 man alone and that by 1 boy alone to finish the work.**

**Solution:**
Suppose 1 man alone can finish the work in $x$ days and 1 boy alone can finish it in $y$ days.
Then, 1 man's 1 day's work $=\frac{1}{x}$.
And, 1 boy's 1 day's work $=\frac{1}{y}$.
8 men and 12 boys can finish the work in 5 days
$\Rightarrow \quad(8$ men's 1 day's work $)+(12$ boys' 1 day's work $)=\frac{1}{5}$
$\Rightarrow \frac{8}{x}+\frac{12}{y}=\frac{1}{5}$
$\Rightarrow 8 u+12 v=\frac{1}{5}$
$\ldots$ (i), where $\frac{1}{x}=u$ and $\frac{1}{y}=v$.
Again, 6 men and 8 boys can finish the work in 7 days
$\Rightarrow \quad(6$ men's 1 day's work $)+(8$ boys' 1 day's work $)=\frac{1}{7}$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\Rightarrow \frac{6}{x}+\frac{8}{y}=\frac{1}{7} \tag{ii}
\end{equation*}
$$

$\Rightarrow \quad 6 u+8 v=\frac{1}{7}$.

On multiplying (i) by 3, (ii) by 4 and subtracting the results, we get

$$
4 v=\left(\frac{3}{5}-\frac{4}{7}\right)=\frac{1}{35} \Rightarrow v=\frac{1}{140} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{y}=\frac{1}{140} \Rightarrow y=140 .
$$

On putting $v=\frac{1}{140}$ in (ii), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
6 u & =\left(\frac{1}{7}-\frac{8}{140}\right)=\frac{12}{140} \Rightarrow u=\left(\frac{12}{140} \times \frac{1}{6}\right)=\frac{1}{70} \\
\Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{x} & =\frac{1}{70} \Rightarrow x=70 .
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad$ one man alone can finish the work in 70 days, and one boy alone can finish the work in 140 days.

#### Example 31
**If the length of a rectangle is reduced by 5 units and its breadth is increased by 2 units then the area of the rectangle is reduced by 80 sq units. However, if we increase its length by 10 units and decrease the breadth by 5 units, its area is increased by 50 sq units. Find the length and breadth of the rectangle.**

**Solution:**
Let the length and breadth of the rectangle be $x$ units and $y$ units respectively.
Then, area of the rectangle $=x y$ sq units.

Then, new length $=(x-5)$ units
and new breadth $=(y+2)$ units.
$\therefore \quad$ new area $=(x-5)(y+2)$ sq units.
$\therefore \quad x y-(x-5)(y+2)=80 \Rightarrow 5 y-2 x=70$.

**Case II:** When the length is increased by 10 units and the breadth is decreased by 5 units.
Then, new length $=(x+10)$ units
and new breadth $=(y-5)$ units.
$\therefore \quad$ new area $=(x+10)(y-5)$ sq units.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\therefore \quad(x+10)(y-5)-x y=50 \tag{ii}
\end{equation*}
$$

Putting $y=30$ in (ii), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& (2 \times 30)-x=20 \Rightarrow 60-x=20 \Rightarrow x=(60-20)=40 . \\
\therefore & x=40 \text { and } y=30 .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, length $=40$ units and breadth $=30$ units.

#### Example 32
**In a $\triangle A B C, \angle C=3 \angle B=2(\angle A+\angle B)$. Find the angles.**

**Solution:**
Let $\angle A=x^{\circ}$ and $\angle B=y^{\circ}$.
Then, $\angle C=3 \angle B=(3 y)^{\circ}$.
Now, $\angle C=2(\angle A+\angle B)$
$\Rightarrow \quad 3 y=2(x+y) \Rightarrow 2 x-y=0$.

We know that the sum of the angles of a triangle is $180^{\circ}$.

$$
\begin{align*}
\therefore \quad \angle A+\angle B+\angle C=180^{\circ} & \Rightarrow x+y+3 y=180 \\
& \Rightarrow x+4 y=180 . \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$

On multiplying (i) by 4 and adding the result with (ii), we get

$$
8 x+x=180 \Rightarrow 9 x=180 \Rightarrow x=20
$$

Putting $x=20$ in (i), we get $y=(2 \times 20)=40$.
Thus, $x=20$ and $y=40$.
$\therefore \angle A=20^{\circ}, \angle B=40^{\circ}$ and $\angle C=\left(3 \times 40^{\circ}\right)=120^{\circ}$.

**Find the four angles of a cyclic quadrilateral $A B C D$ in which $\angle A=(2 x-1)^{\circ}, \angle B=(y+5)^{\circ}, \angle C=(2 y+15)^{\circ}$ and $\angle D=(4 x-7)^{\circ}$.**

**Solution:**
