## Solving Simultaneous Linear Equations by Algebraic Methods

---

### Substitution Method

**Method:**
Suppose we are given two linear equations in $x$ and $y$. For solving these equations by the **substitution method**, we proceed according to the following steps.

1.  Express $y$ in terms of $x$ in one of the given equations.
2.  Substitute this value of $y$ in terms of $x$ in the other equation. This gives a linear equation in $x$.
3.  Solve the linear equation in $x$ obtained in Step 2.
4.  Substitute this value of $x$ in the relation taken in Step 1 to obtain a linear equation in $y$.
5.  Solve the above linear equation in $y$ to get the value of $y$.

**Remark:** We may interchange the role of $x$ and $y$ in the above method.

---

### Example 1

Solve for $x$ and $y$, using the substitution method:
$$
2x+y=7, \quad 4x-3y+1=0
$$

#### Solution

The given system of equations is:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 2x+y=7 \tag{i} \\
& 4x-3y=-1 \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
From (i), we get $y=(7-2x)$.

Substituting $y=(7-2x)$ in (ii), we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 4x-3(7-2x)=-1 \\
\Rightarrow & 4x-21+6x=-1 \\
\Rightarrow & 10x=20 \\
\Rightarrow & x=2 .
\end{aligned}
$$
Substituting $x=2$ in (i), we get:
$$
2 \times 2+y=7 \Rightarrow y=7-4=3
$$
Hence, the solution is **$x=2, y=3$**.

### Example 2

Solve for $x$ and $y$, using the substitution method:
$$
\frac{3x}{2}-\frac{5y}{3}=-2, \quad \frac{x}{3}+\frac{y}{2}=\frac{13}{6}
$$

#### Solution

The given system of equations may be written as:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 9x-10y+12=0 \tag{i} \\
& 2x+3y-13=0 \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
From (ii), we get $y=\frac{13-2x}{3}$.

Substituting $y=\frac{13-2x}{3}$ in (i), we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 9x-\frac{10(13-2x)}{3}+12=0 \\
\Rightarrow & 27x-10(13-2x)+36=0 \\
\Rightarrow & 27x-130+20x+36=0 \\
\Rightarrow & 47x-94=0 \Rightarrow 47x=94 \Rightarrow x=\frac{94}{47}=2
\end{aligned}
$$
Substituting $x=2$ in (i), we get:
$$
9 \times 2-10y+12=0 \Rightarrow 10y=30 \Rightarrow y=\frac{30}{10}=3
$$
Hence, **$x=2$ and $y=3$** is the required solution.

### Elimination Method

**Method:**
In this method, we eliminate one of the unknown variables and proceed using the following steps.

1.  Multiply the given equations by suitable numbers so as to make the coefficients of one of the unknown variables numerically equal.
2.  If the numerically equal coefficients are opposite in sign then **add** the new equations. Otherwise, **subtract** them.
3.  The resulting equation is linear in one unknown variable. Solve it to get the value of one of the unknown quantities.
4.  Substitute this value in any of the given equations.
5.  Solve it to get the value of the other unknown variable.

---

### Example 3

Solve for $x$ and $y$ using the elimination method:
$$
10x+3y=75, \quad 6x-5y=11
$$

#### Solution

The given equations are:
$$
\begin{align*}
10x+3y &= 75 \tag{i} \\
6x-5y &= 11 \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Multiplying (i) by 5 and (ii) by 3, we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 50x+15y=375 \tag{iii} \\
& 18x-15y=33 \tag{iv}
\end{align*}
$$
Adding (iii) and (iv), we get:
$$
68x=408 \Rightarrow x=\frac{408}{68} \Rightarrow x=6.
$$
Putting $x=6$ in (i), we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& (10 \times 6)+3y=75 \Rightarrow 60+3y=75 \\
\Rightarrow & 3y=75-60 \Rightarrow 3y=15 \Rightarrow y=5.
\end{aligned}
$$
$\therefore$ **$x=6, y=5$**.

### Example 4

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
$$
11x+15y+23=0, \quad 7x-2y-20=0
$$

#### Solution

The given equations are:
$$
\begin{align*}
11x+15y &= -23 \tag{i} \\
7x-2y &= 20 \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Multiplying (i) by 2 and (ii) by 15 and adding the results, we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 22x+105x=-46+300 \\
\Rightarrow & 127x=254 \\
\Rightarrow & x=\frac{254}{127}=2
\end{aligned}
$$
Putting $x=2$ in (i), we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 22+15y=-23 \\
\Rightarrow & 15y=-23-22 \\
\Rightarrow & 15y=-45 \Rightarrow y=\frac{-45}{15}=-3.
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, **$x=2$ and $y=-3$**.

### Example 6

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
$$
\frac{4}{x}+3y=8, \quad \frac{6}{x}-4y=-5
$$

#### Solution

Putting $\frac{1}{x}=u$, the given equations become:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 4u+3y=8 \tag{i} \\
& 6u-4y=-5 \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Multiplying (i) by 4 and (ii) by 3 and adding the results, we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 16u+18u=32-15 \\
\Rightarrow & 34u=17 \\
\Rightarrow & u=\frac{17}{34}=\frac{1}{2}. \tag{iii}
\end{align*}
$$
Putting $u=\frac{1}{2}$ in (i), we get:
$$
\left(4 \times \frac{1}{2}\right)+3y=8 \Rightarrow 2+3y=8 \Rightarrow 3y=8-2 \Rightarrow 3y=6 \Rightarrow y=2.
$$
Now, $u=\frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{x}=\frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow x=2$.

Hence, **$x=2$ and $y=2$**.

### Example 10

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
$$
\frac{3a}{x}-\frac{2b}{y}+5=0, \quad \frac{a}{x}+\frac{3b}{y}-2=0 \quad (x \neq 0, y \neq 0)
$$

#### Solution

Putting $\frac{1}{x}=u$ and $\frac{1}{y}=v$, the given equations become:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 3au-2bv=-5 \tag{i} \\
& au+3bv=2 \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Multiplying (ii) by 3 and subtracting (i) from the result, we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 9bv+2bv=6+5 \\
\Rightarrow & 11bv=11 \Rightarrow v=\frac{11}{11b}=\frac{1}{b}
\end{aligned}
$$
Putting $v=\frac{1}{b}$ in (ii), we get:
$$
au+3=2 \Rightarrow au=-1 \Rightarrow u=\frac{-1}{a}
$$
Now, $u=\frac{-1}{a} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{x}=\frac{-1}{a} \Rightarrow -x=a \Rightarrow x=-a$.

And, $v=\frac{1}{b} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{y}=\frac{1}{b} \Rightarrow y=b$.

Hence, **$x=-a$ and $y=b$**.

### Example 11

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
$$
6x+3y=7xy, \quad 3x+9y=11xy \quad (x \neq 0, y \neq 0)
$$

#### Solution

On dividing each of the given equations by $xy$, we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
& \frac{6}{y}+\frac{3}{x}=7 \tag{i} \\
& \frac{3}{y}+\frac{9}{x}=11. \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Putting $\frac{1}{x}=u$ and $\frac{1}{y}=v$ in (i) and (ii), we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 6v+3u=7 \tag{iii} \\
& 3v+9u=11. \tag{iv}
\end{align*}
$$
Multiplying (iii) by 3 and subtracting (iv) from the result, we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 18v-3v=21-11 \\
\Rightarrow & 15v=10 \\
\Rightarrow & v=\frac{10}{15}=\frac{2}{3}
\end{aligned}
$$
Putting $v=\frac{2}{3}$ in (iii), we get:
$$
\left(6 \times \frac{2}{3}\right)+3u=7 \Rightarrow 4+3u=7 \Rightarrow 3u=3 \Rightarrow u=1
$$
Now, $u=1 \Rightarrow \frac{1}{x}=1 \Rightarrow x=1$.

And, $v=\frac{2}{3} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{y}=\frac{2}{3} \Rightarrow 2y=3 \Rightarrow y=\frac{3}{2}$.

Hence, **$x=1$ and $y=\frac{3}{2}$**.

### Example 12

Solve the following system of equations for $x$ and $y$:
$$
\frac{5}{x-1}+\frac{1}{y-2}=2, \quad \frac{6}{x-1}-\frac{3}{y-2}=1

\begin{align*}

\left(5 \times \frac{1}{3}\right)+v=2 \Rightarrow \frac{5}{3}+v=2 \Rightarrow v=2-\frac{5}{3} \Rightarrow v=\frac{1}{3}.

Now, $u=\frac{1}{3} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{x-1}=\frac{1}{3} \Rightarrow x-1=3 \Rightarrow x=4$.

### Example 14

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
$$
\frac{1}{2(2x+3y)}+\frac{12}{7(3x-2y)}=\frac{1}{2}, \quad \frac{7}{(2x+3y)}+\frac{4}{(3x-2y)}=2
$$
where $(2x+3y) \neq 0$ and $(3x-2y) \neq 0$.

#### Solution

Putting $\frac{1}{(2x+3y)}=u$ and $\frac{1}{3x-2y}=v$, the given equations become:
$$
\frac{u}{2}+\frac{12v}{7}=\frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow 7u+24v=7 \tag{i}
$$
and $7u+4v=2$. (ii)

Subtracting (ii) from (i), we get:
$$
20v=5 \Rightarrow v=\frac{5}{20}=\frac{1}{4}
$$
Putting $v=\frac{1}{4}$ in (i), we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 7u+\left(24 \times \frac{1}{4}\right)=7 \\
\Rightarrow & 7u+6=7 \Rightarrow 7u=1 \Rightarrow u=\frac{1}{7}.
\end{align*}
$$
Now, $u=\frac{1}{7} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{(2x+3y)}=\frac{1}{7} \Rightarrow 2x+3y=7$. (iii)

and $v=\frac{1}{4} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{(3x-2y)}=\frac{1}{4} \Rightarrow 3x-2y=4$. (iv)

Multiplying (iii) by 2 and (iv) by 3 and adding the results, we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 4x+9x=14+12 \\
\Rightarrow & 13x=26 \Rightarrow x=2
\end{aligned}
$$
Putting $x=2$ in (iii), we get:
$$
(2 \times 2)+3y=7 \Rightarrow 3y=(7-4)=3 \Rightarrow y=1
$$
Hence, **$x=2$ and $y=1$**.

### Example 16

Solve the following system of linear equations:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& (a-b)x+(a+b)y=a^{2}-2ab-b^{2} \\
& (a+b)(x+y)=a^{2}+b^{2}
\end{aligned}
$$

#### Solution

The given equations may be written as:
$$
\begin{align*}
& (a-b)x+(a+b)y=a^{2}-2ab-b^{2} \tag{i} \\
& (a+b)x+(a+b)y=a^{2}+b^{2} \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Subtracting (i) from (ii), we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \{(a+b)-(a-b)\}x=2ab+2b^{2} \\
\Rightarrow & 2bx=2b(a+b) \Rightarrow x=\frac{2b(a+b)}{2b} \\
\Rightarrow & x=(a+b)
\end{aligned}
$$
Putting $x=(a+b)$ in (ii), we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& (a+b)^{2}+(a+b)y=a^{2}+b^{2} \\
\Rightarrow & (a+b)y=(a^{2}+b^{2})-(a+b)^{2} \\
\Rightarrow & (a+b)y=(a^{2}+b^{2})-(a^{2}+b^{2}+2ab) \\
\Rightarrow & (a+b)y=-2ab \Rightarrow y=\frac{-2ab}{(a+b)}
\end{aligned}
$$
$\therefore$ **$x=(a+b)$ and $y=\frac{-2ab}{(a+b)}$** is the required solution.

### Example 17

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
$$
ax+by-a+b=0, \quad bx-ay-a-b=0.
$$

#### Solution

The given equations may be written as:
$$
\begin{align*}
& ax+by=a-b \tag{i} \\
& bx-ay=a+b \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Multiplying (i) by $a$ and (ii) by $b$ and adding the results, we get:
$$
(a^{2}+b^{2})x=(a^{2}+b^{2}) \Rightarrow x=1.
$$
Putting $x=1$ in (i), we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& (a \times 1)+by=a-b \\
\Rightarrow & a+by=a-b \\
\Rightarrow & by=-b \Rightarrow y=\frac{-b}{b}=-1.
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, **$x=1$ and $y=-1$**.

### Example 18

Solve the following system of linear equations:
$$
2(ax-by)+(a+4b)=0, \quad 2(bx+ay)+(b-4a)=0.
$$

#### Solution

The given equations may be written as:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 2ax-2by=-a-4b \tag{i} \\
& 2bx+2ay=4a-b \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Multiplying (i) by $a$ and (ii) by $b$ and adding, we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& (2a^{2}+2b^{2})x=(-a^{2}-b^{2}) \\
\Rightarrow & 2(a^{2}+b^{2})x = -(a^{2}+b^{2}) \Rightarrow x=\frac{-1}{2}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Putting $x=\frac{-1}{2}$ in (i), we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 2a \times\left(\frac{-1}{2}\right)-2by=-a-4b \\
\Rightarrow & -a-2by=-a-4b \\
\Rightarrow & 2by=4b \Rightarrow y=\frac{4b}{2b}=2.
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, **$x=\frac{-1}{2}$ and $y=2$**.

### Example 19

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
$$
\frac{ax}{b}-\frac{by}{a}=a+b, \quad ax-by=2ab.
$$

#### Solution

The given equations may be written as:
$$
\begin{align*}
& a^{2}x-b^{2}y=a^{2}b+ab^{2} \tag{i} \\
& ax-by=2ab \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Multiplying (ii) by $b$ and subtracting the result from (i), we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
(a^{2}-ab)x &= a^{2}b-ab^{2} \\
\Rightarrow (a^{2}-ab)x &= b(a^{2}-ab) \Rightarrow x=b.
\end{aligned}
$$
Putting $x=b$ in (ii), we get:
$$
ab-by=2ab \Rightarrow by=-ab \Rightarrow y=\frac{-ab}{b}=-a
$$
Hence, **$x=b$ and $y=-a$**.

## Special Case: Interchanged Coefficients

When the coefficients of $x$ and $y$ in one equation are interchanged in the other.

**Method:**

1.  **Add** the given equations and from it obtain an equation of the form $x+y=a$.
2.  **Subtract** one equation from the other and from it obtain an equation of the form $x-y=b$.
3.  Now, the equations $x+y=a$ and $x-y=b$ can be solved easily.

---

### Example 21

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
$$
37x+43y=123, \quad 43x+37y=117
$$

#### Solution

The given equations are:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 37x+43y=123 \tag{i} \\
& 43x+37y=117 \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Clearly, the coefficients of $x$ and $y$ in one equation are interchanged in the other.

**Adding (i) and (ii)**, we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
& (37+43)x+(43+37)y=(123+117) \\
\Rightarrow & 80x+80y=240 \\
\Rightarrow & 80(x+y)=240 \\
\Rightarrow & x+y=\frac{240}{80} \Rightarrow x+y=3 \tag{iii}
\end{align*}
$$
**Subtracting (i) from (ii)**, we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
6x-6y &= -6 \\
\Rightarrow 6(x-y) &= -6 \Rightarrow x-y=-1 \tag{iv}
\end{align*}
$$
Adding (iii) and (iv), we get $2x=2 \Rightarrow x=1$.

Subtracting (iv) from (iii), we get $2y=4 \Rightarrow y=2$.

Hence, **$x=1$ and $y=2$**.

### Example 22

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
$$
152x-378y=-74, \quad -378x+152y=-604.
$$

#### Solution

The given equations are:
$$
\begin{align*}
& 152x-378y=-74 \tag{i} \\
& -378x+152y=-604 \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Clearly, the coefficients of $x$ and $y$ in one equation are interchanged in the other.

**Adding (i) and (ii)**, we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
& \{152+(-378)\}x+\{(-378)+152\}y=-(74+604) \\
\Rightarrow & (-226)x+(-226)y=-678 \\
\Rightarrow & (-226)(x+y)=-678 \\
\Rightarrow & (x+y)=\frac{-678}{-226} \Rightarrow x+y=3 \tag{iii}
\end{align*}
$$
**Subtracting (ii) from (i)**, we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
& (152+378)x+(-378-152)y=(-74+604) \\
\Rightarrow & 530x-530y=530 \\
\Rightarrow & 530(x-y)=530 \Rightarrow x-y=1 \tag{iv}
\end{align*}
$$
Adding (iii) and (iv), we get $2x=4 \Rightarrow x=2$.

Subtracting (iv) from (iii), we get $2y=2 \Rightarrow y=1$.

Hence, **$x=2$ and $y=1$**.

### Example 25

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
$$
\frac{xy}{x+y}=\frac{6}{5}, \quad \frac{xy}{y-x}=6 \quad (x \neq 0, y \neq 0 \text{ and } x \neq y).
$$

#### Solution

The given equations may be written as:
$$
\begin{align*}
& \frac{x+y}{xy}=\frac{5}{6} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{y}+\frac{1}{x}=\frac{5}{6} \tag{i} \\
& \frac{y-x}{xy}=\frac{1}{6} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{x}-\frac{1}{y}=\frac{1}{6} \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Adding (i) and (ii), we get:
$$
\frac{2}{x}=\left(\frac{5}{6}+\frac{1}{6}\right)=\frac{6}{6}=1 \Rightarrow x=2.
$$
Subtracting (ii) from (i), we get:
$$
\frac{2}{y}=\left(\frac{5}{6}-\frac{1}{6}\right)=\frac{4}{6}=\frac{2}{3} \Rightarrow y=3.
$$
Hence, **$x=2$ and $y=3$**.
