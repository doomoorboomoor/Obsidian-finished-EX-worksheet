## Arithmetic Mean

If $a, A, b$ are in an **Arithmetic Progression (AP)**, we say that $A$ is the **arithmetic mean** between $a$ and $b$, abbreviated as **AM**.

---

## Arithmetic Mean (AM) Between Two Numbers

Let the given numbers be $a$ and $b$. Let $A$ be the AM between $a$ and $b$.

Then, $a, A, b$ are in AP.

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow (A-a) = (b-A) \\
& \Rightarrow 2A = a+b \\
& \Rightarrow A = \frac{1}{2}(a+b)
\end{aligned}
$$

> The **Arithmetic Mean** between $a$ and $b$ is $\frac{1}{2}(a+b)$.

---

## Solved Examples

### Example 1

Find the AM between:
1.  13 and 19
2.  $(a-b)$ and $(a+b)$

#### Solution:

1.  AM between 13 and 19 = $\frac{1}{2}(13+19) = \mathbf{16}$.
2.  AM between $(a-b)$ and $(a+b) = \frac{1}{2}[(a-b)+(a+b)] = \mathbf{a}$.

---

### Example 2

If the numbers $(2n-1), (3n+2)$ and $(6n-1)$ are in AP, find $n$ and hence find these numbers. [CBSE 2013C]

#### Solution:

Since $(2n-1), (3n+2)$ and $(6n-1)$ are in AP, the common difference is the same.

$$
\begin{aligned}
& (3n+2) - (2n-1) = (6n-1) - (3n+2) \\
& \Rightarrow n+3 = 3n-3 \\
& \Rightarrow 2n = 6 \\
& \Rightarrow n = 3
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $n=3$ and these numbers are $(2(3)-1), (3(3)+2), (6(3)-1)$, which are **5, 11, and 17**.

---

## An Important Result

When solving problems, it is often convenient to choose terms in an AP as follows:
- **3 numbers** in AP: $(a-d), a, (a+d)$
- **4 numbers** in AP: $(a-3d), (a-d), (a+d), (a+3d)$
- **5 numbers** in AP: $(a-2d), (a-d), a, (a+d), (a+2d)$

---

### Example 3

The sum of three numbers in AP is 21 and their product is 231. Find the numbers.

#### Solution:

Let the required numbers be $(a-d), a,$ and $(a+d)$.

Sum of the numbers:
$(a-d) + a + (a+d) = 21 \Rightarrow 3a = 21 \Rightarrow a = 7$.

Product of the numbers:
$(a-d) \cdot a \cdot (a+d) = 231 \Rightarrow a(a^2 - d^2) = 231$.

Substitute $a=7$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 7(7^2 - d^2) = 231 \\
& \Rightarrow 7(49 - d^2) = 231 \\
& \Rightarrow 49 - d^2 = 33 \\
& \Rightarrow d^2 = 16 \\
& \Rightarrow d = \pm 4
\end{aligned}
$$

Thus, with $a=7$ and $d=\pm 4$, the required numbers are (**3, 7, 11**) or (**11, 7, 3**).

---

### Example 4

Find four numbers in AP whose sum is 20 and the sum of whose squares is 120.

#### Solution:

Let the required numbers be $(a-3d), (a-d), (a+d),$ and $(a+3d)$.

Sum of the numbers:
$(a-3d) + (a-d) + (a+d) + (a+3d) = 20 \Rightarrow 4a=20 \Rightarrow a=5$.

Sum of the squares:
$(a-3d)^2 + (a-d)^2 + (a+d)^2 + (a+3d)^2 = 120$

Expanding and simplifying:
$(a^2 - 6ad + 9d^2) + (a^2 - 2ad + d^2) + (a^2 + 2ad + d^2) + (a^2 + 6ad + 9d^2) = 120$
$4a^2 + 20d^2 = 120 \Rightarrow a^2 + 5d^2 = 30$.

Substitute $a=5$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& (5)^2 + 5d^2 = 30 \\
& \Rightarrow 25 + 5d^2 = 30 \\
& \Rightarrow 5d^2 = 5 \\
& \Rightarrow d^2 = 1 \\
& \Rightarrow d = \pm 1
\end{aligned}
$$

Thus, with $a=5$ and $d=\pm 1$, the required numbers are (**2, 4, 6, 8**) or (**8, 6, 4, 2**).
