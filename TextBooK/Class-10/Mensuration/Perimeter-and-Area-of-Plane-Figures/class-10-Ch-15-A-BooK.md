## Perimeter and Area of Plane Figures

**PERIMETER** The perimeter of a plane figure is the length of its boundary.
In case of a triangle or a polygon, the perimeter is the sum of the lengths of its sides.
The unit of perimeter is same as the unit of length.

**AREA** The area of a plane figure is the measure of the surface enclosed by its boundary.
The area of a triangle or a polygon is the measure of the surface enclosed by its sides.
Area is measured in square units such as square centimetres and square metres, written as $\text{cm}^{2}$ and $\text{m}^{2}$ respectively.

---

## Perimeter and Area of Triangles [Formulae]

1.  (i) Area of a triangle = $\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{corresponding height}$.

    $$
    \operatorname{ar}(\triangle ABC) = \left(\frac{1}{2} \times BC \times AD\right) \text{ sq units.}
    $$

    (ii) **Heron's formula**:

    Let $a$, $b$, $c$ be the sides of a $\triangle ABC$. Then,

    ![](https://cdn.mathpix.com/cropped/2025_09_25_ec977b3f43b62157e306g-01.jpg?height=178&width=274&top_left_y=1057&top_left_x=904)

    $s = \frac{1}{2}(a+b+c)$ is called its **semiperimeter**.

    $$
    \operatorname{ar}(\triangle ABC) = \sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}
    $$

2.  In a right $\triangle ABC$, let $\angle B = 90^{\circ}$. Then,

    $$
    \operatorname{ar}(\triangle ABC) = \left(\frac{1}{2} \times BC \times AB\right) \text{ sq units.}
    $$

3.  In an equilateral triangle of side $a$, we have:
    (i) **Height** = $\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} a\right)$ units.
    (ii) **Area** = $\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} a^{2}\right)$ sq units.
    (iii) **Perimeter** = $3a$ units.

    ![](https://cdn.mathpix.com/cropped/2025_09_25_ec977b3f43b62157e306g-01.jpg?height=258&width=224&top_left_y=1637&top_left_x=950)

4.  For an isosceles $\triangle ABC$ in which $AB = AC = a$ and $BC = b$, we have:
    (i) **Height** = $\frac{\sqrt{4a^{2}-b^{2}}}{2}$ units.
    (ii) **Area** = $\left(\frac{1}{4} b \sqrt{4a^{2}-b^{2}}\right)$ sq units.
    (iii) **Perimeter** = $(2a+b)$ units.

    ![](https://cdn.mathpix.com/cropped/2025_09_25_ec977b3f43b62157e306g-02.jpg?height=269&width=228&top_left_y=189&top_left_x=934)

## Solved Examples

### Example 1

Find the area of a triangle having base 25 cm and height 10.8 cm.

#### Solution:

Area of the given triangle = $(\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height})$

$$
= \left(\frac{1}{2} \times 25 \times 10.8\right) \text{cm}^{2} = 135 \text{ cm}^{2}.
$$

---

### Example 2

The base of a triangular field is three times its altitude. If the cost of sowing the field at ₹960 per hectare is ₹12960, find its base and height.

#### Solution:

Total cost of sowing the field = ₹12960.
Rate per hectare = ₹960.

$$
\text{Area} = \frac{\text{total cost}}{\text{rate}} = \left(\frac{12960}{960}\right) \text{ hectares} = 13.5 \text{ hectares}
$$

$$
= (13.5 \times 10000) \text{ m}^{2} = 135000 \text{ m}^{2}.
$$

Let the altitude of the field be $x$ metres.
Then, its base = $3x$ metres.

$$
\text{Area} = \left(\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}\right) = \left(\frac{1}{2} \times 3x \times x\right) \text{ m}^{2} = \left(\frac{3x^{2}}{2}\right) \text{ m}^{2}.
$$

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{3x^{2}}{2} = 135000 \implies x^{2} = \left(135000 \times \frac{2}{3}\right) = 90000
$$

$$
\implies x = \sqrt{90000} = 300.
$$

$\therefore$ **base** = $(3 \times 300) \text{ m} = 900 \text{ m}$ and **altitude** = $300 \text{ m}$.

### Example 3

The lengths of the sides of a triangle are in the ratio $3:4:5$, and its perimeter is 144 cm. Find (i) the area of the triangle, and (ii) the height corresponding to the longest side.

#### Solution:

On dividing 144 cm in the ratio $3:4:5$, we get:

$$
a = \left(144 \times \frac{3}{12}\right) \text{ cm} = 36 \text{ cm}, \quad b = \left(144 \times \frac{4}{12}\right) \text{ cm} = 48 \text{ cm}
$$

$$
\text{and } c = \left(144 \times \frac{5}{12}\right) \text{ cm} = 60 \text{ cm}.
$$

$$
\therefore s = \frac{1}{2}(36+48+60) \text{ cm} = 72 \text{ cm}.
$$

$$
(s-a) = (72-36) \text{ cm} = 36 \text{ cm},
$$

$$
(s-b) = (72-48) \text{ cm} = 24 \text{ cm}
$$

$$
\text{and } (s-c) = (72-60) \text{ cm} = 12 \text{ cm}.
$$

(i) Area of the triangle = $\sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}$

$$
= \sqrt{72 \times 36 \times 24 \times 12} \text{ cm}^{2}
$$

$$
= \sqrt{746496} \text{ cm}^{2} = 864 \text{ cm}^{2}.
$$

(ii) Let base = $60 \text{ cm}$ and the corresponding height = $h \text{ cm}$.

Then, area of the triangle = $\left(\frac{1}{2} \times 60 \times h\right) \text{ cm}^{2} = (30h) \text{ cm}^{2}$.

$$
\therefore \quad 30h = 864 \implies h = \frac{864}{30} = 28.8.
$$

**Longest side** = $60 \text{ cm}$, **corresponding height** = $28.8 \text{ cm}$.

### Example 4

Each side of an equilateral triangle measures 10 cm. Calculate (i) the area of the triangle, and (ii) the height of the triangle. [Given, $\sqrt{3} = 1.732$.]

#### Solution:

Here, $a = 10 \text{ cm}$.

(i) Area of the triangle = $\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} \times a^{2}\right)$ sq units

$$
= \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} \times 10 \times 10\right) \text{cm}^{2} = (25 \times \sqrt{3}) \text{ cm}^{2}
$$

$$
= (25 \times 1.732) \text{ cm}^{2} = 43.3 \text{ cm}^{2}.
$$

(ii) Height of the triangle = $\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times a\right)$ units

$$
= \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times 10\right) \text{cm} = (5 \times \sqrt{3}) \text{ cm}
$$

$$
= (5 \times 1.732) \text{ cm} = 8.66 \text{ cm}.
$$

### Example 5

The height of an equilateral triangle is 15 cm. Find its area. [Given, $\sqrt{3} = 1.73$.]

#### Solution:

Let each side of the triangle be $a \text{ cm}$. Then, its height = $\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times a\right) \text{ cm}$.

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times a = 15 \implies a = \left(\frac{30}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}\right) = 10\sqrt{3}.
$$

Thus, each side of the triangle = $a \text{ cm} = 10\sqrt{3} \text{ cm}$.

$$
\text{Area of the triangle} = \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} \times a^{2}\right) = \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} \times 10\sqrt{3} \times 10\sqrt{3}\right) \text{cm}^{2}
$$

$$
= (75\sqrt{3}) \text{ cm}^{2} = \left(\frac{75 \times 173}{100}\right) \text{ cm}^{2}
$$

$$
= \frac{12975}{100} \text{ cm}^{2} = 129.75 \text{ cm}^{2}.
$$

### Example 6

Find the area of an isosceles triangle, each of whose equal sides is 13 cm and whose base is 24 cm.

#### Solution:

Here, each equal side, $a = 13 \text{ cm}$ and base, $b = 24 \text{ cm}$.

$$
\therefore \quad \text{area of the triangle} = \left(\frac{1}{4} b \cdot \sqrt{4a^{2}-b^{2}}\right) \text{cm}^{2}
$$

$$
= \left[\frac{1}{4} \times 24 \times \sqrt{4 \times (13)^2 - (24)^2}\right] \text{cm}^{2}
$$

$$
= \left[6 \times \sqrt{4 \times 169 - 576}\right] \text{cm}^{2}
$$

$$
= \left[6 \times \sqrt{676 - 576}\right] \text{cm}^{2}
$$

$$
= \left[6 \times \sqrt{100}\right] \text{cm}^{2} = (6 \times 10) \text{ cm}^{2} = 60 \text{ cm}^{2}.
$$

### Example 7

The base of an isosceles triangle measures 24 cm and its area is $192 \text{ cm}^{2}$. Find its perimeter.

#### Solution:

Here, base, $b = 24 \text{ cm}$, and let each equal side be $a \text{ cm}$. Then,

$$
\text{area} = \frac{1}{4} b \sqrt{4a^{2}-b^{2}} \text{ sq units} = \frac{1}{4} \times 24 \times \sqrt{4a^{2}-576} \text{ cm}^{2}
$$

$$
= 6 \sqrt{4(a^{2}-144)} \text{ cm}^{2} = 6 \times 2 \sqrt{a^{2}-144} \text{ cm}^{2} = 12 \sqrt{a^{2}-144} \text{ cm}^{2}.
$$

But, area = $192 \text{ cm}^{2}$ [given].

$$
\therefore \quad 12 \sqrt{a^{2}-144} = 192 \implies \sqrt{a^{2}-144} = 16
$$

$$
\implies a^{2}-144 = 256 \implies a^{2} = 400 \implies a=20.
$$

$\therefore$ perimeter of the triangle = $(2a+b) \text{ cm}$

$$
= (2 \times 20 + 24) \text{ cm} = 64 \text{ cm}.
$$

### Example 8

The difference between the sides at right angles in a right-angled triangle is 14 cm. The area of the triangle is $120 \text{ cm}^{2}$. Calculate the perimeter of the triangle.

#### Solution:

Let the sides containing the right angle be $x \text{ cm}$ and $(x-14) \text{ cm}$.
Then, its area = $\left[\frac{1}{2} \times x \times (x-14)\right] \text{ cm}^{2}$.

But, area = $120 \text{ cm}^{2}$ [given].

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{1}{2} x(x-14) = 120 \implies x^{2}-14x-240 = 0
$$

$$
\implies x^{2}-24x+10x-240 = 0 \implies x(x-24)+10(x-24) = 0
$$

$$
\implies (x-24)(x+10) = 0 \implies x = 24 \quad [\text{neglecting } x=-10].
$$

$\therefore$ one side = $24 \text{ cm}$, and other side = $(24-14) \text{ cm} = 10 \text{ cm}$.

$$
\text{Hypotenuse} = \sqrt{(24)^{2}+(10)^{2}} \text{ cm} = \sqrt{576+100} \text{ cm}
$$

$$
= \sqrt{676} \text{ cm} = 26 \text{ cm}.
$$

$\therefore$ perimeter of the triangle = $(24+10+26) \text{ cm} = 60 \text{ cm}$.
