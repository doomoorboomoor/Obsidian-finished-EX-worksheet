## Trigonometric Ratios of Complementary Angles

---

## Complementary Angles

Two angles are said to be **complementary** if their sum is $90^{\circ}$.

Thus, $\theta$ and $(90^{\circ}-\theta)$ are complementary angles.

---

## Trigonometric Ratios of Complementary Angles

Consider a $\triangle OMP$, right-angled at $M$ in which $\angle MOP = \theta$ and therefore, $\angle OPM = (90^{\circ}-\theta)$.

Let $OM = x$, $MP = y$ and $OP = r$. Then,

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \sin \theta=\frac{y}{r}, \cos \theta=\frac{x}{r}, \tan \theta=\frac{y}{x}, \\
& \operatorname{cosec} \theta=\frac{r}{y}, \sec \theta=\frac{r}{x}, \cot \theta=\frac{x}{y}.
\end{aligned}
$$

![](https://cdn.mathpix.com/cropped/2025_09_25_07394079effe35d69a0fg-01.jpg?height=321&width=326&top_left_y=704&top_left_x=873)

For considering the trigonometric ratios of $(90^{\circ}-\theta)$, we have
**base** $= MP$, **perpendicular** $= OM$ and **hypotenuse** $= OP$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad & \sin(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \frac{OM}{OP} = \frac{x}{r} = \cos \theta \\
& \cos(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \frac{PM}{OP} = \frac{y}{r} = \sin \theta \\
& \tan(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \frac{OM}{PM} = \frac{x}{y} = \cot \theta \\
\therefore \quad & \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \frac{1}{\sin(90^{\circ}-\theta)} = \frac{1}{\cos \theta} = \sec \theta \\
& \sec(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \frac{1}{\cos(90^{\circ}-\theta)} = \frac{1}{\sin \theta} = \operatorname{cosec} \theta \\
& \cot(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \frac{1}{\tan(90^{\circ}-\theta)} = \frac{1}{\cot \theta} = \tan \theta
\end{aligned}
$$

---

## Summary

1.  $\sin(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \cos \theta$
2.  $\cos(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \sin \theta$
3.  $\tan(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \cot \theta$
4.  $\cot(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \tan \theta$
5.  $\sec(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \operatorname{cosec} \theta$
6.  $\operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-\theta) = \sec \theta$

## Solved Examples

### Example 1:

Without using trigonometric tables, evaluate:

1.  $\frac{\cos 53^{\circ}}{\sin 37^{\circ}}$
2.  $\frac{\tan 68^{\circ}}{\cot 22^{\circ}}$
3.  $\frac{\sec 49^{\circ}}{\operatorname{cosec} 41^{\circ}}$
4.  $\frac{\sin 30^{\circ} 17'}{\cos 59^{\circ} 43'}$

#### Solution:

1.  $$\frac{\cos 53^{\circ}}{\sin 37^{\circ}} = \frac{\cos(90^{\circ}-37^{\circ})}{\sin 37^{\circ}} = \frac{\sin 37^{\circ}}{\sin 37^{\circ}} = 1 \quad [\because \cos(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sin \theta]$$
2.  $$\frac{\tan 68^{\circ}}{\cot 22^{\circ}} = \frac{\tan(90^{\circ}-22^{\circ})}{\cot 22^{\circ}} = \frac{\cot 22^{\circ}}{\cot 22^{\circ}} = 1 \quad [\because \tan(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\cot \theta]$$
3.  $$\frac{\sec 49^{\circ}}{\operatorname{cosec} 41^{\circ}} = \frac{\sec(90^{\circ}-41^{\circ})}{\operatorname{cosec} 41^{\circ}} = \frac{\operatorname{cosec} 41^{\circ}}{\operatorname{cosec} 41^{\circ}} = 1 \quad [\because \sec(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\operatorname{cosec} \theta]$$
4.  $$\frac{\sin 30^{\circ} 17'}{\cos 59^{\circ} 43'} = \frac{\sin[90^{\circ}-(59^{\circ} 43')]}{\cos 59^{\circ} 43'} = \frac{\cos 59^{\circ} 43'}{\cos 59^{\circ} 43'} = 1$$

### Example 2:

Without using trigonometric tables, evaluate:

1.  $\cos 48^{\circ} - \sin 42^{\circ}$
2.  $\operatorname{cosec} 31^{\circ} - \sec 59^{\circ}$
3.  $\cot 34^{\circ} - \tan 56^{\circ}$
4.  $\cos^{2} 13^{\circ} - \sin^{2} 77^{\circ}$

#### Solution:

We have

1.  $$\cos 48^{\circ} - \sin 42^{\circ} = \cos(90^{\circ}-42^{\circ}) - \sin 42^{\circ} = \sin 42^{\circ} - \sin 42^{\circ} = 0 \quad [\because \cos(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sin \theta]$$
2.  $$\operatorname{cosec} 31^{\circ} - \sec 59^{\circ} = \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-59^{\circ}) - \sec 59^{\circ} = \sec 59^{\circ} - \sec 59^{\circ} = 0 \quad [\because \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sec \theta]$$
3.  $$\cot 34^{\circ} - \tan 56^{\circ} = \cot(90^{\circ}-56^{\circ}) - \tan 56^{\circ} = \tan 56^{\circ} - \tan 56^{\circ} = 0 \quad [\because \cot(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\tan \theta]$$
4.  $$\cos^{2} 13^{\circ} - \sin^{2} 77^{\circ} = \cos^{2}(90^{\circ}-77^{\circ}) - \sin^{2} 77^{\circ} = \sin^{2} 77^{\circ} - \sin^{2} 77^{\circ} = 0 \quad [\because \cos(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sin \theta]$$

### Example 3:

Without using trigonometric tables, prove that:

1.  $\sin 43^{\circ} \cos 47^{\circ} + \cos 43^{\circ} \sin 47^{\circ} = 1$
2.  $\cos 38^{\circ} \cos 52^{\circ} - \sin 38^{\circ} \sin 52^{\circ} = 0$
3.  $\sec 50^{\circ} \sin 40^{\circ} + \cos 40^{\circ} \operatorname{cosec} 50^{\circ} = 2$
4.  $\sec 70^{\circ} \sin 20^{\circ} - \cos 20^{\circ} \operatorname{cosec} 70^{\circ} = 0$

#### Solution:

We have

1.  $$\begin{aligned} & \sin 43^{\circ} \cos 47^{\circ} + \cos 43^{\circ} \sin 47^{\circ} \\ &= \sin(90^{\circ}-47^{\circ}) \cos 47^{\circ} + \cos(90^{\circ}-47^{\circ}) \sin 47^{\circ} \\ &= \cos^{2} 47^{\circ} + \sin^{2} 47^{\circ} = 1 \quad [\because \sin(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\cos \theta, \cos(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sin \theta] \end{aligned}$$
2.  $$\begin{aligned} & \cos 38^{\circ} \cos 52^{\circ} - \sin 38^{\circ} \sin 52^{\circ} \\ &= \cos(90^{\circ}-52^{\circ}) \cos 52^{\circ} - \sin(90^{\circ}-52^{\circ}) \sin 52^{\circ} \\ &= \sin 52^{\circ} \cos 52^{\circ} - \cos 52^{\circ} \sin 52^{\circ} = 0 \quad [\because \cos(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sin \theta \text{ and } \sin(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\cos \theta] \end{aligned}$$
3.  $$\begin{aligned} & \sec 50^{\circ} \sin 40^{\circ} + \cos 40^{\circ} \operatorname{cosec} 50^{\circ} \\ &= \sec(90^{\circ}-40^{\circ}) \sin 40^{\circ} + \cos 40^{\circ} \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-40^{\circ}) \\ &= \operatorname{cosec} 40^{\circ} \sin 40^{\circ} + \cos 40^{\circ} \sec 40^{\circ} \quad [\because \sec(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\operatorname{cosec} \theta, \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sec \theta] \\ &= \frac{\sin 40^{\circ}}{\sin 40^{\circ}} + \frac{\cos 40^{\circ}}{\cos 40^{\circ}} = 1+1=2. \end{aligned}$$
4.  $$\begin{aligned} & \sec 70^{\circ} \sin 20^{\circ} - \cos 20^{\circ} \operatorname{cosec} 70^{\circ} \\ &= \sec(90^{\circ}-20^{\circ}) \sin 20^{\circ} - \cos 20^{\circ} \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-20^{\circ}) \\ &= \operatorname{cosec} 20^{\circ} \sin 20^{\circ} - \cos 20^{\circ} \sec 20^{\circ} \quad [\because \sec(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\operatorname{cosec} \theta, \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sec \theta] \\ &= \frac{\sin 20^{\circ}}{\sin 20^{\circ}} - \frac{\cos 20^{\circ}}{\cos 20^{\circ}} = 1-1=0. \end{aligned}$$

### Example 4:

Without using trigonometric tables, prove that:

1.  $\tan 48^{\circ} \tan 23^{\circ} \tan 42^{\circ} \tan 67^{\circ}=1$
2.  $\tan 7^{\circ} \tan 23^{\circ} \tan 60^{\circ} \tan 67^{\circ} \tan 83^{\circ}=\sqrt{3}$
3.  $\cot 12^{\circ} \cot 38^{\circ} \cot 52^{\circ} \cot 60^{\circ} \cot 78^{\circ}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$

#### Solution:

1.  $$\begin{aligned} & \tan 48^{\circ} \tan 23^{\circ} \tan 42^{\circ} \tan 67^{\circ} \\ &= (\tan 48^{\circ} \tan 42^{\circ})(\tan 23^{\circ} \tan 67^{\circ}) \\ &= \{\tan 48^{\circ} \cdot \tan(90^{\circ}-48^{\circ})\}\{\tan 23^{\circ} \cdot \tan(90^{\circ}-23^{\circ})\} \\ &= (\tan 48^{\circ} \cot 48^{\circ})(\tan 23^{\circ} \cot 23^{\circ}) \quad [\because \tan(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\cot \theta] \\ &= 1 \times 1 = 1. \end{aligned}$$
2.  $$\begin{aligned} & \tan 7^{\circ} \tan 23^{\circ} \tan 60^{\circ} \tan 67^{\circ} \tan 83^{\circ} \\ &= (\tan 7^{\circ} \cdot \tan 83^{\circ}) \cdot (\tan 23^{\circ} \cdot \tan 67^{\circ}) \cdot \tan 60^{\circ} \\ &= \tan 7^{\circ} \cdot \tan(90^{\circ}-7^{\circ}) \cdot \tan 23^{\circ} \cdot \tan(90^{\circ}-23^{\circ}) \cdot \tan 60^{\circ} \\ &= (\tan 7^{\circ} \cdot \cot 7^{\circ}) \cdot (\tan 23^{\circ} \cdot \cot 23^{\circ}) \cdot \tan 60^{\circ} \quad [\because \tan(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\cot \theta] \\ &= 1 \times 1 \times \sqrt{3} = \sqrt{3} \quad [\because \tan \theta \cdot \cot \theta = 1 \text{ and } \tan 60^{\circ} = \sqrt{3}]. \end{aligned}$$
3.  $$\begin{aligned} & \cot 12^{\circ} \cdot \cot 38^{\circ} \cdot \cot 52^{\circ} \cdot \cot 60^{\circ} \cdot \cot 78^{\circ} \\ &= (\cot 12^{\circ} \cdot \cot 78^{\circ}) \cdot (\cot 38^{\circ} \cdot \cot 52^{\circ}) \cdot \cot 60^{\circ} \\ &= \{\cot 12^{\circ} \cdot \cot(90^{\circ}-12^{\circ})\}\{\cot 38^{\circ} \cdot \cot(90^{\circ}-38^{\circ})\} \cdot \cot 60^{\circ} \\ &= (\cot 12^{\circ} \cdot \tan 12^{\circ}) \cdot (\cot 38^{\circ} \cdot \tan 38^{\circ}) \cdot \cot 60^{\circ} \quad [\because \cot(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\tan \theta] \\ &= 1 \times 1 \times \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \quad [\because \cot \theta \cdot \tan \theta=1 \text{ and } \cot 60^{\circ}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}]. \end{aligned}$$

### Example 5:

Prove that $\tan 1^{\circ} \tan 2^{\circ} \tan 3^{\circ} \ldots \tan 89^{\circ} = 1$.

#### Solution:

We have

$$ \begin{aligned} & \tan 1^{\circ} \tan 2^{\circ} \tan 3^{\circ} \ldots \tan 89^{\circ} \\ &= \tan 1^{\circ} \tan 2^{\circ} \ldots \tan 44^{\circ} \tan 45^{\circ} \tan 46^{\circ} \ldots \tan 88^{\circ} \tan 89^{\circ} \\ &= (\tan 1^{\circ} \cdot \tan 89^{\circ})(\tan 2^{\circ} \cdot \tan 88^{\circ}) \ldots (\tan 44^{\circ} \cdot \tan 46^{\circ}) \cdot \tan 45^{\circ} \\ &= \{\tan 1^{\circ} \cdot \tan(90^{\circ}-1^{\circ})\} \cdot \{\tan 2^{\circ} \cdot \tan(90^{\circ}-2^{\circ})\} \ldots \{\tan 44^{\circ} \cdot \tan(90^{\circ}-44^{\circ})\} \cdot \tan 45^{\circ} \\ &= (\tan 1^{\circ} \cdot \cot 1^{\circ})(\tan 2^{\circ} \cdot \cot 2^{\circ}) \ldots (\tan 44^{\circ} \cot 44^{\circ}) \cdot 1 \quad [\because \tan(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\cot \theta \text{ and } \tan 45^{\circ}=1] \\ &= 1 \times 1 \times \ldots \times 1 \times 1 = 1. \end{aligned} $$

### Example 6:

Evaluate: $\frac{\cos 58^{\circ}}{\sin 32^{\circ}} + \frac{\sin 22^{\circ}}{\cos 68^{\circ}} - \frac{\cos 38^{\circ} \operatorname{cosec} 52^{\circ}}{\tan 18^{\circ} \tan 35^{\circ} \tan 60^{\circ} \tan 72^{\circ} \tan 55^{\circ}}$ [CBSE 2009]

#### Solution:

Given expression
$$ \begin{aligned} &= \frac{\cos 58^{\circ}}{\sin 32^{\circ}} + \frac{\sin 22^{\circ}}{\cos 68^{\circ}} - \frac{\cos 38^{\circ} \operatorname{cosec} 52^{\circ}}{\tan 18^{\circ} \tan 35^{\circ} \tan 60^{\circ} \tan 72^{\circ} \tan 55^{\circ}} \\ &= \frac{\cos 58^{\circ}}{\sin(90^{\circ}-58^{\circ})} + \frac{\sin 22^{\circ}}{\cos(90^{\circ}-22^{\circ})} - \frac{\cos 38^{\circ} \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-38^{\circ})}{(\tan 18^{\circ} \tan 72^{\circ})(\tan 35^{\circ} \tan 55^{\circ}) \tan 60^{\circ}} \\ &= \frac{\cos 58^{\circ}}{\cos 58^{\circ}} + \frac{\sin 22^{\circ}}{\sin 22^{\circ}} - \frac{\cos 38^{\circ} \sec 38^{\circ}}{\{\tan 18^{\circ} \tan(90^{\circ}-18^{\circ})\}\{\tan 35^{\circ} \tan(90^{\circ}-35^{\circ})\} \tan 60^{\circ}} \\ &= (1+1) - \frac{1}{(\tan 18^{\circ} \cot 18^{\circ})(\tan 35^{\circ} \cot 35^{\circ}) \sqrt{3}} \\ &= \left(2-\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right) = \frac{2 \sqrt{3}-1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{2 \sqrt{3}-1}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{1}{3}(6-\sqrt{3}). \end{aligned} $$

### Example 7:

Without using trigonometric tables, evaluate each of the following:

1.  $\sin^{2} 65^{\circ} + \sin^{2} 25^{\circ}$
2.  $\cos^{2} 17^{\circ} - \sin^{2} 73^{\circ}$
3.  $\operatorname{cosec}^{2} 67^{\circ} - \tan^{2} 23^{\circ}$
4.  $\sec^{2} 36^{\circ} - \cot^{2} 54^{\circ}$

#### Solution:

We have

1.  $$\begin{aligned} \sin^{2} 65^{\circ} + \sin^{2} 25^{\circ} &= \{\sin(90^{\circ}-25^{\circ})\}^{2} + \sin^{2} 25^{\circ} \\ &= (\cos 25^{\circ})^{2} + \sin^{2} 25^{\circ} \quad [\because \sin(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\cos \theta] \\ &= (\cos^{2} 25^{\circ} + \sin^{2} 25^{\circ}) = 1. \end{aligned}$$
2.  $$\begin{aligned} \cos^{2} 17^{\circ} - \sin^{2} 73^{\circ} &= [\cos(90^{\circ}-73^{\circ})]^{2} - \sin^{2} 73^{\circ} \\ &= (\sin 73^{\circ})^{2} - \sin^{2} 73^{\circ} \quad [\because \cos(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sin \theta] \\ &= (\sin^{2} 73^{\circ} - \sin^{2} 73^{\circ}) = 0. \end{aligned}$$
3.  $$\begin{aligned} \operatorname{cosec}^{2} 67^{\circ} - \tan^{2} 23^{\circ} &= [\operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-23^{\circ})]^{2} - \tan^{2} 23^{\circ} \\ &= (\sec 23^{\circ})^{2} - \tan^{2} 23^{\circ} \quad [\because \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sec \theta] \\ &= (\sec^{2} 23^{\circ} - \tan^{2} 23^{\circ}) = 1 \quad [\because \sec^{2} \theta - \tan^{2} \theta=1]. \end{aligned}$$
4.  $$\begin{aligned} \sec^{2} 36^{\circ} - \cot^{2} 54^{\circ} &= [\sec(90^{\circ}-54^{\circ})]^{2} - \cot^{2} 54^{\circ} \\ &= (\operatorname{cosec} 54^{\circ})^{2} - \cot^{2} 54^{\circ} \quad [\because \sec(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\operatorname{cosec} \theta] \\ &= (\operatorname{cosec}^{2} 54^{\circ} - \cot^{2} 54^{\circ}) = 1 \quad [\because \operatorname{cosec}^{2} \theta - \cot^{2} \theta=1]. \end{aligned}$$

### Example 8:

Evaluate $\frac{2 \sin^{2} 63^{\circ} + 1 + 2 \sin^{2} 27^{\circ}}{3 \cos^{2} 17^{\circ} - 2 + 3 \cos^{2} 73^{\circ}}$. [CBSE 2009C]

#### Solution:

Given expression
$$\begin{aligned} &= \frac{2 \sin^{2} 63^{\circ} + 1 + 2 \sin^{2} 27^{\circ}}{3 \cos^{2} 17^{\circ} - 2 + 3 \cos^{2} 73^{\circ}} = \frac{2(\sin^{2} 63^{\circ} + \sin^{2} 27^{\circ}) + 1}{3(\cos^{2} 17^{\circ} + \cos^{2} 73^{\circ}) - 2} \\ &= \frac{2[\sin^{2} 63^{\circ} + \sin^{2}(90^{\circ}-63^{\circ})] + 1}{3[\cos^{2} 17^{\circ} + \cos^{2}(90^{\circ}-17^{\circ})] - 2} \\ &= \frac{2[\sin^{2} 63^{\circ} + \cos^{2} 63^{\circ}] + 1}{3[\cos^{2} 17^{\circ} + \sin^{2} 17^{\circ}] - 2} \quad [\because \sin(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\cos \theta \text{ and } \cos(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\sin \theta] \\ &= \frac{(2 \times 1)+1}{(3 \times 1)-2} = \frac{2+1}{3-2} = \frac{3}{1} = 3. \end{aligned}$$

### Example 10:

If $\sin 3A = \cos(A-10^{\circ})$, where $3A$ is an acute angle then find the value of $A$. [CBSE 2009C]

#### Solution:

$$\begin{aligned} \sin 3A &= \cos(A-10^{\circ}) \\ \Rightarrow \cos(90^{\circ}-3A) &= \cos(A-10^{\circ}) \quad [\because \sin \theta = \cos(90^{\circ}-\theta)] \\ \Rightarrow 90^{\circ}-3A &= A-10^{\circ} \\ \Rightarrow 4A &= 100^{\circ} \\ \Rightarrow A &= 25^{\circ}. \end{aligned}$$
Hence, $A = 25^{\circ}$.

### Example 11:

If $\tan 2A = \cot(A-21^{\circ})$, where $2A$ is an acute angle then find the value of $A$. [CBSE 2009C]

Hence, $A=37^{\circ}$.

### Example 12:

If $\sec 5A = \operatorname{cosec}(A-30^{\circ})$, where $5A$ is an acute angle then find the value of $A$.

#### Solution:

Hence, $A=20^{\circ}$.

### Example 13:

If $\sin(\theta+34^{\circ}) = \cos \theta$ and $(\theta+34^{\circ})$ is acute show that $\theta = 28^{\circ}$.

#### Solution:

$$\begin{aligned} \sin(\theta+34^{\circ}) &= \cos \theta \\ \Rightarrow \sin(\theta+34^{\circ}) &= \sin(90^{\circ}-\theta) \quad [\because \cos \theta = \sin(90^{\circ}-\theta)] \\ \Rightarrow \theta+34^{\circ} &= 90^{\circ}-\theta \\ \Rightarrow 2\theta &= (90^{\circ}-34^{\circ}) = 56^{\circ} \\ \Rightarrow \theta &= 28^{\circ}. \end{aligned}$$
Hence, $\theta=28^{\circ}$.

### Example 14:

Express each of the following in terms of trigonometric ratios of angles between $0^{\circ}$ and $45^{\circ}$.

1.  $\sin 75^{\circ} + \operatorname{cosec} 75^{\circ}$
2.  $\tan 65^{\circ} + \cot 49^{\circ}$
3.  $\sec 67^{\circ} + \operatorname{cosec} 58^{\circ}$
4.  $\cos 83^{\circ} - \sec 76^{\circ}$

#### Solution:

1.  $\sin 75^{\circ} + \operatorname{cosec} 75^{\circ} = \sin(90^{\circ}-15^{\circ}) + \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-15^{\circ}) = (\cos 15^{\circ} + \sec 15^{\circ}).$
2.  $\tan 65^{\circ} + \cot 49^{\circ} = \tan(90^{\circ}-25^{\circ}) + \cot(90^{\circ}-41^{\circ}) = (\cot 25^{\circ} + \tan 41^{\circ}).$
3.  $\sec 67^{\circ} + \operatorname{cosec} 58^{\circ} = \sec(90^{\circ}-23^{\circ}) + \operatorname{cosec}(90^{\circ}-32^{\circ}) = (\operatorname{cosec} 23^{\circ} + \sec 32^{\circ}).$
4.  $\cos 83^{\circ} - \sec 76^{\circ} = \cos(90^{\circ}-7^{\circ}) - \sec(90^{\circ}-14^{\circ}) = (\sin 7^{\circ} - \operatorname{cosec} 14^{\circ}).$

### Example 15:

If $A$ and $B$ are acute angles such that $\sin A = \cos B$, prove that $(A+B)=90^{\circ}$.

#### Solution:

$$\begin{aligned} \sin A &= \cos B \\ \Rightarrow \sin A &= \sin(90^{\circ}-B) \\ \Rightarrow A &= 90^{\circ}-B \quad [\because A \text{ and } (90^{\circ}-B) \text{ are acute}] \\ \Rightarrow A+B &= 90^{\circ}. \end{aligned}$$

### Example 16:

If $A, B, C$ are the angles of a $\triangle ABC$, show that $\sin\left(\frac{B+C}{2}\right) = \cos \frac{A}{2}$. [CBSE 2008C]

#### Solution:

We know that the sum of the angles of a triangle is $180^{\circ}$.

$$\begin{aligned} \therefore \quad A+B+C &= 180^{\circ} \\ \Rightarrow B+C &= 180^{\circ}-A \\ \Rightarrow \frac{B+C}{2} &= 90^{\circ}-\frac{A}{2} \\ \Rightarrow \sin\left(\frac{B+C}{2}\right) &= \sin\left(90^{\circ}-\frac{A}{2}\right) \\ \Rightarrow \sin\left(\frac{B+C}{2}\right) &= \cos \frac{A}{2} \quad [\because \sin(90^{\circ}-\theta)=\cos \theta]. \end{aligned}$$
