## T-Ratios of Some Particular Angles

## Trigonometric Ratios of $45^{\circ}, 60^{\circ}$ and $30^{\circ}$ (Geometrically)

---

### Trigonometric Ratios of $45^{\circ}$

Let $\triangle ABC$ be a right-angled triangle in which $\angle B=90^{\circ}$ and $\angle A=45^{\circ}$. Then, clearly, $\angle C=45^{\circ}$.

$$
\angle A = \angle C \Rightarrow AB = BC.
$$

Let $AB = BC = a$ units. Then, by the Pythagorean theorem:

$$
AC = \sqrt{AB^{2} + BC^{2}} = \sqrt{a^{2} + a^{2}} = \sqrt{2a^{2}} = \sqrt{2}a \text{ units.}
$$

For $\angle 45^{\circ}$, we have:
- **Base** $AB=a$
- **Perpendicular** $BC=a$
- **Hypotenuse** $AC=\sqrt{2}a$

$$
\begin{array}{rlrl}
\therefore & \sin 45^{\circ} = \frac{BC}{AC} = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}a} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}; & \cos 45^{\circ} = \frac{AB}{AC} = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}a} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}; \\
& \tan 45^{\circ} = \frac{BC}{AB} = \frac{a}{a} = 1; & \operatorname{cosec} 45^{\circ} = \frac{1}{\sin 45^{\circ}} = \sqrt{2}; \\
& \sec 45^{\circ} = \frac{1}{\cos 45^{\circ}} = \sqrt{2}; & \cot 45^{\circ} = \frac{1}{\tan 45^{\circ}} = 1.
\end{array}
$$

### Trigonometric Ratios of $60^{\circ}$ and $30^{\circ}$

Consider an equilateral $\triangle ABC$ with each side equal to $2a$. Then, each angle of $\triangle ABC$ is $60^{\circ}$. From vertex $A$, draw the altitude $AD \perp BC$.

In $\triangle ADB$ and $\triangle ADC$:
- $AB = AC$ (Sides of an equilateral triangle)
- $AD = AD$ (Common side)
- $\angle ADB = \angle ADC = 90^{\circ}$

Therefore, $\triangle ADB \cong \triangle ADC$ (RHS congruence). This implies $BD = DC = a$ and $\angle BAD = \angle CAD = 30^{\circ}$.

From the right-angled $\triangle ADB$, we have:

$$
AD = \sqrt{AB^{2} - BD^{2}} = \sqrt{(2a)^{2} - a^{2}} = \sqrt{4a^{2} - a^{2}} = \sqrt{3a^{2}} = \sqrt{3}a.
$$

#### T-Ratios of $60^{\circ}$

In the right-angled $\triangle ADB$, for $\angle B = 60^{\circ}$:
- **Base** $BD=a$
- **Perpendicular** $AD=\sqrt{3}a$
- **Hypotenuse** $AB=2a$

$$
\begin{array}{rlr}
\therefore \quad & \sin 60^{\circ} = \frac{AD}{AB} = \frac{\sqrt{3}a}{2a} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}; & \cos 60^{\circ} = \frac{BD}{AB} = \frac{a}{2a} = \frac{1}{2}; \\
& \tan 60^{\circ} = \frac{AD}{BD} = \frac{\sqrt{3}a}{a} = \sqrt{3}; & \operatorname{cosec} 60^{\circ} = \frac{1}{\sin 60^{\circ}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}; \\
& \sec 60^{\circ} = \frac{1}{\cos 60^{\circ}} = 2; & \cot 60^{\circ} = \frac{1}{\tan 60^{\circ}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}.
\end{array}
$$

#### T-Ratios of $30^{\circ}$

In the right-angled $\triangle ADB$, for $\angle BAD = 30^{\circ}$:
- **Base** $AD=\sqrt{3}a$
- **Perpendicular** $BD=a$
- **Hypotenuse** $AB=2a$

$$
\begin{array}{ll}
\therefore \quad & \sin 30^{\circ} = \frac{BD}{AB} = \frac{a}{2a} = \frac{1}{2}; & \cos 30^{\circ} = \frac{AD}{AB} = \frac{\sqrt{3}a}{2a} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}; \\
& \tan 30^{\circ} = \frac{BD}{AD} = \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}a} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}; & \operatorname{cosec} 30^{\circ} = \frac{1}{\sin 30^{\circ}} = 2; \\
& \sec 30^{\circ} = \frac{1}{\cos 30^{\circ}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}; & \cot 30^{\circ} = \frac{1}{\tan 30^{\circ}} = \sqrt{3}.
\end{array}
$$

### Axioms for T-Ratios of $0^{\circ}$

We define:
1. $\sin 0^{\circ} = 0$
2. $\cos 0^{\circ} = 1$
3. $\tan 0^{\circ} = 0$
4. $\sec 0^{\circ} = 1$

**NOTE**: $\operatorname{cosec} 0^{\circ}$ and $\cot 0^{\circ}$ are not defined.

---

### Axioms for T-Ratios of $90^{\circ}$

We define:
1. $\sin 90^{\circ} = 1$
2. $\cos 90^{\circ} = 0$
3. $\operatorname{cosec} 90^{\circ} = 1$
4. $\cot 90^{\circ} = 0$

**NOTE**: $\tan 90^{\circ}$ and $\sec 90^{\circ}$ are not defined.

## Solved Examples

### Example 1:

Evaluate:
(i) $\sin 60^{\circ} \cdot \cos 30^{\circ} - \cos 60^{\circ} \cdot \sin 30^{\circ}$
(ii) $\tan 30^{\circ} \cdot \operatorname{cosec} 60^{\circ} + \tan 60^{\circ} \cdot \sec 30^{\circ}$

#### Solution:

On substituting the values of various T-ratios, we get:

(i)
$$
\sin 60^{\circ} \cdot \cos 30^{\circ} - \cos 60^{\circ} \cdot \sin 30^{\circ} = \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} - \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2}\right) = \left(\frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{4}\right) = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}
$$

(ii)
$$
\tan 30^{\circ} \cdot \operatorname{cosec} 60^{\circ} + \tan 60^{\circ} \cdot \sec 30^{\circ} = \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}} + \sqrt{3} \times \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}\right) = \left(\frac{2}{3} + 2\right) = 2\frac{2}{3}
$$

### Example 2:

Evaluate:
(i) $\sin^{2} 30^{\circ} \cdot \cos^{2} 45^{\circ} + 4 \tan^{2} 30^{\circ} + \frac{1}{2} \sin^{2} 90^{\circ} + \frac{1}{8} \cot^{2} 60^{\circ}$
(ii) $\frac{\tan^{2} 60^{\circ} + 4 \sin^{2} 45^{\circ} + 3 \sec^{2} 30^{\circ} + 5 \cos^{2} 90^{\circ}}{\operatorname{cosec} 30^{\circ} + \sec 60^{\circ} - \cot^{2} 30^{\circ}}$

#### Solution:

On substituting the values of various T-ratios, we get:

(i)
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \sin^{2} 30^{\circ} \cdot \cos^{2} 45^{\circ} + 4 \tan^{2} 30^{\circ} + \frac{1}{2} \sin^{2} 90^{\circ} + \frac{1}{8} \cot^{2} 60^{\circ} \\
& = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{2} \cdot \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^{2} + 4 \times \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^{2} + \frac{1}{2} \times (1)^{2} + \frac{1}{8} \times \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^{2} \\
& = \left(\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{2} + 4 \times \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{2} \times 1 + \frac{1}{8} \times \frac{1}{3}\right) \\
& = \left(\frac{1}{8} + \frac{4}{3} + \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{24}\right) = \frac{3+32+12+1}{24} = \frac{48}{24} = 2
\end{aligned}
$$

(ii)
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \frac{\tan^{2} 60^{\circ} + 4 \sin^{2} 45^{\circ} + 3 \sec^{2} 30^{\circ} + 5 \cos^{2} 90^{\circ}}{\operatorname{cosec} 30^{\circ} + \sec 60^{\circ} - \cot^{2} 30^{\circ}} \\
& = \frac{(\sqrt{3})^{2} + 4 \times \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^{2} + 3 \times \left(\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^{2} + 5 \times 0^{2}}{2 + 2 - (\sqrt{3})^{2}} \\
& = \left(\frac{3 + 4 \times \frac{1}{2} + 3 \times \frac{4}{3} + 5 \times 0}{4 - 3}\right) = \left(\frac{3+2+4+0}{1}\right) = 9
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 3:

Show that:
(i) $\cos 60^{\circ} \cdot \cos 30^{\circ} - \sin 60^{\circ} \cdot \sin 30^{\circ} = \cos 90^{\circ}$
(ii) $\cos 60^{\circ} = 1 - 2 \sin^{2} 30^{\circ} = 2 \cos^{2} 30^{\circ} - 1$
(iii) $\frac{\tan 60^{\circ} - \tan 30^{\circ}}{1 + \tan 60^{\circ} \cdot \tan 30^{\circ}} = \tan 30^{\circ}$

#### Solution:

We have:

(i) **LHS**:
$$
\cos 60^{\circ} \cos 30^{\circ} - \sin 60^{\circ} \sin 30^{\circ} = \left(\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times \frac{1}{2}\right) = \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\right) = 0
$$
**RHS**:
$$
\cos 90^{\circ} = 0
$$
Since LHS = RHS, the identity is verified.

(ii) First, $\cos 60^{\circ} = \frac{1}{2}$.
$$
1 - 2 \sin^{2} 30^{\circ} = \left[1 - 2 \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{2}\right] = \left(1 - 2 \times \frac{1}{4}\right) = \left(1 - \frac{1}{2}\right) = \frac{1}{2}
$$
$$
2 \cos^{2} 30^{\circ} - 1 = \left[2 \times \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^{2} - 1\right] = \left(2 \times \frac{3}{4} - 1\right) = \left(\frac{3}{2} - 1\right) = \frac{1}{2}
$$
Thus, $\cos 60^{\circ} = 1 - 2 \sin^{2} 30^{\circ} = 2 \cos^{2} 30^{\circ} - 1$.

(iii) **LHS**:
$$
\frac{\tan 60^{\circ} - \tan 30^{\circ}}{1 + \tan 60^{\circ} \tan 30^{\circ}} = \frac{\left(\sqrt{3} - \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)}{\left(1 + \sqrt{3} \times \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)} = \frac{\frac{3-1}{\sqrt{3}}}{1+1} = \frac{\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}}{2} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}
$$
**RHS**:
$$
\tan 30^{\circ} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}
$$
Since LHS = RHS, the identity is verified.

### Example 4:

Taking $\theta = 30^{\circ}$, verify each of the following:
(i) $\sin 2\theta = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta$
(ii) $\cos 2\theta = 2 \cos^{2} \theta - 1 = 1 - 2 \sin^{2} \theta$
(iii) $\tan 2\theta = \frac{2 \tan \theta}{1 - \tan^{2} \theta}$

#### Solution:

When $\theta = 30^{\circ}$, we have $2\theta = 60^{\circ}$.

(i) **LHS**: $\sin 2\theta = \sin 60^{\circ} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$.
**RHS**: $2 \sin \theta \cos \theta = 2 \sin 30^{\circ} \cos 30^{\circ} = \left(2 \times \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$.
$\therefore \sin 2\theta = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta$.

(ii) **LHS**: $\cos 2\theta = \cos 60^{\circ} = \frac{1}{2}$.
**RHS**: $2 \cos^{2} \theta - 1 = 2 \cos^{2} 30^{\circ} - 1 = \left[2 \times \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^{2} - 1\right] = \left(2 \times \frac{3}{4} - 1\right) = \left(\frac{3}{2} - 1\right) = \frac{1}{2}$.
**RHS**: $1 - 2 \sin^{2} \theta = 1 - 2 \sin^{2} 30^{\circ} = \left[1 - 2 \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{2}\right] = \left(1 - 2 \times \frac{1}{4}\right) = \left(1 - \frac{1}{2}\right) = \frac{1}{2}$.
$\therefore \cos 2\theta = 2 \cos^{2} \theta - 1 = 1 - 2 \sin^{2} \theta$.

(iii) **LHS**: $\tan 2\theta = \tan 60^{\circ} = \sqrt{3}$.
**RHS**: $\frac{2 \tan \theta}{1 - \tan^{2} \theta} = \frac{2 \tan 30^{\circ}}{1 - \tan^{2} 30^{\circ}} = \frac{2 \times \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}}{1 - \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^{2}} = \frac{\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}}{1 - \frac{1}{3}} = \frac{\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}}{\frac{2}{3}} = \left(\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{3}{2}\right) = \sqrt{3}$.
$\therefore \tan 2\theta = \frac{2 \tan \theta}{1 - \tan^{2} \theta}$.

### Example 5:

Taking $\theta = 30^{\circ}$, verify that:
(i) $\sin 3\theta = 3 \sin \theta - 4 \sin^{3} \theta$
(ii) $\cos 3\theta = 4 \cos^{3} \theta - 3 \cos \theta$

#### Solution:

When $\theta = 30^{\circ}$, we have $3\theta = 90^{\circ}$.

(i) **LHS**: $\sin 3\theta = \sin 90^{\circ} = 1$.
**RHS**: $3 \sin \theta - 4 \sin^{3} \theta = 3 \sin 30^{\circ} - 4 \sin^{3} 30^{\circ} = \left[3 \times \frac{1}{2} - 4 \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{3}\right] = \left(\frac{3}{2} - 4 \times \frac{1}{8}\right) = \left(\frac{3}{2} - \frac{1}{2}\right) = 1$.
$\therefore \sin 3\theta = 3 \sin \theta - 4 \sin^{3} \theta$.

(ii) **LHS**: $\cos 3\theta = \cos 90^{\circ} = 0$.
**RHS**: $4 \cos^{3} \theta - 3 \cos \theta = 4 \cos^{3} 30^{\circ} - 3 \cos 30^{\circ} = \left[4 \times \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^{3} - 3 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right] = \left(4 \times \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{8} - \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}\right) = \left(\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} - \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}\right) = 0$.
$\therefore \cos 3\theta = 4 \cos^{3} \theta - 3 \cos \theta$.

### Example 6:

Taking $A = 60^{\circ}$ and $B = 30^{\circ}$, verify that $\sin(A - B) = \sin A \cos B - \cos A \sin B$.

#### Solution:

Given $A = 60^{\circ}$ and $B = 30^{\circ} \Rightarrow A - B = (60^{\circ} - 30^{\circ}) = 30^{\circ}$.

**LHS**: $\sin(A - B) = \sin 30^{\circ} = \frac{1}{2}$.

**RHS**: $\sin A \cos B - \cos A \sin B = \sin 60^{\circ} \cos 30^{\circ} - \cos 60^{\circ} \sin 30^{\circ}$
$$
= \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} - \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2}\right) = \left(\frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{4}\right) = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}
$$
$\therefore \sin(A - B) = \sin A \cos B - \cos A \sin B$.

### Example 7:

If $\sin(A + B) = 1$ and $\cos(A - B) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$, find $A$ and $B$.

#### Solution:

$$
\begin{align*}
\sin(A+B) = 1 \quad &\Rightarrow \quad \sin(A+B) = \sin 90^{\circ} \\
&\Rightarrow \quad A+B = 90^{\circ} \tag{i} \\
\cos(A-B) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \quad &\Rightarrow \quad \cos(A-B) = \cos 30^{\circ} \\
&\Rightarrow \quad A-B = 30^{\circ} \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Adding equations (i) and (ii), we get $2A = 120^{\circ} \Rightarrow A = 60^{\circ}$.
Substituting $A=60^{\circ}$ into (i), we get $60^{\circ} + B = 90^{\circ} \Rightarrow B = 30^{\circ}$.
Hence, **$A = 60^{\circ}$** and **$B = 30^{\circ}$**.

### Example 8:

If $\tan(A + B) = \sqrt{3}$ and $\tan(A - B) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$, where $0^{\circ} < A+B < 90^{\circ}$ and $A > B$, find the values of $A$ and $B$.

#### Solution:

$$
\begin{align*}
\tan(A+B) = \sqrt{3} \quad &\Rightarrow \quad \tan(A+B) = \tan 60^{\circ} \\
&\Rightarrow \quad A+B = 60^{\circ} \tag{i} \\
\tan(A-B) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \quad &\Rightarrow \quad \tan(A-B) = \tan 30^{\circ} \\
&\Rightarrow \quad A-B = 30^{\circ} \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$
Adding equations (i) and (ii), we get $2A = 90^{\circ} \Rightarrow A = 45^{\circ}$.
Substituting $A=45^{\circ}$ into (i), we get $45^{\circ} + B = 60^{\circ} \Rightarrow B = 15^{\circ}$.
Hence, **$A = 45^{\circ}$** and **$B = 15^{\circ}$**.

### Example 10:

In the adjoining figure, $\triangle ABC$ is right-angled at B. If $\angle A=30^{\circ}$ and $AB=9 \text{ cm}$, find (i) $BC$ and (ii) $AC$.

#### Solution:

From the right-angled $\triangle ABC$, we have:

(i) To find $BC$:
$$
\frac{BC}{AB} = \tan 30^{\circ} \Rightarrow \frac{BC}{9} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}
$$
$$
BC = \frac{9}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{9\sqrt{3}}{3} = 3\sqrt{3} \text{ cm}
$$

(ii) To find $AC$:
$$
\frac{AC}{BC} = \operatorname{cosec} 30^{\circ} \Rightarrow \frac{AC}{3\sqrt{3}} = 2 \Rightarrow AC = 6\sqrt{3} \text{ cm}
$$
Hence, **$BC = 3\sqrt{3}$ cm** and **$AC = 6\sqrt{3}$ cm**.

### Example 11:

In right $\triangle ABC, \angle B=90^{\circ}, AB=3 \text{ cm}$ and $AC=6 \text{ cm}$. Find $\angle C$ and $\angle A$.

#### Solution:

$$
\sin C = \frac{AB}{AC} = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}
$$
Since $\sin 30^{\circ} = \frac{1}{2}$, we have $\angle C = 30^{\circ}$.
The sum of angles in a triangle is $180^{\circ}$, so:
$$
\angle A = 180^{\circ} - (90^{\circ} + 30^{\circ}) = 180^{\circ} - 120^{\circ} = 60^{\circ}
$$
Hence, **$\angle C = 30^{\circ}$** and **$\angle A = 60^{\circ}$**.

### Example 12:

If $6x = \sec \theta$ and $\frac{6}{x} = \tan \theta$, find the value of $9\left(x^{2} - \frac{1}{x^{2}}\right)$.

#### Solution:

We are given:
$$
6x = \sec \theta \tag{i}
$$
$$
\frac{6}{x} = \tan \theta \tag{ii}
$$
Adding (i) and (ii), we get:
$$
6x + \frac{6}{x} = \sec \theta + \tan \theta \Rightarrow 6\left(x + \frac{1}{x}\right) = \sec \theta + \tan \theta \tag{iii}
$$
Subtracting (ii) from (i), we get:
$$
6x - \frac{6}{x} = \sec \theta - \tan \theta \Rightarrow 6\left(x - \frac{1}{x}\right) = \sec \theta - \tan \theta \tag{iv}
$$
Multiplying the corresponding sides of (iii) and (iv):
$$
36\left(x + \frac{1}{x}\right)\left(x - \frac{1}{x}\right) = (\sec \theta + \tan \theta)(\sec \theta - \tan \theta)
$$
$$
36\left(x^{2} - \frac{1}{x^{2}}\right) = \sec^{2} \theta - \tan^{2} \theta
$$
Using the identity $\sec^{2} \theta - \tan^{2} \theta = 1$:
$$
36\left(x^{2} - \frac{1}{x^{2}}\right) = 1
$$
Dividing by 4:
$$
9\left(x^{2} - \frac{1}{x^{2}}\right) = \frac{1}{4}
$$
