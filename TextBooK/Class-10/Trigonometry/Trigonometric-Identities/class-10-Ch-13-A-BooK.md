## Trigonometric Identity

An equation involving **trigonometric ratios** of an angle is called a **trigonometric identity** if it is true for all values of the angle.
some trigonometric identities In Chapter 10, we proved some identities, summarised below.

## Summary

1.  $\sin ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta=1$
2.  $1+\tan ^{2} \theta=\sec ^{2} \theta$
3.  $1+\cot ^{2} \theta=\operatorname{cosec}^{2} \theta$
4.  $\tan \theta=\frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}$ and $\cot \theta=\frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta}$
5.  $\tan \theta \times \cot \theta=1$

## Solved Examples

### Example 1:

Prove that

(i) $\left(1-\sin ^{2} \theta\right) \sec ^{2} \theta=1$
(ii) $\left(1+\tan ^{2} \theta\right) \cos ^{2} \theta=1$
(iii) $\left(1+\tan ^{2} \theta\right)(1-\sin \theta)(1+\sin \theta)=1$
(iv) $2 \cos ^{2} \theta+\frac{2}{\left(1+\cot ^{2} \theta\right)}=2$
[CBSE 2009C]

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{array}{lr}
\text { (i) } \begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =\left(1-\sin ^{2} \theta\right) \sec ^{2} \theta \\
& =\cos ^{2} \theta \cdot \sec ^{2} \theta \\
& =\cos ^{2} \theta \cdot \frac{1}{\cos ^{2} \theta}=1=\text { RHS. } \\
\therefore \quad \text { LHS }=\text { RHS. } & {\left[\because 1-\sin ^{2} \theta=\cos ^{2} \theta\right] } \\
\text { (ii) LHS } & =\left(1+\tan ^{2} \theta\right) \cos ^{2} \theta \\
& =\sec ^{2} \theta \cdot \cos ^{2} \theta \\
& =\frac{1}{\cos ^{2} \theta} \cdot \cos ^{2} \theta=1=\text { RHS. } \\
\therefore \quad \text { LHS }=\text { RHS. } & {\left[\because 1+\tan ^{2} \theta=\sec ^{2} \theta\right] }
\end{aligned}
\end{array}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \text { (iii) } \begin{aligned}
& \text { LHS }=\left(1+\tan ^{2} \theta\right)(1-\sin \theta)(1+\sin \theta) \\
&=\left(1+\tan ^{2} \theta\right)\left(1-\sin ^{2} \theta\right)=\sec ^{2} \theta \cdot \cos ^{2} \theta \\
& \quad\left[\because\left(1+\tan ^{2} \theta\right)=\sec ^{2} \theta,\left(1-\sin ^{2} \theta\right)=\cos ^{2} \theta\right] \\
&= \frac{1}{\cos ^{2} \theta} \cdot \cos ^{2} \theta \quad\left[\because \sec ^{2} \theta=\frac{1}{\cos ^{2} \theta}\right] \\
&= 1=\text { RHS } . \\
& \therefore \quad \text { LHS }=\text { RHS } .
\end{aligned}
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\text { (iv) } \begin{array}{rlr}
\text { LHS } & =2 \cos ^{2} \theta+\frac{2}{\left(1+\cot ^{2} \theta\right)} & \\
& =2 \cos ^{2} \theta+\frac{2}{\operatorname{cosec}^{2} \theta} & {\left[\because 1+\cot ^{2} \theta=\operatorname{cosec}^{2} \theta\right]} \\
& =2 \cos ^{2} \theta+2 \sin ^{2} \theta & {\left[\because \frac{1}{\operatorname{cosec}^{2} \theta}=\sin ^{2} \theta\right]} \\
& =2\left(\cos ^{2} \theta+\sin ^{2} \theta\right)=2 \times 1 & {\left[\because \cos ^{2} \theta+\sin ^{2} \theta=1\right]} \\
& =2=\text { RHS } . & \\
\therefore \quad \text { LHS }=\text { RHS } . &
\end{array}
$$

### Example 2:

Prove that
(i) $\left(\operatorname{cosec}^{2} \theta-1\right) \tan ^{2} \theta=1$
(ii) $\left(\sec ^{2} \theta-1\right)\left(1-\operatorname{cosec}^{2} \theta\right)=-1$

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{array}{rlr}
\text { (i) } \begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =\left(\operatorname{cosec}^{2} \theta-1\right) \tan ^{2} \theta \\
& =\left(1+\cot ^{2} \theta-1\right) \tan ^{2} \theta \\
& =\cot ^{2} \theta \cdot \tan ^{2} \theta \\
& =\frac{1}{\tan ^{2} \theta} \cdot \tan ^{2} \theta \\
& =1=\text { RHS. } \\
\text { (ii) } & \text { LHS }
\end{aligned}=\left(\because \sec ^{2} \theta-1\right)\left(1-\operatorname{cosec}^{2} \theta=1+\cot ^{2} \theta\right] \\
& =\left(1+\tan ^{2} \theta-1\right)\left[1-\left(1+\cot ^{2} \theta\right)\right] \\
& \quad\left[\because \sec ^{2} \theta=1+\tan ^{2} \theta \text { and } \operatorname{cosec}^{2} \theta=1+\cot ^{2} \theta\right] \\
& =\tan ^{2} \theta \cdot\left(-\cot ^{2} \theta\right) \\
& =\tan ^{2} \theta \cdot\left(\frac{-1}{\tan ^{2} \theta}\right) \\
& =-1=\text { RHS. } & {\left[\because \cot \theta=\frac{1}{\tan \theta}\right]}
\end{array}
$$

### Example 3:

Prove that

$$
\frac{\sin ^{3} \theta+\cos ^{3} \theta}{\sin \theta+\cos \theta}+\sin \theta \cos \theta=1 .
$$

[CBSE 2009]

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =\frac{\sin ^{3} \theta+\cos ^{3} \theta}{\sin \theta+\cos \theta}+\sin \theta \cos \theta \\
& =\frac{(\sin \theta+\cos \theta)\left(\sin ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta-\sin \theta \cos \theta\right)}{(\sin \theta+\cos \theta)}+\sin \theta \cos \theta \\
& {\left[\because\left(a^{3}+b^{3}\right)=(a+b)\left(a^{2}+b^{2}-a b\right)\right] } \\
& =(1-\sin \theta \cos \theta)+\sin \theta \cos \theta=1\left[\because \sin ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta=1\right] \\
& =\text { RHS } . \\
\therefore \quad \text { LHS } & =\text { RHS } .
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 4:

Prove that

$$
(\sin \theta-\operatorname{cosec} \theta)(\cos \theta-\sec \theta)=\frac{1}{\tan \theta+\cot \theta} \cdot \text { [CBSE 2009C, '10] }
$$

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =(\sin \theta-\operatorname{cosec} \theta)(\cos \theta-\sec \theta) \\
& =\left(\sin \theta-\frac{1}{\sin \theta}\right)\left(\cos \theta-\frac{1}{\cos \theta}\right)=\frac{\left(\sin ^{2} \theta-1\right)}{\sin \theta} \cdot \frac{\left(\cos ^{2} \theta-1\right)}{\cos \theta} \\
& =\frac{\left\{-\left(1-\sin ^{2} \theta\right)\right\} \cdot\left[-\left(1-\cos ^{2} \theta\right)\right\}}{\sin \theta \cos \theta} \\
& =\frac{\left(-\cos ^{2} \theta\right)\left(-\sin ^{2} \theta\right)}{\sin \theta \cos \theta} \\

\text { RHS } & =\frac{1}{\tan \theta+\cot \theta} \\
& =\frac{1}{\frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}+\frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta}}=\frac{1}{\frac{\sin ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta}{\cos \theta \sin \theta}}=\frac{\cos \theta \sin \theta}{\left(\sin ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta\right)} \\
& =\cos \theta \sin \theta \\
\therefore \quad \text { LHS } & =\text { RHS. }
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 5:

Prove that

$$
(1+\cot \theta-\operatorname{cosec} \theta)(1+\tan \theta+\sec \theta)=2 .
$$

[CBSE 2005, '07, '08]

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \text { LHS }=(1+\cot \theta-\operatorname{cosec} \theta)(1+\tan \theta+\sec \theta) \\
&=\left(1+\frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta}-\frac{1}{\sin \theta}\right)\left(1+\frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}+\frac{1}{\cos \theta}\right) \\
&=\frac{(\sin \theta+\cos \theta-1)}{\sin \theta} \cdot \frac{(\cos \theta+\sin \theta+1)}{\cos \theta} \\
&=\frac{[(\sin \theta+\cos \theta)-1] \cdot[(\sin \theta+\cos \theta)+1]}{\sin \theta \cos \theta} \\
&=\frac{(\sin \theta+\cos \theta)^{2}-1}{\sin \theta \cos \theta} \\
&=\frac{\sin ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta+2 \sin \theta \cos \theta-1}{\sin \theta \cos \theta} \\
&=\frac{1+2 \sin \theta \cos \theta-1}{\sin \theta \cos \theta} \quad\left[\because \sin ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta=1\right] \\
&=\frac{2 \sin \theta \cos \theta}{\sin \theta \cos \theta}=2=\text { RHS. } \\
& \therefore \quad \text { LHS }=\text { RHS. }
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 6:

Prove that

$$
\frac{\cos \theta}{(1-\tan \theta)}+\frac{\sin \theta}{(1-\cot \theta)}=(\cos \theta+\sin \theta)
$$

[CBSE 2007]

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =\frac{\cos \theta}{(1-\tan \theta)}+\frac{\sin \theta}{(1-\cot \theta)}=\frac{\cos \theta}{\left(1-\frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}\right)}+\frac{\sin \theta}{\left(1-\frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta}\right)} \\
& =\frac{\cos ^{2} \theta}{(\cos \theta-\sin \theta)}+\frac{\sin ^{2} \theta}{(\sin \theta-\cos \theta)} \\
& =\frac{\cos ^{2} \theta}{\cos \theta-\sin \theta}-\frac{\sin ^{2} \theta}{\cos \theta-\sin \theta} \\
& =\frac{\cos ^{2} \theta-\sin ^{2} \theta}{(\cos \theta-\sin \theta)}=\frac{(\cos \theta-\sin \theta)(\cos \theta+\sin \theta)}{(\cos \theta-\sin \theta)} \\
& =(\cos \theta+\sin \theta)=\text { RHS. } \\
\therefore \quad \text { LHS } & =\text { RHS. }
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 8:

Prove that

$$
\begin{equation*}
\frac{\tan A}{(1-\cot A)}+\frac{\cot A}{(1-\tan A)}=(1+\tan A+\cot A) \tag{CBSE2010}
\end{equation*}
$$

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =\frac{\tan A}{(1-\cot A)}+\frac{\cot A}{(1-\tan A)}=\frac{\frac{\sin A}{\cos A}}{\left(1-\frac{\cos A}{\sin A}\right)}+\frac{\frac{\cos A}{\sin A}}{\left(1-\frac{\sin A}{\cos A}\right)} \\
& =\frac{\sin ^{2} A}{\cos A(\sin A-\cos A)}+\frac{\cos ^{2} A}{\sin A(\cos A-\sin A)} \\
& =\frac{\sin ^{2} A}{\cos A\left(\sin ^{A-\cos A)}\right.}-\frac{\cos ^{2} A}{\sin A(\sin A-\cos A)} \\
& =\frac{\sin ^{3} A-\cos ^{3} A}{\sin A \cos A(\sin A-\cos A)} \\
& =\frac{(\sin A-\cos A)\left(\sin ^{2} A+\cos ^{2} A+\sin A \cos A\right)}{\sin A \cos A(\sin A-\cos A)} \\
& =\frac{1+\sin A \cos A}{\sin A \cos A} \cdot \quad\left[\because\left(a^{3}-b^{3}\right)=(a-b)\left(a^{2}+b^{2}+a b\right)\right] \\
\text { RHS } & =(1+\tan A+\cot A) \\
& =\left(1+\frac{\sin A}{\cos A}+\frac{\cos A}{\sin A}\right)=\frac{\sin A \cos A+\sin ^{2} A+\cos ^{2} A}{\sin A \cos A} \\
& =\frac{(1+\sin A \cos A)}{\sin A \cos A} . \\
\therefore \quad \text { LHS } & =\text { RHS. }
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 9:

Prove that

$$
(\operatorname{cosec} A-\sin A)(\sec A-\cos A)=\frac{1}{(\tan A+\cot A)}
$$

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{LHS} & =(\operatorname{cosec} A-\sin A)(\sec A-\cos A) \\
& =\left(\frac{1}{\sin A}-\sin A\right)\left(\frac{1}{\cos A}-\cos A\right)=\left(\frac{1-\sin ^{2} A}{\sin A}\right) \cdot\left(\frac{1-\cos ^{2} A}{\cos A}\right) \\
& =\frac{\cos ^{2} A \sin ^{2} A}{\cos A \sin A}=\cos A \sin A \\
\mathrm{RHS} & =\frac{1}{(\tan A+\cot A)}=\frac{1}{\left(\frac{\sin A}{\cos A}+\frac{\cos A}{\sin A}\right)} \\
& =\frac{\cos A \sin A}{\left(\sin ^{2} A+\cos ^{2} A\right)}=\cos A \sin A \quad\left[\because \sin ^{2} A+\cos ^{2} A=1\right]
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\mathrm{LHS}=\mathrm{RHS}$.

### Example 10:

Prove that

$$
\left(\frac{1+\tan ^{2} A}{1+\cot ^{2} A}\right)=\left(\frac{1-\tan A}{1-\cot A}\right)^{2}=\tan ^{2} A

[CBSE 2008C]

$$
\begin{gathered}

\end{gathered}
