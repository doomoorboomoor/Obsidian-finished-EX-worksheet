## Value of a Polynomial

The **value of a polynomial** $p(x)$ at $x=\alpha$ is obtained by putting $x=\alpha$ in $p(x)$ and it is denoted by $p(\alpha)$.

---

### Example 1

(i) If $p(x)=3 x^{2}-5 x+6$, find $p(2)$.
(ii) If $q(x)=x^{2}-2 \sqrt{2} x+1$, find $q(2 \sqrt{2})$.
(iii) If $r(x)=5 x-4 x^{2}+3$, find $r(-1)$.

#### Solution:

We have:

(i) $p(x)=3 x^{2}-5 x+6$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \quad p(2)=3 \times 2^{2}-5 \times 2+6=(12-10+6)=8 . \\
& \therefore \quad p(2)=8 .
\end{aligned}
$$

(ii) $q(x)=x^{2}-2 \sqrt{2} x+1$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \quad q(2 \sqrt{2})=(2 \sqrt{2})^{2}-(2 \sqrt{2})(2 \sqrt{2})+1=(8-8+1)=1 . \\
& \therefore \quad q(2 \sqrt{2})=1 .
\end{aligned}
$$

(iii) $r(x)=5 x-4 x^{2}+3 \Rightarrow r(x)=-4 x^{2}+5 x+3$.
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
\therefore & r(-1)=(-4) \times(-1)^{2}+5 \times(-1)+3=(-4) \times 1-5+3 \\
& =(-4-5+3)=-6 . \\
\therefore & r(-1)=-6 .
\end{array}
$$

## Zeros of a Polynomial

Let $p(x)$ be a polynomial. If $p(\alpha)=0$ then we say that $\alpha$ is a **zero of the polynomial** $p(x)$.

> **Remark:** Finding the zeros of a polynomial $p(x)$ means solving the equation $p(x)=0$.

---

### Example 2

If $p(x)=x^{3}+2 x^{2}-5 x-6$, find $p(2), p(-1), p(-3)$ and $p(0)$. What do you conclude about the zeros of $p(x)$? Is $0$ a zero of $p(x)$?

#### Solution:

Given, $p(x)=x^{3}+2 x^{2}-5 x-6$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad p(2) & =2^{3}+2 \times 2^{2}-5 \times 2-6=(8+8-10-6)=0 . \\
p(-1) & =(-1)^{3}+2 \times(-1)^{2}-5 \times(-1)-6=(-1+2+5-6)=0 . \\
p(-3) & =(-3)^{3}+2 \times(-3)^{2}-5 \times(-3)-6 \\
& =(-27+18+15-6)=0 . \\
p(0) & =0^{3}+2 \times 0^{2}-5 \times 0-6=(0+0-0-6)=-6 \neq 0 .
\end{aligned}
$$

Since $p(2)=0, p(-1)=0$ and $p(-3)=0$, it follows that $2, -1$ and $-3$ are the **zeros** of $p(x)$.

But, $p(0)=-6 \neq 0$.

Therefore, $0$ is not a zero of $p(x)$.

---

### Example 3

Check whether $-2$ and $2$ are the zeros of the polynomial $x+2$.

#### Solution:

Let $p(x)=x+2$. Then,
$$
p(-2)=(-2+2)=0 \text { and } p(2)=(2+2)=4 \neq 0
$$
$\therefore -2$ is a zero of $x+2$ and $2$ is not a zero of $x+2$.

---

### Example 4

Find a zero of the polynomial:
(i) $p(x)=x-3$
(ii) $q(x)=3 x+2$

#### Solution:

We have:

(i) $p(x)=0 \Rightarrow x-3=0 \Rightarrow x=3$.
$\therefore 3$ is a zero of the polynomial $p(x)=x-3$.

(ii) $q(x)=0 \Rightarrow 3 x+2=0$
$$
\Rightarrow 3 x=-2 \Rightarrow x=\frac{-2}{3}
$$
$\therefore \frac{-2}{3}$ is a zero of the polynomial $q(x)=3 x+2$.

---

### Example 5

Find a zero of the polynomial $p(x)=a x+b, a \neq 0$ and $a, b$ are real numbers.

#### Solution:

Let $p(x)=0$. Then,
$$
\begin{aligned}
p(x)=0 & \Rightarrow a x+b=0 \\
& \Rightarrow a x=-b \Rightarrow x=\frac{-b}{a}
\end{aligned}
$$
$\therefore \frac{-b}{a}$ is a zero of the polynomial, $p(x)=a x+b$.

---

### Example 6

If $p(x)=x+3$ then find $p(x)+p(-x)$.

#### Solution:

We have, $p(x)=x+3$.

Replacing $x$ by $-x$, we get
$$
p(-x)=-x+3 \tag{ii}
$$
Adding the corresponding sides of (i) and (ii), we get
$$
p(x)+p(-x)=6
$$

---

## Some Important Observations

1. A **constant polynomial** does not have any zero.
2. Every **linear polynomial** has one and only one zero.
3. $0$ may or may not be the zero of a given polynomial.
4. A polynomial can have **repeated zeros**.
   For example, $p(x)=x^{2}-2 x+1$ has $1$ as repeated zeros.
5. The **number of zeros** of a polynomial cannot exceed its **degree**.
