## Factorisation of Quadratic Trinomials

### Polynomials of The Form $\boldsymbol{x}^{2}+\boldsymbol{b} \boldsymbol{x}+\boldsymbol{c}$

We find integers **$p$** and **$q$** such that $p+q=b$ and $p q=c$. Then,

$$
\begin{aligned}
x^{2}+b x+c & =x^{2}+(p+q) x+p q \\
& =x^{2}+p x+q x+p q \\
& =x(x+p)+q(x+p) \\
& =(x+p)(x+q) .
\end{aligned}
$$

---

## Solved Examples

### Example: 1

Factorise $x^{2}+9 x+18$.

#### Solution:

The given expression is $x^{2}+9 x+18$.

We try to split $9$ into two parts whose sum is $9$ and product is $18$.
Clearly, $6+3=9$ and $6 \times 3=18$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad x^{2}+9 x+18 & =x^{2}+6 x+3 x+18 \\
& =x(x+6)+3(x+6) \\
& =(x+6)(x+3) .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $x^{2}+9 x+18=(x+6)(x+3)$.

### Example: 2

Factorise $x^{2}+5 x-24$.

#### Solution:

The given expression is $x^{2}+5 x-24$.

We try to split $5$ into two parts whose sum is $5$ and product is $-24$.
Clearly, $8+(-3)=5$ and $8 \times(-3)=-24$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad x^{2}+5 x-24 & =x^{2}+8 x-3 x-24 \\
& =x(x+8)-3(x+8) \\
& =(x+8)(x-3) .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $x^{2}+5 x-24=(x+8)(x-3)$.

### Example: 3

Factorise $x^{2}-4 x-21$.

#### Solution:

The given expression is $x^{2}-4 x-21$.

We split $-4$ into two parts whose sum is $-4$ and product is $-21$.
Clearly, $(-7)+3=-4$ and $(-7) \times 3=-21$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad x^{2}-4 x-21 & =x^{2}-7 x+3 x-21 \\
& =x(x-7)+3(x-7) \\
& =(x-7)(x+3)
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $x^{2}-4 x-21=(x-7)(x+3)$.

### Example: 4

Factorise:
(i) $x^{2}+5 \sqrt{3} x+12$
(ii) $x^{2}+3 \sqrt{3} x-30$

#### Solution:

(i) The given expression is $x^{2}+5 \sqrt{3} x+12$.

We split $5 \sqrt{3}$ into two parts whose sum is $5 \sqrt{3}$ and product is $12$.
Clearly, $(4 \sqrt{3}+\sqrt{3})=5 \sqrt{3}$ and $(4 \sqrt{3} \times \sqrt{3})=12$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad x^{2}+5 \sqrt{3} x+12 & =x^{2}+4 \sqrt{3} x+\sqrt{3} x+12 \\
& =x(x+4 \sqrt{3})+\sqrt{3}(x+4 \sqrt{3}) \\
& =(x+4 \sqrt{3})(x+\sqrt{3})
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $x^{2}+5 \sqrt{3} x+12=(x+4 \sqrt{3})(x+\sqrt{3})$.

(ii) The given expression is $x^{2}+3 \sqrt{3} x-30$.

We split $3 \sqrt{3}$ into two parts whose sum is $3 \sqrt{3}$ and product is $-30$.
Clearly, $(5 \sqrt{3}-2 \sqrt{3})=3 \sqrt{3}$ and $5 \sqrt{3} \times\{-2 \sqrt{3}\}=-30$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad x^{2}+3 \sqrt{3} x-30 & =x^{2}+5 \sqrt{3} x-2 \sqrt{3} x-30 \\
& =x(x+5 \sqrt{3})-2 \sqrt{3}(x+5 \sqrt{3}) \\
& =(x+5 \sqrt{3})(x-2 \sqrt{3})
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $x^{2}+3 \sqrt{3} x-30=(x+5 \sqrt{3})(x-2 \sqrt{3})$.

### Example: 5

Factorise $(p+q)^{2}-20(p+q)-125$.

#### Solution:

The given expression is $(p+q)^{2}-20(p+q)-125$.
Putting $(p+q)=x$, it becomes $x^{2}-20 x-125$.
We split $-20$ into two parts whose sum is $-20$ and product is $-125$.
Clearly, $(-25+5)=-20$ and $(-25) \times 5=-125$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad x^{2}-20 x-125 &=x^{2}-25 x+5 x-125 \\
& =x(x-25)+5(x-25) \\
& =(x-25)(x+5) \\
\therefore \quad(p+q)^{2}-20(p+q)-125 &=(p+q-25)(p+q+5) \quad [\because x=p+q]
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example: 6

Factorise $a^{4}+4 a^{2}+3$.

#### Solution:

The given expression is $a^{4}+4 a^{2}+3$.
Putting $a^{2}=x$, it becomes $x^{2}+4 x+3$.
We split $4$ into two parts whose sum is $4$ and product is $3$.
Clearly, $(3+1)=4$ and $(3 \times 1)=3$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad x^{2}+4 x+3 & =x^{2}+3 x+x+3 \\
& =x(x+3)+(x+3)=(x+3)(x+1) \\
\therefore \quad (a^{4}+4 a^{2}+3) &= \left(a^{2}+3\right)\left(a^{2}+1\right) \quad\left[\because x=a^{2}\right]
\end{aligned}
$$

### Factorisation of Polynomials of The Form $\boldsymbol{a x^{2}+b x+c}$

We find integers **$p$** and **$q$** such that $p+q=b$ and $p q=a c$. Then,

$$
\begin{aligned}
a x^{2}+b x+c & =a x^{2}+(p+q) x+\frac{p q}{a} \\
& =a^{2} x^{2}+a p x+a q x+p q \\
& =a x(a x+p)+q(a x+p) \\
& =(a x+p)(a x+q)
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\left(a x^{2}+b x+c\right)=(a x+p)(a x+q)$.

### Example: 7

Factorise $6 x^{2}+17 x+5$.

#### Solution:

The given expression is $6 x^{2}+17 x+5$. Here, $6 \times 5=30$.
We split $17$ into two parts whose sum is $17$ and product is $30$.
Clearly, $(15+2)=17$ and $(15 \times 2)=30$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad 6 x^{2}+17 x+5 & =6 x^{2}+15 x+2 x+5 \\
& =3 x(2 x+5)+(2 x+5) \\
& =(2 x+5)(3 x+1)
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $6 x^{2}+17 x+5=(2 x+5)(3 x+1)$.

### Example: 8

Factorise $\sqrt{2} x^{2}+9 x+4 \sqrt{2}$.

#### Solution:

The given expression is $\sqrt{2} x^{2}+9 x+4 \sqrt{2}$. Here, $\sqrt{2} \times 4 \sqrt{2}=8$.
We split $9$ into two parts whose sum is $9$ and product is $8$.
Clearly, $(8+1)=9$ and $(8 \times 1)=8$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad \sqrt{2} x^{2}+9 x+4 \sqrt{2} & =\sqrt{2} x^{2}+8 x+x+4 \sqrt{2} \\
& =\sqrt{2} x(x+4 \sqrt{2})+(x+4 \sqrt{2}) \\
& =(x+4 \sqrt{2})(\sqrt{2} x+1) .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\left(\sqrt{2} x^{2}+9 x+4 \sqrt{2}\right)=(x+4 \sqrt{2})(\sqrt{2} x+1)$.

### Example: 15

Factorise $42-r-r^{2}$.

#### Solution:

The given expression may be written as $-r^{2}-r+42$. Here, $(-1) \times 42=-42$.
So, we split $-1$ into two parts whose sum is $-1$ and product is $-42$.
Clearly, $(-7)+6=-1$ and $(-7) \times 6=-42$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad 42-r-r^{2} & =-r^{2}-r+42 \\
& =-r^{2}-7 r+6 r+42 \\
& =-r(r+7)+6(r+7) \\
& =(r+7)(-r+6)=(7+r)(6-r) .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\left(42-r-r^{2}\right)=(7+r)(6-r)$.

### Example: 16

Factorise $2 x^{2}-\frac{5}{6} x+\frac{1}{12}$.

#### Solution:

We have
$$
\begin{aligned}
2 x^{2}-\frac{5}{6} x+\frac{1}{12} & =\frac{\left(24 x^{2}-10 x+1\right)}{12} \\
& =\frac{1}{12}\left(24 x^{2}-10 x+1\right) \\
& =\frac{1}{12}\left(24 x^{2}-6 x-4 x+1\right) \\
& =\frac{1}{12}[6 x(4 x-1)-(4 x-1)] \\
& =\frac{1}{12}(4 x-1)(6 x-1) \\
& =\left(\frac{4 x}{12}-\frac{1}{12}\right)(6 x-1)=\left(\frac{1}{3} x-\frac{1}{12}\right)(6 x-1)
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\left(2 x^{2}-\frac{5}{6} x+\frac{1}{12}\right)=\left(\frac{1}{3} x-\frac{1}{12}\right)(6 x-1)$.

### Example: 17

Factorise $5(3 x+y)^{2}+6(3 x+y)-8$.

#### Solution:

Putting $(3 x+y)=z$ in the given expression, we get
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 5(3 x+y)^{2}+6(3 x+y)-8 \\
= & 5 z^{2}+6 z-8, \text { where } 3 x+y=z \\
= & 5 z^{2}+10 z-4 z-8 \quad [\because 10+(-4)=6 \text { and } 10 \times(-4)=-40] \\
= & 5 z(z+2)-4(z+2) \\
= & (z+2)(5 z-4) \\
= & (3 x+y+2)[5(3 x+y)-4] \\
= & (3 x+y+2)(15 x+5 y-4)
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $5(3 x+y)^{2}+6(3 x+y)-8=(3 x+y+2)(15 x+5 y-4)$.

### Example: 18

Factorise $x^{4}-3 x^{2}+2$.

#### Solution:

Putting $x^{2}=y$, we get
$$
\begin{aligned}
& x^{4}-3 x^{2}+2 \\
= & y^{2}-3 y+2, \text { where } x^{2}=y \\
= & y^{2}-2 y-y+2 \\
= & y(y-2)-(y-2) \\
= & (y-2)(y-1) \\
= & \left(x^{2}-2\right)\left(x^{2}-1\right) \quad \left[\because y=x^{2}\right] \\
= & (x+\sqrt{2})(x-\sqrt{2})(x+1)(x-1) .
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, $\left(x^{4}-3 x^{2}+2\right)=(x+\sqrt{2})(x-\sqrt{2})(x+1)(x-1)$.

$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
