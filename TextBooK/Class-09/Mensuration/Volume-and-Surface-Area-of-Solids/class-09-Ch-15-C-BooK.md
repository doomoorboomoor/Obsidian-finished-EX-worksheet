# Right Circular Cone

We see around us many objects such as an ice-cream cone, a conical vessel, a clown's cap, etc. These objects are said to have the shape of a right circular cone. In geometry, we define it as under.

A **right circular cone** is the solid generated by the rotation of a right-angled triangle about one of the sides containing the right angle.

Thus, on rotating a right-angled triangular lamina $AOB$ about $OA$, it generates a cone.

![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-09/Mensuration/Volume-and-Surface-Area-of-Solids/class-09-Ch-15-C-BooK-001.jpg)

- The point **A** is the **vertex** of the cone.
- Its **base** is a circle with centre **O** and radius **OB**.
- The length **OA** is the **height** of the cone and the length **AB** is called its **slant height**.

Clearly, $\angle AOB=90^{\circ}$.

If radius of the base = $r$ units, height = $h$ units and slant height = $l$ units then:

$$
l^{2} = (h^{2}+r^{2}) \Rightarrow l = \sqrt{h^{2}+r^{2}}
$$

### Formulae

For a right circular cone of radius = $r$ units, height = $h$ units and slant height = $l$ units, we have:

1.  **Slant height of the cone ($l$)** = $\sqrt{h^{2}+r^{2}}$ units.
2.  **Volume of the cone** = $\frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h$ cubic units.
3.  **Area of curved surface** = $(\pi r l)$ sq units = $(\pi r \sqrt{h^{2}+r^{2}})$ sq units.
4.  **Total surface area** = (area of the curved surface) + (area of the base) = $(\pi r l+\pi r^{2})$ sq units = $\pi r(l+r)$ sq units.

> **Note**: Take $\pi=\frac{22}{7}$, unless stated otherwise.

## Solved Examples

### Example: 1

The height of a cone is 24 cm and the diameter of its base is 14 cm. Find the slant height, volume, area of curved surface and the total surface area of the cone.

#### Solution:

Here, $h=24 \text{ cm}$ and $r=7 \text{ cm}$.
Let the slant height be $l \text{ cm}$. Then,

$$
l = \sqrt{h^{2}+r^{2}} = \sqrt{(24)^{2}+(7)^{2}} = \sqrt{625} = 25
$$

$\therefore$ **Slant height** = $l \text{ cm} = 25 \text{ cm}$.

![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-09/Mensuration/Volume-and-Surface-Area-of-Solids/class-09-Ch-15-C-BooK-002.jpg)

**Volume of the cone**
$$
= \frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h = \left(\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 7 \times 24\right) \text{cm}^{3} = 1232 \text{ cm}^{3}
$$

**Curved surface area of the cone**
$$
= \pi r l = \left(\frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 25\right) \text{cm}^{2} = 550 \text{ cm}^{2}
$$

**Total surface area of the cone**
$$
= \pi r(l+r) = \left[\frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times (25+7)\right] \text{cm}^{2} = 704 \text{ cm}^{2}
$$

### Example: 2

How many metres of cloth, 5 m wide, will be required to make a conical tent, the radius of whose base is 7 m and height is 24 m?

#### Solution:

Radius of the tent, $r=7 \text{ m}$ and its height, $h=24 \text{ m}$.

$\therefore$ **Slant height**,
$$
l = \sqrt{r^{2}+h^{2}} = \sqrt{(7)^{2}+(24)^{2}} \text{ m} = \sqrt{625} \text{ m} = 25 \text{ m}
$$

**Area of the curved surface**
$$
= (\pi r l) \text{ sq units} = \left(\frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 25\right) \text{m}^{2} = 550 \text{ m}^{2}
$$

Thus, the area of the cloth = $550 \text{ m}^{2}$.

**Length of the cloth required**
$$
= \left(\frac{\text{area}}{\text{width}}\right) = \left(\frac{550}{5}\right) \text{ m} = 110 \text{ m}
$$

Hence, length of the cloth required is 110 m.

### Example: 3

The height and the slant height of a cone are 21 cm and 28 cm respectively. Find the volume of the cone.

#### Solution:

We have, $h=21 \text{ cm}$ and $l=28 \text{ cm}$.

$$
\therefore l^{2}=r^{2}+h^{2} \Rightarrow r = \sqrt{l^{2}-h^{2}} = \sqrt{(28)^{2}-(21)^{2}} \text{ cm}
$$

$$
\Rightarrow r = \sqrt{(28+21) \times (28-21)} \text{ cm} = \sqrt{49 \times 7} \text{ cm} = 7 \sqrt{7} \text{ cm}
$$

**Volume of the cone**
$$
= \frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h = \left\{\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times (7 \sqrt{7})^{2} \times 21\right\} \text{cm}^{3} = (22 \times 343) \text{cm}^{3} = 7546 \text{cm}^{3}
$$

### Example: 4

The volume of a right circular cone is $9856 \text{ cm}^{3}$. If the diameter of the base is 28 cm, find:
(i) height of the cone,
(ii) slant height of the cone,
(iii) curved surface area of the cone.

#### Solution:

We have, $V=9856 \text{ cm}^{3}$ and $r=14 \text{ cm}$.

**(i)** Let the height of the cone be $h \text{ cm}$.
Then, volume = $\frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h$.

$$
\Rightarrow 9856 = \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 14 \times 14 \times h
$$

$$
\Rightarrow h = \left(\frac{9856 \times 3 \times 7}{22 \times 14 \times 14}\right) = 48
$$

$\therefore$ **Height of the cone** is 48 cm.

**(ii)** Let the slant height of the cone be $l \text{ cm}$. Then,

$$
l^{2} = r^{2}+h^{2}
$$

$$
\Rightarrow l^{2} = (14)^{2}+(48)^{2} = 196+2304 = 2500
$$

$$
\Rightarrow l = \sqrt{2500} = 50
$$

$\therefore$ **Slant height of the cone** is 50 cm.

**(iii) Curved surface area of the cone**
$$
= \pi r l = \left(\frac{22}{7} \times 14 \times 50\right) \text{cm}^{2} = 2200 \text{cm}^{2}
$$

$\therefore$ Curved surface area of the cone is $2200 \text{ cm}^{2}$.

### Example: 5

The radius and the height of a right circular cone are in the ratio of $5:12$ and its volume is 2512 cu cm. Find the curved surface area and the total surface area of the cone. (Use $\pi=3.14$.)

#### Solution:

Let radius = $5x \text{ cm}$ and height = $12x \text{ cm}$. Then,

$$
\text{volume} = \left[\frac{1}{3} \times 3.14 \times (5x)^{2} \times (12x)\right] \text{cm}^{3} = (314 x^{3}) \text{cm}^{3}
$$

$$
\therefore 314 x^{3} = 2512 \Rightarrow x^{3} = \left(\frac{2512}{314}\right) = 8 \Rightarrow x=2
$$

$\therefore$ **Radius** = 10 cm and **height** = 24 cm.

$\therefore$ **Slant height**
$$
= \sqrt{r^{2}+h^{2}} = \sqrt{(10)^{2}+(24)^{2}} \text{ cm} = \sqrt{676} \text{ cm} = 26 \text{ cm}
$$

So, **area of the curved surface**
$$
= \pi r l = (3.14 \times 10 \times 26) \text{cm}^{2} = 816.4 \text{ cm}^{2}
$$

**Total surface area**
$$
= (\text{curved surface area} + \text{base area}) = (816.4 + 3.14 \times 10 \times 10) \text{cm}^{2} = (816.4+314) \text{cm}^{2} = 1130.4 \text{ cm}^{2}
$$

### Example: 6

A cone of height 8 m has a curved surface area 188.4 square metres. Find its volume. (Take $\pi=3.14$.)

#### Solution:

Let the base radius be $r$ metres and slant height be $l$ metres. Then,
**Curved surface area**
$$
= \pi r l = \pi r \sqrt{r^{2}+h^{2}} = (3.14 \times r \times \sqrt{r^{2}+64}) \text{ m}^{2}
$$

$$
\therefore 3.14 \times r \times \sqrt{r^{2}+64} = 188.4
$$

$$
\Rightarrow r \sqrt{r^{2}+64} = \frac{188.4}{3.14} = 60
$$

$$
\Rightarrow r^{2}(r^{2}+64) = 3600
$$

$$
\Rightarrow r^{4}+64 r^{2}-3600 = 0 \quad [\text{Let } r^2 = x, \text{ then } x^{2}+64 x-3600=0]
$$

$$
\Rightarrow (r^{2}+100)(r^{2}-36) = 0 \quad [\text{i.e. } (x+100)(x-36)=0]
$$

$$
\Rightarrow r^{2}=36 \quad [\because r^{2}=-100 \text{ gives imaginary value of } r]
$$

$$
\Rightarrow r=6
$$

So, the radius of the base = 6 m.

**Volume**
$$
= \left(\frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h\right) \text{ cubic units} = \left(\frac{1}{3} \times 3.14 \times 6 \times 6 \times 8\right) \text{m}^{3} = 301.44 \text{ m}^{3}
$$

Hence, the volume of the cone is $301.44 \text{ m}^{3}$.

### Example: 7

What length of tarpaulin 3 m wide will be required to make a conical tent of height 8 m and base radius 6 m? Assume that the extra length of material required for stitching margins and wastage in cutting is 20 cm. (Use $\pi=3.14$.)

#### Solution:

Base radius of the tent, $r=6 \text{ m}$ and its height, $h=8 \text{ m}$.

$$
\therefore l = \sqrt{r^{2}+h^{2}} = \sqrt{6^{2}+8^{2}} \text{ m} = \sqrt{36+64} \text{ m} = \sqrt{100} \text{ m} = 10 \text{ m}
$$

**Curved surface area of the conical tent**
$$
= \pi r l = (3.14 \times 6 \times 10) \text{m}^{2} = 188.4 \text{ m}^{2}
$$

Area of tarpaulin required = $188.4 \text{ m}^{2}$.

Width of tarpaulin = 3 m.

**Length of tarpaulin**
$$
= \frac{\text{area}}{\text{width}} = \frac{188.4}{3} \text{ m} = 62.8 \text{ m}
$$

Extra length required = $20 \text{ cm} = \frac{20}{100} \text{ m} = 0.2 \text{ m}$.

**Total length of tarpaulin required**
$$
= (62.8+0.2) \text{ m} = 63 \text{ m}
$$

### Example: 8

A corn cob, shaped somewhat like a cone, has the radius of its broadest end as 2.1 cm and length as 20 cm. If each $1 \text{ cm}^{2}$ of the surface of the cob carries an average of four grains, find how many grains you would find on the entire cob.

#### Solution:

We know that the grains of corn are found on the curved surface of the corn cob.

$\therefore$ **Number of grains on the corn cob**
$$
= (\text{curved surface area of the corn cob}) \times (\text{number of grains of corn on } 1 \text{ cm}^{2})
$$

So, we shall find the curved surface area of the corn cob.
We have, $r=2.1 \text{ cm}$ and $h=20 \text{ cm}$.

$$
\therefore l^{2} = r^{2}+h^{2}=(2.1)^{2}+(20)^{2}=404.41
$$

So, $l = \sqrt{404.41} \text{ cm} = 20.109 \text{ cm} \approx 20.11 \text{ cm}$.

**Curved surface area of the corn cob**
$$
= \pi r l = \left(\frac{22}{7} \times 2.1 \times 20.11\right) \text{cm}^{2} = 132.726 \text{ cm}^{2} \approx 132.73 \text{ cm}^{2}
$$

Number of grains on $1 \text{ cm}^{2} = 4$.

$\therefore$ **Number of grains on entire corn cob**
$$
= (132.73 \times 4) = 530.92 \approx 531
$$

So, there would be approximately 531 grains on the corn cob.

### Example: 9

A tent is in the form of a right circular cylinder, surmounted by a cone. The diameter of the cylinder is 24 m. The height of the cylindrical portion is 11 m, while the vertex of the cone is 16 m above the ground. Find the area of the canvas required for the tent.

#### Solution:

![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-09/Mensuration/Volume-and-Surface-Area-of-Solids/class-09-Ch-15-C-BooK-003.jpg)

**Area of the curved surface of the cylindrical portion**
$$
= 2 \pi R H \text{ sq units} = (2\pi \times 12 \times 11) \text{m}^{2}=(264\pi) \text{m}^{2}
$$

Radius of the cone, $r=12 \text{ m}$ and its height, $h=(16-11) \text{ m} = 5 \text{ m}$.

**Slant height of the cone,** $l=\sqrt{r^{2}+h^{2}}$
$$
\therefore l=\sqrt{(12)^{2}+(5)^{2}}=\sqrt{169}=13 \text{ m}
$$

**Area of curved surface of the cone**
$$
= (\pi r l) \text{ sq units} = (\pi \times 12 \times 13) \text{m}^{2}=(156\pi) \text{m}^{2}
$$

**Area of canvas** = (curved surface area of the cylindrical part) + (curved surface area of the conical part)
$$
= (264\pi+156\pi) \text{m}^{2} = (420\pi) \text{m}^{2} = \left(420 \times \frac{22}{7}\right) \text{m}^{2} = 1320 \text{m}^{2}
$$

Hence, the area of canvas required is $1320 \text{ m}^{2}$.

### Example: 10

A right $\triangle ABC$ with sides 5 cm, 12 cm and 13 cm is revolved about the side 12 cm. Find the volume of the solid so formed. If $\triangle ABC$ is revolved about the side 5 cm then find the volume of the solid so formed. Find also the ratio of the volumes of the two solids obtained.

#### Solution:

Let $\triangle ABC$ be right angled at $B$ with $AB=5 \text{ cm}, BC=12 \text{ cm}$ and $AC=13 \text{ cm}$.

On revolving $\triangle ABC$ about the side $BC=12 \text{ cm}$, we obtain a right circular cone of radius 5 cm and height 12 cm.

![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-09/Mensuration/Volume-and-Surface-Area-of-Solids/class-09-Ch-15-C-BooK-004.jpg)

**Volume of the cone so formed ($V_1$)**
$$
= \left\{\frac{1}{3} \pi \times (5)^{2} \times 12\right\} \text{cm}^{3} = (100\pi) \text{cm}^{3}
$$

On revolving $\triangle ABC$ about the side $AB=5 \text{ cm}$, we obtain a right circular cone of radius 12 cm and height 5 cm.

**Volume of the cone so formed ($V_2$)**
$$
= \left\{\frac{1}{3} \pi \times (12)^{2} \times 5\right\} \text{cm}^{3} = (240\pi) \text{cm}^{3}
$$

**Ratio of volumes**
$$
\therefore \frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}=\frac{100 \pi}{240 \pi}=\frac{5}{12} \Rightarrow V_{1}: V_{2}=5: 12
$$

### Example: 11

A semicircular sheet of diameter 28 cm is bent to form an open conical cup. Find the capacity of the cup. (Use $\sqrt{3}=1.732$.)

#### Solution:

When the semicircular sheet is bent into an open conical cup, the radius of the sheet becomes the slant height of the conical cup.

$\therefore l=14 \text{ cm}$.

**Circumference of the base of the cone** = length of arc ABC
$$
= (\pi \times 14) \text{ cm} = \left(\frac{22}{7} \times 14\right) \text{ cm} = 44 \text{ cm}
$$

Let the radius of the cone be $r \text{ cm}$. Then,
$$
2 \pi r=44 \Rightarrow 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times r=44 \Rightarrow r=7 \text{ cm}
$$

Let the height of the cone be $h \text{ cm}$. Then,
$$
h^{2} = l^{2}-r^{2}=(14)^{2}-(7)^{2}=196-49=147
$$

$$
\therefore h = \sqrt{147}=\sqrt{7 \times 7 \times 3}=7 \sqrt{3} \text{ cm} = (7 \times 1.732) \text{ cm} = 12.12 \text{ cm}
$$

**Capacity of the conical cup** = volume of the conical cup
$$
= \frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h = \left(\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 7 \times 12.12\right) \text{cm}^{3} = (154 \times 4.04) \text{cm}^{3} = 622.16 \text{cm}^{3}
$$

Hence, the capacity of the conical cup is $622.16 \text{ cm}^{3}$.

### Example: 12

A conical tent is to accommodate 11 persons. Each person must have $4 \text{ m}^{2}$ of the space on the ground and $20 \text{ m}^{3}$ of air to breath. Find the height of the tent.

#### Solution:

Let the radius of the base of the conical tent be $r$ metres and its height be $h$ metres.

Area of the base = $(4 \times 11) \text{ m}^{2}=44 \text{ m}^{2} \Rightarrow \pi r^{2}=44 \text{ m}^{2}$ ... (i)

Volume of the cone = $(20 \times 11) \text{ m}^{3}=220 \text{ m}^{3}$
$\Rightarrow \frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h=220 \text{ m}^{3} \Rightarrow \pi r^{2} h=660 \text{ m}^{3}$ ... (ii)

On dividing (ii) by (i), we get:
$$
\frac{\pi r^{2} h}{\pi r^{2}}=\frac{660 \text{ m}^{3}}{44 \text{ m}^{2}} \Rightarrow h=15 \text{ m}
$$

Hence, the height of the tent is 15 metres.

### Example: 13

A cylinder lies within a cube touching all its vertical faces and a cone lies inside the cylinder. If their heights are same with the same base, find the ratio of their volumes.

#### Solution:

Let the length of each edge of the given cube be $a$ units.

Then, **volume of the cube ($V_1$)**
$$
= a^{3} \text{ cubic units}
$$

It is given that the cylinder lies within the cube and touches all its vertical faces.
So, the radius of the base of the cylinder = $\frac{a}{2}$ units and height of the cylinder = $a$ units.

**Volume of the cylinder ($V_2$)**
$$
= \pi r^{2} h = \left\{\frac{22}{7} \times \left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^{2} \times a\right\} \text{ cubic units} = \left(\frac{11 a^{3}}{14}\right) \text{ cubic units}
$$

A cone is drawn inside the cylinder such that both have the same base and same height.
$\therefore$ radius of the base of the cone = $\frac{a}{2}$ units and height of the cone = $a$ units.

$\therefore$ **volume of the cone ($V_3$)**
$$
= \frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h = \left(\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times \frac{a^{2}}{4} \times a\right) \text{ cubic units} = \frac{11 a^{3}}{42} \text{ cubic units}
$$

$\therefore$ **Ratio of their volumes** is given as:
$$
V_{1}: V_{2}: V_{3} = a^{3}: \frac{11 a^{3}}{14}: \frac{11 a^{3}}{42} = 42: 33: 11
$$

### Example: 14

Find the volume of the largest right circular cone that can be cut out of a cube whose edge is 21 cm.

#### Solution:

The base of the largest right circular cone will be the circle inscribed in a face of the cube and its height will be equal to an edge of the cube.

$\therefore$ radius of the base of the cone, $r=\frac{21}{2} \text{ cm}$ and height of the cone, $h=21 \text{ cm}$.

$\therefore$ **volume of the required cone**
$$
= \frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h = \left(\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times \frac{21}{2} \times \frac{21}{2} \times 21\right) \text{cm}^{3} = \frac{4851}{2} \text{ cm}^{3} = 2425.5 \text{ cm}^{3}
$$

### Example: 15

The height of a cone is 30 cm. A small cone is cut off at the top by a plane parallel to the base. If its volume be $\frac{1}{27}$ of the volume of the given cone, at what height above the base, the section has been made?

#### Solution:

![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/TextBooK/Class-09/Mensuration/Volume-and-Surface-Area-of-Solids/class-09-Ch-15-C-BooK-005.jpg)

Let the smaller cone have radius = $r \text{ cm}$ and height = $h \text{ cm}$.
And, let the radius of the given original cone be $R \text{ cm}$.

From similar triangles $\triangle OCD \sim \triangle OAB$, we have:
$$
\frac{r}{R} = \frac{h}{30} \Rightarrow r = \frac{h R}{30} \quad \ldots \text{ (i)}
$$

Given, Volume of smaller cone = $\frac{1}{27} \times$ (volume of larger cone)
$$
\therefore \frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h = \frac{1}{27} \times \left(\frac{1}{3} \pi R^{2} \times 30\right)
$$

$$
\Rightarrow r^{2} h = \frac{10}{9} R^{2}
$$

$$
\Rightarrow \left(\frac{h R}{30}\right)^{2} \times h = \frac{10}{9} R^{2} \quad [\text{using (i)}]
$$

$$
\Rightarrow h^{3} = \left(\frac{10}{9} \times 900\right) = 1000 = (10)^{3} \Rightarrow h=10 \text{ cm}
$$

$\therefore$ Required height from the base, $AC = OA - OC = (30-10) \text{ cm} = 20 \text{ cm}$.

Hence, the section has been made at a height of 20 cm from the base.
