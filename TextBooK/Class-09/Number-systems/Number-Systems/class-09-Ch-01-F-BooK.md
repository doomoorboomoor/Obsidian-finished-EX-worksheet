## Rationalisation

**Rationalisation** is the process of converting a number with an irrational denominator into an equivalent expression with a rational denominator. This is achieved by multiplying both the numerator and the denominator by a suitable number.

---

### Example 1

Simplify $\frac{3}{\sqrt{5}}$ by rationalising the denominator.

#### Solution

On multiplying the numerator and denominator of the given number by $\sqrt{5}$, we get:

$$
\frac{3}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{3}{\sqrt{5}} \times \frac{\sqrt{5}}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{3 \sqrt{5}}{5}
$$

---

## Rationalising Factor (RF)

If the product of two irrational numbers is a rational number, then each one is called the **rationalising factor (RF)** of the other.

If $a$ and $b$ are integers and $x, y$ are natural numbers, then:

- $(a+\sqrt{b})$ and $(a-\sqrt{b})$ are RF of each other, as $(a+\sqrt{b})(a-\sqrt{b}) = (a^{2}-b)$, which is rational.
- $(a+b \sqrt{x})$ and $(a-b \sqrt{x})$ are RF of each other, as $(a+b \sqrt{x})(a-b \sqrt{x}) = (a^{2}-b^{2} x)$, which is rational.
- $(\sqrt{x}+\sqrt{y})$ and $(\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{y})$ are RF of each other, as $(\sqrt{x}+\sqrt{y})(\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{y})=(x-y)$, which is rational.

To rationalise the denominator of a number, we multiply its numerator and denominator by its rationalising factor.

For example, the rationalising factor of $\frac{1}{3+\sqrt{2}}$ is $(3-\sqrt{2})$ since:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{1}{3+\sqrt{2}} \times \frac{3-\sqrt{2}}{3-\sqrt{2}} &= \frac{(3-\sqrt{2})}{(3+\sqrt{2})(3-\sqrt{2})} = \frac{(3-\sqrt{2})}{(3)^{2}-(\sqrt{2})^{2}} \\
&= \frac{3-\sqrt{2}}{9-2} = \frac{3-\sqrt{2}}{7}
\end{aligned}
$$

Similarly, the rationalising factor of $\frac{2}{7+4 \sqrt{3}}$ is $(7-4 \sqrt{3})$, and the rationalising factor of $\frac{1}{\sqrt{7}-\sqrt{6}}$ is $(\sqrt{7}+\sqrt{6})$.

## Solved Examples

### Example 1

Rationalise the denominator of $\frac{1}{(3+\sqrt{2})}$.

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{1}{(3+\sqrt{2})} &= \frac{1}{(3+\sqrt{2})} \times \frac{(3-\sqrt{2})}{(3-\sqrt{2})} = \frac{(3-\sqrt{2})}{(3)^{2}-(\sqrt{2})^{2}} \\
&= \frac{(3-\sqrt{2})}{9-2} = \frac{(3-\sqrt{2})}{7}
\end{aligned}
$$

---

### Example 2

Rationalise the denominator of $\frac{1}{(8+5 \sqrt{2})}$.

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{1}{(8+5 \sqrt{2})} &= \frac{1}{(8+5 \sqrt{2})} \times \frac{(8-5 \sqrt{2})}{(8-5 \sqrt{2})} = \frac{(8-5 \sqrt{2})}{(8)^{2}-(5 \sqrt{2})^{2}} \\
&= \frac{(8-5 \sqrt{2})}{64-50} = \frac{(8-5 \sqrt{2})}{14}
\end{aligned}
$$

---

### Example 3

Rationalise the denominator of $\frac{6}{(\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{2})}$.

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{6}{(\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{2})} &= \frac{6}{(\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{2})} \times \frac{(\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2})}{(\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2})} = \frac{6(\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2})}{(\sqrt{5})^{2}-(\sqrt{2})^{2}} \\
&= \frac{6(\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2})}{5-2} = \frac{6(\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2})}{3} = 2(\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2})
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 4

Rationalise the denominator of $\frac{2}{(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{5})}$. *[2011]*

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{2}{(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{5})} &= \frac{2}{(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{5})} \times \frac{(\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{5})}{(\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{5})} = \frac{2(\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{5})}{(\sqrt{3})^{2}-(\sqrt{5})^{2}} \\
&= \frac{2(\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{5})}{3-5} = \frac{2(\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{5})}{-2} = -(\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{5})
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 5

Simplify by rationalising the denominator: $\frac{6-4 \sqrt{3}}{6+4 \sqrt{3}}$. *[2010, '14]*

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{6-4 \sqrt{3}}{6+4 \sqrt{3}} &= \frac{(6-4 \sqrt{3})}{(6+4 \sqrt{3})} \times \frac{(6-4 \sqrt{3})}{(6-4 \sqrt{3})} = \frac{(6-4 \sqrt{3})^{2}}{(6)^{2}-(4 \sqrt{3})^{2}} \\
&= \frac{6^{2}+(4 \sqrt{3})^{2}-2 \times 6 \times 4 \sqrt{3}}{36-48} = \frac{36+48-48 \sqrt{3}}{-12} \\
&= \frac{84-48 \sqrt{3}}{-12} = \frac{48 \sqrt{3}-84}{12} = (4 \sqrt{3}-7)
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 6

Find the values of $a$ and $b$ if $\frac{3+\sqrt{2}}{3-\sqrt{2}}=a+b \sqrt{2}$. *[2014, '15]*

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{3+\sqrt{2}}{3-\sqrt{2}} &= \frac{(3+\sqrt{2})}{(3-\sqrt{2})} \times \frac{(3+\sqrt{2})}{(3+\sqrt{2})} = \frac{(3+\sqrt{2})^{2}}{(3)^{2}-(\sqrt{2})^{2}} \\
&= \frac{3^{2}+(\sqrt{2})^{2}+2 \times 3 \times \sqrt{2}}{9-2} = \frac{9+2+6 \sqrt{2}}{7} = \frac{11+6 \sqrt{2}}{7}
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{3+\sqrt{2}}{3-\sqrt{2}} = a+b \sqrt{2} \Leftrightarrow \frac{11}{7}+\frac{6}{7} \sqrt{2} = a+b \sqrt{2}
$$

$$
\Leftrightarrow a=\frac{11}{7} \text{ and } b=\frac{6}{7}
$$

### Example 7

If $a$ and $b$ are rational numbers and $\frac{\sqrt{11}-\sqrt{7}}{\sqrt{11}+\sqrt{7}}=a-b \sqrt{77}$, find the values of $a$ and $b$.

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{\sqrt{11}-\sqrt{7}}{\sqrt{11}+\sqrt{7}} &= \frac{(\sqrt{11}-\sqrt{7})}{(\sqrt{11}+\sqrt{7})} \times \frac{(\sqrt{11}-\sqrt{7})}{(\sqrt{11}-\sqrt{7})} \\
&= \frac{(\sqrt{11}-\sqrt{7})^{2}}{(\sqrt{11})^{2}-(\sqrt{7})^{2}} = \frac{(11+7-2 \times \sqrt{11} \times \sqrt{7})}{11-7} \\
&= \frac{(18-2 \sqrt{77})}{4} = \left(\frac{18}{4}-\frac{2}{4} \cdot \sqrt{77}\right) = \left(\frac{9}{2}-\frac{1}{2} \cdot \sqrt{77}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{\sqrt{11}-\sqrt{7}}{\sqrt{11}+\sqrt{7}} = a-b \sqrt{77} \Leftrightarrow \frac{9}{2}-\frac{1}{2} \cdot \sqrt{77} = a-b \sqrt{77}
$$

$$
\Leftrightarrow a=\frac{9}{2} \text{ and } b=\frac{1}{2}
$$

Hence, $a=\frac{9}{2}$ and $b=\frac{1}{2}$.

### Example 8

If $a$ and $b$ are rational numbers and $\frac{4+3 \sqrt{5}}{4-3 \sqrt{5}}=a+b \sqrt{5}$, find the values of $a$ and $b$.

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{4+3 \sqrt{5}}{4-3 \sqrt{5}} &= \frac{(4+3 \sqrt{5})}{(4-3 \sqrt{5})} \times \frac{(4+3 \sqrt{5})}{4+3 \sqrt{5}} \\
&= \frac{(4+3 \sqrt{5})^{2}}{(4)^{2}-(3 \sqrt{5})^{2}} = \frac{(4+3 \sqrt{5})^{2}}{16-45} = \frac{(4+3 \sqrt{5})^{2}}{-29} \\
&= \frac{(4)^{2}+(3 \sqrt{5})^{2}+2 \times 4 \times 3 \sqrt{5}}{-29} \\
&= \frac{(16+45+24 \sqrt{5})}{-29} \\
&= \frac{(61+24 \sqrt{5})}{-29} = \left(-\frac{61}{29}\right)+\left(-\frac{24}{29}\right) \sqrt{5}
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{4+3 \sqrt{5}}{4-3 \sqrt{5}} = a+b \sqrt{5} \Leftrightarrow \left(-\frac{61}{29}\right)+\left(-\frac{24}{29}\right) \sqrt{5} = a+b \sqrt{5}
$$

$$
\therefore \quad a=-\frac{61}{29} \text{ and } b=-\frac{24}{29}
$$

### Example 9

Evaluate after rationalising the denominator of $\frac{25}{\sqrt{40}-\sqrt{80}}$, it being given that $\sqrt{5}=2.236$ and $\sqrt{10}=3.162$. *[2011]*

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{25}{\sqrt{40}-\sqrt{80}} &= \frac{25}{2 \sqrt{10}-4 \sqrt{5}} = \frac{25}{2(\sqrt{10}-2 \sqrt{5})} \times \frac{(\sqrt{10}+2 \sqrt{5})}{(\sqrt{10}+2 \sqrt{5})} \\
&= \frac{25(\sqrt{10}+2 \sqrt{5})}{2(\sqrt{10}-2 \sqrt{5})(\sqrt{10}+2 \sqrt{5})} = \frac{25(\sqrt{10}+2 \sqrt{5})}{2\left[(\sqrt{10})^{2}-(2 \sqrt{5})^{2}\right]} \\
&= \frac{25(\sqrt{10}+2 \sqrt{5})}{2(10-20)} = \frac{25(\sqrt{10}+2 \sqrt{5})}{-20} \\
&= \frac{5(\sqrt{10}+2 \sqrt{5})}{-4} = \frac{5(3.162+2 \times 2.236)}{-4} \\
&= \frac{5 \times (3.162 + 4.472)}{-4} = \frac{5 \times 7.634}{-4} = \frac{38.17}{-4} = -9.5425 \approx -9.543
\end{aligned}
$$
*Note: The calculation in the source document appears to contain an error. $(\sqrt{10})^2 - (2\sqrt{5})^2 = 10-20 = -10$, not $100-20=80$. The calculation above is corrected to follow standard algebraic rules, while the final numeric evaluation is adjusted accordingly.*

### Example 10

Find the values of $a$ and $b$ if $\frac{7+3 \sqrt{5}}{3+\sqrt{5}}-\frac{7-3 \sqrt{5}}{3-\sqrt{5}}=a+\sqrt{5} b$. *[2010, '11, '15]*

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \frac{7+3 \sqrt{5}}{3+\sqrt{5}}-\frac{7-3 \sqrt{5}}{3-\sqrt{5}} \\
&= \frac{(7+3 \sqrt{5})}{(3+\sqrt{5})} \times \frac{(3-\sqrt{5})}{(3-\sqrt{5})} - \frac{(7-3 \sqrt{5})}{(3-\sqrt{5})} \times \frac{(3+\sqrt{5})}{(3+\sqrt{5})} \\
&= \frac{(21-7 \sqrt{5}+9 \sqrt{5}-15)}{(3+\sqrt{5})(3-\sqrt{5})} - \frac{(21+7 \sqrt{5}-9 \sqrt{5}-15)}{(3-\sqrt{5})(3+\sqrt{5})} \\
&= \frac{(6+2 \sqrt{5})}{(3)^{2}-(\sqrt{5})^{2}} - \frac{(6-2 \sqrt{5})}{(3)^{2}-(\sqrt{5})^{2}} \\
&= \frac{(6+2 \sqrt{5})}{(9-5)} - \frac{(6-2 \sqrt{5})}{(9-5)} = \frac{6+2 \sqrt{5}}{4} - \frac{6-2 \sqrt{5}}{4} \\
&= \frac{6+2 \sqrt{5}-6+2 \sqrt{5}}{4} = \frac{4 \sqrt{5}}{4} = \sqrt{5}
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\therefore \quad \frac{7+3 \sqrt{5}}{3+\sqrt{5}}-\frac{7-3 \sqrt{5}}{3-\sqrt{5}} = a+\sqrt{5} b \Rightarrow \sqrt{5} = a+\sqrt{5} b
$$

$$
\Rightarrow a=0 \text{ and } b=1
$$

### Example 11

Show that $\frac{1}{3-\sqrt{8}}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{8}-\sqrt{7}}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{7}-\sqrt{6}}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}-\sqrt{5}}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}-2}=5$. *[2011, '15]*

#### Solution

We rationalise each term separately:

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \frac{1}{3-\sqrt{8}} = \frac{1}{(3-\sqrt{8})} \times \frac{(3+\sqrt{8})}{(3+\sqrt{8})} = \frac{3+\sqrt{8}}{9-8} = 3+\sqrt{8} \\
& \frac{1}{\sqrt{8}-\sqrt{7}} = \frac{1}{(\sqrt{8}-\sqrt{7})} \times \frac{(\sqrt{8}+\sqrt{7})}{(\sqrt{8}+\sqrt{7})} = \frac{\sqrt{8}+\sqrt{7}}{8-7} = \sqrt{8}+\sqrt{7} \\
& \frac{1}{\sqrt{7}-\sqrt{6}} = \frac{1}{(\sqrt{7}-\sqrt{6})} \times \frac{(\sqrt{7}+\sqrt{6})}{(\sqrt{7}+\sqrt{6})} = \frac{\sqrt{7}+\sqrt{6}}{7-6} = \sqrt{7}+\sqrt{6} \\
& \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}-\sqrt{5}} = \frac{1}{(\sqrt{6}-\sqrt{5})} \times \frac{(\sqrt{6}+\sqrt{5})}{(\sqrt{6}+\sqrt{5})} = \frac{\sqrt{6}+\sqrt{5}}{6-5} = \sqrt{6}+\sqrt{5} \\
& \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}-2} = \frac{1}{(\sqrt{5}-2)} \times \frac{(\sqrt{5}+2)}{(\sqrt{5}+2)} = \frac{\sqrt{5}+2}{5-4} = \sqrt{5}+2
\end{aligned}
$$

Substituting these back into the expression:

$$
\begin{aligned}
& (3+\sqrt{8}) - (\sqrt{8}+\sqrt{7}) + (\sqrt{7}+\sqrt{6}) - (\sqrt{6}+\sqrt{5}) + (\sqrt{5}+2) \\
&= 3+\sqrt{8}-\sqrt{8}-\sqrt{7}+\sqrt{7}+\sqrt{6}-\sqrt{6}-\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{5}+2 \\
&= 3+2 = 5
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 12

If $\sqrt{2}=1.414, \sqrt{3}=1.732, \sqrt{5}=2.236$ and $\sqrt{6}=2.449$, find the value of $\frac{2+\sqrt{3}}{2-\sqrt{3}}+\frac{2-\sqrt{3}}{2+\sqrt{3}}+\frac{\sqrt{3}-1}{\sqrt{3}+1}$. *[CBSE Sample Paper]*

#### Solution

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \frac{2+\sqrt{3}}{2-\sqrt{3}}+\frac{2-\sqrt{3}}{2+\sqrt{3}}+\frac{\sqrt{3}-1}{\sqrt{3}+1} \\
&= \frac{(2+\sqrt{3})}{(2-\sqrt{3})} \times \frac{(2+\sqrt{3})}{(2+\sqrt{3})} + \frac{(2-\sqrt{3})}{(2+\sqrt{3})} \times \frac{(2-\sqrt{3})}{(2-\sqrt{3})} + \frac{(\sqrt{3}-1)}{(\sqrt{3}+1)} \times \frac{(\sqrt{3}-1)}{(\sqrt{3}-1)} \\
&= \frac{(2+\sqrt{3})^{2}}{4-3} + \frac{(2-\sqrt{3})^{2}}{4-3} + \frac{(\sqrt{3}-1)^{2}}{3-1} \\
&= (4+3+4\sqrt{3}) + (4+3-4\sqrt{3}) + \frac{3+1-2\sqrt{3}}{2} \\
&= (7+4\sqrt{3}) + (7-4\sqrt{3}) + \frac{4-2\sqrt{3}}{2} \\
&= 14 + (2-\sqrt{3}) = 16 - \sqrt{3} \\
&= 16 - 1.732 = 14.268
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 13

If $x=3+2 \sqrt{2}$, find the value of $(x^{2}+\frac{1}{x^{2}})$. *[2015]*

#### Solution

Given $x=3+2 \sqrt{2}$.

First, find $\frac{1}{x}$:
$$
\frac{1}{x} = \frac{1}{3+2 \sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{3+2 \sqrt{2}} \times \frac{3-2 \sqrt{2}}{3-2 \sqrt{2}} = \frac{3-2 \sqrt{2}}{9-8} = 3-2 \sqrt{2}
$$

Next, find $x+\frac{1}{x}$:
$$
x+\frac{1}{x} = (3+2 \sqrt{2})+(3-2 \sqrt{2})=6
$$

Now, we square this result:
$$
\left(x+\frac{1}{x}\right)^{2} = 6^{2} = 36
$$

$$
x^{2} + 2 \cdot x \cdot \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{x^{2}} = 36
$$

$$
x^{2} + 2 + \frac{1}{x^{2}} = 36
$$

$$
x^{2} + \frac{1}{x^{2}} = 36 - 2 = 34
$$

Hence, $(x^{2}+\frac{1}{x^{2}})=34$.

### Example 14

If $x=\frac{5-\sqrt{21}}{2}$, prove that $(x^{3}+\frac{1}{x^{3}})-5(x^{2}+\frac{1}{x^{2}})+(x+\frac{1}{x})=0$. *[2011]*

#### Solution

Given $x=\frac{5-\sqrt{21}}{2}$.

First, find $\frac{1}{x}$:
$$
\frac{1}{x} = \frac{2}{5-\sqrt{21}} = \frac{2(5+\sqrt{21})}{(5-\sqrt{21})(5+\sqrt{21})} = \frac{2(5+\sqrt{21})}{25-21} = \frac{2(5+\sqrt{21})}{4} = \frac{5+\sqrt{21}}{2}
$$

Now, find the values of the required components:
$$
x+\frac{1}{x} = \left(\frac{5-\sqrt{21}}{2}\right)+\left(\frac{5+\sqrt{21}}{2}\right) = \frac{5-\sqrt{21}+5+\sqrt{21}}{2} = \frac{10}{2} = 5
$$

$$
x^{2}+\frac{1}{x^{2}} = \left(x+\frac{1}{x}\right)^{2} - 2 = (5)^{2} - 2 = 25 - 2 = 23
$$

$$
x^{3}+\frac{1}{x^{3}} = \left(x+\frac{1}{x}\right)^{3} - 3\left(x+\frac{1}{x}\right) = (5)^{3} - 3(5) = 125 - 15 = 110
$$

Substitute these values into the expression to be proven:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& (x^{3}+\frac{1}{x^{3}}) - 5(x^{2}+\frac{1}{x^{2}}) + (x+\frac{1}{x}) \\
&= 110 - 5(23) + 5 \\
&= 110 - 115 + 5 \\
&= 115 - 115 = 0
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 15

If $x=\frac{\sqrt{2}+1}{\sqrt{2}-1}$ and $y=\frac{\sqrt{2}-1}{\sqrt{2}+1}$ find the value of $x^{2}+y^{2}+x y$. *[2015]*

#### Solution

First, simplify $x$ and $y$ by rationalising their denominators:
$$
x = \frac{\sqrt{2}+1}{\sqrt{2}-1} \times \frac{\sqrt{2}+1}{\sqrt{2}+1} = \frac{(\sqrt{2}+1)^2}{2-1} = 2+1+2\sqrt{2} = 3+2\sqrt{2}
$$

$$
y = \frac{\sqrt{2}-1}{\sqrt{2}+1} \times \frac{\sqrt{2}-1}{\sqrt{2}-1} = \frac{(\sqrt{2}-1)^2}{2-1} = 2+1-2\sqrt{2} = 3-2\sqrt{2}
$$

The expression $x^2+y^2+xy$ can be rewritten as $(x+y)^2 - xy$.

Calculate $x+y$ and $xy$:
$$
x+y = (3+2\sqrt{2}) + (3-2\sqrt{2}) = 6
$$

$$
xy = (3+2\sqrt{2})(3-2\sqrt{2}) = 3^2 - (2\sqrt{2})^2 = 9 - 8 = 1
$$

Now substitute these into the rewritten expression:
$$
x^2+y^2+xy = (x+y)^2 - xy = (6)^2 - 1 = 36-1 = 35
$$

### Example 16

Express $\frac{3}{\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2}+\sqrt{5}}$ with a rational denominator.

#### Solution

Group the terms in the denominator as $(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2})+\sqrt{5}$ and multiply by the conjugate:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{3}{(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2})+\sqrt{5}} &= \frac{3}{(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2})+\sqrt{5}} \times \frac{(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2})-\sqrt{5}}{(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2})-\sqrt{5}} \\
&= \frac{3(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2}-\sqrt{5})}{(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2})^{2}-(\sqrt{5})^{2}} \\
&= \frac{3(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2}-\sqrt{5})}{(3+2-2\sqrt{6})-5} = \frac{3(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2}-\sqrt{5})}{-2\sqrt{6}}
\end{aligned}
$$

Now, rationalise the remaining irrational denominator:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{3(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{2}-\sqrt{5})}{-2\sqrt{6}} \times \frac{\sqrt{6}}{\sqrt{6}} &= \frac{3(\sqrt{18}-\sqrt{12}-\sqrt{30})}{-2 \times 6} \\
&= \frac{3(3\sqrt{2}-2\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{30})}{-12} \\
&= \frac{3\sqrt{2}-2\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{30}}{-4} \\
&= \frac{-(3\sqrt{2}-2\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{30})}{4} = \frac{2\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{30}-3\sqrt{2}}{4}
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 17

If $x=\frac{1}{3-\sqrt{8}}$, find the value of $x^{3}-2 x^{2}-7 x+5$.

#### Solution

First, simplify $x$:
$$
x = \frac{1}{3-\sqrt{8}} \times \frac{3+\sqrt{8}}{3+\sqrt{8}} = \frac{3+\sqrt{8}}{9-8} = 3+\sqrt{8}
$$

Now, rearrange the equation for $x$ to find a polynomial relationship:
$$
x = 3+\sqrt{8} \Rightarrow x-3 = \sqrt{8}
$$

Squaring both sides:
$$
(x-3)^2 = (\sqrt{8})^2
$$

$$
x^2 - 6x + 9 = 8
$$

$$
x^2 - 6x + 1 = 0
$$

We can use this relationship to simplify the target expression by polynomial long division or substitution. Let's use substitution:
The expression is $x^3 - 2x^2 - 7x + 5$.
We can factor it as $x(x^2 - 6x + 1) + 4x^2 - 8x + 5$.
Since $x^2 - 6x + 1 = 0$, the expression simplifies to $4x^2 - 8x + 5$.
From $x^2 - 6x + 1 = 0$, we have $x^2 = 6x-1$.
Substituting this in:
$$
\begin{aligned}
4(6x-1) - 8x + 5 &= 24x - 4 - 8x + 5 \\
&= 16x + 1
\end{aligned}
$$
Now substitute the value of $x = 3+\sqrt{8}$:
$$
16(3+\sqrt{8}) + 1 = 48 + 16\sqrt{8} + 1 = 49 + 16(2\sqrt{2}) = 49+32\sqrt{2}
$$
