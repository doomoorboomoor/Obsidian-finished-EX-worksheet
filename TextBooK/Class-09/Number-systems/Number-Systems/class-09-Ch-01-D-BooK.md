## Real Numbers

A number whose square is non-negative, is called a **real number**.
In fact, all rational and all irrational numbers form the collection of all real numbers.

Every real number is either rational or irrational. Consider a real number.
- If it is an integer or it has a terminating or repeating decimal representation then it is **rational**.
- If it has a non-terminating and non-repeating decimal representation then it is **irrational**.

The totality of rationals and irrationals forms the collection of all real numbers.

**COMPLETENESS PROPERTY:** On the number line, each point corresponds to a unique real number. And, every real number can be represented by a unique point on the real line.

**DENSITY PROPERTY:** Between any two real numbers, there exist infinitely many real numbers.

---

## Addition Properties of Real Numbers

- **CLOSURE PROPERTY:** The sum of two real numbers is always a real number.
- **ASSOCIATIVE LAW:** $(a+b)+c=a+(b+c)$ for all real numbers $a, b, c$.
- **COMMUTATIVE LAW:** $a+b=b+a$ for all real numbers $a$ and $b$.
- **EXISTENCE OF ADDITIVE IDENTITY:** Clearly, $0$ is a real number such that $0+a=a+0=a$ for every real number $a$. $0$ is called the **additive identity** for real numbers.
- **EXISTENCE OF ADDITIVE INVERSE:** For each real number $a$, there exists a real number $(-a)$ such that $a+(-a)=(-a)+a=0$. $a$ and $(-a)$ are called the **additive inverse** (or negative) of each other.

---

## Multiplication Properties of Real Numbers

- **CLOSURE PROPERTY:** The product of two real numbers is always a real number.
- **ASSOCIATIVE LAW:** $(ab)c=a(bc)$ for all real numbers $a, b, c$.
- **COMMUTATIVE LAW:** $ab=ba$ for all real numbers $a$ and $b$.
- **EXISTENCE OF MULTIPLICATIVE IDENTITY:** Clearly, $1$ is a real number such that $1 \cdot a=a \cdot 1=a$ for every real number $a$. $1$ is called the **multiplicative identity** for real numbers.
- **EXISTENCE OF MULTIPLICATIVE INVERSE:** For each nonzero real number $a$, there exists a real number $(\frac{1}{a})$ such that $a \cdot \frac{1}{a}=\frac{1}{a} \cdot a=1$. $a$ and $\frac{1}{a}$ are called the **multiplicative inverse** (or reciprocal) of each other.
- **DISTRIBUTIVE LAWS OF MULTIPLICATION OVER ADDITION:** We have $a(b+c)=ab+ac$ and $(a+b)c=ac+bc$ for all real numbers $a, b, c$.

---

## Some More Results on Real Numbers

For all positive real numbers $a$ and $b$, we have:
- $\sqrt{ab}=\sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b}$
- $\sqrt{\frac{a}{b}}=\frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}$

---

## Solved Examples

### Example: 1

Add $(3\sqrt{2}+7\sqrt{3})$ and $(\sqrt{2}-5\sqrt{3})$.

#### Solution:

We have
$$
\begin{aligned}
(3\sqrt{2}+7\sqrt{3})+(\sqrt{2}-5\sqrt{3}) & =(3\sqrt{2}+\sqrt{2})+(7\sqrt{3}-5\sqrt{3}) \\
& =(3+1)\sqrt{2}+(7-5)\sqrt{3} \\
& =(4\sqrt{2}+2\sqrt{3})
\end{aligned}
$$

---

### Example: 2

Multiply $5\sqrt{11}$ by $3\sqrt{11}$.

#### Solution:

We have
$$
5\sqrt{11} \times 3\sqrt{11}=5 \times 3 \times \sqrt{11} \times \sqrt{11}=(5 \times 3 \times 11)=165
$$

---

### Example: 3

Divide $15\sqrt{15}$ by $3\sqrt{5}$.

#### Solution:

We have
$$
15\sqrt{15} \div 3\sqrt{5}=\frac{15\sqrt{15}}{3\sqrt{5}}=\frac{5 \times \sqrt{5} \times \sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{5}}=5\sqrt{3}
$$

---

### Example: 4

Simplify
- (i) $(3+\sqrt{3})(2+\sqrt{2})$
- (ii) $(6+\sqrt{6})(6-\sqrt{6})$
- (iii) $(\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{2})^{2}$
- (iv) $(\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2})(\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{2})$

#### Solution:

We have
(i)
$$
\begin{aligned}
(3+\sqrt{3})(2+\sqrt{2}) &= 3 \times 2+3 \times \sqrt{2}+\sqrt{3} \times 2+\sqrt{3} \times \sqrt{2} \\
&=6+3\sqrt{2}+2\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{6}
\end{aligned}
$$
(ii)
$$
\begin{aligned}
(6+\sqrt{6})(6-\sqrt{6}) &= (6)^{2}-(\sqrt{6})^{2} \quad [\because(a+b)(a-b)=a^{2}-b^{2}] \\
&=(36-6)=30
\end{aligned}
$$
(iii)
$$
\begin{aligned}
(\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{2})^{2} &= (\sqrt{3})^{2}+(\sqrt{2})^{2}+2 \times \sqrt{3} \times \sqrt{2} \quad [\because(a+b)^{2}=a^{2}+b^{2}+2ab] \\
&=3+2+2\sqrt{6}=5+2\sqrt{6}
\end{aligned}
$$
(iv)
$$
\begin{aligned}
(\sqrt{5}-\sqrt{2})(\sqrt{5}+\sqrt{2}) &= (\sqrt{5})^{2}-(\sqrt{2})^{2} \quad [\because(a-b)(a+b)=a^{2}-b^{2}] \\
&=5-2=3
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example: 5

Simplify $\frac{\sqrt{72}}{5\sqrt{72}+3\sqrt{288}-2\sqrt{648}}$.

#### Solution:

We have
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{\sqrt{72}}{5\sqrt{72}+3\sqrt{288}-2\sqrt{648}} & =\frac{\sqrt{72}}{5\sqrt{72}+3\sqrt{4 \times 72}-2\sqrt{9 \times 72}} \\
& =\frac{\sqrt{72}}{5\sqrt{72}+3 \cdot \sqrt{4} \cdot \sqrt{72}-2 \cdot \sqrt{9} \cdot \sqrt{72}} \\
& =\frac{\sqrt{72}}{5\sqrt{72}+3 \times 2 \times \sqrt{72}-2 \times 3 \times \sqrt{72}} \\
& =\frac{\sqrt{72}}{5\sqrt{72}+6\sqrt{72}-6\sqrt{72}} \\
& =\frac{\sqrt{72}}{(5+6-6)\sqrt{72}}=\frac{1}{5}
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example: 6

Simplify $\frac{\sqrt{a^{2}-b^{2}}+a}{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}+b} \div \frac{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}-b}{a-\sqrt{a^{2}-b^{2}}}$.

#### Solution:

We have
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{\sqrt{a^{2}-b^{2}}+a}{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}+b} \div \frac{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}-b}{a-\sqrt{a^{2}-b^{2}}} & =\frac{a+\sqrt{a^{2}-b^{2}}}{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}+b} \times \frac{a-\sqrt{a^{2}-b^{2}}}{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}-b} \\
& =\frac{(a)^{2}-(\sqrt{a^{2}-b^{2}})^{2}}{(\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}})^{2}-(b)^{2}} \\
& =\frac{a^{2}-(a^{2}-b^{2})}{(a^{2}+b^{2})-b^{2}}=\frac{a^{2}-a^{2}+b^{2}}{a^{2}+b^{2}-b^{2}}=\frac{b^{2}}{a^{2}}
\end{aligned}
$$
