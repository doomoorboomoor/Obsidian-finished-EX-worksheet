## MEDIAN OF UNGROUPED DATA

median After arranging the given data in an ascending or a descending order of magnitude, the value of the middle-most observation(s) is called the median of the data.

## MEDIAN OF AN UNGROUPED DATA

method Arrange the data in an increasing or a decreasing order of magnitude. Let the total number of observations be $n$.
(i) If $n$ is odd, then median $=$ value of $\left(\frac{n+1}{2}\right)$ th observation.
(ii) If $n$ is even, then median $=$ mean of $\left(\frac{n}{2}\right)$ th and $\left(\frac{n}{2}+1\right)$ th observations.

## SOLVED EXAMPLES

EXAMPLE 1 The runs scored by 11 members of a cricket team are

$$
15,29,43,13,31,50,20,0,27,56,34 .
$$

Find the median score.
SOLUTION Arranging the runs in an ascending order, we have

$$
0,13,15,20,27,29,31,34,43,50,56 .
$$

Here, $n=11$, which is odd.
$\therefore \quad$ median score $=$ value of $\left(\frac{11+1}{2}\right)$ th term

$$
=\text { value of } 6 \text { th term }=29 .
$$

Hence, the median score is 29 .
EXAMPLE 2 The weights of 10 students (in kilograms) are

$$
36,45,31,52,35,40,55,60,38,44 .
$$

Find the median weight.
SOLUTION Arranging the weights in an ascending order, we have

$$
31,35,36,38,40,44,45,52,55,60 .
$$

Here, $n=10$, which is even.
$\therefore \quad$ median weight $=$ average of $\left(\frac{10}{2}\right)$ th and $\left(\frac{10}{2}+1\right)$ th terms
$=$ average of 5th and 6th terms

$$
=\frac{1}{2}(40+44) \mathrm{kg}=\frac{84}{2} \mathrm{~kg}=42 \mathrm{~kg} .
$$

Hence, the median weight is 42 kg .
EXAMPLE 3 Ten observations $6,14,15,17, x+1,2 x-13,30,32,34,43$ are written in an ascending order. The median of the data is 24 . Find the value of $x$.
solution Here $n=10$, which is even.
$\therefore \quad$ median $=$ mean of $\left(\frac{10}{2}\right)$ th and $\left(\frac{10}{2}+1\right)$ th terms

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\text { mean of } 5 \text { th and } 6 \text { th terms } \\
& =\frac{1}{2}[(x+1)+(2 x-13)]=\frac{1}{2}(3 x-12) .
\end{aligned}
$$

But, median $=24$ (given).
$\therefore \quad \frac{1}{2}(3 x-12)=24 \Rightarrow 3 x-12=48$
$\Rightarrow 3 x=60 \Rightarrow x=20$.
Hence, $x=20$.
EXAMPLE 4 Find the median of the data $61,92,41,57,43,71,58,99,108$. If 58 is replaced by 85, what will be the new median?
solution Arranging the given data in an ascending order, we get

$$
\text { 41, 43, 57, 58, 61, 71, 92, 99, } 108 .
$$

The given number of observations, $n=9$ (odd).

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad \text { median } & =\text { value of }\left(\frac{n+1}{2}\right) \text { th observation } \\
& =\text { value of }\left(\frac{9+1}{2}\right) \text { th observation } \\
& =\text { value of } 5 \text { th observation }=61
\end{aligned}
$$

On replacing 58 by 85 and arranging the new observations in an ascending order, we get

$$
\begin{array}{ll} 
& 41,43,57,61,71,85,92,99,108 . \\
\therefore \quad & \text { new median }=\text { value of } 5 \text { th observation }=71 .
\end{array}
$$
