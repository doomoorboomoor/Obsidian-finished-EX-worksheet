## Properties of AP

**THEOREM 1** If to each term of an AP a fixed nonzero number is added then the resulting progression is an AP.

**PROOF** Let $a, a+d, a+2 d, a+3 d, \ldots$ be a given AP and let $k$ be a fixed nonzero number. Then,
$(a+k),\{(a+d)+k\},\{(a+2 d)+k\}, \ldots$ is an AP
in which first term $=(a+k)$ and common difference $=d$.

**THEOREM 2** If each term of a given AP is multiplied or divided by a given nonzero fixed number $k$ then the resulting progression is an $A P$.

**PROOF**
(i) Let $a, a+d, a+2 d, \ldots$ be the given AP and let $k$ be a given nonzero number. Then,
$a k,(a+d) k,(a+2 d) k, \ldots$ is clearly an AP
in which the first term $=a k$ and the common difference $=d k$.

(ii) Let $a, a+d, a+2 d, \ldots$ be the given AP and let $k$ be a given nonzero number. Then,
$\frac{a}{k}, \frac{(a+d)}{k}, \frac{(a+2 d)}{k}, \ldots$ is clearly an AP
in which the first term $=\frac{a}{k}$ and the common difference $=\frac{d}{k}$.

---

## Summary

If $a_{1}, a_{2}, a_{3}, a_{4}, \ldots$ are in AP and $k$ is a nonzero number then

(i) $\left(a_{1}+k\right),\left(a_{2}+k\right),\left(a_{3}+k\right),\left(a_{4}+k\right), \ldots$ are in AP .

(ii) $\left(a_{1}-k\right),\left(a_{2}-k\right),\left(a_{3}-k\right),\left(a_{4}-k\right), \ldots$ are in AP .

(iii) $a_{1} k, a_{2} k, a_{3} k, a_{4} k, \ldots$ are in AP .

(iv) $\frac{a_{1}}{k}, \frac{a_{2}}{k}, \frac{a_{3}}{k}, \frac{a_{4}}{k}, \ldots$ are in AP.

## Solved Examples

### Example 1:

If $a, b, c$, are in $A P$, show that

(i) $(b+c),(c+a)$ and $(a+b)$ are in $A P$.

(ii) $a^{2}(b+c), b^{2}(c+a)$ and $c^{2}(a+b)$ are in $A P$.

#### Solution:

Since $a, b, c$ are in AP, we have

$$
\begin{equation*}
2 b=(a+c) . \tag{i}
\end{equation*}
$$

(i) $(b+c),(c+a),(a+b)$ will be in AP

$$
\text { if }(c+a)-(b+c)=(a+b)-(c+a)
$$

i.e., if $a-b=b-c$
i.e., if $2 b=a+c$, which is true by (i).
$\therefore \quad a, b, c$ are in $\mathrm{AP} \Rightarrow(b+c),(c+a),(a+b)$ are in AP .

(ii) $a^{2}(b+c), b^{2}(c+a), c^{2}(a+b)$ will be in AP

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \qquad \text { if } b^{2}(c+a)-a^{2}(b+c)=c^{2}(a+b)-b^{2}(c+a) \\
& \text { i.e., if } c\left(b^{2}-a^{2}\right)+a b(b-a)=a\left(c^{2}-b^{2}\right)+b c(c-b) \\
& \text { i.e., if }(b-a)(a b+b c+c a)=(c-b)(a b+b c+c a) \\
& \text { i.e., if }(b-a)=(c-b) \\
& \text { i.e., if } 2 b=(a+c) \text {, which is true by (i). } \\
& \therefore \quad a, b, c \text { are in } \mathrm{AP} \Rightarrow a^{2}(b+c), b^{2}(c+a), c^{2}(a+b) \text { are in } \mathrm{AP} \text { . }
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 2:

If $a, b, c$ are in AP, show that

$$
\frac{1}{(\sqrt{b}+\sqrt{c})}, \frac{1}{(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})}, \frac{1}{(\sqrt{a}+\sqrt{b})} \text { are in } A P \text .
$$

#### Solution:

Since $a, b, c$ are in AP, we have

$$
\begin{equation*}
2 b=(a+c) \tag{i}
\end{equation*}
$$

Now, $\frac{1}{(\sqrt{b}+\sqrt{c})}, \frac{1}{(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})}, \frac{1}{(\sqrt{a}+\sqrt{b})}$ will be in AP

$$
\text { if } \frac{1}{(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})}-\frac{1}{(\sqrt{b}+\sqrt{c})}=\frac{1}{(\sqrt{a}+\sqrt{b})}-\frac{1}{(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})}
$$

i.e., if $\frac{(\sqrt{b}+\sqrt{c})-(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})}{(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})(\sqrt{b}+\sqrt{c})}=\frac{(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})-(\sqrt{a}+\sqrt{b})}{(\sqrt{a}+\sqrt{b})(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})}$

i.e., if $\frac{(\sqrt{b}-\sqrt{a})}{(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})(\sqrt{b}+\sqrt{c})}=\frac{(\sqrt{c}-\sqrt{b})}{(\sqrt{a}+\sqrt{b})(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})}$

i.e., $\quad$ if $\frac{(\sqrt{b}-\sqrt{a})}{(\sqrt{b}+\sqrt{c})}=\frac{(\sqrt{c}-\sqrt{b})}{(\sqrt{a}+\sqrt{b})}$

i.e., $\quad$ if $b-a=c-b$

i.e., if $2 b=a+c$, which is true by (i).

$\therefore \quad a, b, c$ are in $\mathrm{AP} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{(\sqrt{b}+\sqrt{c})}, \frac{1}{(\sqrt{c}+\sqrt{a})}, \frac{1}{(\sqrt{a}+\sqrt{b})}$ are in
AP.

### Example 3:

If $a, b, c$ are in $A P$, show that

$$
\left[(b+c)^{2}-a^{2}\right],\left[(c+a)^{2}-b^{2}\right],\left[(a+b)^{2}-c^{2}\right] \text { are in } A P .
$$

#### Solution:

Since $a, b, c$ are in AP, we have

$$
\begin{equation*}
2 b=(a+c) \tag{i}
\end{equation*}
$$

Now, $\left[(b+c)^{2}-a^{2}\right],\left[(c+a)^{2}-b^{2}\right],\left[(a+b)^{2}-c^{2}\right]$ will be in AP

$$
\text { if }\left[(c+a)^{2}-b^{2}\right]-\left[(b+c)^{2}-a^{2}\right]=\left[(a+b)^{2}-c^{2}\right]-\left[(c+a)^{2}-b^{2}\right]
$$

i.e., if $(a+b+c)(c+a-b)-(a+b+c)(b+c-a)$

$$
=(a+b+c)(a+b-c)-(a+b+c)(c+a-b)
$$

i.e., if $(a+b+c)[(c+a-b)-(b+c-a)]$

$$
=(a+b+c)[(a+b-c)-(c+a-b)]
$$

i.e., if $2(a-b)=2(b-c)$

i.e., if $(a-b)=(b-c)$

i.e., if $2 b=(a+c)$, which is true by (i).

$\therefore \quad a, b, c$ are in AP

$\Rightarrow \quad\left[(b+c)^{2}-a^{2}\right],\left[(c+a)^{2}-b^{2}\right],\left[(a+b)^{2}-c^{2}\right]$ are in AP.

### Example 4:

If $a, b, c$ are in $A P$, show that

(i) $\frac{1}{b c}, \frac{1}{c a}, \frac{1}{a b}$ are in $A P$.

(ii) $a\left(\frac{1}{b}+\frac{1}{c}\right), b\left(\frac{1}{c}+\frac{1}{a}\right), c\left(\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}\right)$ are in $A P$.

#### Solution:

(i) Since $a, b, c$ are in AP , the terms obtained by dividing each term of this AP by $a b c$ are also in AP .
Consequently, $\frac{1}{b c}, \frac{1}{c a}, \frac{1}{a b}$ are in AP .

(ii)
> $a, b, c$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{b c}, \frac{1}{c a}, \frac{1}{a b}$ are in AP [dividing each term by $a b c$ ]
> $\Rightarrow\left(\frac{a b+b c+c a}{b c}\right),\left(\frac{(a b+b c+c a}{c a}\right),\left(\frac{a b+b c+c a}{a b}\right)$ are in AP
> [multiplying each term by $(a b+b c+c a)$ ]
> $\Rightarrow\left(\frac{a b+b c+c a}{b c}-1\right),\left(\frac{a b+b c+c a}{c a}-1\right),\left(\frac{a b+b c+c a}{a b}-1\right)$ are in AP [adding -1 to each term]
> $\Rightarrow \quad \frac{(a c+a b)}{b c}, \frac{(a b+b c)}{c a}, \frac{(b c+c a)}{a b}$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad a\left(\frac{c+b}{b c}\right), b\left(\frac{a+c}{c a}\right), c\left(\frac{b+a}{a b}\right)$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad a\left(\frac{1}{b}+\frac{1}{c}\right), b\left(\frac{1}{c}+\frac{1}{a}\right), c\left(\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}\right)$ are in AP.

### Example 5:

If $a^{2}, b^{2}, c^{2}$ are in $A P$, prove that

$$
\frac{1}{(b+c)}, \frac{1}{(c+a)}, \frac{1}{(a+b)} \text { are in } A P .
$$

#### Solution:

$\frac{1}{(b+c)}, \frac{1}{(c+a)}, \frac{1}{(a+b)}$ will be in AP

$$
\text { if } \frac{1}{(c+a)}-\frac{1}{(b+c)}=\frac{1}{(a+b)}-\frac{1}{(c+a)}
$$

i.e., if $\frac{(b+c)-(c+a)}{(c+a)(b+c)}=\frac{(c+a)-(a+b)}{(a+b)(c+a)}$

i.e., if $\frac{(b-a)}{(c+a)(b+c)}=\frac{(c-b)}{(a+b)(c+a)}$

i.e., $\quad$ if $\frac{(b-a)}{(b+c)}=\frac{(c-b)}{(a+b)}$

i.e., if $(b-a)(b+a)=(c+b)(c-b)$

i.e., $\quad$ if $b^{2}-a^{2}=c^{2}-b^{2}$

i.e., if $a^{2}, b^{2}, c^{2}$ are in AP .

Hence, $a^{2}, b^{2}, c^{2}$ are in $\mathrm{AP} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{(b+c)}, \frac{1}{(c+a)}, \frac{1}{(a+b)}$ are in AP .

### Example 6:

If $a^{2}, b^{2}, c^{2}$ are in $A P$, prove that

$$
\frac{a}{b+c}, \frac{b}{c+a}, \frac{c}{a+b} \text { are in } A P
$$

#### Solution:

$\frac{a}{b+c}, \frac{b}{c+a}, \frac{c}{a+b}$ will be in AP

> if $\frac{a}{b+c}+1, \frac{b}{c+a}+1, \frac{c}{a+b}+1 \text { are in } \mathrm{AP}$
> [on adding 1 to each term]
> i.e., if $\frac{a+b+c}{b+c}, \frac{a+b+c}{c+a}, \frac{a+b+c}{a+b}$ are in AP
> i.e., if $\frac{1}{b+c}, \frac{1}{c+a}, \frac{1}{a+b}$ are in AP
> [on dividing each term by $(a+b+c)$ ]
> i.e., $\quad$ if $\frac{1}{c+a}-\frac{1}{b+c}=\frac{1}{a+b}-\frac{1}{c+a}$
> i.e., $\quad$ if $\frac{(b+c)-(c+a)}{(c+a)(b+c)}=\frac{(c+a)-(a+b)}{(a+b)(c+a)}$
> i.e., $\quad$ if $\frac{(b-a)}{(b+c)}=\frac{(c-b)}{(a+b)}$
> i.e., if $(b-a)(b+a)=(c-b)(c+b)$
> i.e., $\quad$ if $b^{2}-a^{2}=c^{2}-b^{2}$
> i.e., if $a^{2}, b^{2}, c^{2}$ are in AP .

Hence, $a^{2}, b^{2}, c^{2}$ are in $\mathrm{AP} \Rightarrow \frac{a}{(b+c)}, \frac{b}{(c+a)}, \frac{c}{(a+b)}$ are in AP .

### Example 7:

Show that $\left(a^{2}+a b+b^{2}\right),\left(c^{2}+a c+a^{2}\right)$ and $\left(b^{2}+b c+c^{2}\right)$ are in $A P$, if $a, b, c$ are in $A P$.

#### Solution:

The terms $\left(a^{2}+a b+b^{2}\right),\left(c^{2}+a c+a^{2}\right),\left(b^{2}+b c+c^{2}\right)$ will be in AP

$$
\text { if }\left(c^{2}+a c+a^{2}\right)-\left(a^{2}+a b+b^{2}\right)=\left(b^{2}+b c+c^{2}\right)-\left(c^{2}+a c+a^{2}\right)
$$

i.e., $\quad$ if $c^{2}+a c-a b-b^{2}=b^{2}+b c-a c-a^{2}$

i.e., $\quad$ if $\left(a^{2}+c^{2}+2 a c\right)-b^{2}=b^{2}+b c+a b$

i.e., $\quad$ if $(a+c)^{2}-b^{2}=b(a+b+c)$

i.e., if $(a+c+b)(a+c-b)=b(a+b+c)$

i.e., if $a+c-b=b$

i.e., if $a+c=2 b$

i.e., if $a, b, c$ are in AP .

Hence, $a, b, c$ are in AP $\Rightarrow\left(a^{2}+a b+b^{2}\right),\left(c^{2}+a c+a^{2}\right)$ and $\left(b^{2}+b c+c^{2}\right)$
are in AP .

### Example 8:

If $(b-c)^{2},(c-a)^{2},(a-b)^{2}$ are in AP, prove that

$$
\frac{1}{(b-c)}, \frac{1}{(c-a)}, \frac{1}{(a-b)} \text { are in } A P
$$

#### Solution:

$(b-c)^{2},(c-a)^{2},(a-b)^{2}$ are in AP

$$
\begin{align*}
& \Rightarrow \quad(c-a)^{2}-(b-c)^{2}=(a-b)^{2}-(c-a)^{2} \\
& \Rightarrow \quad(b-a)(2 c-a-b)=(c-b)(2 a-b-c) . \tag{i}
\end{align*}
$$

Now, $\frac{1}{(b-c)}, \frac{1}{(c-a)}, \frac{1}{(a-b)}$ will be in AP

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \text { if } \frac{1}{(c-a)}-\frac{1}{(b-c)}=\frac{1}{(a-b)}-\frac{1}{(c-a)} \\
& \text { i.e., if } \frac{(b-c)-(c-a)}{(c-a)(b-c)}=\frac{(c-a)-(a-b)}{(a-b)(c-a)} \\
& \text { i.e., if } \frac{(a+b-2 c)}{(c-a)(b-c)}=\frac{(b+c-2 a)}{(a-b)(c-a)} \\
& \text { i.e., if }(a-b)(a+b-2 c)=(b-c)(b+c-2 a) \\
& \text { i.e., if }(b-a)(2 c-a-b)=(c-b)(2 a-b-c) \text {, which is true by (i). } \\
& \text { Hence, }(b-c)^{2},(c-a)^{2},(a-b)^{2} \text { are in AP } \\
& \Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{(b-c)}, \frac{1}{(c-a)}, \frac{1}{(a-b)} \text { are in AP. }
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 9:

If $\left(a^{2}+2 b c\right),\left(b^{2}+2 a c\right),\left(c^{2}+2 a b\right)$ are in $A P$, show that

$$
\frac{1}{(b-c)}, \frac{1}{(c-a)}, \frac{1}{(a-b)} \text { are in } A P
$$

#### Solution:

> $a^{2}+2 b c, b^{2}+2 a c, c^{2}+2 a b$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad \left(a^{2}+2 b c\right)-(a b+b c+a c),\left(b^{2}+2 a c\right)-(a b+b c+a c),\left(c^{2}+2 a b\right) \quad-(a b+b c+a c)$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad \left(a^{2}+b c-a b-a c\right),\left(b^{2}+a c-a b-b c\right),\left(c^{2}+a b-b c-a c\right)$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad (a-b)(a-c),(b-c)(b-a),(c-b)(c-a)$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad -(a-b)(c-a),-(a-b)(b-c),-(b-c)(c-a)$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad \frac{-1}{(b-c)}, \frac{-1}{(c-a)}, \frac{-1}{(a-b)}$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{(b-c)}, \frac{1}{(c-a)}, \frac{1}{(a-b)}$ are in AP

Hence, the result follows.

### Example 10:

If $\frac{(b+c-a)}{a}, \frac{(c+a-b)}{b}, \frac{(a+b-c)}{c}$ are in $A P$, prove that $\frac{1}{a}, \frac{1}{b}, \frac{1}{c}$ are in $A P$.

#### Solution:

> $\frac{(b+c-a)}{a}, \frac{(c+a-b)}{b}, \frac{(a+b-c)}{c}$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad\left\{\frac{(b+c-a)}{a}+2\right\},\left\{\frac{(c+a-b)}{b}+2\right\},\left\{\frac{(a+b-c)}{c}+2\right\}$ are in AP
> [adding 2 to each term]
> $\Rightarrow \quad \frac{(a+b+c)}{a}, \frac{(a+b+c)}{b}, \frac{(a+b+c)}{c}$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{a}, \frac{1}{b}, \frac{1}{c}$ are in AP $\quad[$ on dividing each term by $(a+b+c)]$.

### Example 11:

If $a^{2}(b+c), b^{2}(c+a), c^{2}(a+b)$ are in AP then show that either $a, b, c$ are in $A P$ or $a b+b c+c a=0$.

#### Solution:

> $a^{2}(b+c), b^{2}(c+a), c^{2}(a+b)$ are in AP
> $\Rightarrow \quad b^{2}(c+a)-a^{2}(b+c)=c^{2}(a+b)-b^{2}(c+a)$
> $\Rightarrow \quad \left(b^{2} a-a^{2} b\right)+\left(b^{2} c-a^{2} c\right)=\left(c^{2} b-b^{2} c\right)+\left(c^{2} a-b^{2} a\right)$
> $\Rightarrow \quad a b(b-a)+c\left(b^{2}-a^{2}\right)=b c(c-b)+a\left(c^{2}-b^{2}\right)$
> $\Rightarrow \quad (b-a)(a b+b c+c a)=(c-b)(a b+b c+c a)$
> $\Rightarrow \quad (a b+b c+c a)\{(b-a)-(c-b)\}=0$
> $\Rightarrow \quad (a b+b c+c a)(2 b-a-c)=0$
> $\Rightarrow \quad a b+b c+c a=0$ or $2 b-a-c=0$
> $\Rightarrow \quad a b+b c+c a=0$ or $2 b=a+c$
> $\Rightarrow \quad a b+b c+c a=0$ or $a, b, c$ are in AP.

### Example 12:

If $a, b, c$ are in AP, prove that $(a-c)^{2}=4\left(b^{2}-a c\right)$.

#### Solution:

Since $a, b, c$ are in AP, we have $b=\frac{1}{2}(a+c)$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad \mathrm{RHS} & =4\left(b^{2}-a c\right)=4 \times\left\{\frac{1}{4}(a+c)^{2}-a c\right\} \\
& =(a+c)^{2}-4 a c=(a-c)^{2}=\mathrm{LHS}
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $(a-c)^{2}=4\left(b^{2}-a c\right)$.

### Example 13:

If $a, b, c$ are in AP, prove that $a^{3}+4 b^{3}+c^{3}=3 b\left(a^{2}+c^{2}\right)$.

#### Solution:

Since $a, b, c$ are in AP, we have $b=\frac{1}{2}(a+c)$.

$$
\begin{array}{ll}
\therefore & \text { RHS }=3 \cdot \frac{1}{2}(a+c)\left(a^{2}+c^{2}\right)=\frac{3}{2}\left(a^{3}+c^{3}+a c^{2}+a^{2} c\right) \\
\text { and } & \text { LHS }=a^{3}+4 \cdot\left(\frac{a+c}{2}\right)^{3}+c^{3}=\frac{3}{2}\left(a^{3}+c^{3}+a c^{2}+a^{2} c\right) . \\
\therefore & \text { LHS }=\text { RHS } .
\end{array}
$$
