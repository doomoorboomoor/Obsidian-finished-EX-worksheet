## Square Roots of a Complex Number

To evaluate $\sqrt{a+i b}$:

Let $\sqrt{a+i b}=(x+i y)$.

On squaring both sides, we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
(a+i b) &= (x+i y)^{2} \\
\Rightarrow \quad (a+i b) &= (x^{2}-y^{2})+i(2 x y)
\end{align*}
$$
On comparing the **real** and **imaginary parts** on both sides, we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
(x^{2}-y^{2}) &= a \quad \text{and} \quad 2xy = b \\
\Rightarrow \quad (x^{2}+y^{2}) &= \sqrt{(x^{2}-y^{2})^{2}+4 x^{2} y^{2}}=\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}} \\
\therefore \quad (x^{2}-y^{2}) &= a \quad \ldots \text{(iii)} \quad \text{and} \quad (x^{2}+y^{2})=\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}} \quad \ldots \text{(iv)}
\end{align*}
$$
On solving (iii) and (iv), we get:
$$
x^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left\{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}+a\right\} \quad \text{and} \quad y^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left\{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}-a\right\}
$$
Hence,
$$
\sqrt{a+i b}= \pm\left[\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}\left\{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}+a\right\}}+i \sqrt{\frac{1}{2}\left\{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}-a\right\}}\right]
$$
**Remark:** Similarly, by assuming that $\sqrt{a-i b}=(x-i y)$, we may find $\sqrt{a-i b}$.

## Solved Examples

### Example 1: Evaluate $\sqrt{6+8 i}$.

#### Solution:

Let $\sqrt{6+8 i}=(x+i y)$.

On squaring both sides, we get:
$$
6+8 i=(x+i y)^{2} \Rightarrow 6+8 i=\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)+i(2 x y)
$$
On comparing **real** and **imaginary parts**, we get:
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
& x^{2}-y^{2}=6 \text { and } 2 x y=8 \\
\Rightarrow & x^{2}-y^{2}=6 \text { and } x y=4 \\
\Rightarrow & \left(x^{2}+y^{2}\right)=\sqrt{\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)^{2}+4 x^{2} y^{2}}=\sqrt{6^{2}+4 \times 16}=\sqrt{100}=10 \\
\Rightarrow & x^{2}-y^{2}=6 \text { and } x^{2}+y^{2}=10 \\
\Rightarrow & 2 x^{2}=16 \text { and } 2 y^{2}=4 \\
\Rightarrow & x^{2}=8 \text { and } y^{2}=2 \\
\Rightarrow & x= \pm 2 \sqrt{2} \text { and } y= \pm \sqrt{2}
\end{array}
$$
Since $xy > 0$, $x$ and $y$ are of the same sign.

$\therefore \quad (x=2 \sqrt{2}$ and $y=\sqrt{2})$ or $(x=-2 \sqrt{2}$ and $y=-\sqrt{2})$.

Hence, $\sqrt{6+8 i}=(2 \sqrt{2}+\sqrt{2} i)$ or $(-2 \sqrt{2}-\sqrt{2} i)$.

### Example 2: Evaluate $\sqrt{-5+12 i}$.

#### Solution:

Let $\sqrt{-5+12 i}=(x+i y)$.

On squaring both sides, we get:
$$
-5+12 i=(x+i y)^{2} \Rightarrow -5+12 i=\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)+i(2 x y)
$$
On comparing **real** and **imaginary parts**, we get:
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
& x^{2}-y^{2}=-5 \text { and } 2 x y=12 \\
\Rightarrow & x^{2}-y^{2}=-5 \text { and } x y=6 \\
\Rightarrow & \left(x^{2}+y^{2}\right)=\sqrt{\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)^{2}+4 x^{2} y^{2}}=\sqrt{(-5)^{2}+4 \times 36}=\sqrt{169}=13 \\
\Rightarrow & x^{2}-y^{2}=-5 \text { and } x^{2}+y^{2}=13 \\
\Rightarrow & 2 x^{2}=8 \text { and } 2 y^{2}=18 \\
\Rightarrow & x^{2}=4 \text { and } y^{2}=9 \\
\Rightarrow & x= \pm 2 \text { and } y= \pm 3
\end{array}
$$
Since $xy > 0$, $x$ and $y$ are of the same sign.

$\therefore \quad (x=2$ and $y=3)$ or $(x=-2$ and $y=-3)$.

Hence, $\sqrt{-5+12 i}=(2+3 i)$ or $(-2-3 i)$.

### Example 3: Evaluate $\sqrt{8-15 i}$.

#### Solution:

Let $\sqrt{8-15 i}=(x-i y)$.

On squaring both sides, we get:
$$
(8-15 i)=(x-i y)^{2} \Rightarrow (8-15 i)=\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)-i(2 x y)
$$
On comparing **real** and **imaginary parts**, we get:
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
& x^{2}-y^{2}=8 \text { and } 2 x y=15 \\
\Rightarrow & x^{2}-y^{2}=8 \text { and } x y=\frac{15}{2} \\
\Rightarrow & \left(x^{2}+y^{2}\right)=\sqrt{\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)^{2}+4 x^{2} y^{2}}=\sqrt{64+225}=\sqrt{289}=17 \\
\Rightarrow & x^{2}-y^{2}=8 \text { and } x^{2}+y^{2}=17 \\
\Rightarrow & 2 x^{2}=25 \text { and } 2 y^{2}=9 \\
\Rightarrow & x^{2}=\frac{25}{2} \text { and } y^{2}=\frac{9}{2} \\
\Rightarrow & x= \pm \frac{5}{\sqrt{2}} \text { and } y= \pm \frac{3}{\sqrt{2}}
\end{array}
$$
Since $xy > 0$, $x$ and $y$ are of the same sign.

Hence, $\sqrt{8-15 i}=\left(\frac{5}{\sqrt{2}}-\frac{3}{2} i\right)$ or $\left(\frac{-5}{\sqrt{2}}+\frac{3}{\sqrt{2}} i\right)$.

### Example 4: Evaluate $\sqrt{-24-10 i}$.

#### Solution:

Let $\sqrt{-24-10 i}=(x-i y)$.

On squaring both sides, we get:
$$
(-24-10 i)=\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)-i(2 x y)
$$
On comparing **real** and **imaginary parts**, we get:
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
& x^{2}-y^{2}=-24 \text { and } 2 x y=10 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & x^{2}-y^{2}=-24 \text { and } x y=5 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & \left(x^{2}+y^{2}\right)=\sqrt{\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)^{2}+4 x^{2} y^{2}}=\sqrt{(-24)^{2}+4 \times 25}=\sqrt{676}=26 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & x^{2}-y^{2}=-24 \text { and } x^{2}+y^{2}=26 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & 2 x^{2}=2 \text { and } 2 y^{2}=50 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & x^{2}=1 \text { and } y^{2}=25 \Rightarrow x= \pm 1 \text { and } y= \pm 5
\end{array}
$$
Since $xy > 0$, $x$ and $y$ are of the same sign.

$\therefore \quad \sqrt{-24-10 i}=(1-5 i)$ or $(-1+5 i)$.

### Example 5: Evaluate $\sqrt{-i}$.

#### Solution:

Let $\sqrt{-i}=(x-i y)$.

On squaring both sides, we get:
$$
(-i)=(x-i y)^{2} \Rightarrow -i=\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)-i(2 x y)
$$
On comparing **real** and **imaginary parts**, we get:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right) & =0 \text { and } 2 x y=1 \\
\therefore \quad\left(x^{2}+y^{2}\right) & =\sqrt{\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)^{2}+4 x^{2} y^{2}}=\sqrt{0^{2}+1^{2}}=\sqrt{0+1}=\sqrt{1}=1
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus, $(x^{2}-y^{2})=0$ ... (iii) and $(x^{2}+y^{2})=1$ ... (iv).

On solving (iii) and (iv), we get $x^{2}=\frac{1}{2}$ and $y^{2}=\frac{1}{2}$.

$\therefore \quad x= \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$ and $y= \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$.

Since $xy > 0$, so $x$ and $y$ are of the same sign.

$\therefore \quad (x=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}, y=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}})$ or $(x=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}}, y=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}})$.

Hence, $\sqrt{-i}=\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}-i \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)$ or $\left(\frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}}+i \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)$.

### Example 6: Evaluate $\sqrt{4+3 \sqrt{-20}}+\sqrt{4-3 \sqrt{-20}}$.

#### Solution:

First, simplify the expressions:
$\sqrt{4+3 \sqrt{-20}}=\sqrt{4+6 \sqrt{5}} i$ and $\sqrt{4-3 \sqrt{-20}}=\sqrt{4-6 \sqrt{5}} i$.

Let $\sqrt{4+6 \sqrt{5} i}=(x+i y)$.

On squaring both sides, we get:
$$
(4+6 \sqrt{5} i)=(x+i y)^{2} \Rightarrow (4+6 \sqrt{5} i)=\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)+i(2 x y)
$$
On comparing **real** and **imaginary parts**, we get:
$$
\begin{align*}
\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right) &= 4 \text { and } 2 x y=6 \sqrt{5} \\
\therefore \quad\left(x^{2}+y^{2}\right) &= \sqrt{\left(x^{2}-y^{2}\right)^{2}+4 x^{2} y^{2}}=\sqrt{(4)^{2}+(6 \sqrt{5})^{2}} \\
&= \sqrt{16+180}=\sqrt{196}=14
\end{align*}
$$
Thus, $(x^{2}-y^{2})=4$ ... (iii) and $(x^{2}+y^{2})=14$ ... (iv).

On solving (iii) and (iv), we get $x^{2}=9$ and $y^{2}=5$.

$\therefore \quad x= \pm 3$ and $y= \pm \sqrt{5}$.

Since $xy > 0$, $x$ and $y$ are of the same sign.

$\therefore \quad(x=3, y=\sqrt{5})$ or $(x=-3, y=-\sqrt{5})$.

$\therefore \quad \sqrt{4+3 \sqrt{-20}}=\sqrt{4+6 \sqrt{5} i}=(3+\sqrt{5} i)$ or $(-3-\sqrt{5} i)$.

Similarly, $\sqrt{4-3 \sqrt{-20}}=\sqrt{4-6 \sqrt{5} i}=(3-\sqrt{5} i)$ or $(-3+\sqrt{5} i)$.

$$
\therefore \quad \sqrt{4+3 \sqrt{-20}}+\sqrt{4-3 \sqrt{-20}}=\left\{\begin{array}{c}
\{(3+\sqrt{5} i)+(3-\sqrt{5} i)\}=6 \\
\text { or } \\
\{-3-\sqrt{5} i\}+\{-3+\sqrt{5} i\}=-6
\end{array}\right.
$$
Hence, the result is **6** or **-6**.
