## Trigonometric Functions of Sum and Difference of Numbers

### Theorem 1
(i) **$\cos (\theta+\phi)=\cos \theta \cos \phi-\sin \theta \sin \phi$**
(ii) **$\cos (\theta-\phi)=\cos \theta \cos \phi+\sin \theta \sin \phi$**

#### Proof:
Let $X'OX$ and $YOY'$ be the coordinate axes. With $O$ as the centre and taking a unit radius, draw a circle, cutting the axes at $A$, $A'$, $B$ and $B'$ as shown in the figure given below.

Let $\angle AOP=\theta$ and $\angle POQ=\phi$. Then, $\angle AOQ=(\theta+\phi)$.
Let $\angle AOR=-\phi$. Join $PR$ and $AQ$.

![](https://cdn.mathpix.com/cropped/2025_09_25_f51ce93f12442103e178g-10.jpg?height=399&width=631&top_left_y=1041&top_left_x=332)

(i) The points on the circle are given by
$P(\cos \theta, \sin \theta)$, $Q[\cos (\theta+\phi), \sin (\theta+\phi)]$, $R[\cos (-\phi), \sin (-\phi)]$,
i.e., $R(\cos \phi,-\sin \phi)$, $A(1,0)$, $B(0,1)$, $A'(-1,0)$ and $B'(0,1)$.

In $\triangle POR$ and $\triangle QOA$, we have
$PO=QO=1$ unit, $OR=OA=1$ unit and
$\angle POR=\angle QOA$ [each equal to $(\phi+\angle QOR)$].
$\therefore \triangle POR \cong \triangle QOA$.
Hence, $PR=QA$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \therefore \quad PR^{2}=QA^{2} \\
& \Rightarrow \quad(\cos \theta-\cos \phi)^{2}+(\sin \theta+\sin \phi)^{2} =[\cos (\theta+\phi)-1]^{2}+[\sin (\theta+\phi)-0]^{2} \\
& \Rightarrow \quad\left(\cos ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \phi-2 \cos \theta \cos \phi\right)+\left(\sin ^{2} \theta+\sin ^{2} \phi+2 \sin \theta \sin \phi\right) \\
& \quad=\left[\cos ^{2}(\theta+\phi)+1-2 \cos (\theta+\phi)\right]+\sin ^{2}(\theta+\phi) \\
& \Rightarrow \quad\left(\cos ^{2} \theta+\sin ^{2} \theta\right)+\left(\cos ^{2} \phi+\sin ^{2} \phi\right)-2(\cos \theta \cos \phi-\sin \theta \sin \phi) \\
& \quad=\left[\cos ^{2}(\theta+\phi)+\sin ^{2}(\theta+\phi)\right]+1-2 \cos (\theta+\phi) \\
& \Rightarrow \quad 2-2(\cos \theta \cos \phi-\sin \theta \sin \phi)=2-2 \cos (\theta+\phi) \\
& \Rightarrow \quad(\cos \theta \cos \phi-\sin \theta \sin \phi)=\cos (\theta+\phi) \quad (1)
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\cos (\theta+\phi)=\cos \theta \cos \phi-\sin \theta \sin \phi$.

(ii) Replacing $\phi$ by $-\phi$ in identity (1), we get
$$
\cos (\theta-\phi)=\cos \theta \cos (-\phi)-\sin \theta \sin (-\phi) .
$$
Hence, $\cos (\theta-\phi)=\cos \theta \cos \phi+\sin \theta \sin \phi$
$$
[\because \cos (-\phi)=\cos \phi \text { and } \sin (-\phi)=-\sin \phi] .
$$

### Summary
- (i) $\cos (\theta+\phi)=\cos \theta \cos \phi-\sin \theta \sin \phi$
- (ii) $\cos (\theta-\phi)=\cos \theta \cos \phi+\sin \theta \sin \phi$

### Theorem 2
(i) **$\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\sin x$**
(ii) **$\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\cos x$**
(iii) **$\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\cot x$**
(iv) **$\cot \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\tan x$**
(v) **$\sec \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\operatorname{cosec} x$**
(vi) **$\operatorname{cosec}\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\sec x$**

#### Proof:
(i) $\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\cos \frac{\pi}{2} \cos x+\sin \frac{\pi}{2} \sin x$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =(0 \times \cos x)+(1 \times \sin x)\left[\because \cos \frac{\pi}{2}=0 \text{ and } \sin \frac{\pi}{2}=1\right] \\
& =\sin x
\end{aligned}
$$
(ii) $\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\sin \theta$, where $\theta=\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)$
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
=\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right) & {\left[\because \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right)=\sin \theta\right]} \\
=\cos \left\{\frac{\pi}{2}-\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)\right\} & \\
=\cos x &
\end{array}
$$
(iii) $\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\frac{\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)}{\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)}$
$$
=\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}=\cot x \quad[\text { using (ii) and (i) }]
$$
(iv) $\cot \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\frac{\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)}{\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)}=\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}=\tan x \quad$ [using (i) and (ii)].

(v) $\sec \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\frac{1}{\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)}=\frac{1}{\sin x}=\operatorname{cosec} x\left[\because \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\sin x\right]$.

(vi) $\operatorname{cosec}\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\frac{1}{\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)}=\frac{1}{\cos x}=\sec x \quad\left[\because \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\cos x\right]$.

### Summary
- (i) $\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\sin x$
- (ii) $\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\cos x$
- (iii) $\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\cot x$
- (iv) $\cot \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\tan x$
- (v) $\sec \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\operatorname{cosec} x$
- (vi) $\operatorname{cosec}\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\sec x$

### Theorem 3
(i) **$\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\sin x$**
(ii) **$\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\cos x$**
(iii) **$\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\cot x$**
(iv) **$\cot \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\tan x$**
(v) **$\sec \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\operatorname{cosec} x$**
(vi) **$\operatorname{cosec}\left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\sec x$**

#### Proof:
(i) $\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\cos \frac{\pi}{2} \cos x-\sin \frac{\pi}{2} \sin x$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =(0 \times \cos x)-(1 \times \sin x)\left[\because \cos \frac{\pi}{2}=0 \text{ and } \sin \frac{\pi}{2}=1\right] \\
& =-\sin x
\end{aligned}
$$
(ii)
$$
\begin{aligned}
\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right) & =\sin \left[\frac{\pi}{2}-(-x)\right] & & \\
& =\cos (-x) & & {\left[\because \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right)=\cos \theta\right] } \\
& =\cos x & & {[\because \cos (-x)=\cos x] }
\end{aligned}
$$
(iii) $\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\frac{\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)}{\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)}$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\frac{\cos x}{-\sin x} \quad\left[\because \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\cos x \text{ and } \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\sin x\right] \\
& =-\cot x
\end{aligned}
$$
(iv) $\cot \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\frac{1}{\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)}=\frac{1}{-\cot x}=-\tan x \quad\left[\because \tan \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\cot x\right]$.

(v) $\sec \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\frac{1}{\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)}=\frac{1}{-\sin x}=-\operatorname{cosec} x \quad\left[\because \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\sin x\right]$.

(vi) $\operatorname{cosec}\left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\frac{1}{\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)}=\frac{1}{\cos x}=\sec x \quad\left[\because \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\cos x\right]$.

### Summary
- (i) $\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\sin x$
- (ii) $\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\cos x$
- (iii) $\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\cot x$
- (iv) $\cot \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\tan x$
- (v) $\sec \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=-\operatorname{cosec} x$
- (vi) $\operatorname{cosec}\left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)=\sec x$

### Theorem 4
(i) **$\cos (\pi-x)=-\cos x$**
(ii) **$\sin (\pi-x)=\sin x$**
(iii) **$\tan (\pi-x)=-\tan x$**
(iv) **$\cot (\pi-x)=-\cot x$**
(v) **$\sec (\pi-x)=-\sec x$**
(vi) **$\operatorname{cosec}(\pi-x)=\operatorname{cosec} x$**

#### Proof:
(i) $\cos (\pi-x)=\cos \pi \cos x+\sin \pi \sin x$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =(-1 \times \cos x)+(0 \times \sin x) \quad[\because \cos \pi=-1 \text { and } \sin \pi=0] \\
& =-\cos x
\end{aligned}
$$
(ii) $\sin (\pi-x)=\sin \left[\frac{\pi}{2}+\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)\right]$
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
=\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right) & {\left[\because \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+\theta\right)=\cos \theta\right]} \\
=\sin x & {\left[\because \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)=\sin x\right]}
\end{array}
$$
(iii) $\tan (\pi-x)=\frac{\sin (\pi-x)}{\cos (\pi-x)}$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\frac{\sin x}{-\cos x} \quad[\because \sin (\pi-x)=\sin x, \cos (\pi-x)=-\cos x] \\
& =-\tan x
\end{aligned}
$$
(iv) $\cot (\pi-x)=\frac{1}{\tan (\pi-x)}=\frac{1}{-\tan x}=-\cot x \quad[\because \tan (\pi-x)=-\tan x]$.

(v) $\sec (\pi-x)=\frac{1}{\cos (\pi-x)}=\frac{1}{-\cos x}=-\sec x \quad[\because \cos (\pi-x)=-\cos x]$.

(vi) $\operatorname{cosec}(\pi-x)=\frac{1}{\sin (\pi-x)}=\frac{1}{\sin x}=\operatorname{cosec} x \quad[\because \sin (\pi-x)=\sin x]$.

### Summary
- (i) $\cos (\pi-x)=-\cos x$
- (ii) $\sin (\pi-x)=\sin x$
- (iii) $\tan (\pi-x)=-\tan x$
- (iv) $\cot (\pi-x)=-\cot x$
- (v) $\sec (\pi-x)=-\sec x$
- (vi) $\operatorname{cosec}(\pi-x)=\operatorname{cosec} x$

### Theorem 5
(i) **$\cos (\pi+x)=-\cos x$**
(ii) **$\sin (\pi+x)=-\sin x$**
(iii) **$\tan (\pi+x)=\tan x$**
(iv) **$\cot (\pi+x)=\cot x$**
(v) **$\sec (\pi+x)=-\sec x$**
(vi) **$\operatorname{cosec}(\pi+x)=-\operatorname{cosec} x$**

#### Proof:
(i) $\cos (\pi+x)=\cos \pi \cos x-\sin \pi \sin x$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =(-1) \cdot \cos x-(0 \times \sin x)[\because \cos \pi=-1 \text { and } \sin \pi=0] \\
& =-\cos x
\end{aligned}
$$
(ii) $\sin (\pi+x)=\sin \left[\frac{\pi}{2}+\left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)\right]$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right) \quad\left[\because \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+\theta\right)=\cos \theta\right] \\
& =-\sin x
\end{aligned}
$$
(iii) $\tan (\pi+x)=\frac{\sin (\pi+x)}{\cos (\pi+x)}=\frac{-\sin x}{-\cos x}=\tan x \quad[\because \sin (\pi+x)=-\sin x, \cos (\pi+x)=-\cos x]$.

(iv) $\cot (\pi+x)=\frac{1}{\tan (\pi+x)}=\frac{1}{\tan x}=\cot x \quad[\because \tan (\pi+x)=\tan x]$.

(v) $\sec (\pi+x)=\frac{1}{\cos (\pi+x)}=\frac{1}{-\cos x}=-\sec x \quad[\because \cos (\pi+x)=-\cos x]$.

(vi) $\operatorname{cosec}(\pi+x)=\frac{1}{\sin (\pi+x)}=\frac{1}{-\sin x}=-\operatorname{cosec} x \quad[\because \sin (\pi+x)=-\sin x]$.

### Summary
- (i) $\cos (\pi+x)=-\cos x$
- (ii) $\sin (\pi+x)=-\sin x$
- (iii) $\tan (\pi+x)=\tan x$
- (iv) $\cot (\pi+x)=\cot x$
- (v) $\sec (\pi+x)=-\sec x$
- (vi) $\operatorname{cosec}(\pi+x)=-\operatorname{cosec} x$

### Theorem 7
(i) **$\sin (\theta+\phi)=\sin \theta \cos \phi+\cos \theta \sin \phi$**
(ii) **$\sin (\theta-\phi)=\sin \theta \cos \phi-\cos \theta \sin \phi$**

#### Proof:
(i) $\sin (\theta+\phi)=\cos \left[\frac{\pi}{2}-(\theta+\phi)\right]$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\cos \left[\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right)-\phi\right] \\
& =\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right) \cos \phi+\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right) \sin \phi \\
& =\sin \theta \cos \phi+\cos \theta \sin \phi .
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, $\sin (\theta+\phi)=\sin \theta \cos \phi+\cos \theta \sin \phi$.

(ii) Replacing $\phi$ by $-\phi$ in (i), we get
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \sin [\theta+(-\phi)]=\sin \theta \cos (-\phi)+\cos \theta \sin (-\phi) \\
& \Rightarrow \sin (\theta-\phi)=\sin \theta \cos \phi-\cos \theta \sin \phi \\
& {[\because \cos (-\phi)=\cos \phi, \sin (-\phi)=-\sin \phi] }
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, $\sin (\theta-\phi)=\sin \theta \cos \phi-\cos \theta \sin \phi$.

### Summary
- (i) $\sin (\theta+\phi)=\sin \theta \cos \phi+\cos \theta \sin \phi$
- (ii) $\sin (\theta-\phi)=\sin \theta \cos \phi-\cos \theta \sin \phi$

### Theorem 8
If none of the angles $\theta, \phi$ and $(\theta+\phi)$ is an odd multiple of $\pi / 2$ then
(i) **$\tan (\theta+\phi)=\frac{\tan \theta+\tan \phi}{1-\tan \theta \tan \phi}$**
(ii) **$\tan (\theta-\phi)=\frac{\tan \theta-\tan \phi}{1+\tan \theta \tan \phi}$**

#### Proof:
(i) $\tan (\theta+\phi)=\frac{\sin (\theta+\phi)}{\cos (\theta+\phi)}$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\frac{(\sin \theta \cos \phi+\cos \theta \sin \phi)}{(\cos \theta \cos \phi-\sin \theta \sin \phi)} \\
& =\frac{\left(\frac{\sin \theta \cos \phi}{\cos \theta \cos \phi}+\frac{\cos \theta \sin \phi}{\cos \theta \cos \phi}\right)}{\left(\frac{\cos \theta \cos \phi}{\cos \theta \cos \phi}-\frac{\sin \theta \sin \phi}{\cos \theta \cos \phi}\right)} \quad \text{[dividing num. and denom. by } \cos \theta \cos \phi \text{]} \\
& =\frac{\tan \theta+\tan \phi}{1-\tan \theta \tan \phi} .
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, $\tan (\theta+\phi)=\frac{\tan \theta+\tan \phi}{1-\tan \theta \tan \phi}$.

(ii) Replacing $\phi$ by $-\phi$ in (i), we get
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \tan [\theta+(-\phi)]=\frac{\tan \theta+\tan (-\phi)}{1-\tan \theta \tan (-\phi)} \\
\Rightarrow & \tan (\theta-\phi)=\frac{\tan \theta-\tan \phi}{1+\tan \theta \tan \phi} \quad [\because \tan(-\phi)=-\tan \phi]
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, $\tan (\theta-\phi)=\frac{\tan \theta-\tan \phi}{1+\tan \theta \tan \phi}$.

### Theorem 9
If none of the angles $\theta, \phi$ and $(\theta+\phi)$ is a multiple of $\pi$ then
(i) **$\cot (\theta+\phi)=\frac{\cot \theta \cot \phi-1}{\cot \phi+\cot \theta}$**
(ii) **$\cot (\theta-\phi)=\frac{\cot \theta \cot \phi+1}{\cot \phi-\cot \theta}$**

#### Proof:
(i) $\cot (\theta+\phi)=\frac{\cos (\theta+\phi)}{\sin (\theta+\phi)}$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\frac{(\cos \theta \cos \phi-\sin \theta \sin \phi)}{(\sin \theta \cos \phi+\cos \theta \sin \phi)} \\
& =\frac{\left(\frac{\cos \theta \cos \phi}{\sin \theta \sin \phi}-\frac{\sin \theta \sin \phi}{\sin \theta \sin \phi}\right)}{\left(\frac{\sin \theta \cos \phi}{\sin \theta \sin \phi}+\frac{\cos \theta \sin \phi}{\sin \theta \sin \phi}\right)} \quad \text{[dividing num. and denom. by } \sin \theta \sin \phi \text{]} \\
& =\frac{\cot \theta \cot \phi-1}{\cot \phi+\cot \theta} .
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, $\cot (\theta+\phi)=\frac{\cot \theta \cot \phi-1}{\cot \phi+\cot \theta}$.

(ii) Replacing $\phi$ by $-\phi$ in (i), we get
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \cot [\theta+(-\phi)]=\frac{\cot \theta \cot (-\phi)-1}{\cot (-\phi)+\cot \theta} \\
\Rightarrow & \cot (\theta-\phi)=\frac{-\cot \theta \cot \phi-1}{-\cot \phi+\cot \theta} \quad[\because \cot (-\phi)=-\cot \phi] \\
\Rightarrow & \cot (\theta-\phi)=\frac{\cot \theta \cot \phi+1}{\cot \phi-\cot \theta}
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, $\cot (\theta-\phi)=\frac{\cot \theta \cot \phi+1}{\cot \phi-\cot \theta}$.

### Summary
- (i) $\tan (\theta+\phi)=\frac{\tan \theta+\tan \phi}{1-\tan \theta \tan \phi}$
- (ii) $\tan (\theta-\phi)=\frac{\tan \theta-\tan \phi}{1+\tan \theta \tan \phi}$
- (iii) $\cot (\theta+\phi)=\frac{\cot \theta \cot \phi-1}{\cot \phi+\cot \theta}$
- (iv) $\cot (\theta-\phi)=\frac{\cot \theta \cot \phi+1}{\cot \phi-\cot \theta}$

### Theorem 10
For all $x, y \in R$, we have
(i) **$\sin (x+y) \sin (x-y)=\sin ^{2} x-\sin ^{2} y$**
(ii) **$\cos (x+y) \cos (x-y)=\cos ^{2} x-\sin ^{2} y$**

#### Proof:
(i)
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \sin (x+y) \sin (x-y) \\
& =(\sin x \cos y+\cos x \sin y)(\sin x \cos y-\cos x \sin y) \\
& =\sin ^{2} x \cos ^{2} y-\cos ^{2} x \sin ^{2} y \\
& =\sin ^{2} x\left(1-\sin ^{2} y\right)-\left(1-\sin ^{2} x\right) \sin ^{2} y \\
& =\sin ^{2} x-\sin ^{2} x \sin ^{2} y-\sin ^{2} y+\sin ^{2} x \sin ^{2} y \\
& =\sin ^{2} x-\sin ^{2} y
\end{aligned}
$$
(ii)
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \cos (x+y) \cos (x-y) \\
& =(\cos x \cos y-\sin x \sin y)(\cos x \cos y+\sin x \sin y) \\
& =\cos ^{2} x \cos ^{2} y-\sin ^{2} x \sin ^{2} y \\
& =\cos ^{2} x\left(1-\sin ^{2} y\right)-\left(1-\cos ^{2} x\right) \sin ^{2} y \\
& =\cos ^{2} x-\cos ^{2} x \sin ^{2} y-\sin ^{2} y+\cos ^{2} x \sin ^{2} y \\
& =\cos ^{2} x-\sin ^{2} y
\end{aligned}
$$

## Solved Examples

### Example 1:

Find the value of
(i) $\cos 480^{\circ}$
(ii) $\sin 1230^{\circ}$
(iii) $\cot \left(-135^{\circ}\right)$
(iv) $\operatorname{cosec}\left(-1410^{\circ}\right)$
(v) $\cos \left(-870^{\circ}\right)$
(vi) $\tan 330^{\circ}$

#### Solution:

(i) $\cos 480^{\circ}=\cos \left(360^{\circ}+120^{\circ}\right)$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\cos 120^{\circ} \quad\left[\because \cos \left(360^{\circ}+\theta\right)=\cos \theta\right] \\
& =\cos \left(180^{\circ}-60^{\circ}\right)=-\cos 60^{\circ}\left[\because \cos \left(180^{\circ}-\theta\right)=-\cos \theta\right] \\
& =-\frac{1}{2}
\end{aligned}
$$
(ii) $\sin 1230^{\circ}=\sin \left(3 \times 360^{\circ}+150^{\circ}\right)$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\sin 150^{\circ} \quad[\because \sin (2 n \pi+\theta)=\sin \theta] \\
& =\sin \left(180^{\circ}-30^{\circ}\right)=\sin 30^{\circ} \quad\left[\because \sin \left(180^{\circ}-\theta\right)=\sin \theta\right] \\
& =\frac{1}{2}
\end{aligned}
$$
(iii) $\cot \left(-135^{\circ}\right)=-\cot 135^{\circ} \quad[\because \cot (-\theta)=-\cot \theta]$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =-\cot \left(90^{\circ}+45^{\circ}\right)=-(-\tan 45^{\circ}) \quad\left[\because \cot \left(90^{\circ}+\theta\right)=-\tan \theta\right] \\
& =1 .
\end{aligned}
$$
(iv) $\operatorname{cosec}\left(-1410^{\circ}\right)=-\operatorname{cosec} 1410^{\circ}=-\operatorname{cosec}\left(4 \times 360^{\circ}-30^{\circ}\right)$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =-(-\operatorname{cosec} 30^{\circ}) \quad[\because \operatorname{cosec}(2 n \pi-\theta)=-\operatorname{cosec} \theta] \\
& =2
\end{aligned}
$$
(v) $\cos \left(-870^{\circ}\right)=\cos 870^{\circ} \quad[\because \cos (-\theta)=\cos \theta]$
$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\cos \left(2 \times 360^{\circ}+150^{\circ}\right) \\
& =\cos 150^{\circ} \quad[\because \cos (2 n \pi+\theta)=\cos \theta] \\

\text{LHS} & =\tan 56^{\circ}=\tan \left(45^{\circ}+11^{\circ}\right) \\
& =\frac{\tan 45^{\circ}+\tan 11^{\circ}}{1-\tan 45^{\circ} \tan 11^{\circ}} \\
& =\frac{1+\tan 11^{\circ}}{1-\tan 11^{\circ}} \\
& =\frac{1+\frac{\sin 11^{\circ}}{\cos 11^{\circ}}}{1-\frac{\sin 11^{\circ}}{\cos 11^{\circ}}}=\frac{\frac{\cos 11^{\circ}+\sin 11^{\circ}}{\cos 11^{\circ}}}{\frac{\cos 11^{\circ}-\sin 11^{\circ}}{\cos 11^{\circ}}} = \frac{\cos 11^{\circ}+\sin 11^{\circ}}{\cos 11^{\circ}-\sin 11^{\circ}}=\text{RHS}
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 19:

Prove that $\tan 70^{\circ}=\tan 20^{\circ}+2 \tan 50^{\circ}$.

#### Solution:

We have
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \tan 70^{\circ}=\tan \left(20^{\circ}+50^{\circ}\right) \\
\Rightarrow & \tan 70^{\circ}=\frac{\tan 20^{\circ}+\tan 50^{\circ}}{1-\tan 20^{\circ} \tan 50^{\circ}} \\
\Rightarrow & \tan 70^{\circ}\left(1-\tan 20^{\circ} \tan 50^{\circ}\right) = \tan 20^{\circ}+\tan 50^{\circ} \\
\Rightarrow & \tan 70^{\circ}-\tan 70^{\circ} \tan 20^{\circ} \tan 50^{\circ}=\tan 20^{\circ}+\tan 50^{\circ} \\
\Rightarrow & \tan 70^{\circ}-\tan \left(90^{\circ}-20^{\circ}\right) \tan 20^{\circ} \tan 50^{\circ}=\tan 20^{\circ}+\tan 50^{\circ} \\
\Rightarrow & \tan 70^{\circ}-\cot 20^{\circ} \tan 20^{\circ} \tan 50^{\circ}=\tan 20^{\circ}+\tan 50^{\circ} \\
\Rightarrow & \tan 70^{\circ}-(1) \tan 50^{\circ}=\tan 20^{\circ}+\tan 50^{\circ} \\
\Rightarrow & \tan 70^{\circ}=\tan 20^{\circ}+2 \tan 50^{\circ}
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 20:

Show that $\cot 2 x \cot x-\cot 3 x \cot 2 x-\cot 3 x \cot x=1$.

#### Solution:

We have
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \cot 3 x=\cot (2 x+x) \\
\Leftrightarrow & \cot 3 x=\frac{\cot 2 x \cot x-1}{\cot 2 x+\cot x} \\
\Leftrightarrow & \cot 3 x (\cot 2 x+\cot x)=\cot 2 x \cot x-1 \\
\Leftrightarrow & \cot 3 x \cot 2 x+\cot 3 x \cot x=\cot 2 x \cot x-1 \\
\Leftrightarrow & \cot 2 x \cot x-\cot 3 x \cot 2 x-\cot 3 x \cot x=1
\end{aligned}
$$
