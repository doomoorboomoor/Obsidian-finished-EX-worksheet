## Trigonometrical Functions of Half Angles

---

### Theorem 1

For all $x \in R$, we have:
(i) $\sin x=2 \sin \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2}$
(ii) $1-\cos x=2 \sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}$
(iii) $1+\cos x=2 \cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}$

#### Proof

(i) $\sin x=\sin \left(\frac{x}{2}+\frac{x}{2}\right)$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\sin \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2}+\cos \frac{x}{2} \sin \frac{x}{2} \\
& =2 \sin \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2} \\
\therefore \sin x & =2 \sin \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2} .
\end{aligned}
$$

(ii) $\cos x=\cos \left(\frac{x}{2}+\frac{x}{2}\right)$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\cos \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2}-\sin \frac{x}{2} \sin \frac{x}{2} \\
& =\cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}-\sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2} \\
& =\left(1-\sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}\right)-\sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2} \\
& =\left(1-2 \sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}\right) . \\
\therefore \quad & (1-\cos x)=2 \sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2} .
\end{aligned}
$$

(iii) $\cos x=\cos \left(\frac{x}{2}+\frac{x}{2}\right)$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\cos \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2}-\sin \frac{x}{2} \sin \frac{x}{2} \\
& =\cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}-\sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2} \\
& =\cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}-\left(1-\cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}\right) \\
& =\left(2 \cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}-1\right) . \\
\therefore \quad & (1+\cos x)=2 \cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2} .
\end{aligned}
$$

### Theorem 2

For all $x \in R$, we have:
(i) $\sin x=\frac{2 \tan (x / 2)}{1+\tan ^{2}(x / 2)}$
(ii) $\cos x=\frac{1-\tan ^{2}(x / 2)}{1+\tan ^{2}(x / 2)}$

#### Proof

(i) $\sin x=2 \sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)$

$$
\begin{aligned}
= & \frac{2 \sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)+\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)} \\
& =\frac{\left\{\frac{2 \sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}\right\}}{1+\left\{\frac{\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}\right\}}\left[\text { dividing num. and denom. by } \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\right] \\
& =\frac{2 \tan \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\left\{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\right\}} . \\
\therefore \quad \sin x & =\frac{2 \tan ^{\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}}{\left\{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\right\}} .
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\text { (ii) } \begin{aligned}
\cos x & =\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) \\
& =\frac{\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)+\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}\left[\because 1=\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)+\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\right] \\
& =\frac{1-\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}\left[\text { dividing num. and denom. by } \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\right] \\
\therefore \quad \cos x & =\frac{1-\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}
\end{aligned}
$$

---

## Summary

- (i) $\sin x=2 \sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)$
- (ii) $(1-\cos x)=2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)$
- (iii) $(1+\cos x)=2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)$
- (iv) $\sin x=\frac{2 \tan \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}$
- (v) $\cos x=\frac{1-\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}$

## Solved Examples

### Example 1

If $\tan x=\frac{-4}{3}$ and $\frac{\pi}{2}<x<\pi$, find the values of
(i) $\sin \frac{x}{2}$,
(ii) $\cos \frac{x}{2}$,
(iii) $\tan \frac{x}{2}$.

#### Solution:

Since $x$ lies in Quadrant II, we have $\cos x<0$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\because \quad \tan x=\frac{-4}{3} & \Rightarrow \sec ^{2} x=\left(1+\tan ^{2} x\right)=\left(1+\frac{16}{9}\right)=\frac{25}{9} \\
& \Rightarrow \cos ^{2} x=\frac{1}{\sec ^{2} x}=\frac{9}{25} \\
& \Rightarrow \cos x=-\sqrt{\frac{9}{25}}=-\frac{3}{5}
\end{aligned}
$$

Also, $\frac{\pi}{2}<x<\pi \Rightarrow \frac{\pi}{4}<\frac{x}{2}<\frac{\pi}{2}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \frac{x}{2} \text { lies in Quadrant I } \\
& \Rightarrow \sin \frac{x}{2}>0 \text { and } \cos \frac{x}{2}>0 .
\end{aligned}
$$

(i) $2 \sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=(1-\cos x)=\left(1+\frac{3}{5}\right)=\frac{8}{5}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=\frac{8}{10}=\frac{4}{5} \\
& \Rightarrow \sin \frac{x}{2}=+\sqrt{\frac{4}{5}}=\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\left[\because \sin \frac{x}{2}>0\right] .
\end{aligned}
$$

(ii) $2 \cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=(1+\cos x)=\left(1-\frac{3}{5}\right)=\frac{2}{5}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=\left(\frac{2}{5 \times 2}\right)=\frac{1}{5} \\
& \Rightarrow \cos \frac{x}{2}=+\sqrt{\frac{1}{5}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \quad\left[\because \cos \frac{x}{2}>0\right]
\end{aligned}
$$

(iii) $\tan \frac{x}{2}=\frac{\sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}=\left(\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} \times \sqrt{5}\right)=2$.

### Example 2

If $\tan x=\frac{3}{4}$ and $\pi<x<\frac{3 \pi}{2}$, find the values of
(i) $\sin \frac{x}{2}$,
(ii) $\cos \frac{x}{2}$,
(iii) $\tan \frac{x}{2}$.

#### Solution:

Since $x$ lies in Quadrant III, we have $\cos x<0$.

$$
\text{ Now, } \begin{aligned}
\tan x=\frac{3}{4} & \Rightarrow \sec ^{2} x=\left(1+\tan ^{2} x\right)=\left(1+\frac{9}{16}\right)=\frac{25}{16} \\
& \Rightarrow \cos ^{2} x=\frac{1}{\sec ^{2} x}=\frac{16}{25} \\
& \Rightarrow \cos x=-\sqrt{\frac{16}{25}}=\frac{-4}{5}
\end{aligned}
$$

Also, $\pi<x<\frac{3 \pi}{2} \Rightarrow \frac{\pi}{2}<\frac{x}{2}<\frac{3 \pi}{4}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \frac{x}{2} \text { lies in Quadrant II } \\
& \Rightarrow \sin \frac{x}{2}>0 \text { and } \cos \frac{x}{2}<0
\end{aligned}
$$

(i) $2 \sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=(1-\cos x)=\left(1+\frac{4}{5}\right)=\frac{9}{5}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=\frac{9}{10} \\
& \Rightarrow \sin \frac{x}{2}=+\sqrt{\frac{9}{10}}=\frac{3}{\sqrt{10}} \quad\left[\because \sin \frac{x}{2}>0\right]
\end{aligned}
$$

(ii) $2 \cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=(1+\cos x)=\left(1-\frac{4}{5}\right)=\frac{1}{5}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=\frac{1}{10} \\
& \Rightarrow \cos \frac{x}{2}=-\sqrt{\frac{1}{10}}=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{10}} \quad\left[\because \cos \frac{x}{2}<0\right]
\end{aligned}
$$

(iii) $\tan \frac{x}{2}=\frac{\sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}=\left(\frac{3}{\sqrt{10}} \times \frac{\sqrt{10}}{-1}\right)=-3$.

### Example 3

If $\cos x=-\frac{1}{3}$ and $x$ lies in Quadrant III, find the values of
(i) $\sin \frac{x}{2}$,
(ii) $\cos \frac{x}{2}$,
(iii) $\tan \frac{x}{2}$.

#### Solution:

Since $x$ lies in Quadrant III, we have

$$
\begin{aligned}
\pi<x<\frac{3 \pi}{2} & \Rightarrow \frac{\pi}{2}<\frac{x}{2}<\frac{3 \pi}{4} \\
& \Rightarrow \frac{x}{2} \text { lies in Quadrant II } \\
& \Rightarrow \sin \frac{x}{2}>0 \text { and } \cos \frac{x}{2}<0
\end{aligned}
$$

(i) $2 \sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=(1-\cos x)=\left(1+\frac{1}{3}\right)=\frac{4}{3}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=\frac{2}{3} \\
& \Rightarrow \sin \frac{x}{2}=+\sqrt{\frac{2}{3}}=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{\sqrt{6}}{3}\left[\because \sin \frac{x}{2}>0\right] .
\end{aligned}
$$

(ii) $2 \cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=(1+\cos x)=\left(1-\frac{1}{3}\right)=\frac{2}{3}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=\frac{1}{3} \\
& \Rightarrow \cos \frac{x}{2}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}=-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} .
\end{aligned}
$$

(iii) $\tan \frac{x}{2}=\frac{\sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}=\left(\frac{\sqrt{6}}{3} \times \frac{3}{-\sqrt{3}}\right)=-\sqrt{2}$.

### Example 4

Prove that $\frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x}=(\operatorname{cosec} x+\cot x)^{2}$.

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =\frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x} \\
& =\frac{2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}=\cot ^{2} \frac{x}{2}\left[\begin{array}{r}
\because 1+\cos x=2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) \\
1-\cos x=2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)
\end{array}\right]
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text { RHS } & =(\operatorname{cosec} x+\cot x)^{2} \\
& =\left(\frac{1}{\sin x}+\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}\right)^{2}=\left(\frac{1+\cos x}{\sin x}\right)^{2} \\
& =\left\{\frac{2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{2 \sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}\right\}^{2}=\left\{\frac{\cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}\right\}^{2}=\cot ^{2} \frac{x}{2} \\
\therefore \quad \text { LHS } & =\text { RHS. }
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 5

Prove that $\frac{\cos x}{(1-\sin x)}=\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{x}{2}\right)$.

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =\frac{\cos x}{(1-\sin x)} \\
& =\frac{\left(\cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}-\sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}\right)}{\left(\cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}+\sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}-2 \sin \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2}\right)} \\
& \left.\begin{array}{c}
\because \cos x=\left(\cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}-\sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}\right), \cos ^{2} \frac{x}{2}+\sin ^{2} \frac{x}{2}=1 \\
\text { and } \sin x=2 \sin \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2}
\end{array}\right] \\
& =\frac{\left(\cos \frac{x}{2}-\sin \frac{x}{2}\right)\left(\cos \frac{x}{2}+\sin \frac{x}{2}\right)}{\left(\cos \frac{x}{2}-\sin \frac{x}{2}\right)^{2}} \\
& =\frac{\left(\cos \frac{x}{2}+\sin \frac{x}{2}\right)}{\left(\cos \frac{x}{2}-\sin \frac{x}{2}\right)}=\frac{\left(1+\tan \frac{x}{2}\right)}{\left(1-\tan \frac{x}{2}\right)}=\frac{\left(\tan \frac{\pi}{4}+\tan \frac{x}{2}\right)}{\left(1-\tan \frac{\pi}{4} \cdot \tan \frac{x}{2}\right)} \\
& =\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{x}{2}\right)=\text { RHS. }
\end{aligned}
$$

### Example 6

If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ be two distinct real numbers such that $(\alpha-\beta) \neq 2 n \pi$ for any intetger $n$, satisfying the equation $a \cos \theta+b \sin \theta=c$ then prove that

(i) $\cos (\alpha+\beta)=\frac{a^{2}-b^{2}}{a^{2}+b^{2}} \quad$ (ii) $\sin (\alpha+\beta)=\frac{2 a b}{a^{2}+b^{2}} .$

#### Solution:

Since $\alpha$ and $\beta$ satisfy the equation $a \cos \theta+b \sin \theta=c$, we have

$$
\begin{align*}
& a \cos \alpha+b \sin \alpha=c  \tag{i}\\
& a \cos \beta+b \sin \beta=c \tag{ii}
\end{align*}
$$

Subtracting (ii) from (i), we get

$$
\left.\begin{array}{rl} 
& a(\cos \alpha-\cos \beta)+b(\sin \alpha-\sin \beta)=0 \\
\Rightarrow & -2 a \sin \left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)+2 b \cos \left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)=0 \\
\Rightarrow & -2 \sin \left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)\left[a \sin \left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)-b \cos \left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)\right]=0 \\
\Rightarrow & a \sin \left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)-b \cos \left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)=0 \\
& \left.\quad \because(\alpha-\beta) \neq 2 n \pi \Rightarrow\left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right) \neq n \pi\right] \\
\therefore & \sin \left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right) \neq \sin n \pi \neq 0
\end{array}\right]
$$

$$
\Rightarrow \tan \left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)=\frac{b}{a} . \quad \text { (iii) }
$$

$$
\begin{array}{l}
\text { (i) } \cos (\alpha+\beta)=\frac{1-\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)} \quad\left[\because \cos x=\frac{1-\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}\right] \\
\quad=\frac{\left(1-\frac{b^{2}}{a^{2}}\right)}{\left(1+\frac{b^{2}}{a^{2}}\right)}=\frac{a^{2}-b^{2}}{a^{2}+b^{2}} \quad[\text { using (iii) }] . \\
\text { (ii) } \sin (\alpha+\beta)=\frac{2 \tan \left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)} \quad\left[\because \sin x=\frac{2 \tan \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}\right] \\
\quad=\frac{2\left(\frac{b}{a}\right)}{\left(1+\frac{b^{2}}{a^{2}}\right)}=\frac{2 a b}{a^{2}+b^{2}} \quad[\text { using (iii) }] .
\end{array}
$$
