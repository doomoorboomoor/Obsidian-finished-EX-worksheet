## PROPERTIES OF INVERSE FUNCTIONS

### Theorem 1
Prove that:
- (i) $\sin ^{-1}(\sin x)=x, x \in\left[\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$
- (ii) $\cos ^{-1}(\cos x)=x, x \in[0, \pi]$
- (iii) $\tan ^{-1}(\tan x)=x, x \in\left(\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right)$
- (iv) $\operatorname{cosec}^{-1}(\operatorname{cosec} x)=x, x \in\left[\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]-\{0\}$
- (v) $\sec ^{-1}(\sec x)=x, x \in[0, \pi]-\left\{\frac{\pi}{2}\right\}$
- (vi) $\cot ^{-1}(\cot x)=x, x \in(0, \pi)$

**Proof (i):** Let $\sin x=y$, where $x \in\left[\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$.
Then, $\sin ^{-1} y=x \Rightarrow \sin ^{-1}(\sin x)=x \quad[\because \quad y=\sin x]$.
$\therefore \sin ^{-1}(\sin x)=x$.

**Proof (ii):** Let $\cos x=y$, where $x \in[0, \pi]$.
Then, $\cos ^{-1} y=x \Rightarrow \cos ^{-1}(\cos x)=x \quad[\because \quad y=\cos x]$.
$\therefore \cos ^{-1}(\cos x)=x$.

Similarly, the other results may be proved.

### Theorem 2
Prove that:
- (i) $\sin \left(\sin ^{-1} x\right)=x, x \in[-1,1]$
- (ii) $\cos \left(\cos ^{-1} x\right)=x, x \in[-1,1]$
- (iii) $\tan \left(\tan ^{-1} x\right)=x, x \in R$
- (iv) $\operatorname{cosec}\left(\operatorname{cosec}^{-1} x\right)=x, x \in R-[-1,1]$
- (v) $\sec \left(\sec ^{-1} x\right)=x, x \in R-[-1,1]$
- (vi) $\cot \left(\cot ^{-1} x\right)=x, x \in R$

**Proof (i):** Let $\sin ^{-1} x=\theta$, where $x \in[-1,1]$.
Then, $\sin \theta=x \Rightarrow \sin \left(\sin ^{-1} x\right)=x \quad\left[\because \quad \theta=\sin ^{-1} x\right]$.
$\therefore \sin \left(\sin ^{-1} x\right)=x$.

**Proof (ii):** Let $\cos ^{-1} x=\theta$, where $x \in[-1,1]$.
Then, $\cos \theta=x \quad \Rightarrow \cos \left(\cos ^{-1} x\right)=x \quad\left[\because \quad \theta=\cos ^{-1} x\right]$.
$\therefore \quad \cos \left(\cos ^{-1} x\right)=x$.

Similarly, the other results may be proved.

### Theorem 3
Prove that:
- (i) $\sin ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\operatorname{cosec}^{-1} x,(x \geq 1$ or $x \leq-1)$
- (ii) $\cos ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\sec ^{-1} x,(x \geq 1$ or $x \leq-1)$
- (iii) $\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\cot ^{-1} x,(x>0)$

**Proof (i):** $\sin ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\theta \Rightarrow \sin \theta=\frac{1}{x}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \operatorname{cosec} \theta=x \\
& \Rightarrow \theta=\operatorname{cosec}^{-1} x \\
& \Rightarrow \sin ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\operatorname{cosec}^{-1} x .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\sin ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\operatorname{cosec}^{-1} x$.

**Proof (ii):** $\cos ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\theta \Rightarrow \cos \theta=\frac{1}{x}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \sec \theta=x \\
& \Rightarrow \theta=\sec ^{-1} x \\
& \Rightarrow \cos ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\sec ^{-1} x .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\cos ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\sec ^{-1} x$.

**Proof (iii):** $\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\theta \Rightarrow \tan \theta=\frac{1}{x}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \cot \theta=x \\
& \Rightarrow \theta=\cot ^{-1} x \\
& \Rightarrow \tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\cot ^{-1} x .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\cot ^{-1} x$.

### Theorem 4
Prove that:
- (i) $\sin ^{-1}(-x)=-\sin ^{-1} x, x \in[-1,1]$
- (ii) $\tan ^{-1}(-x)=-\tan ^{-1} x, x \in R$
- (iii) $\operatorname{cosec}^{-1}(-x)=-\operatorname{cosec}^{-1} x,|x| \geq 1$

**Proof (i):** Let $\sin ^{-1}(-x)=\theta$. Then,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\sin ^{-1}(-x)=\theta & \Rightarrow-x=\sin \theta \\
& \Rightarrow x=-\sin \theta=\sin (-\theta) \\
& \Rightarrow-\theta=\sin ^{-1} x \\
& \Rightarrow \theta=-\sin ^{-1} x \\
& \Rightarrow \sin ^{-1}(-x)=-\sin ^{-1} x
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad \sin ^{-1}(-x)=-\sin ^{-1} x$.
Similarly, the other results may be proved.

### Theorem 5
Prove that:
- (i) $\cos ^{-1}(-x)=\pi-\cos ^{-1} x, \quad x \in[-1,1]$
- (ii) $\sec ^{-1}(-x)=\pi-\sec ^{-1} x, \quad|x| \geq 1$
- (iii) $\cot ^{-1}(-x)=\pi-\cot ^{-1} x, x \in R$

**Proof (i):** Let $\cos ^{-1}(-x)=\theta$. Then,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\cos ^{-1}(-x)=\theta & \Rightarrow-x=\cos \theta \\
& \Rightarrow x=-\cos \theta=\cos (\pi-\theta) \\
& \Rightarrow \cos ^{-1} x=(\pi-\theta)=\pi-\cos ^{-1}(-x) \\
& \Rightarrow \cos ^{-1}(-x)=\pi-\cos ^{-1} x . \\
\therefore \quad \cos ^{-1}(-x)=\pi & -\cos ^{-1} x .
\end{aligned}
$$

Similarly, the other results may be proved.

### Theorem 6
Prove that:
- (i) $\sin ^{-1} x+\cos ^{-1} x=\frac{\pi}{2}, x \in[-1,1]$
- (ii) $\tan ^{-1} x+\cot ^{-1} x=\frac{\pi}{2}, x \in R$
- (iii) $\operatorname{cosec}^{-1} x+\sec ^{-1} x=\frac{\pi}{2},|x| \geq 1$

**Proof (i):** Let $\sin ^{-1} x=\theta$. Then,

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \sin ^{-1} x=\theta \Rightarrow x=\sin \theta=\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right) \\
& \Rightarrow \cos ^{-1} x=\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta \\
& \Rightarrow \cos ^{-1} x=\frac{\pi}{2}-\sin ^{-1} x \\
& \Rightarrow \sin ^{-1} x+\cos ^{-1} x=\frac{\pi}{2} \\
& \therefore \quad \sin ^{-1} x+\cos ^{-1} x=\frac{\pi}{2}
\end{aligned}
$$

Similarly, the other results may be proved.

### Theorem 7
Prove that:
- (i) $\tan ^{-1} x+\tan ^{-1} y=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{x+y}{1-x y}\right)$, if $x y<1$
- (ii) $\tan ^{-1} x-\tan ^{-1} y=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{x-y}{1+x y}\right)$, if $x y>-1$
- (iii) $2 \tan ^{-1} x=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1-x^{2}}\right)$, if $|x|<1$

**Proof (i):** Let $x y<1, \tan ^{-1} x=\theta$ and $\tan ^{-1} y=\phi$. Then,

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \tan \theta=x \text { and } \tan \phi=y \\
\Rightarrow & \tan (\theta+\phi)=\frac{\tan \theta+\tan \phi}{1-\tan \theta \tan \phi}=\frac{x+y}{1-x y} \\
\Rightarrow & \theta+\phi=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{x+y}{1-x y}\right) \\
\Rightarrow & \tan ^{-1} x+\tan ^{-1} y=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{x+y}{1-x y}\right) \\
\therefore & \tan ^{-1} x+\tan ^{-1} y=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{x+y}{1-x y}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

**Proof (ii):** Let $x y>-1$.
On replacing $y$ by $-y$ in (i), we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \tan ^{-1} x+\tan ^{-1}(-y)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{x-y}{1+x y}\right) \\
\Rightarrow & \tan ^{-1} x-\tan ^{-1} y=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{x-y}{1+x y}\right) .
\end{aligned}
$$

**Proof (iii):** Let $|x|<1$.
Replacing $y$ by $x$ in (i), we get

$$
2 \tan ^{-1} x=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1-x^{2}}\right)
$$

### Theorem 8
Prove that:
- (i) $2 \tan ^{-1} x=\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1+x^{2}}\right),|x|<1$
- (ii) $2 \tan ^{-1} x=\cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-x^{2}}{1+x^{2}}\right),|x| \geq 0$
- (iii) $2 \tan ^{-1} x=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1-x^{2}}\right),|x|<1$

**Proof:** Let $\tan ^{-1} x=\theta$. Then, $x=\tan \theta$.

(i)
$$
\begin{aligned}
\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1+x^{2}}\right) & =\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 \tan \theta}{1+\tan ^{2} \theta}\right) \quad[\because \quad x=\tan \theta] \\
& =\sin ^{-1}(\sin 2 \theta) \\
& =2 \theta=2 \tan ^{-1} x \\
\therefore \quad 2 \tan ^{-1} x & =\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1+x^{2}}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

(ii)
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-x^{2}}{1+x^{2}}\right)=\cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-\tan ^{2} \theta}{1+\tan ^{2} \theta}\right) \quad[\because \quad x=\tan \theta] \\
&=\cos ^{-1}(\cos 2 \theta) \\
&=2 \theta=2 \tan ^{-1} x \\
& \therefore 2 \tan ^{-1} x= \cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-x^{2}}{1+x^{2}}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

(iii)
$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1-x^{2}}\right) & =\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 \tan \theta}{1-\tan ^{2} \theta}\right) \quad[\because x=\tan \theta] \\
& =\tan ^{-1}(\tan 2 \theta) \\
& =2 \theta=2 \tan ^{-1} x
\end{aligned}
$$
$\therefore 2 \tan ^{-1} x= \tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1-x^{2}}\right)$

### Theorem 9
Prove that $2 \sin ^{-1} x=\sin ^{-1}\left[2 x \sqrt{1-x^{2}}\right], \frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}} \leq x \leq \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$.

**Proof:** Let $\sin ^{-1} x=\theta$. Then, $x=\sin \theta$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \sin 2 \theta & =2 \sin \theta \cos \theta \\
& =2 \sin \theta \cdot \sqrt{\left(1-\sin ^{2} \theta\right)} \\
& =2 x \sqrt{1-x^{2}} \\
\Rightarrow 2 \theta= & \sin ^{-1}\left\{2 x \sqrt{1-x^{2}}\right\} \Rightarrow 2 \sin ^{-1} x=\sin ^{-1}\left\{2 x \sqrt{1-x^{2}}\right\} .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $2 \sin ^{-1} x=\sin ^{-1}\left\{2 x \sqrt{1-x^{2}}\right\}$.

### Theorem 10
Prove that $2 \cos ^{-1} x=\cos ^{-1}\left(2 x^{2}-1\right), \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \leq x \leq 1$.

**Proof:** Let $\cos ^{-1} x=\theta$. Then, $x=\cos \theta$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \therefore \cos 2 \theta=\left(2 \cos ^{2} \theta-1\right)=\left(2 x^{2}-1\right) \\
& \Rightarrow 2 \theta=\cos ^{-1}\left(2 x^{2}-1\right) \\
& \Rightarrow 2 \cos ^{-1} x=\cos ^{-1}\left(2 x^{2}-1\right) \quad\left[\because \theta=\cos ^{-1} x\right]
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $2 \cos ^{-1} x=\cos ^{-1}\left(2 x^{2}-1\right)$.

### Theorem 11
Prove that $3 \sin ^{-1} x=\sin ^{-1}\left(3 x-4 x^{3}\right), x \in\left[-\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}\right]$.

**Proof:** Put $\sin ^{-1} x=\theta$. Then, $x=\sin \theta$.
Now, $\sin 3 \theta=\left(3 \sin \theta-4 \sin ^{3} \theta\right)=\left(3 x-4 x^{3}\right)$
$\Rightarrow 3 \theta=\sin ^{-1}\left(3 x-4 x^{3}\right) \Rightarrow 3 \sin ^{-1} x=\sin ^{-1}\left(3 x-4 x^{3}\right)\left[\because \theta=\sin ^{-1} x\right]$.
Hence, $3 \sin ^{-1} x=\sin ^{-1}\left(3 x-4 x^{3}\right)$.

### Theorem 12
Prove that $3 \cos ^{-1} x=\cos ^{-1}\left(4 x^{3}-3 x\right), x \in\left[\frac{1}{2}, 1\right]$.

Now, $\cos 3 \theta=\left(4 \cos ^{3} \theta-3 \cos \theta\right)=\left(4 x^{3}-3 x\right)$

$$
\Rightarrow 3 \theta=\cos ^{-1}\left(4 x^{3}-3 x\right) \Rightarrow 3 \cos ^{-1} x=\cos ^{-1}\left(4 x^{3}-3 x\right)\left[\because \quad \theta=\cos ^{-1} x\right] .

### Theorem 13
Prove that:
- (i) $\sin ^{-1} x+\sin ^{-1} y=\sin ^{-1}\left\{x \sqrt{1-y^{2}}+y \sqrt{1-x^{2}}\right\}$
- (ii) $\sin ^{-1} x-\sin ^{-1} y=\sin ^{-1}\left\{x \sqrt{1-y^{2}}-y \sqrt{1-x^{2}}\right\}$
- (iii) $\cos ^{-1} x+\cos ^{-1} y=\cos ^{-1}\left\{x y-\sqrt{\left(1-x^{2}\right)\left(1-y^{2}\right)}\right\}$
- (iv) $\cos ^{-1} x-\cos ^{-1} y=\cos ^{-1}\left\{x y+\sqrt{\left(1-x^{2}\right)\left(1-y^{2}\right)}\right\}$

**Proof (i):** Let $\sin ^{-1} x=\theta_{1}$, and $\sin ^{-1} y=\theta_{2}$. Then,

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \sin \theta_{1}=x \text { and } \sin \theta_{2}=y \\
\Rightarrow & \sin \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)= \\
& =\left\{\sin \theta_{1} \cos \theta_{2}+\cos \theta_{1} \sin \theta_{2}\right. \\
& =\left\{x \sqrt{1-y^{2}}+y \sqrt{1-\sin ^{2} \theta_{2}}\right\}+\left\{\sqrt{1-\sin ^{2} \theta_{1}} \cdot \sin \theta_{2}\right\} \\
\Rightarrow & \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)=\sin ^{-1}\left\{x \sqrt{1-y^{2}}+y \sqrt{1-x^{2}}\right\} \\
\Rightarrow & \sin ^{-1} x+\sin ^{-1} y=\sin ^{-1}\left\{x \sqrt{1-y^{2}}+y \sqrt{1-x^{2}}\right\} .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\sin ^{-1} x+\sin ^{-1} y=\sin ^{-1}\left\{x \sqrt{1-y^{2}}+y \sqrt{1-x^{2}}\right\}$.
The other results can be proved similarly.

### Theorem 14
Prove that:
(i)
$$
\begin{aligned}
\sin ^{-1} x & =\cos ^{-1} \sqrt{1-x^{2}}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\right)=\operatorname{cosec}^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{x}\right) \\
= & \sec ^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\right)=\cot ^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}{x}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

(ii)
$$
\begin{aligned}
\cos ^{-1} x & =\sin ^{-1} \sqrt{1-x^{2}}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}{x}\right) \\
& =\operatorname{cosec}^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\right)=\sec ^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)=\cot ^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

(iii)
$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan ^{-1} x & =\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{\sqrt{1+x^{2}}}\right)=\cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+x^{2}}}\right) \\
& =\operatorname{cosec}^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{1+x^{2}}}{x}\right)=\sec ^{-1}\left(\sqrt{1+x^{2}}\right)=\cot ^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

**Proof (i):** Let $\sin ^{-1} x=\theta$. Then, $\sin \theta=x$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \therefore \cos \theta=\sqrt{1-x^{2}}, \tan \theta=\frac{x}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}, \operatorname{cosec} \theta=\frac{1}{x} \\
& \sec \theta=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}} \text { and } \cot \theta=\frac{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}{x} \\
& \therefore \quad \theta=\cos ^{-1} \sqrt{1-x^{2}}, \theta=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\right), \theta=\operatorname{cosec}^{-1} \frac{1}{x}, \\
& \quad \theta=\sec ^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\right) \text { and } \theta=\cot ^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}{x}\right) . \\
& \text { Hence, } \sin ^{-1} x=\cos ^{-1} \sqrt{1-x^{2}}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\right) \\
& \quad=\operatorname{cosec}^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\sec ^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\right) \\
& \quad=\cot ^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}{x}\right) .
\end{aligned}
$$

Similarly, the other results can be proved.

## Solved Examples

### Example 1:

Find the value of:
- (i) $\sin ^{-1}\left(\sin \frac{\pi}{3}\right)$
- (ii) $\cos ^{-1}\left(\cos \frac{2 \pi}{3}\right)$
- (iii) $\tan ^{-1}\left(\tan \frac{\pi}{4}\right)$

#### Solution:

We have
(i) $\sin ^{-1}\left(\sin \frac{\pi}{3}\right)=\frac{\pi}{3}$, since $\frac{\pi}{3} \in\left[\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$.
(ii) $\cos ^{-1}\left(\cos \frac{2 \pi}{3}\right)=\frac{2 \pi}{3}$, since $\frac{2 \pi}{3} \in[0, \pi]$.
(iii) $\tan ^{-1}\left(\tan \frac{\pi}{4}\right)=\frac{\pi}{4}$, since $\frac{\pi}{4} \in\left(\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right)$.

### Example 2:

Find the value of:
- (i) $\sin ^{-1}\left(\sin \frac{2 \pi}{3}\right)$
- (ii) $\cos ^{-1}\left(\cos \frac{7 \pi}{6}\right)$
- (iii) $\tan ^{-1}\left(\tan \frac{3 \pi}{4}\right)$

#### Solution:

(i) We know that the principal-value branch of $\sin ^{-1}$ is $\left[\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$.
$\therefore \sin ^{-1}\left(\sin \frac{2 \pi}{3}\right) \neq \frac{2 \pi}{3}$.
Now, $\sin ^{-1}\left(\sin \frac{2 \pi}{3}\right)=\sin ^{-1}\left\{\sin \left(\pi-\frac{\pi}{3}\right)\right\}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\sin ^{-1}\left(\sin \frac{\pi}{3}\right)[\because \sin (\pi-\theta)=\sin \theta] \\
& =\frac{\pi}{3}\left\{\because \frac{\pi}{3} \in\left[\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]\right\} .
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence, $\sin ^{-1}\left(\sin \frac{2 \pi}{3}\right)=\frac{\pi}{3}$.

(ii) We know that the principal-value branch of $\cos ^{-1}$ is $[0, \pi]$.
$\therefore \cos ^{-1}\left(\cos \frac{7 \pi}{6}\right) \neq \frac{7 \pi}{6}$.
Now, $\cos ^{-1}\left(\cos \frac{7 \pi}{6}\right)=\cos ^{-1}\left\{\cos \left(2 \pi-\frac{5 \pi}{6}\right)\right\}$
$=\cos ^{-1}\left\{\cos \frac{5 \pi}{6}\right\}\{\because \cos (2 \pi-\theta)=\cos \theta\} =\frac{5 \pi}{6}$.
Hence, $\cos ^{-1}\left(\cos \frac{7 \pi}{6}\right)=\frac{5 \pi}{6}$.

(iii) We know that the principal-value branch of $\tan ^{-1}$ is $\left(\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right)$.
$\therefore \tan ^{-1}\left(\tan \frac{3 \pi}{4}\right) \neq \frac{3 \pi}{4}$.
Now, $\tan ^{-1}\left(\tan \frac{3 \pi}{4}\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left\{\tan \left(\pi-\frac{\pi}{4}\right)\right\}$
$=\tan ^{-1}\left\{-\tan \frac{\pi}{4}\right\} \quad\left[\because \tan \left(\pi-\frac{\pi}{4}\right)=-\tan \frac{\pi}{4}\right]$
$=\tan ^{-1}\left\{\tan \left(\frac{-\pi}{4}\right)\right\} \quad[\because \quad-\tan \theta=\tan (-\theta)]$
$=\frac{-\pi}{4}$.
Hence, $\tan ^{-1}\left(\tan \frac{3 \pi}{4}\right)=\frac{-\pi}{4}$.

### Example 3:

Hence, $\sin ^{-1}\left(\sin \frac{3 \pi}{5}\right)=\frac{2 \pi}{5}$.

Now, $\cos ^{-1}\left(\cos \frac{13 \pi}{6}\right)=\cos ^{-1}\left\{\cos \left(2 \pi+\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\right\}$

(iii) We know that the principal-value branch of $\tan ^{-1}$ is $\left(\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right)$.

$$
\begin{aligned}

### Example 4:

- (i) Show that $\sin ^{-1}\left\{\sin \frac{3 \pi}{4}\right\} \neq \frac{3 \pi}{4}$ and find its value.
- (ii) Show that $\cos ^{-1}\left\{\cos \left(\frac{-\pi}{3}\right)\right\} \neq \frac{-\pi}{3}$ and find its value.
- (iii) Show that $\tan ^{-1}\left\{\tan \frac{5 \pi}{6}\right\} \neq \frac{5 \pi}{6}$ and find its value.

#### Solution:

(i) We know that the principal-value branch of $\sin ^{-1}$ is $\left[\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$.
$\therefore \quad \sin ^{-1}\left\{\sin \frac{3 \pi}{4}\right\} \neq \frac{3 \pi}{4}$.
Now, $\sin ^{-1}\left\{\sin \frac{3 \pi}{4}\right\}=\sin ^{-1}\left\{\sin \left(\pi-\frac{\pi}{4}\right)\right\}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\sin ^{-1}\left\{\sin \frac{\pi}{4}\right\}\left[\because \sin \left(\pi-\frac{\pi}{4}\right)=\sin \frac{\pi}{4}\right] \\
& =\frac{\pi}{4}\left\{\because \frac{\pi}{4} \in\left[\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]\right\} .
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad \sin ^{-1}\left\{\sin \frac{3 \pi}{4}\right\}=\frac{\pi}{4}$.

Prove that $\tan ^{-1}\left(\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos x}{1+\cos x}}\right)=\frac{x}{2}, x<\pi$.

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \text { LHS }=\tan ^{-1}\left(\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos x}{1+\cos x}}\right)=\tan ^{-1} \sqrt{\frac{2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}} \\
&=\tan ^{-1}\left(\tan \frac{x}{2}\right)=\frac{x}{2}=\text { RHS } .
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad \tan ^{-1}\left(\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos x}{1+\cos x}}\right)=\frac{x}{2}$.

### Example 27:

Prove that $\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\cos x-\sin x}{\cos x+\sin x}\right)=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-x\right), x<\pi$.

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \text { LHS }=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\cos x-\sin x}{\cos x+\sin x}\right) \\
&=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-\tan x}{1+\tan x}\right) \text { [dividing num. and denom. by } \cos x \text { ] } \\
&=\tan ^{-1}\left\{\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}-x\right)\right\}=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-x\right)=\text { RHS. }
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad \tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\cos x-\sin x}{\cos x+\sin x}\right)=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-x\right)$

### Example 28:

Prove that $\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\cos x}{1+\sin x}\right)=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right)$. [CBSE 2012]

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \text { LHS }=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\cos x}{1+\sin x}\right) \\
&=\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)}{1+\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)}\right\} \\
&=\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{2 \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right)}{2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right)}\right\} \\
&=\tan ^{-1}\left\{\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right)\right\}=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right)=\text { RHS. }
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad \tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\cos x}{1+\sin x}\right)=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right)$.

### Example 29:

Prove that $\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\cos x}{1-\sin x}\right)=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{x}{2}\right), x \in\left(\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right)$. [CBSE 2012]

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\mathrm{LHS}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\cos x}{1-\sin x}\right)
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)}{1-\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\right)}\right\} \\
& =\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{2 \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right)}{2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right)}\right\} \\
& =\tan ^{-1}\left\{\cot \left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right)\right\}=\tan ^{-1}\left[\tan \left\{\frac{\pi}{2}-\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{x}{2}\right)\right\}\right] \\
& =\tan ^{-1}\left\{\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{x}{2}\right)\right\} \\
& =\left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{x}{2}\right)=\text { RHS }
\end{aligned}
$$

**Hence,** $\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\cos x}{1-\sin x}\right)=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{x}{2}\right)$.

### Example 30:

Prove that $\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{3 a^{2} x-x^{3}}{a^{3}-3 a x^{2}}\right)=3 \tan ^{-1} \frac{x}{a}$.

#### Solution:

Putting $x=a \tan \theta$, we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{3 a^{2} x-x^{3}}{a^{3}-3 a x^{2}}\right) & =\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{3 a^{3} \tan \theta-a^{3} \tan ^{3} \theta}{a^{3}-3 a^{3} \tan ^{2} \theta}\right) \\
& =\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{3 \tan \theta-\tan ^{3} \theta}{1-3 \tan ^{2} \theta}\right)=\tan ^{-1}(\tan 3 \theta) \\
& =3 \theta=3 \tan ^{-1} \frac{x}{a}
\end{aligned}
$$

**Hence,** $\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{3 a^{2} x-x^{3}}{a^{3}-3 a x^{2}}\right)=3 \tan ^{-1} \frac{x}{a}$.

### Example 31:

Prove that $\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{a \cos x-b \sin x}{b \cos x-a \sin x}\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)-x$.

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\mathrm{LHS}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{a \cos x-b \sin x}{b \cos x-a \sin x}\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{\frac{a \cos x-b \sin x}{b \cos x}}{\frac{b \cos x-a \sin x}{b \cos x}}\right\}
$$

[on dividing num. and denom. by $b \cos x$]
$=\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{\frac{a}{b}-\tan x}{1-\frac{a}{b} \tan x}\right\}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{p-q}{1-p q}\right)$, where $\frac{a}{b}=p$ and $\tan x=q$
$=\tan ^{-1} p-\tan ^{-1} q=\tan ^{-1} \frac{a}{b}-\tan ^{-1}(\tan x)$
$=\left(\tan ^{-1} \frac{a}{b}-x\right)=$ RHS.

**Hence,** $\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{a \cos x-b \sin x}{b \cos x-a \sin x}\right)=\left(\tan ^{-1} \frac{a}{b}-x\right)$.

### Example 32:

Prove that $\cot ^{-1}\left\{\frac{\sqrt{1+\sin x}+\sqrt{1-\sin x}}{\sqrt{1+\sin x}-\sqrt{1-\sin x}}\right\}=\frac{x}{2}, x \in\left(0, \frac{\pi}{4}\right)$. [CBSE 2011, '14]

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =\cot ^{-1}\left\{\frac{(\sqrt{1+\sin x}+\sqrt{1-\sin x})}{(\sqrt{1+\sin x}-\sqrt{1-\sin x})} \times \frac{(\sqrt{1+\sin x}+\sqrt{1-\sin x})}{(\sqrt{1+\sin x}+\sqrt{1-\sin x})}\right\} \\
& =\cot ^{-1}\left\{\frac{(1+\sin x)+(1-\sin x)+2 \sqrt{1-\sin ^{2} x}}{(1+\sin x)-(1-\sin x)}\right\} \\
& =\cot ^{-1}\left\{\frac{2(1+\cos x)}{2 \sin x}\right\}=\cot ^{-1}\left(\frac{1+\cos x}{\sin x}\right) \\
& =\cot ^{-1}\left\{\frac{2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{2 \sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}\right\}=\cot ^{-1}\left(\cot \frac{x}{2}\right)=\frac{x}{2}=\text { RHS }
\end{aligned}
$$

**Hence,** $\mathrm{LHS}=\mathrm{RHS}$.

### Example 33:

Prove that $\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{\sqrt{1+x}-\sqrt{1-x}}{\sqrt{1+x}+\sqrt{1-x}}\right\}=\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{1}{2} \cos ^{-1} x$. [CBSE 2011, '14]

#### Solution:

Putting $x=\cos \theta$, we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{\sqrt{1+\cos \theta}-\sqrt{1-\cos \theta}}{\sqrt{1+\cos \theta}+\sqrt{1-\cos \theta}}\right\} \\
& =\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{\sqrt{2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)}-\sqrt{2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)}}{\sqrt{2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)}+\sqrt{2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)}}\right\}=\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{\cos \frac{\theta}{2}-\sin \frac{\theta}{2}}{\cos \frac{\theta}{2}+\sin \frac{\theta}{2}}\right\}
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{1-\tan \frac{\theta}{2}}{1+\tan \frac{\theta}{2}}\right\} \quad \text { [dividing num. and denom. by } \cos \frac{\theta}{2} \text { ] } \\
& =\tan ^{-1}\left\{\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{\theta}{2}\right)\right\}=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{\theta}{2}\right) \\
& =\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{1}{2} \cos ^{-1} x\right)=\text { RHS }
\end{aligned}
$$

**Hence,** LHS = RHS.

### Example 39:

Prove that $2 \tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{x^{2}+1}\right)$.

#### Solution:

**Let** $\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\theta$. Then, $\frac{1}{x}=\tan \theta \Rightarrow x=\cot \theta$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore \quad \text { LHS } & =2 \tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=2 \theta . \\
\text { RHS } & =\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 \cot \theta}{\cot ^{2} \theta+1}\right)=\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 \tan \theta}{1+\tan ^{2} \theta}\right) \\
& =\sin ^{-1}(\sin 2 \theta)=2 \theta .
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad$ **LHS** $=$ **RHS**.
**Hence,** $2 \tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{x}=\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{x^{2}+1}\right)$.

### Example 40:

Prove that $\tan \left\{\frac{1}{2} \sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1-x^{2}}\right)+\frac{1}{2} \cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-y^{2}}{1+y^{2}}\right)\right\}=\left(\frac{x+y}{1-x y}\right)$, where $|x|<1, y>0$ and $x y<1$. [CBSE 2013]

#### Solution:

Putting $x=\tan \theta$ and $y=\tan \phi$, we get

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \text { LHS }=\tan \left\{\frac{1}{2} \sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1-x^{2}}\right)+\frac{1}{2} \cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-y^{2}}{1+y^{2}}\right)\right\} \\
&=\tan \left\{\frac{1}{2} \sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 \tan \theta}{1-\tan ^{2} \theta}\right)+\frac{1}{2} \cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-\tan ^{2} \phi}{1+\tan ^{2} \phi}\right)\right\} \\
&=\tan \left\{\frac{1}{2} \sin ^{-1}(\sin 2 \theta)+\frac{1}{2} \cos ^{-1}(\cos 2 \phi)\right\} \\
&=\tan \left\{\left(\frac{1}{2} \times 2 \theta\right)+\left(\frac{1}{2} \times 2 \phi\right)\right\} \\
&=\tan (\theta+\phi)=\frac{\tan \theta+\tan \phi}{1-\tan \theta \tan \phi}=\frac{(x+y)}{(1-x y)}=\text { RHS } .
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad \tan \left\{\frac{1}{2} \sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1-x^{2}}\right)+\frac{1}{2} \cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-y^{2}}{1+y^{2}}\right)\right\}=\frac{x+y}{1-x y}$.

### Example 41:

Prove that $\cot ^{-1}\left(\frac{a b+1}{a-b}\right)+\cot ^{-1}\left(\frac{b c+1}{b-c}\right)+\cot ^{-1}\left(\frac{c a+1}{c-a}\right)=0$.

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
& \text { LHS }=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{a-b}{1+a b}\right)+\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{b-c}{1+b c}\right)+\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{c-a}{1+c a}\right) \\
&=\left(\tan ^{-1} a-\tan ^{-1} b\right)+\left(\tan ^{-1} b-\tan ^{-1} c\right)+\left(\tan ^{-1} c-\tan ^{-1} a\right) \\
&=0=\text { RHS } .
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore \quad$ **LHS** $=$ **RHS** .

## Some More Examples

### Example 1:

Prove that $\cos ^{-1} \frac{4}{5}+\cos ^{-1} \frac{12}{13}=\cos ^{-1} \frac{33}{65}$. [CBSE 2010, '12]

#### Solution:

We have

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text { LHS } & =\cos ^{-1} \frac{4}{5}+\cos ^{-1} \frac{12}{13} \\
& =\cos ^{-1}\left\{\left(\frac{4}{5} \times \frac{12}{13}\right)-\sqrt{1-\left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^{2}} \cdot \sqrt{1-\left(\frac{12}{13}\right)^{2}}\right\} \\
& =\cos ^{-1}\left\{\frac{48}{65}-\sqrt{1-\left(\frac{16}{25}\right)} \cdot \sqrt{1-\frac{144}{169}}\right\}
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\cos ^{-1}\left\{\frac{48}{65}-\sqrt{\frac{9}{25}} \cdot \sqrt{\frac{25}{169}}\right\} \\
& =\cos ^{-1}\left\{\frac{48}{65}-\frac{3}{5} \times \frac{5}{13}\right\}=\cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{48}{65}-\frac{15}{65}\right) \\
& =\cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{33}{65}\right)=\text { RHS }
\end{aligned}
$$

**Hence,** $\cos ^{-1} \frac{4}{5}+\cos ^{-1} \frac{12}{13}=\cos ^{-1} \frac{33}{65}$.

$$

**Hence,** $\sin ^{-1} \frac{8}{17}+\sin ^{-1} \frac{3}{5}=\cos ^{-1} \frac{36}{85}$.

### Example 4:

Prove that $2 \tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{5}+\sec ^{-1} \frac{5 \sqrt{2}}{7}+2 \tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{8}=\frac{\pi}{4}$. [CBSE 2014]

#### Solution:

**Let** $\sec ^{-1} \frac{5 \sqrt{2}}{7}=\theta$. Then, $\sec \theta=\frac{5 \sqrt{2}}{7}$. So, $\cos \theta=\frac{7}{5 \sqrt{2}}$.
Draw a right triangle with base $=7$ units
and hypotenuse $=5 \sqrt{2}$ units.
$\therefore \quad$ perpendicular $=\sqrt{(5 \sqrt{2})^{2}-7^{2}}=\sqrt{50-49}=1$ unit.
$\therefore \quad \tan \theta=\frac{1}{7} \Rightarrow \theta=\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{7}$.
$\therefore \quad$ **LHS** $=2\left(\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{5}+\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{8}\right)+\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{7}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =2 \cdot \tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{\left(\frac{1}{5}+\frac{1}{8}\right)}{\left(1-\frac{1}{5} \times \frac{1}{8}\right)}\right\}+\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{7} \\
& =2 \tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{13}{40} \times \frac{40}{30}\right)+\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{7}=2 \tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{3}+\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{7} \\
& =\tan ^{-1}\left\{\frac{2 \times \frac{1}{3}}{1-\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{2}}\right\}+\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{7}=\tan ^{-1} \frac{3}{4}+\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{7} \\
& =\tan ^{-1} \frac{\left(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{7}\right)}{\left(1-\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{1}{7}\right)}=\tan ^{-1} \frac{25}{25}=\tan ^{-1} 1 \\
& =\frac{\pi}{4}=\text { RHS. }
\end{aligned}
$$

**Hence,** $\mathrm{LHS}=\mathrm{RHS}$.

### Example 6:

Prove that $\cos \left[\tan ^{-1}\left\{\sin \left(\cot ^{-1} x\right)\right\}\right]=\sqrt{\frac{x^{2}+1}{x^{2}+2}}$. [CBSE 2010]

#### Solution:

**Let** $\cot ^{-1} x=\theta$. Then, $x=\cot \theta$.
$\therefore \quad \operatorname{cosec} \theta=\sqrt{1+\cot ^{2} \theta}=\sqrt{1+x^{2}}$
$\Rightarrow \sin \theta=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+x^{2}}}$
$\Rightarrow \sin \left(\cot ^{-1} x\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+x^{2}}}\left[\because \theta=\cot ^{-1} x\right]$
$\Rightarrow \tan ^{-1}\left\{\sin \left(\cot ^{-1} x\right)\right\}=\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+x^{2}}}=\phi$ (say)
$\Rightarrow \cos \left[\tan ^{-1}\left\{\sin \left(\cot ^{-1} x\right\}\right]=\cos \phi\right$. (i)

Now, $\tan ^{-1} \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+x^{2}}}=\phi \Rightarrow \tan \phi=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+x^{2}}}$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\Rightarrow \sec \phi=\sqrt{1+\tan ^{2} \phi}=\sqrt{1+\frac{1}{\left(1+x^{2}\right)}}=\sqrt{\frac{2+x^{2}}{1+x^{2}}} \tag{ii}
\end{equation*}
$$

$\Rightarrow \cos \phi=\sqrt{\frac{1+x^{2}}{2+x^{2}}}$.

From (i) and (ii), we get

$$

#### Solution:

$$
\begin{aligned}

Solve for $x: 2 \tan ^{-1}(\cos x)=\tan ^{-1}(2 \operatorname{cosec} x)$. [CBSE 2009, '10C]

$$
\begin{aligned}
