### Answer to Question 03

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-1/Chapter-04/Section-A/Q-03

- Answer:

-1

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{-\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)=\theta \Rightarrow \cos \theta=\frac{-\sqrt{3}}{2}=-\cos \frac{\pi}{6}=\cos \left(\pi-\frac{\pi}{6}\right)=\cos \frac{5 \pi}{6} \Rightarrow \theta=\frac{5 \pi}{6}$.
> 
> $\therefore$ given expression $=\cos \left(\frac{5 \pi}{6}+\frac{\pi}{6}\right)=\cos \pi=-1$.
>
