### Answer to Question 03

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-I/Q-03

- Answer:

$\vec{r} \cdot(10 \hat{i}+5 \hat{j}-4 \hat{k})=37,10 x+5 y-4 z=37$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> The given lines are $\vec{r}=\overrightarrow{a_{1}}+\lambda \overrightarrow{b_{1}}$ and $\vec{r}=\overrightarrow{a_{2}}+\lambda \overrightarrow{b_{2}}$, where $\overrightarrow{a_{1}}=(2 \hat{i}+\hat{j}-3 \hat{k}), \overrightarrow{a_{2}}=(3 \hat{i}+3 \hat{j}+2 \hat{k})$, $\overrightarrow{b_{1}}=(\hat{i}+2 \hat{j}+5 \hat{k}), \overrightarrow{b_{2}}=(3 \hat{i}-2 \hat{j}+5 \hat{k})$.
>
> $$\begin{aligned} \therefore \quad\left(\overrightarrow{b_{1}} \times \overrightarrow{b_{2}}\right) & =\left|\begin{array}{ccc} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 3 & -2 & 5 \end{array}\right|=(10+10) \hat{i}-(5-15) \hat{j}+(-2-6) \hat{k} \\ & =(20 \hat{i}+10 \hat{j}-8 \hat{k}). \end{aligned}$$
>
> Vector equation of the required plane is
>
> $$\begin{aligned} & \vec{r} \cdot\left(\overrightarrow{b_{1}} \times \overrightarrow{b_{2}}\right)=\overrightarrow{a_{1}} \cdot\left(\overrightarrow{b_{1}} \times \overrightarrow{b_{2}}\right) \\ \Rightarrow & \vec{r} \cdot(20 \hat{i}+10 \hat{j}-8 \hat{k})=(2 \hat{i}+\hat{j}-3 \hat{k}) \cdot(20 \hat{i}+10 \hat{j}-8 \hat{k}) \\ & =(40+10+24)=74 \\ \Rightarrow & \vec{r} \cdot(10 \hat{i}+5 \hat{j}-4 \hat{k})=37. \end{aligned}$$
>
> The Cartesian equation is
>
> $$(x \hat{i}+y \hat{j}+z \hat{k}) \cdot(10 \hat{i}+5 \hat{j}-4 \hat{k})=37 \Rightarrow 10 x+5 y-4 z=37.$$
>
> The third line is $\vec{r}=(2 \hat{i}+5 \hat{j}+2 \hat{k})+p(3 \hat{i}-2 \hat{j}+5 \hat{k})$.
>
> Now, the line (iii) will lie in the plane (ii) if $(2,5,2)$ lies on (ii) and $(3 \hat{i}-2 \hat{j}+5 \hat{k})$ is perpendicular to the normal of (ii).
>
> Now, $10 \times 2+5 \times 5-4 \times 2=37$ shows that $(2,5,2)$ lies on (ii).
>
> Also $10 \times 3+5 \times(-2)-4 \times 5=0$ shows that $(3 \hat{i}-2 \hat{j}+5 \hat{k})$ is perpendicular to the normal of (ii).
>
> Hence, the line (iii) lies in plane (ii).
>
