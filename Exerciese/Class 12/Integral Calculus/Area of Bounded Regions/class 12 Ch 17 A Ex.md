### Answer to Question 08

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-17/Section-A/Q-08

- Answer:

$\frac{3 \pi}{2}$ sq units


> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> The given ellipse meets the $x$-axis at $A(-2,0)$ and $B(2,0)$.
> $\frac{x^{2}}{4}+\frac{y^{2}}{9}=1 \Rightarrow y=\frac{3}{2} \cdot \sqrt{4-x^{2}}$.
> $\therefore$ required area $=\int_{-2}^{2} y d x=\frac{3}{2} \cdot \int_{-2}^{2} \sqrt{4-x^{2}} d x$
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & =2 \times \frac{3}{2} \times \int_{0}^{2} \sqrt{4-x^{2}} d x \\
> & =6 \cdot \int_{0}^{\pi / 2} \cos ^{2} \theta d \theta, \text{ where } x=2 \sin \theta \\
> & =3 \cdot \int_{0}^{\pi / 2}(1+\cos 2 \theta) d \theta
> \end{aligned}
> $$
