### Answer to Question 13

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-4/Chapter-10/Section-C/Q-13

- Answer:

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $A B C D$ be a rhombus and $P, Q, R, S$ be the midpoints of $A B, B C, C D$ and $D A$ respectively. Then, $P Q R S$ is a $\| \mathrm{gm}$ [prove it].
>
> Diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at right angles.
>
> $\therefore \angle E O F=90^{\circ}$.
>
> Now, $R Q\|D B \Rightarrow R E\| F O, S R\|A C \Rightarrow F R\| O E$.
>
> $\therefore \quad O E R F$ is a $\| \mathrm{gm}$. So, $\angle F R E=\angle E O F=90^{\circ}$.
>
> ![](https://cdn.mathpix.com/cropped/2025_09_25_2884ef595d718654ac8bg-43.jpg?height=267&width=293&top_left_y=1357&top_left_x=904)
>
> Thus, $P Q R S$ is a $\| \mathrm{gm}$ with $\angle R=90^{\circ}$.
>
> Hence, $P Q R S$ is a rectangle.

### Answer to Question 17

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-4/Chapter-10/Section-C/Q-17

- Answer:

> [!INFO]
>
> $P, Q, R$ and $S$ are midpoints of sides $A B, B C, C D$ and $D A$ respectively.
>
> $\therefore \quad P S \| B D$ and $P Q \| A C$
>
> $\Rightarrow \quad P E \| F O$ and $P F \| E O$, i.e., $E P F O$ is a $\| \mathrm{gm}$.

>
> Now, $P Q R S$ is a $\| \mathrm{gm}$ with $\angle S P Q=90^{\circ}$.
>
> $\therefore \quad P Q R S$ is a rectangle.
