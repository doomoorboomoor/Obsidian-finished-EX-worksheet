### Answer to Question 01

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-4/Chapter-12/Section-Q/Q-01

- Answer:
(b)

> [!INFO]
> Let $O$ be the centre of a circle with radius $O A=13 \mathrm{~cm}$.
> 
> Let $A B$ be the chord of length 10 cm. Draw $O L \perp A B$.
> Then, $L$ is the midpoint of $A B$. Thus, $A L=5 \mathrm{~cm}$.
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & O L^{2}=O A^{2}-A L^{2}=(13)^{2}-(5)^{2}=169-25=144 \\
> \Rightarrow \quad & O L=\sqrt{144}=12 \mathrm{~cm}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Distance of the chord from the centre $=12 \mathrm{~cm}$.
> 
> ![](file:///C:/Obsidian-finished-EX-worksheet_resources/Exerciese/Class%2009/Geometry/Circles/class%2009%20Ch%2012%20Q%20Ex-012.jpg)
