### Answer to Question 08

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-4/Chapter-07/Section-B/Q-08

- Answer:

$x=18, \angle A O D=36^{\circ}, \angle C O E=90^{\circ}, \angle A O E=54^{\circ}$

> [!INFO]
>
> $\angle D O F=\angle E O C=(5 x)^{\circ} \quad[$ vertically opposite $\angle s]$.
> 
> Now, $E O F$ is a line. Sum of all angles above it is $180^{\circ}$.
> $3 x+5 x+2 x=180 \Rightarrow 10 x=180 \Rightarrow x=18$.
> $\therefore \quad \angle B O F=(3 \times 18)^{\circ}=54^{\circ}, \angle D O F=\angle E O C=(5 \times 18)^{\circ}=90^{\circ}, \angle A O D=(2 \times 18)^{\circ}=36^{\circ}$. $\angle A O E=\angle B O F=54^{\circ}, \angle C O E=\angle D O F=90^{\circ}$.

### Answer to Question 12

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-4/Chapter-07/Section-B/Q-12

- Answer:

$\angle A O C=75^{\circ}=\angle B O D, \angle A O D=105^{\circ}=\angle B O C$

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $A O C=(5 x)^{\circ}$ and $\angle A O D=(7 x)^{\circ}$.
> $\angle A O C+\angle A O D=180^{\circ} \Rightarrow 5 x+7 x=180 \Rightarrow 12 x=180 \Rightarrow x=15$.
> $\therefore \quad \angle A O C=(5 \times 15)^{\circ}=75^{\circ}$ and $\angle A O D=(7 \times 15)^{\circ}=105^{\circ}$.
> $\angle B O D=\angle A O C=75^{\circ} \quad$ [vertically opposite $\left.\angle\right]$ ].
> $\angle B O C=\angle A O D=105^{\circ}$ [vertically opposite $\angle s$ ].
