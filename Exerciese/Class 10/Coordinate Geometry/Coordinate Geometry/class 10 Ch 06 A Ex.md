### Answer to Question 16

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-3/Chapter-06/Section-A/Q-16

- Answer:

$(k=-3 \Rightarrow AP=\sqrt{41}),(k=-1 \Rightarrow AP=5)$

> [!INFO]
>
> $PA=PB \Rightarrow PA^{2}=PB^{2}$
>
> $$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow(2+2)^{2}+(2-k)^{2}=(2+2k)^{2}+(2+3)^{2} \\
& \Rightarrow(2-k)^{2}-(2+2k)^{2}=25-16=9 \\
& \Rightarrow\left(4+k^{2}-4k\right)-\left(4+4k^{2}+8k\right)=9 \\
& \Rightarrow 3k^{2}+12k+9=0 \\
& \Rightarrow k^{2}+4k+3=0 \Rightarrow(k+3)(k+1)=0 \\
& \Rightarrow k=-3 \text { or } k=-1.
\end{aligned}
$$
>
> Case 1. When $k=-3$, we have
>
> $$
AP=\text{ distance between } A(-2,-3) \text{ and } P(2,2)=\sqrt{41} \text{ units.}
$$
>
> Case 2. When $k=-1$, we have
>
> $$
AP=\text{ distance between } A(-2,-1) \text{ and } P(2,2)=\sqrt{25}=5 \text{ units.}
$$
