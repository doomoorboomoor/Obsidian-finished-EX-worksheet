### Answer to Question 14

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-6/Chapter-17/Section-C/Q-14

- Answer:

$1068.57 \mathrm{~m}^{2}$

> [!INFO]
>
> $R=10 \mathrm{~m}, r=3 \mathrm{~m}$ and $h=24 \mathrm{~m}$.
> 
> Let $l$ be the slant height of the frustum. Then,
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> l & =\sqrt{h^{2}+(R-r)^{2}}=\sqrt{(24)^{2}+7^{2}} \mathrm{~m} \\
> & =\sqrt{576+49} \mathrm{~m}=\sqrt{625} \mathrm{~m}=25 \mathrm{~m}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Let $L$ be the slant height of the conical part.
> 
> For this conical part, $r=3 \mathrm{~m}$ and $H=4 \mathrm{~m}$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad L=\sqrt{3^{2}+4^{2}} \mathrm{~m}=\sqrt{25} \mathrm{~m}=5 \mathrm{~m}$.
> 
> Quantity of canvas
> 
> = (lateral surface area of the frustum)
> 
> + (lateral surface area of the cone)
> 
> $=\{\pi l(R+r)+\pi r L\} \mathrm{m}^{2}=\frac{22}{7} \times\{(25 \times 13)+(3 \times 5)\} \mathrm{m}^{2}=1068.57 \mathrm{~m}^{2}$.
>
