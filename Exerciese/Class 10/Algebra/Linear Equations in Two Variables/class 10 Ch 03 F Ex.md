### Answer to Question 02

- [ ] InBox/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-2/Chapter-03/Section-F/Q-02

- Answer:

$k=7$

> [!INFO]
>
> $2 x+3 y-7=0$ and $(k-1) x+(k+2) y-3 k=0$.
>
> For infinitely many solutions, we have $\frac{a_{1}}{a_{2}}=\frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}}=\frac{c_{1}}{c_{2}}$.
>
> $\therefore \quad \frac{2}{(k-1)}=\frac{3}{(k+2)}$ and $\frac{3}{(k+2)}=\frac{7}{3 k}$
>
> $\Rightarrow \quad 2 k+4=3 k-3$ and $9 k=7 k+14 \Rightarrow k=7$.
