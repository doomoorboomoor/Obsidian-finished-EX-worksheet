### Answer to Question 01

- [ ] InBox/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-2/Chapter-02/Section-C/Q-01

- Answer:

$(2-\sqrt{3})$

> [!INFO]
>
> Let the other zero be $\alpha$.
>
> Then, sum of zeros $=\frac{-(\text{coefficient of } x)}{\left(\text{coefficient of } x^{2}\right)}=\frac{-(-4)}{1}=4$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad (2+\sqrt{3})+\alpha=4 \Rightarrow \alpha=(2-\sqrt{3})$.

- [ ] InBox/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-2/Chapter-02/Section-C/Q-02

- Answer:

$-(p+1)$ and $p$

> [!INFO]
>
> $x^{2}+x-p(p+1)=x^{2}+(p+1) x-p x-p(p+1)$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & =x\{x+(p+1)\}-p\{x+(p+1)\} \\

> \end{aligned}

> 
> So, its zeros are $-(p+1)$ and $p$.

### Answer to Question 25

- [ ] InBox/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-2/Chapter-02/Section-C/Q-25

- Answer:

$a=1$ and $b= \pm \sqrt{2}$
