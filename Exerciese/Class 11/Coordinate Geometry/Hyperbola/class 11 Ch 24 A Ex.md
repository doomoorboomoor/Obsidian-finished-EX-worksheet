### Answer to Question 10

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-24/Section-A/Q-10

- Answer:

(i) $\frac{12}{\sqrt{5}}$ units, 4 units
(ii) $A\left(0,-\frac{6}{\sqrt{5}}\right), B\left(0, \frac{6}{\sqrt{5}}\right)$
(iii) $F_{1}\left(0,-2 \sqrt{\frac{14}{5}}\right), F_{2}\left(0,2 \sqrt{\frac{14}{5}}\right)$
(iv) $e=\frac{\sqrt{14}}{3}$
(v) $\frac{4 \sqrt{5}}{3}$ units

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Given equation is $\frac{y^{2}}{(36 / 5)}-\frac{x^{2}}{4}=1$.
> $\therefore \quad a^{2}=\frac{36}{5}, b^{2}=4$ and $c^{2}=\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)=\left(\frac{36}{5}+4\right)=\frac{56}{5}$.
> Also, $e=\frac{c}{a}=\left(\frac{\sqrt{56}}{\sqrt{5}} \times \frac{\sqrt{5}}{6}\right)=\frac{2 \sqrt{14}}{6}=\frac{\sqrt{14}}{3}$.
>

### Answer to Question 18

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-24/Section-A/Q-18

- Answer:

$16 x^{2}-2 y^{2}=1$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $e=3 \Leftrightarrow \frac{c}{a}=3 \Leftrightarrow c=3 a \Leftrightarrow c^{2}=9 a^{2} \Leftrightarrow a^{2}+b^{2}=9 a^{2} \Leftrightarrow 8 a^{2}=b^{2}$.
> $$
> \begin{align*}
> & \frac{2 b^{2}}{a}=4 \Leftrightarrow b^{2}=2 a .  \tag{ii}\\
> \therefore & 8 a^{2}=2 a \Leftrightarrow 2 a(4 a-1)=0 \Leftrightarrow a=\frac{1}{4} . \\
> \therefore & a^{2}=\frac{1}{16} \text { and } b^{2}=\left(2 \times \frac{1}{4}\right)=\frac{1}{2} .
> \end{align*}
> $$
>

### Answer to Question 19

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-24/Section-A/Q-19

> [!INFO] Hint
>

> Now, $e=\frac{c}{a} \Rightarrow a=\frac{c}{e}=\frac{8}{\sqrt{2}}=4 \sqrt{2}$.

> Thus, $a^{2}=32$ and $b^{2}=32$.
