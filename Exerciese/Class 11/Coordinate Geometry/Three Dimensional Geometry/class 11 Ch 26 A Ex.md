### Answer to Question 05



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-26/Section-A/Q-05



- Answer:



PROOF Let $O$ be the origin, and let $P\left(x_{1}, y_{1}, z_{1}\right)$ and $Q\left(x_{2}, y_{2}, z_{2}\right)$ be the given point. From $P$ and $Q$, draw perpendiculars $P M$ and $Q N$ respectively on the $x y$-plane.

Then, the coordinates of $M$ and $N$ are

$$
M\left(x_{1}, y_{1}, 0\right) \text { and } N\left(x_{2}, y_{2}, 0\right)
$$

The two-dimensional coordinates of $M$ and $N$ referred to $O X$ and $O Y$ are

$$
\begin{gathered}
M\left(x_{1}, y_{1}\right) \text { and } N\left(x_{2} y_{2}\right) . \\
\therefore \quad M N^{2}=\left(x_{2}-x_{1}\right)^{2}+\left(y_{2}-y_{1}\right)^{2} .
\end{gathered}
$$

Now, from $P$ draw $P R \perp Q N$.

Then, $P R$ is parallel and equal to $M N$.

Now, in right triangle $P R Q$, we have

$$
\begin{aligned}
P Q^{2} & =P R^{2}+R Q^{2} \\
& =M N^{2}+(Q N-R N)^{2} \\
& =M N^{2}+(Q N-P M)^{2} \\
& =\left(x_{2}-x_{1}\right)^{2}+\left(y_{2}-y_{1}\right)^{2}+\left(z_{2}-z_{1}\right)^{2} \quad\left[\because P M=z_{1} \text { and } Q N=z_{2}\right] \\
\therefore P Q & =\sqrt{\left(x_{2}-x_{1}\right)^{2}+\left(y_{2}-y_{1}\right)^{2}+\left(z_{2}-z_{1}\right)^{2}}
\end{aligned}
$$

corollary The distance of the point $P(x, y, z)$ from the origin $O(0,0,0)$ is

$$
O P=\sqrt{(x-0)^{2}+(y-0)^{2}+(z-0)^{2}}=\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}
$$

### Answer to Question 09

SOLUTION Let $P(x, y, z)$ be any point on the given curve, and let $A(3,4,-5)$ and $B(-2,1,4)$ be the given points.

Then, $P A=P B$

$\Rightarrow P A^{2}=P B^{2}$

$\Rightarrow(x-3)^{2}+(y-4)^{2}+(z+5)^{2}=(x+2)^{2}+(y-1)^{2}+(z-4)^{2}$

$\Rightarrow 10 x+6 y-18 z-29=0$.

Hence, the required curve is $10 x+6 y-18 z-29=0$.

### Answer to Question 10



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-26/Section-A/Q-10



- Answer:



SOLUTION Let $P(x, y, z)$ be an arbitrary point on the given curve. Then,

$$
\begin{align*}
& P A+P B=10 \\
\Rightarrow & \sqrt{(x-4)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}+\sqrt{(x+4)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}=10 \\
\Rightarrow & \sqrt{(x+4)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}=10-\sqrt{(x-4)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}  \tag{i}\\
\Rightarrow & (x+4)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}=100+(x-4)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}-20 \sqrt{(x-4)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}
\end{align*}
$$

[on squaring both sides of (i)]

$\Rightarrow \quad 16 x=100-20 \sqrt{(x-4)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}$

$\Rightarrow 5 \sqrt{(x-4)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}=(25-4 x)$

$\Rightarrow 25\left[(x-4)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}\right]=625+16 x^{2}-200 x$

$\Rightarrow 9 x^{2}+25 y^{2}+25 z^{2}-225=0$.

Hence, the required equation of the curve is

$$
9 x^{2}+25 y^{2}+25 z^{2}-225=0
$$
