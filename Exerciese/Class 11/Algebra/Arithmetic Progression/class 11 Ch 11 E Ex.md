### Answer to Question 06


- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-11/Section-E/Q-06


- Answer:






> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{a(b+c)}{b c}, \frac{b(c+a)}{c a}, \frac{c(a+b)}{a b}$ will be in AP
> 
> $$
> \text { if } \frac{a(b+c)}{b c}+1, \frac{b(c+a)}{c a}+1, \frac{c(a+b)}{a b}+1 \text { are in } \mathrm{AP}
> $$
> 
> i.e., if $\frac{a b+b c+c a}{b c}, \frac{a b+b c+c a}{c a}, \frac{a b+b c+c a}{a b}$ are in AP
> i.e., if $\frac{1}{b c}, \frac{1}{c a}, \frac{1}{a b}$ are in AP
> i.e., if $\frac{a b c}{b c}, \frac{a b c}{c a}, \frac{a b c}{a b}$ are in AP
> i.e., if $a, b, c$ are in AP .
>
