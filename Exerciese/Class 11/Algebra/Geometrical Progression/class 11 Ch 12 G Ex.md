$\left(8+\frac{24}{5}+\frac{72}{25}+\ldots \infty\right)$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $a$ be the first term and $r$ be the common ratio of the given series.
> Then, $\frac{a}{(1-r)}=20 \Rightarrow \frac{a^{2}}{(1-r)^{2}}=400$.

### Answer to Question 13

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-12/Section-G/Q-13

- Answer:

$19, \frac{38}{3}, \frac{76}{9}, \frac{152}{27}, \ldots \infty$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $a$ be the first term and $r$ be the common ratio of the given GP.
> Then, $\frac{a}{1-r}=57 \Rightarrow \frac{a^{3}}{(1-r)^{3}}=(57)^{3}$.
> And, $a^{3}+a^{3} r^{3}+a^{3} r^{6}+\ldots \infty=9747$
> $\Rightarrow \quad \frac{a^{3}}{\left(1-r^{3}\right)}=9747$.
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \therefore \quad \frac{a^{3}}{(1-r)^{3}} \times \frac{\left(1-r^{3}\right)}{a^{3}}=\frac{(57)^{3}}{9747} & \Rightarrow \frac{\left(1-r^{3}\right)}{(1-r)^{3}}=19 \Rightarrow \frac{1+r+r^{2}}{(1-r)^{2}}=19  \tag{ii}\\
> & \Rightarrow 1+r+r^{2}=19 r^{2}-38 r+19 \\
> & \Rightarrow 18 r^{2}-39 r+18=0 \\
> & \Rightarrow 18 r^{2}-27 r-12 r+18=0 \\
> & \Rightarrow(3 r-2)(6 r-9)=0 \Rightarrow r=\frac{2}{3} \\
> & {\left[\because r \neq \frac{3}{2}, \text { as }-1<r<1 \text { for infinite GP }\right] }
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> $\therefore \quad \frac{a}{\left(1-\frac{2}{3}\right)}=57 \Rightarrow a=\left(57 \times \frac{1}{3}\right)=19$.
> Hence, the required GP is $19, \frac{38}{3}, \frac{76}{9}, \frac{152}{27}, \ldots \infty$.
