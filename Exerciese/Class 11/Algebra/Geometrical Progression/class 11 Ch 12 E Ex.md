### Answer to Question 01

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-12/Section-E/Q-01

- Answer:



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> We have, $2 q=p+r$.
> Let $T_{p}, T_{q}, T_{r}$ be the given terms of a GP with first term $=A$ and common ratio $=R$. Then,
> 
> $$
\left(T_{q}\right)^{2}=\left\{A R^{(q-1)}\right\}^{2}=A^{2} R^{(2 q-2)}
$$
> 
> and $\left(T_{p} \times T_{r}\right)=\left\{A R^{(p-1)} \times A R^{(r-1)}\right\}=A^{2} R^{(p+r-2)}=A^{2} R^{(2 q-2)}$.
> $\therefore \quad\left(T_{q}\right)^{2}=\left(T_{p} \times T_{r}\right)$ and hence $T_{p}, T_{q}, T_{r}$ are in GP.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 08

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-12/Section-E/Q-08

- Answer:



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $r$ be the common ratio of the given GP. Then,
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
& b=a r \text{ and } c=a r^{2} . \\
\therefore \quad & \text{LHS }=\frac{a^{2}+a^{2} r+a^{2} r^{2}}{a^{2} r+a^{2} r^{3}+a^{2} r^{2}}=\frac{a^{2}\left(1+r+r^{2}\right)}{a^{2} r\left(1+r+r^{2}\right)}=\frac{1}{r} \\
& \text{RHS }=\frac{a r+a}{a r^{2}+a r}=\frac{a(1+r)}{a r(1+r)}=\frac{1}{r} .

> Hence, $\text{LHS}=\text{RHS}$.

### Answer to Question 12

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-12/Section-E/Q-12

- Answer:



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $b=a r$ and $c=a r^{2}$. Then,
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
& \frac{1}{a+b}=\frac{1}{a+a r}=\frac{1}{a(1+r)}, \frac{1}{2 b}=\frac{1}{2 a r}, \text{ and } \frac{1}{(b+c)}=\frac{1}{a r(1+r)} . \\
\therefore \quad & \frac{1}{(a+b)}+\frac{1}{(b+c)}=\frac{1}{a(1+r)}+\frac{1}{a r(1+r)}=\frac{(1+r)}{a r(1+r)}=\frac{1}{a r}=2 \times\left(\frac{1}{2 b}\right) .
\end{aligned}
$$
> 
> Hence, $\frac{1}{(a+b)}, \frac{1}{2 b}, \frac{1}{(b+c)}$ are in AP.
