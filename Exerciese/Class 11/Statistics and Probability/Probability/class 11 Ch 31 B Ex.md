> $$
> \begin{aligned}

> & \Rightarrow \frac{13}{4} \cdot P(A)=1 \Rightarrow P(A)=\frac{4}{13}
> \end{aligned}
> $$

### Answer to Question 16

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-6/Chapter-31/Section-B/Q-16

- Answer:

$\frac{4}{9}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Clearly, $n(S)=(6 \times 6)=36$.
> Let $E_{1}=$ event that the sum of numbers on two faces is divisible by 3 , and $E_{2}=$ event that the sum of numbers on two faces is divisible by 4 .
> Then, $E_{1} \cap E_{2}=$ event that the sum of the numbers on two faces is divisible by both 3 and 4 , i.e., by 12 .
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \therefore \quad E_{1}=\{(1,2),(1,5),(2,1),(2,4),(3,3),(3,6),(4,2),(4,5),(5,1),(5,4),(6,3),(6,6)\}, \\
> & \quad E_{2}=\{(1,3),(2,2),(2,6),(3,1),(3,5),(4,4),(5,3),(6,2),(6,6)\} . \\
> & \begin{aligned}
> \therefore \quad E_{1} \cap E_{2}=\{(6,6)\} . \\
> \text{Thus, } n\left(E_{1}\right)=12, n\left(E_{2}\right)=9 \text{ and } n\left(E_{1} \cap E_{2}\right)=1 . \\
> \text{Required probability } &=P\left[\left(\operatorname{not} E_{1}\right) \text{ and }\left(\operatorname{not} E_{2}\right)\right]=P\left(\bar{E}_{1} \cap \bar{E}_{2}\right) \\
> \quad &=P\left(\overline{E_{1} \cup E_{2}}\right)=1-P\left(E_{1} \cup E_{2}\right) \\
> \quad &=1-\left\{P\left(E_{1}\right)+P\left(E_{2}\right)-P\left(E_{1} \cap E_{2}\right)\right\} .
> \end{aligned}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
>
