### Answer to Question 01

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-19/Section-B/Q-01

- Answer:

55.68 km

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let the original position of each boat be at $O$. Let $A$ and $B$ be their respective final positions, as shown in the figure.
> 
> Then, $\angle N O A=50^{\circ}, \angle S O B=70^{\circ}, O A=60 \mathrm{~km}$ and $O B=50 \mathrm{~km}$
> 
> $\therefore \angle A O B=180^{\circ}-\left(50^{\circ}+70^{\circ}\right)=60^{\circ}$. Let $A B=x \mathrm{~km}$.
> 
> By cosine formula on $\triangle A O B$, we have
> 
> $$
> \cos \angle A O B=\frac{O A^{2}+O B^{2}-A B^{2}}{2 \times O A \times O B}
> $$
> 
> $\Rightarrow \quad \cos 60^{\circ}=\frac{(60)^{2}+(50)^{2}-x^{2}}{2 \times 60 \times 50}$
> 
> $\Rightarrow 3600+2500-x^{2}=\frac{1}{2} \times 2 \times 60 \times 50 \Rightarrow x^{2}=3100 \Rightarrow x=\sqrt{3100}=55.68 \mathrm{~km}$.
> 
> Hence, the required distance between the boats is 55.68 km .
>

### Answer to Question 02

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-19/Section-B/Q-02

- Answer:

21.63 km

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $B$ be the position of the town relative to $A$, as shown in the figure.
> 
> From $B$, draw $B C \perp A S$.
> 
> Then, $A C=12 \mathrm{~km}$ and $B C=18 \mathrm{~km}$.
> 
> Let $\angle B A C=\theta$. Then,
> 
> $$
> \tan \theta=\frac{B C}{A C}=\frac{18}{12}=\frac{3}{2} \Rightarrow \theta=56^{\circ} 20^{\prime} .
> $$
> 
> $\therefore \quad$ bearing of $B$ from $A$ is $\mathrm{S} 56^{\circ} 20^{\prime} \mathrm{W}$.
> 
> Also, $A B=\sqrt{B C^{2}+A C^{2}}=\sqrt{(18)^{2}+(12)^{2}}=\sqrt{324+144}$
> 
> $$
> =\sqrt{468}=21.63 \mathrm{~km} .
> $$
> 
> Hence, the distance of $B$ from $A$ is 21.63 km .
>

### Answer to Question 03

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-19/Section-B/Q-03

- Answer:

1.365 km

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{S D}{F D}=\tan 45^{\circ}=1 \Rightarrow S D=F D=h \mathrm{~km}$
> 
> $$
> \Rightarrow \angle F S D=\angle S F D=45^{\circ} .
> $$
> 
> $$
> \angle A S C=180^{\circ}-\left(60^{\circ}+90^{\circ}\right)=30^{\circ}
> $$
> 
> $\Rightarrow \angle F S A=\left(45^{\circ}-30^{\circ}\right)=15^{\circ}$ and $\angle S F A=15^{\circ}$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad \angle S A F=180^{\circ}-\left(15^{\circ}+15^{\circ}\right)=150^{\circ}$.
> 
> By the sine formula on $\triangle S A F$, we have
> 
> $$
> \frac{A F}{\sin \angle F S A}=\frac{F S}{\sin \angle S A F} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{\sin 15^{\circ}}=\frac{\sqrt{2} h}{\sin 150^{\circ}}=\frac{\sqrt{2} h}{\sin 30^{\circ}} \Rightarrow \frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{(\sqrt{3}-1)}=2 \sqrt{2} h
> $$
> 
> $$
> \left[\because F S=\sqrt{h^{2}+h^{2}}=\sqrt{2} h \text{ and } \sin 15^{\circ}=\frac{(\sqrt{3}-1)}{2 \sqrt{2}} .\right]
> $$
> 
> $\Rightarrow h=\frac{1}{(\sqrt{3}-1)} \times \frac{(\sqrt{3}+1)}{(\sqrt{3}+1)}=\frac{(\sqrt{3}+1)}{2}=\frac{(1.73+1)}{2}=\frac{2.73}{2}=1.365 \mathrm{~km}$. $\square$
