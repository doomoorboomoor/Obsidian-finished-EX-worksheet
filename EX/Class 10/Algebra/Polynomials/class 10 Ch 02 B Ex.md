### Answer to Question 19

- [ ] InBox/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-2/Chapter-02/Section-A/Q-19

- Answer:

$a=3, b=-6$

> [!INFO]
>
> Sum of the roots $=\left(\frac{2}{3}-3\right)=\frac{-7}{3}$.
> 
> Product of roots $=\frac{2}{3} \times(-3)=-2$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad\left\{\frac{-7}{a}=\frac{-7}{3}\right.$ and $\left.\frac{b}{a}=-2\right\} \Rightarrow a=3$ and $b=-6$.

---

### Answer to Question 20

- [ ] InBox/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-2/Chapter-02/Section-A/Q-20

- Answer:

$a=2$

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $f(x)=2 x^{2}+2 a x+5 x+10$.
> 
> Since $(x+a)$ is a factor of $f(x)$, we have $f(-a)=0$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad 2(-a)^{2}+2 a(-a)+5(-a)+10=0$
> 
> $\Rightarrow \quad 2 a^{2}-2 a^{2}-5 a+10=0 \Rightarrow 5 a=10 \Rightarrow a=2$.
