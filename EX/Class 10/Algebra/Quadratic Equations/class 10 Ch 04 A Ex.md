### Answer to Question 69

- [ ] InBox/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-2/Chapter-04/Section-A/Q-69

- Answer:

$x=\frac{(a+b)}{a b}$ or $x=\frac{2}{(a+b)}$

> [!INFO]
>
> The given equation is
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \left(\frac{a}{x-b}-1\right)+\left(\frac{b}{x-a}-1\right) & =0 \\
> \Rightarrow \quad \frac{(a-x+b)}{(x-b)}+\frac{(a-x+b)}{(x-a)} & =0
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \Rightarrow \quad(a-x+b) \cdot\left[\frac{1}{(x-b)}+\frac{1}{(x-a)}\right]=0 \\
> & \Rightarrow \quad(a-x+b)\left[\frac{2 x-(a+b)}{(x-a)(x-b)}\right]=0 \\
> & \Rightarrow \quad(a-x+b)[2 x-(a+b)]=0 \\
> & \Rightarrow \quad x=(a+b) \quad \text { or } \quad x=\frac{(a+b)}{2}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
