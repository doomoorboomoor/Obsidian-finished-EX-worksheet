### Answer to Question 74/iii

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-6/Chapter-17/Section-Q/Q-74/Q-iii

- Answer:

(p)

> [!INFO]
>
> Volume of the sphere $=\frac{4}{3} \pi r^{3}$.
> Volume of the cylinder $=\pi R^{2} h$.
> $\therefore \quad \frac{4}{3} \pi r^{3}=\pi R^{2} h \Rightarrow \frac{4}{3} r^{3}=R^{2} h$
> $\therefore \frac{4}{3} \times 6 \times 6 \times 6=4 \times 4 \times h \Rightarrow h=\frac{8 \times 6 \times 6}{4 \times 4}=18 \mathrm{~cm}$.

### Answer to Question 74/iv

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-6/Chapter-17/Section-Q/Q-74/Q-iv

- Answer:

(r)

> [!INFO]
>
> $$\frac{\frac{4}{3} \pi R^{3}}{\frac{4}{3} \pi r^{3}}=\frac{64}{27} \Rightarrow \frac{R^{3}}{r^{3}}=\frac{64}{27} \Rightarrow\left(\frac{R}{r}\right)^{3}=\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)^{3} \Rightarrow \frac{R}{r}=\frac{4}{3}$$
> Ratio of their surface areas $=\frac{4 \pi R^{2}}{4 \pi r^{2}}=\frac{R^{2}}{r^{2}}=\left(\frac{R}{r}\right)^{2}=\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)^{2}=\frac{16}{9}=16: 9$.

### Answer to Question 76

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-6/Chapter-17/Section-Q/Q-76
