### Answer to Question 05

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-6/Chapter-16/Section-Q/Q-05

- Answer: 

(c)

> [!INFO]
>
> Let original diameter be $100$ units. Then, original radius $= 50$.
>
> $\therefore \quad \text{original area} = \pi \times (50)^{2} = 2500 \pi$.
>
> $\text{New diameter} = 140$. $\text{New radius} = 70$.
>
> $\text{New area} = \pi \times (70)^{2} = 4900 \pi$.
>
> $\text{Increase } \% = \left(\frac{2400}{2500} \times 100\right) \% = 96 \%$.
