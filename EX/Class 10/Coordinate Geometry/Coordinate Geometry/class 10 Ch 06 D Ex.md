### Answer to Question 01

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-3/Chapter-06/Section-D/Q-01

- Answer:

$y=7$ or $y=-1$

> [!INFO]
>
> We know that the radii of a circle are equal.
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \therefore \quad O A=O B & \Rightarrow O A^{2}=O B^{2} \\
> & \Rightarrow(2+1)^{2}+(-3 y-y)^{2}=(2-5)^{2}+(-3 y-7)^{2} \\
> & \Rightarrow 3^{2}+16 y^{2}=(-3)^{2}+\left(9 y^{2}+49+42 y\right) \\
> & \Rightarrow 7 y^{2}-42 y-49=0 \Rightarrow y^{2}-6 y-7=0 \\
> & \Rightarrow(y-7)(y+1)=0 \Rightarrow y=7 \text { or } y=-1
> \end{aligned}
> $$

### Answer to Question 17

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-3/Chapter-06/Section-D/Q-17

- Answer:

$k=0$

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $\left(x_{1}=2, y_{1}=3\right),\left(x_{2}=4, y_{2}=k\right)$ and $\left(x_{3}=6, y_{3}=-3\right)$.
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \therefore \quad \triangle=0 & \Rightarrow x_{1}\left(y_{2}-y_{3}\right)+x_{2}\left(y_{3}-y_{1}\right)+x_{3}\left(y_{1}-y_{2}\right)=0 \\

> & \Rightarrow-4 k=0 \Rightarrow k=0 .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
