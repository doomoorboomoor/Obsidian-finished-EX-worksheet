### Answer to Question 03

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-3/Chapter-06/Section-B/Q-03

- Answer:

$P\left(\frac{-2}{7}, \frac{-20}{7}\right)$

> [!INFO]
>
> $\frac{A P}{A B}=\frac{3}{7} \Rightarrow \frac{A B}{A P}=\frac{7}{3} \Rightarrow \frac{A P+P B}{A P}=\frac{7}{3}$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \Rightarrow \frac{A P}{A P}+\frac{P B}{A P}=\frac{7}{3} \Rightarrow 1+\frac{P B}{A P}=\frac{7}{3} \\
> & \Rightarrow \frac{P B}{A P}=\left(\frac{7}{3}-1\right)=\frac{4}{3} \Rightarrow \frac{A P}{P B}=\frac{3}{4} \Rightarrow A P: P B=3: 4 .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> $\therefore$ coordinates of $P$ are $\left(\frac{m x_{2}+n x_{1}}{m+n}, \frac{m y_{2}+n y_{1}}{m+n}\right)$, where $m=3, n=4$,
> 
> $$
> \text { i.e., } P\left(\frac{3 \times 2+4 \times(-2)}{3+4}, \frac{3 \times(-4)+4 \times(-2)}{3+4}\right)=P\left(\frac{-2}{7}, \frac{-20}{7}\right) .
> $$
>

### Answer to Question 04

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-3/Chapter-06/Section-B/Q-04

> [!INFO]
>
> $\frac{P A}{P Q}=\frac{2}{5} \Rightarrow \frac{P Q}{P A}=\frac{5}{2} \Rightarrow \frac{P A+A Q}{P A}=\frac{5}{2}$

### Answer to Question 14

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-3/Chapter-06/Section-B/Q-14

- Answer:

$1 : 5$

> [!INFO]
>
> Let the required ratio be $k: 1$.
> 
> Then, the coordinates of $P$ are $\left(\frac{2 k+\frac{1}{2}}{k+1}, \frac{-5 k+\frac{3}{2}}{k+1}\right)$.
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \therefore \quad \frac{2 k+\frac{1}{2}}{k+1}=\frac{3}{4} & \Rightarrow \frac{4 k+1}{2(k+1)}=\frac{3}{4} \Rightarrow 16 k+4=6 k+6 \\
> & \Rightarrow 10 k=2 \Rightarrow k=\frac{1}{5}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> $\therefore \quad$ required ratio is $\frac{1}{5}: 1$, i.e., $1: 5$.
