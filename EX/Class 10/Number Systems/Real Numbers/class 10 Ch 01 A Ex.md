### Answer to Question 05

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-1/Chapter-01/Section-A/Q-05

- Answer:

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $n$ be an arbitrary positive integer.
> 
> On dividing $n$ by $2$, let $m$ be the quotient and $r$ be the remainder.
> 
> Then, $n = 2m + r$, where $0 \leq r < 2$ [by Euclid's division lemma].
> 
> $\therefore \quad n = 2m$ or $n = 2m + 1$ for some integer $m$.
> 
> So, $n$ is either even or odd.
