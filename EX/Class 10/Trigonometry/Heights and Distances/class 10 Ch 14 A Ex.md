### Answer to Question 03

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-5/Chapter-14/Section-A/Q-03

- Answer:

53.46 m

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $A B$ be the chimney and $C D$ be the observer.
> 
> Draw $D E \perp A B$.
> Then, $A E=C D=1.5 \mathrm{~m}$.
> Let $A B=x$ metres. Then, $B E=(x-1.5) \mathrm{m}$.
> $\angle E D B=60^{\circ}$.
> Also, $D E=C A=30 \mathrm{~m}$.
> From right $\triangle B E D$, we have
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \frac{B E}{D E}=\tan 60^{\circ} \\
> \Rightarrow & \frac{x-1.5}{30}=\sqrt{3} .
> \end{aligned}
> $$

### Answer to Question 04

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-5/Chapter-14/Section-A/Q-04

- Answer:

10 m

> [!INFO]
>
> $\frac{A B}{A C}=\tan \theta \Rightarrow \frac{h}{5}=\tan \theta$
> $\Rightarrow h=5 \tan \theta$.
> $\frac{A B}{A D}=\tan \left(90^{\circ}-\theta\right)=\cot \theta$.
> 
> $$
> \begin{equation*}
> \Rightarrow \frac{h}{20}=\cot \theta \Rightarrow h=20 \cot \theta \tag{ii}
> \end{equation*}
> $$
>

> 
> $\therefore \quad h^{2}=100 \tan \theta \times \cot \theta=100 \Rightarrow h=10$.

### Answer to Question 06/i

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-5/Chapter-14/Section-A/Q-06/Q-i

- Answer:

23.1 m

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $B C$ be the tower and $C D$ be the water tank.
> 
> Let $A$ be the point of observation. Then, $\angle B A C=30^{\circ}, \angle B A D=45^{\circ}$ and $A B=40 \mathrm{~m}$.
> From right $\triangle A B D$, we have
> 
> $$
> \frac{B D}{A B}=\tan 45^{\circ}=1 \Rightarrow \frac{B D}{40 \mathrm{~m}}=1 \Rightarrow B D=40 \mathrm{~m} .
> $$
> 
> From right $\triangle A B C$, we have

### Answer to Question 32

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-5/Chapter-14/Section-A/Q-32

- Answer:

3.46 m

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $A B$ be the electric pole such that $A B=4 \mathrm{~m}$. Let $C$ be a point 1 m below $B$.
> Then, $A C=4 \mathrm{~m}-1 \mathrm{~m}=3 \mathrm{~m}$.
> Let $O C$ be the ladder. Then, $\angle A O C=60^{\circ}$.
> Let $O C=x$ metres.
> From right $\triangle O A C$, we have
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \frac{O C}{A C}=\operatorname{cosec} 60^{\circ} & \Rightarrow \frac{x}{3}=\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}} \Rightarrow x=\frac{6}{\sqrt{3}} \Rightarrow x=\frac{6}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}=2 \sqrt{3} . \\
> & \Rightarrow x=(2 \times 1.73)=3.46
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Hence, the length of the ladder is 3.46 m .
