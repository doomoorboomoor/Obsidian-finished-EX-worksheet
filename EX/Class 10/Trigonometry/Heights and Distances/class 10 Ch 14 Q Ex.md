### Answer to Question 13

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-5/Chapter-14/Section-Q/Q-13

(d)

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $h \mathrm{~m}$ be the height of the tower and let $a \mathrm{~m}$ and $(a+2 x) \mathrm{m}$ be the lengths of the shadows of the tower when sun's elevation is $45^{\circ}$ and $30^{\circ}$ respectively. Then,
>
> $$
> \begin{array}{ll} 
> & \frac{h}{a}=\tan 45^{\circ}=1 \Rightarrow a=h . \\
> & \frac{h}{a+2 x}=\tan 30^{\circ}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \Rightarrow \frac{h}{h+2 x}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} . \\

> \therefore \quad & h=\left\{\frac{2 x}{(\sqrt{3}-1)} \times \frac{(\sqrt{3}+1)}{(\sqrt{3}+1)}\right\}=x(\sqrt{3}+1) .
> \end{array}
> $$
