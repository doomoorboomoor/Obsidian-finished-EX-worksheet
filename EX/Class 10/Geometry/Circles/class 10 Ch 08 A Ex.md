### Answer to Question 13

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-4/Chapter-08/Section-A/Q-13

- Answer:

$T P=4 \mathrm{~cm}$

> [!INFO]
>
> $R$ is the midpoint of $P Q$, i.e., $P R=Q R=\left(\frac{1}{2} \times 4.8\right) \mathrm{cm}=2.4 \mathrm{~cm}$.
>
> Also, $T O \perp P Q$.
>
> In right $\triangle P R O$, we have
>
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> P O^{2}=P R^{2}+R O^{2} \Rightarrow R O & =\sqrt{P O^{2}-P R^{2}} \\
> & =\sqrt{3^{2}-2 \cdot 4^{2}}=\sqrt{3 \cdot 24}=1.8
> \end{aligned}
> $$
>
> Let $T R=x$ and $T P=y$.
>
> In right $\triangle P T R$, we have $T P^{2}=T R^{2}+P R^{2} \Rightarrow y^{2}=x^{2}+(2.4)^{2}$.
>
> In right $\triangle O T P$, we have
>
> $$
> \begin{array}{ll} 
> & T O^{2}=T P^{2}+P O^{2} \Rightarrow(T R+R O)^{2}=T P^{2}+P O^{2} \\
> \Rightarrow \quad & (x+1.8)^{2}=y^{2}+3^{2} \Rightarrow x^{2}+3.6 x+3.24=y^{2}+9 \tag{ii}
> \end{array}
> $$
>
> Solving (i) and (ii), we get $x=3.2, y=4$.
>
> $\therefore \quad T P=4 \mathrm{~cm}$.
>

### Answer to Question 14

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-4/Chapter-08/Section-A/Q-14

- Answer:



> [!INFO]
>
> Let $A P B$ and $C Q D$ be two parallel tangents to a circle with centre $O$.
>
> Join $O P$ and $O Q$. Draw $R O \| A B$.
>
> Now, $\angle A P O+\angle R O P=180^{\circ}$ [co-interior angles]
>
> $\Rightarrow \angle R O P=90^{\circ} \quad\left[\because \angle A P O=90^{\circ}\right]$.
>
> Similarly, $\angle C Q O+\angle R O Q=180^{\circ}$ [co-interior angles]
>
> $\Rightarrow \angle R O Q=90^{\circ} \quad\left[\because \angle C Q O=90^{\circ}\right]$.
>
> $\therefore \quad \angle P O Q=\angle R O P+\angle R O Q=180^{\circ}$.
>
> Hence, $P O Q$ is a straight line passing through $O$.
>

### Answer to Question 16

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-4/Chapter-08/Section-A/Q-16

- Answer:



> [!INFO]
>
> $\angle A P B=90^{\circ}$ [angle in a semicircle]
>
> $\therefore \angle P A B=90^{\circ}-\angle P B A=90^{\circ}-30^{\circ}=60^{\circ} \quad\left[\because \angle P B A=\angle P B T=30^{\circ}\right]$.
>
> $\angle P A T+\angle P A B=180^{\circ}$ [linear pair]
>
> $\Rightarrow \angle P A T=180^{\circ}-\angle P A B=180^{\circ}-60^{\circ}=120^{\circ}$.
>
> $\angle A P T=\angle P B A=30^{\circ} \quad$ [angles in alternate segments].
>
> In $\triangle P A T$, we have
>
> $\angle A P T+\angle P A T+\angle P T A=180^{\circ}$
>
> $\Rightarrow \angle P T A=180^{\circ}-(\angle A P T+\angle P A T)=180^{\circ}-\left(30^{\circ}+120^{\circ}\right)=30^{\circ}$.
>
> Now, $\angle A P T=\angle P T A=30^{\circ} \Rightarrow A T=A P$
>
> [sides opposite equal angles are equal]
>
> In right $\triangle A P B$, we have
>
> $$
> \begin{equation*}
> \cos (\angle P A B)=\frac{A P}{B A} \Rightarrow \cos 60^{\circ}=\frac{A P}{B A} \Rightarrow B A=2 A P . \tag{ii}
> \end{equation*}
> $$
>
> From (i) and (ii), we get $B A: A T=2 A P: A P=2: 1$.
