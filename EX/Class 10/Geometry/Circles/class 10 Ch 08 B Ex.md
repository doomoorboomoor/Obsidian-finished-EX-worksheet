### Answer to Question 06

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-4/Chapter-08/Section-B/Q-06

- Answer:

$A B=7.5 \mathrm{~cm}, A C=6.5 \mathrm{~cm}$

> [!INFO]
>
> Mark the points of contact $E$ and $F$ of tangents $A C$ and $A B$ respectively.
>
> We know that the tangents to a circle from an external point are equal.
>
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \therefore \quad A E & =A F=x \mathrm{~cm}(\text { say }) ; \\
> B D & =B F=4 \mathrm{~cm} ; C D=C E=3 \mathrm{~cm} .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
>
> Now, $\operatorname{ar}(\triangle A B C)=\frac{1}{2} \times($ perimeter of $\triangle A B C) \times r$
> $\Rightarrow \quad 21=\frac{1}{2} \times\{(x+4)+(4+3)+(3+x)\} \times 2 \Rightarrow x=3.5$.
> $\therefore A B=(x+4) \mathrm{cm}=7.5 \mathrm{~cm} ; A C=(x+3) \mathrm{cm}=6.5 \mathrm{~cm}$.
>

### Answer to Question 07

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-4/Chapter-08/Section-B/Q-07

- Answer:

8 cm

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $A B$ be the chord, $P$ be the point where it touches the smaller circle and $O$ be the common centre.
> $A B$ is tangent to smaller circle and so, $O P \perp A B$.
> $\therefore \quad P$ is the midpoint of $A B$, i.e., $A P=P B$
> $[\because$ a perpendicular from the centre on any chord bisects the chord].
> Now, $O P=3 \mathrm{~cm}, O A=5 \mathrm{~cm} \Rightarrow A P=\sqrt{O A^{2}-O P^{2}}=\sqrt{5^{2}-3^{2}}=4$.
> $\therefore \quad A B=2 A P=8 \mathrm{~cm}$.
>
> ![](https://cdn.mathpix.com/cropped/2025_09_25_c310d75fc3e617fdd4a2g-33.jpg?height=219&width=217&top_left_y=1566&top_left_x=978)

### Answer to Question 12

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-4/Chapter-08/Section-B/Q-12

- Answer:

3 cm

> [!INFO]
>
> Let $A B$ be the chord, $P$ be the point where it touches the smaller circle and $O$ be the common centre.
>
> Then, $A B$ is the tangent to smaller circle at $P$.
> $\therefore \quad O P \perp A B$.
> And so, $P$ is the midpoint of $A B$,

> $[\because$ perpendicular from the centre on any chord bisects the chord $]$.
> Now, $O A=5 \mathrm{~cm}, A P=4 \mathrm{~cm} \Rightarrow O P=\sqrt{O A^{2}-A P^{2}}=\sqrt{5^{2}-4^{2}}=3$.
> $\therefore$ radius of smaller circle $=O P=3 \mathrm{~cm}$.

### Answer to Question 14

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-4/Chapter-08/Section-B/Q-14

- Answer:

$30^{\circ}$

> [!INFO]
>
> $P A=P B \quad[\because$ tangents from an external point are equal $]$
> $\Rightarrow \angle P A B=\angle P B A=\frac{1}{2} \times\left(180^{\circ}-\angle A P B\right)=\frac{1}{2} \times\left(180^{\circ}-60^{\circ}\right)=60^{\circ}$.
> Now, $\angle P A O=90^{\circ} \quad[\because$ radius through the point of contact is perpendicular to the tangent]
> $\Rightarrow \angle O A B+\angle P A B=90^{\circ} \Rightarrow \angle O A B=90^{\circ}-\angle P A B=90^{\circ}-60^{\circ}=30^{\circ}$.
