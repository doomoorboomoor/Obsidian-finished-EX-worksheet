### Answer to Question 12

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-10/Unit-4/Chapter-07/Section-A/Q-12

- Answer:



> [!INFO]
>
> $A B=A C$ and $A D=A E$
> $\Rightarrow A B-A D=A C-A E \Rightarrow D B=E C$
> $\Rightarrow \quad \frac{A D}{A E}=\frac{D B}{E C} \quad$ [each equal to 1]
> $\Rightarrow D E \| B C \quad$ [by the converse of Thales' theorem]
> $\Rightarrow \angle D E C+\angle E C B=180^{\circ}$
> $\Rightarrow \angle D E C+\angle C B D=180^{\circ} \quad[\because A B=A C \Rightarrow \angle C=\angle B]$
> $\Rightarrow$ quad. $B C E D$ is cyclic.
> Hence, the points $B, C, E, D$ are concyclic.
>
