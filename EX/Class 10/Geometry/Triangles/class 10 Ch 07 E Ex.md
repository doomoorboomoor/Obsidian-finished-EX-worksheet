> [!INFO]
>
> Let $D$ and $E$ be the midpoints of sides $A B$ and $A C$ respectively of a $\triangle A B C$.
>
> Then, by the midpoint theorem,
>
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & D E \| B C \Rightarrow \triangle A D E \sim \triangle A B C . \\
> \therefore \quad & \frac{A D}{A B}=\frac{D E}{B C} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{2}=\frac{D E}{B C} \Rightarrow D E=\frac{1}{2} B C .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
