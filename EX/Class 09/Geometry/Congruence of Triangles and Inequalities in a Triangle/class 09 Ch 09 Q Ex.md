### Answer to Question 13

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-4/Chapter-09/Section-Q/Q-13

- Answer:

(a)

> [!INFO]
>
> Join $O A$.
>
> In $\triangle O A B$ and $\triangle O A C$, we have
>
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & A B=A C \text{ (given) } \\
> & O B=O C \text{ (given) }

> $$
>
> and $O A=O A$.
>

>
> $\therefore \quad \angle A B O: \angle A C O=1: 1$.

### Answer to Question 14

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-4/Chapter-09/Section-Q/Q-14

- Answer:

(b)

> [!INFO]
>
> A $\triangle A B C$ is given in which $B L \perp A C$ and $C M \perp A B$ such that $B L=C M$. Then, we have to prove that $A B=A C$.
>
> In $\triangle A B L$ and $\triangle A C M$, we have:
>
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & B L=C M \text{ (given), } \\
> & \angle B A L=\angle C A M \text{ (common), } \\
> & \angle A L B=\angle A M C \text{ (each } 90^{\circ} \text{)} \\
> & \triangle A B L \cong \triangle A C M \text{ and hence } A B=A C . \\
> & \triangle A B C \text{ is isosceles.}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
>
> ![](https://cdn.mathpix.com/cropped/2025_09_25_6efc6062a3fff7670a58g-42.jpg?height=247&width=253&top_left_y=233&top_left_x=944)
