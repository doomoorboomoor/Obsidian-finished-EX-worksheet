### Answer to Question 22

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-4/Chapter-07/Section-C/Q-22

- Answer:



> [!INFO]
>
> Let the normals at $A$ and $B$ meet at $P$.
> 
> Since angle of incidence = angle of reflection, we have
> $\angle 1=\angle 2$ and $\angle 4=\angle 3$.
> As the mirrors are perpendicular to each other, we have
> $B P \| O A$ and $A P \| O B$.
> Since $B P \perp P A$, we have $\angle A P B=90^{\circ}$.
> $\therefore \quad \angle 2+\angle 3=90^{\circ}$.
> $\therefore \quad \angle 1+\angle 4=\angle 2+\angle 3=90^{\circ}$.
> So, $\angle 1+\angle 2+\angle 3+\angle 4=180^{\circ}$.
> $\therefore \quad \angle C A B+\angle A B D=180^{\circ}$.
> Hence, $A C \| B D$.
