### Answer to Question 14.

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-5/Chapter-15/Section-B/Q-14

- Answer:

$770 \mathrm{~cm}^{2}$

> [!INFO]
>
> Suppose that radius $=2 x \mathrm{~cm}$ and height $=3 x \mathrm{~cm}$.
>
> Volume $=\pi r^{2} h=\left(\frac{22}{7} \times 4 x^{2} \times 3 x\right)=\left(\frac{264 x^{3}}{7}\right) \mathrm{cm}^{3}$.
>
> $\therefore \quad \frac{264 x^{3}}{7}=1617 \Rightarrow x^{3}=\left(\frac{1617 \times 7}{264}\right)=\left(\frac{7}{2}\right)^{3} \Rightarrow x=\frac{7}{2}$.
>
> Thus, $r=7 \mathrm{~cm}$ and $h=10.5 \mathrm{~cm}$.
>
> Total surface area $=\left(2 \pi r h+2 \pi r^{2}\right)=2 \pi r(h+r)$.
>

### Answer to Question 15.

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-5/Chapter-15/Section-B/Q-15

- Answer:

$539 \mathrm{~cm}^{3}$

> [!INFO]
>
> Curved surface area $=\left(\frac{1}{3} \times 462\right) \mathrm{cm}^{2}=154 \mathrm{~cm}^{2}$.
>
> $$
> \begin{align*}
> & 2 \pi r h=154 \\
> & \begin{aligned}
> \therefore \quad \frac{2 \pi r h}{2 \pi r(h+r)}=\frac{154}{462}=\frac{1}{3} & \Rightarrow \frac{h}{h+r}=\frac{1}{3} \quad \text { and } 2 \pi r(h+r)=462 \\
> & \Rightarrow 3 h=h+r \Rightarrow r=2 h .
> \end{aligned} \tag{ii}
> \end{align*}
> $$
>
> Putting $r=2 h$ in (i), we get $h^{2}=\frac{49}{4}=\left(\frac{7}{2}\right)^{2} \Rightarrow h=\frac{7}{2}$.
>
> Thus, $r=7$ and $h=3.5$. Now, find the volume.
