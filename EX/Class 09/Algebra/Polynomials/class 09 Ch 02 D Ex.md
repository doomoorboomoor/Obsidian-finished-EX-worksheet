### Answer to Question 25.

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-2/Chapter-02/Section-D/Q-25

- Answer: 

> [!INFO]
> Let $g(x)=\left(x^{2}+2 x-3\right)=\left(x^{2}+3 x-x-3\right)=x(x+3)-(x+3)=(x+3)(x-1)$.
> 
> When a polynomial is divided by a quadratic polynomial, then the remainder is a linear expression, say $a x+b$.
> Let $p(x)=\left(x^{4}+2 x^{3}-2 x^{2}+4 x+6\right)-(a x+b)$. Then,
> $$
> p(x)=x^{4}+2 x^{3}-2 x^{2}+(4-a) x+(6-b)
> $$
> $\therefore \quad p(-3)=0$ and $p(1)=0 \Rightarrow 3 a-b=-3$ and $a+b=11$.
> On solving these equations, we get $a=2$ and $b=9$.
> Hence, the required expression is $(2 x+9)$.

### Answer to Question 26.

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-09/Unit-2/Chapter-02/Section-D/Q-26

- Answer: 

> [!INFO]
> Let $p(x)=\left(x^{n}+a^{n}\right)$, where $n$ is any odd positive integer.
> 
> Let $g(x)=x+a$. Then, $g(x)=0 \Rightarrow x+a=0 \Rightarrow x=-a$.
> Now, $p(-a)=\left\{(-a)^{n}+a^{n}\right\}=\left\{(-1)^{n} a^{n}+a^{n}\right\}=\left\{(-1)^{n}+1\right\} a^{n}$
> $$
> =(-1+1) a^{n}=0 \quad\left[\because n \text{ being odd, } (-1)^{n}=-1\right].
> $$
> By factor theorem, ( $x+a$ ) is a factor of ( $x^{n}+a^{n}$ ), when $n$ is an odd positive integer.
