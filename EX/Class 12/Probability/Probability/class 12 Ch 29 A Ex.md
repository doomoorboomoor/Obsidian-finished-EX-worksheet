### Answer to Question 08

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-6/Chapter-29/Section-A/Q-08

- Answer:

$\frac{1}{2}$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $A=$ event of getting the sum 8 or greater, and
> $B=$ event of getting a 4 on the first die.
> $\therefore A=\{(2,6),(3,5),(3,6),(4,4),(4,5),(4,6),(5,3),(5,4),(5,5),(5,6)$,
> $(6,2),(6,3),(6,4),(6,5),(6,6)\}$
> $B=\{(4,1),(4,2),(4,3),(4,4),(4,5),(4,6)\}$.
> $(A \cap B)=\{(4,4),(4,5),(4,6)\}$.
> $\therefore \quad n(A)=15, n(B)=6$ and $n(A \cap B)=3$.
> Hence, the required probability $=P(A / B)=\frac{n(A \cap B)}{n(B)}=\frac{3}{6}=\frac{1}{2}$.
> 
>
