### Answer to Question 12

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-6/Chapter-29/Section-B/Q-12

- Answer:

$\frac{1}{2}$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $E_{1}=$ event that Arun is selected, and $E_{2}=$ event that Ved is selected.
> Then, $P\left(E_{1}\right)=\frac{1}{4}$ and $P\left(E_{2}\right)=\left(1-\frac{2}{3}\right)=\frac{1}{3}$.
> Clearly, $E_{1}$ and $E_{2}$ are independent events.
> $\therefore P\left(E_{1} \cap E_{2}\right)=P\left(E_{1}\right) \times P\left(E_{2}\right)=\left(\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{3}\right)=\frac{1}{12}$.
> Required probability $=P\left(E_{1} \text{ or } E_{2}\right)=P\left(E_{1} \cup E_{2}\right)$
> $$=P\left(E_{1}\right)+P\left(E_{2}\right)-P\left(E_{1} \cap E_{2}\right)=\left(\frac{1}{4}+\frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{12}\right)=\frac{6}{12}=\frac{1}{2} .$$
> 
>
