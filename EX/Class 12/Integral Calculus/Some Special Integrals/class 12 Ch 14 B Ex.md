### Answer to Question 18

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-14/Section-B/Q-18

- Answer:

$a \sin ^{-1} \frac{x}{a}+\sqrt{a^{2}-x^{2}}+C$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\int \sqrt{\frac{a-x}{a+x}} d x=\int\left\{\frac{\sqrt{a-x}}{\sqrt{a+x}} \times \frac{\sqrt{a-x}}{\sqrt{a-x}}\right\} d x=\int \frac{(a-x)}{\sqrt{a^{2}-x^{2}}} d x$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & =a \int \frac{d x}{\sqrt{a^{2}-x^{2}}}+\frac{1}{2} \cdot \int \frac{(-2 x)}{\sqrt{a^{2}-x^{2}}} d x=a \sin ^{-1} \frac{x}{a}+\frac{1}{2} \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{t}} d t, \text{ where }\left(a^{2}-x^{2}\right)=t \\
> & =a \sin ^{-1} \frac{x}{a}+\frac{1}{2} \times 2 \sqrt{t}+C=a \sin ^{-1} \frac{x}{a}+\sqrt{a^{2}-x^{2}}+C
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
>
