### Answer to Question 08

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-11/Section-G/Q-08

- Answer:





> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $f(x)=\log _{a} x=\frac{\log _{e} x}{\log _{e} a}$.
> 
> Case I Let $\left.x_{1}, x_{2} \in\right] 0, \infty[$ and let $a>1$.
> 
> Then, $x_{1}>x_{2} \Rightarrow \log x_{1}>\log x_{2}$
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \frac{\log x_{1}}{\log a}>\frac{\log x_{2}}{\log a} \\
& \Rightarrow \log _{a} x_{1}>\log _{a} x_{2} .
\end{aligned}
$$
> 
> $\therefore f(x)$ is strictly increasing on $[0, \infty[$ when $a>1$.
> 
> Case II Let $\left.x_{1}, x_{2} \in\right] 0, \infty[$ and let $0<a<1$.
> 
> Then, $x_{1}>x_{2} \Rightarrow \log x_{1}>\log x_{2}$
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow \frac{\log x_{1}}{\log a}<\frac{\log x_{2}}{\log a}\left[\because 0<a<1 \Rightarrow \log a<0 \Rightarrow \frac{1}{\log a}<0\right] \\
& \Rightarrow\left(\log _{a} x_{1}\right)<\left(\log _{a} x_{2}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$
> 
> $\therefore f(x)$ is strictly decreasing on $] 0, \infty[$ when $0<a<1$.
> 
>
