> Now, $2 x^{2}+3 y^{2}=14 \Rightarrow 4 x+6 y \frac{d y}{d x}=0 \Rightarrow \frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{-4 x}{6 y}=\frac{-2 x}{3 y} \Rightarrow\left(\frac{d y}{d x}\right)_{\left(x_{1}, y_{1}\right)}=\frac{-2 x}{3 y_{1}}$.
> $\therefore \quad \frac{-2 x_{1}}{3 y_{1}}=\frac{-1}{3} \Rightarrow 2 x_{1}=y_{1}$
> Also, $\left(x_{1}, y_{1}\right)$ lies on the given curve.

> $$

> $$
> 
> So, the points of contact are $(1,2)$ and $(-1,-2)$.
> The respective tangents are
> 
> $$
> \frac{y-2}{x-1}=\frac{-1}{3} \text{ and } \frac{y+2}{x+1}=\frac{-1}{3}
> $$
> 
> $\Rightarrow 3 y-6=-x+1$ and $3 y+6=-x-1 \Rightarrow 3 y+x=7$ and $3 y+x=-7$.
