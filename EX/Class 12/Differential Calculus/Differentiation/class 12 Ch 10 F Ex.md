### Answer to Question 15

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-10/Section-F/Q-15

- Answer:

$\left[\cos \left(x^{x}\right)\right] x^{x}(1+\log x)$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $x^{x}=t$. Then,
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \log t=x \log x & \Rightarrow \frac{1}{t} \cdot \frac{d t}{d x}=x \cdot \frac{1}{x}+\log x \cdot 1 \\
> & \Rightarrow \frac{d t}{d x}=t[1+\log x]=x^{x}(1+\log x)
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> $\therefore y=\sin t \Rightarrow \frac{d y}{d t}=\cos t=\cos x^{x}$.
> $\therefore \frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{(d y / d t)}{(d x / d t)}=\left[\cos \left(x^{x}\right)\right] x^{x}(1+\log x)$.
> 
>
