### Answer to Question 05

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-25/Section-Q/Q-05

- Answer:

(b)

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $\vec{a}=(\cos \alpha \cos \beta) \hat{i}+(\cos \alpha \sin \beta) \hat{j}+(\sin \alpha) \hat{k}$. Then,
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> |\vec{a}|^{2} & =(\cos \alpha \cos \beta)^{2}+(\cos \alpha \sin \beta)^{2}+(\sin \alpha)^{2} \\
> & =\cos ^{2} \alpha \cos ^{2} \beta+\cos ^{2} \alpha \sin ^{2} \beta+\sin ^{2} \alpha \\
> & =\cos ^{2} \alpha\left(\cos ^{2} \beta+\sin ^{2} \beta\right)+\sin ^{2} \alpha \\
> & =\left(\cos ^{2} \alpha+\sin ^{2} \alpha\right)=1 .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Hence, the given vector $\vec{a}$ is a unit vector.
>
