### Answer to Question 19

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-27/Section-A/Q-19

- Answer:

19. $\vec{r}=(3 \hat{i}-2 \hat{j}+\hat{k})+\lambda(4 \hat{i}-\hat{j}+\hat{k}), \frac{x-3}{4}=\frac{y+2}{1}=\frac{z-1}{1}$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> The equations of $B C$ are $\frac{x-0}{0-2}=\frac{y+1}{-1+3}=\frac{z-3}{3+1} \Rightarrow \frac{x}{-2}=\frac{y+1}{2}=\frac{z-3}{4}$
> $\Rightarrow \frac{x}{-2}=\frac{y+1}{2}=\frac{z-3}{4} \Rightarrow \frac{x}{-1}=\frac{y+1}{1}=\frac{z-3}{2}=r$ (say).
> Any general point on $B C$ is $(-r, r-1,2 r+3)$.
> Let $A P \perp B C$. Then $P(-r, r-1,2 r+3)$ for some fixed value of $r$.
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \text { d.r.'s of } \mathrm{BC} \text { are }-1,1,2 . \\
> & \text { d.r.'s of } A P \text { are }(-r-1),(r-1-8),(2 r+3-4) \text {, i.e., }(-r-1),(r-9),(2 r-1) .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Since $A P \perp B C$, we have $-1 \cdot(-r-1)+1 \cdot(r-9)+2(2 r-1)=0$.
> $\therefore \quad 6 r=10 \Rightarrow r=\frac{5}{3}$.
> Hence, the coordinates of $P$ are $\left(\frac{-5}{3}, \frac{5}{3}-1, \frac{10}{3}+3\right)$, i.e., $\left(\frac{-5}{3}, \frac{2}{3}, \frac{19}{3}\right)$.
>
