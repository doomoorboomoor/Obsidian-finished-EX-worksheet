### Answer to Question 05

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-E/Q-05

- Answer:

$9 x-8 y+7 z=21, \frac{13}{\sqrt{194}}$ units



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let the required plane be
> 
> $$
> \begin{align*}
> & (x-2 y+z-1)+\lambda(2 x+y+z-8)=0 \\
> \Leftrightarrow & (1+2 \lambda) x+(\lambda-2) y+(1+\lambda) z-(1+8 \lambda)=0 . \tag{i}
> \end{align*}
> $$
> 
> The d.r.'s of the normal to this plane are
> 
> $$
> (1+2 \lambda),(\lambda-2),(1+\lambda)
> $$
> 
> The normal to the plane (i) is perpendicular to the line with d.r.'s $1,2,1$.
> $\therefore \quad(1+2 \lambda)+2(\lambda-2)+(1+\lambda)=0 \Rightarrow \lambda=\frac{2}{5}$.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 06

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-E/Q-06

- Answer:

$5 x+2 y+5 z-9=0$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let the required plane be
> 
> $$
> (x+2 y+3 z-5)+\lambda(3 x-2 y-z+1)=0
> $$
> 
> Then,
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & (1+3 \lambda) x+(2-2 \lambda) y+(3-\lambda) z-5+\lambda=0 . \\
> & (1+3 \lambda) x+(2-2 \lambda) y+(3-\lambda) z=5-\lambda \\
> \Rightarrow & \frac{(1+3 \lambda)}{(5-\lambda)} x+\frac{(2-2 \lambda)}{(5-\lambda)} y+\frac{(3-\lambda)}{(5-\lambda)} z=1 \\
> \Rightarrow & \frac{x}{\left(\frac{5-\lambda}{1+3 \lambda}\right)}+\frac{y}{\left(\frac{5-\lambda}{2-2 \lambda}\right)}+\frac{z}{\left(\frac{5-\lambda}{3-\lambda}\right)}=1 .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Since the intercepts on the $x$-axis and $z$-axis are equal, we have
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \frac{5-\lambda}{1+3 \lambda}=\frac{5-\lambda}{3-\lambda} & \Rightarrow 3-\lambda=1+3 \lambda \\
> & \Rightarrow 4 \lambda=2 \Rightarrow \lambda=\frac{1}{2}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
>

### Answer to Question 07

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-E/Q-07

- Answer:

$x-20 y+27 z=14$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let the required plane be
> 
> $$
> \begin{align*}
> & (3 x-4 y+5 z-10)+\lambda(2 x+2 y-3 z-4)=0 \\
> \Rightarrow \quad & (3+2 \lambda) x+(2 \lambda-4) y+(5-3 \lambda) z-10-4 \lambda=0 . \tag{i}
> \end{align*}
> $$
> 
> D.r's of normal to the plane are $(3+2 \lambda),(2 \lambda-4),(5-3 \lambda)$.
> 
> Given line is $\frac{x}{6}=\frac{y}{3}=\frac{z}{2}$.
> D.r.'s of the line are $6,3,2$.
> $\therefore \quad$ This line is perpendicular to the plane.
> $\therefore \quad 6(3+2 \lambda)+3(2 \lambda-4)+2(5-3 \lambda)=0 \Rightarrow 12 \lambda=-16 \Rightarrow \lambda=\frac{-4}{3}$.
>

### Answer to Question 12

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-E/Q-12

- Answer:

$2 x+y-2 z+3=0, x+2 y-2 z-3=0$, and
$$
\vec{r} \cdot(2 \hat{i}+\hat{j}-2 \hat{k})+3=0, \vec{r} \cdot(\hat{i}+2 \hat{j}-2 \hat{k})-3=0
$$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> The equations of the given planes are
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & (x \hat{i}+y \hat{j}+z \hat{k}) \cdot(\hat{i}-\hat{j})+6=0 \text { and } (x \hat{i}+y \hat{j}+z \hat{k}) \cdot(3 \hat{i}+3 \hat{j}-4 \hat{k})=0 \\
> \Rightarrow & x-y+6=0 \text { and } 3 x+3 y-4 z=0 .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Any plane through their intersection is
> 
> $$
> \begin{align*}
> & (x-y+6)+\lambda(3 x+3 y-4 z)=0 \Rightarrow(1+3 \lambda) x+(3 \lambda-1) y-4 \lambda z+6=0 .  \tag{i}\\
> \therefore & \frac{6}{\sqrt{(1+3 \lambda)^{2}+(3 \lambda-1)^{2}+(-4 \lambda)^{2}}}=1 \Rightarrow 34 \lambda^{2}+2=36 \Rightarrow \lambda^{2}=1 \Rightarrow \lambda= \pm 1
> \end{align*}
> $$
> 
> So, the required planes are
> 
> $$
> 2 x+y-2 z+3=0 \text { and } x+2 y-2 z-3=0 .
> $$
> 
> In vector form, they are
> 
> $$
> \vec{r} \cdot(2 \hat{i}+\hat{j}-2 \hat{k})+3=0 \text { and } \vec{r} \cdot(\hat{i}+2 \hat{j}-2 \hat{k})-3=0 .
> $$
> 
>
