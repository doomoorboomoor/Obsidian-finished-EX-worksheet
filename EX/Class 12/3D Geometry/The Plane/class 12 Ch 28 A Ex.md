### Answer to Question 06

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-A/Q-06

- Answer:

$x+y+z=6$


> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let it make intercept $a$ on each of the coordinate axes.
>
> Then, its equation is $\frac{x}{a}+\frac{y}{a}+\frac{z}{a}=1 \Rightarrow x+y+z=a$.
>
> Putting $x=2, y=-3, z=7$, we get $a=2+(-3)+7=6$.
>
> So, the required equation of the plane is $x+y+z=6$.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 09

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-A/Q-09

- Answer:

$x+2 y-3 z-14=0$


> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let the required equation of the plane passing through the point $A(1,2,-3)$ be
> 
> $$
\begin{equation*}
a(x-1)+b(y-2)+c(z+3)=0 \tag{i}
\end{equation*}
$$
> 
> D.r.'s of $O P$ are $(1-0)$, ( $2-0$ ), ( $-3-0$ ), i.e., $1,2,-3$.
> 
> Since the plane is perpendicular to $O P$, so the normal to the plane is parallel to $O P$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad \frac{a}{1}=\frac{b}{2}=\frac{c}{-3}=k$ (say) $\Rightarrow a=k, b=2 k$ and $c=-3 k$.
> 
> $\therefore$ required equation of the plane is $k(x-1)+2 k(y-2)-3 k(z+3)=0$
> 
> $\Rightarrow \quad(x-1)+2(y-2)-3(z+3)=0$.
> 
> $\Rightarrow \quad(x+2 y-3 z)+(-1-4-9)=0$
> 
> $\Rightarrow \quad x+2 y-3 z-14=0$.
> 
>
