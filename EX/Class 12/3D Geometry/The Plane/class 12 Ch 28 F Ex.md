### Answer to Question 10

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-F/Q-10

- Answer:

$\vec{r} \cdot(\hat{i}+\hat{j}+\hat{k})=a+b+c$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> We know that any plane parallel to $\vec{r} \cdot \vec{n}=q$ is $\vec{r} \cdot \vec{n}=q_{1}$.
> 
> Let the required plane parallel to the given plane be $\vec{r} \cdot(\hat{i}+\hat{j}+\hat{k})=q_{1}$
> 
> $\Rightarrow(x \hat{i}+y \hat{j}+z \hat{k}) \cdot(\hat{i}+\hat{j}+\hat{k})=q_{1}$
> 
> $\Rightarrow(a \hat{i}+b \hat{j}+c \hat{k}) \cdot(\hat{i}+\hat{j}+\hat{k})=q_{1}$
> 
> $\Rightarrow q_{1}=(a+b+c)$.
> 
> Hence, the required equation is $\vec{r} \cdot(\hat{i}+\hat{j}+\hat{k})=(a+b+c)$.
>

### Answer to Question 14

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-F/Q-14

- Answer:

$5 x-4 y-z-7=0, \sqrt{42}$ units



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Any plane through $A(1,-1,2)$ is given by
> 
> $$
> \begin{equation*}
> a(x-1)+b(y+1)+c(z-2)=0 . \tag{i}
> \end{equation*}
> $$
> 
> Since it is perpendicular to each of the planes $2 x+3 y-2 z=5$ and $x+2 y-3 z=8$, we have
> 
> $$
> \begin{align*}
> & 2 a+3 b-2 c=0  \tag{ii}\\
> & a+2 b-3 c=0 \tag{iii}
> \end{align*}
> $$
> 
> On solving (ii) and (iii) by cross multiplication, we have
> 
> $$
> \frac{a}{(-9+4)}=\frac{b}{(-2+6)}=\frac{c}{(4-3)}=\lambda \Rightarrow a=-5 \lambda, b=4 \lambda, c=\lambda .
> $$
> 
> Putting these value in (i), we get the required equation as
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & -5 \lambda(x-1)+4 \lambda(y+1)+\lambda(z-2)=0 \\
> \Rightarrow & 5(x-1)-4(y+1)-(z-2)=0 \Rightarrow 5 x-4 y-z-7=0 .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Distance of the point $P(-2,5,5)$ from this plane is given by
> 
> $$
> d=\frac{|5 \times(-2)-4 \times 5-5-7|}{\sqrt{5^{2}+(-4)^{2}+(-1)^{2}}}=\frac{|-42|}{\sqrt{42}}=\frac{42}{\sqrt{42}}=\sqrt{42} \text { units. }
> $$
> 
>

### Answer to Question 18

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-F/Q-18

- Answer:

$18 x+17 y+4 z=49$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Obtain the required equation of the plane as $18 x+17 y+4 z=49 \ldots$ (A)
> 
> The given line is $\vec{r}=(3 \lambda-1) \hat{i}+(3-2 \lambda) \hat{j}+(4-5 \lambda) \hat{k}$.
> 
> Coordinates of any point on this line are ( $3 \lambda-1,3-2 \lambda, 4-5 \lambda$ ).
> 
> These coordinates satisfy (A), as shown below:
> 
> LHS $=18(3 \lambda-1)+17(3-2 \lambda)+4(4-5 \lambda)=49=$ RHS .
> 
> So, $(3 \lambda-1,3-2 \lambda, 4-5 \lambda)$ lies on plane (A).
> 
> Hence, the plane (A) obtained above contains the given line.
>
