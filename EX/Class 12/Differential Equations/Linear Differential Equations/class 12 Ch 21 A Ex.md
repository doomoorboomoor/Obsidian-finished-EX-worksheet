### Answer to Question 08

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-21/Section-A/Q-08

- Answer:

$y=\frac{-1}{2} \sqrt{1-x^{2}} \log \left(1-x^{2}\right)+C \sqrt{1-x^{2}}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{d y}{d x}+\frac{x}{\left(1-x^{2}\right)} \cdot y=\frac{x}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}.$
> $\mathrm{IF}=e^{-\frac{1}{2} \int \frac{-2 x}{\left(1-x^{2}\right)} d x}=e^{-\frac{1}{2} \log \left(1-x^{2}\right)}=e^{\log \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}$
> $\therefore \quad$ its solution is
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> y \times \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}} & =\int \frac{x}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}} d x=\frac{-1}{2} \int \frac{-2 x}{\left(1-x^{2}\right)} d x+C \\
> & =-\frac{1}{2} \log \left(1-x^{2}\right)+C
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
>

### Answer to Question 09

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-21/Section-A/Q-09

- Answer:

$y=a+C \sqrt{1-x^{2}}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{d y}{d x}+\frac{x}{\left(1-x^{2}\right)} \cdot y=\frac{a x}{\left(1-x^{2}\right)}.$
> $$
> \mathrm{IF}=e^{-\frac{1}{2} \int \frac{-2 x}{\left(1-x^{2}\right)} d x}=e^{-\frac{1}{2} \log \left(1-x^{2}\right)}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}} .
> $$
> $\therefore$ its solution is given by
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> y \times \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}} & =\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}} \times \frac{a x}{\left(1-x^{2}\right)} d x+C \\
> & =-\frac{a}{2} \int \frac{-2 x}{\left(1-x^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}} d x+C=-\frac{a}{2} \int \frac{d t}{t^{3 / 2}}+C, \text{ where }\left(1-x^{2}\right)=t \\
> & =-\frac{a}{2} \int t^{-3 / 2} d t+C=\frac{a}{\sqrt{t}}+C=\frac{a}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}+C
> \end{aligned}
> $$
>

### Answer to Question 11

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-21/Section-A/Q-11

- Answer:

$y=2 e^{x}+C e^{-2 x}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{d y}{d x}-\frac{1}{x} \cdot y=\frac{(x-1)}{x} e^{x}.$
> $$
> \mathrm{IF}=e^{-\int \frac{1}{x} d x}=e^{-\log x}=e^{\log (1 / x)}=\frac{1}{x}
> $$
> $\therefore$ its solution is given by
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> y \times \frac{1}{x} & =\int e^{x}\left(\frac{x-1}{x^{2}}\right) d x+C=\int e^{x}\left(\frac{1}{x}-\frac{1}{x^{2}}\right) d x+C \\
> & =e^{x} \cdot \frac{1}{x}+C. \quad \left[\because \int e^{x}\left\{f(x)+f^{\prime}(x)\right\} d x=e^{x} f(x)\right]
> \end{aligned}
> $$
>

### Answer to Question 50

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-21/Section-A/Q-50

- Answer:

$y=\log \left|\frac{2 x+1}{x+1}\right|$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $(x+1) \frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{\left(2-e^{y}\right)}{e^{y}} \Rightarrow \frac{d x}{d y}=\frac{(x+1) e^{y}}{\left(2-e^{y}\right)}$
> $\therefore \quad \frac{d x}{d y}-\frac{e^{y}}{\left(2-e^{y}\right)} \cdot x=\frac{e^{y}}{\left(2-e^{y}\right)}.$
> $\mathrm{IF}=e^{\int \frac{-e^{y}}{\left(2-e^{y}\right)} d y}=e^{\log \left(2-e^{y}\right)}=\left(2-e^{y}\right).$
> $\therefore \quad x \times\left(2-e^{y}\right)=\int \frac{e^{y}}{\left(2-e^{y}\right)} \times\left(2-e^{y}\right) d y+C=\int e^{y} d y+C=e^{y}+C.$
> Now, $y=0$ and $x=0 \Rightarrow C=-1.$
> $\therefore x\left(2-e^{y}\right)=e^{y}-1 \Rightarrow(2 x+1)=(x+1) e^{y}$
> $\therefore \quad e^{y}=\frac{(2 x+1)}{(x+1)} \Rightarrow y=\log \left(\frac{2 x+1}{x+1}\right).$
> 
>

### Answer to Question 51

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-21/Section-A/Q-51

- Answer:

$x e^{\tan ^{-1} y}=\tan ^{-1} y$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\left(1+y^{2}\right) d x=\left(e^{-\tan ^{-1} y}-x\right) d y \Rightarrow \frac{d x}{d y}=\frac{e^{-\tan ^{-1} y}-x}{\left(1+y^{2}\right)}.$
> $\therefore \quad \frac{d x}{d y}+\frac{1}{\left(1+y^{2}\right)} \cdot x=\frac{e^{-\tan ^{-1} y}}{\left(1+y^{2}\right)}.$
> $\mathrm{IF}=e^{\int \frac{1}{\left(1+y^{2}\right)} d y}=e^{\tan ^{-1} y}.$
> $$
> \begin{equation*}
> \therefore \quad x \times e^{\tan ^{-1} y}=\int\left\{\frac{e^{-\tan ^{-1} y}}{\left(1+y^{2}\right)} \times e^{\tan ^{-1} y}\right\} d y=C=\int \frac{1}{\left(1+y^{2}\right)} d y+C \tag{i}
> \end{equation*}
> $$
> $\Rightarrow \quad x \times e^{\tan ^{-1} y}=\tan ^{-1} y+C.$
> Putting $x=0$ and $y=0$ in (i), we get $C=0.$
> 
>
