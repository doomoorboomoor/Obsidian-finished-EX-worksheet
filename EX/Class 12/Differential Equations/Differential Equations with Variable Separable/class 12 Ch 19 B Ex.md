### Answer to Question 07



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-19/Section-B/Q-07



- Answer:



$y=1+C_{1} x y$







> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\int \frac{1}{y(y-1)} d y=\int \frac{1}{x} d x \Rightarrow \int\left\{\frac{1}{(y-1)}-\frac{1}{y}\right\} d y=\int \frac{1}{x} d x \quad$ [by partial fractions]
> 
> $\Rightarrow \log |y-1|-\log |y|=\log |x|+\log |C|$
> 
> $\Rightarrow(y-1)= \pm C x y \Rightarrow(y-1)=C_{1} x y \Rightarrow y=1+C_{1} x y.$
> 
>

### Answer to Question 10



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-19/Section-B/Q-10



- Answer:



$x=C \log y$







> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\int \frac{1}{x} d x=\int \frac{1}{y \log y} d y \Rightarrow \int \frac{1}{x} d x=\int \frac{d t}{t}$, where $\log y=t$.
> 
> $\therefore \log |x|=\log |t|+\log \left|C_{1}\right| \Rightarrow \log |x|-\log |\log y|=\log \left|C_{1}\right|$
> 
> $\Rightarrow \log \left|\frac{x}{\log y}\right|=\log C_{1} \Rightarrow \frac{x}{\log y}= \pm C_{1}=C .$
> 
>
