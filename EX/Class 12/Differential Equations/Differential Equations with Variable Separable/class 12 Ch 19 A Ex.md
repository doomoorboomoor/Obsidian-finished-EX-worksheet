### Answer to Question 13

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-3/Chapter-19/Section-A/Q-13

- Answer:

$\cos \left(\frac{y-2}{x}\right)=a$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{d y}{d x}=\cos ^{-1} a \Rightarrow \int d y=\int\left(\cos ^{-1} a\right) d x \Rightarrow y=\left(\cos ^{-1} a\right) x+C$.
> 
> Putting $x=0$ and $y=2$ in (i), we get $C=2$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad \cos ^{-1} a=\left(\frac{y-2}{x}\right) \Rightarrow \cos \left(\frac{y-2}{x}\right)=a$.
> 
>
