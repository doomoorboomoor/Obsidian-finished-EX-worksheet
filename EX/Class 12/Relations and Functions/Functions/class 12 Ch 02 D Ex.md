\begin{aligned}
y=f(x) & \Rightarrow y=2 x+3 \\

### Answer to Question 06



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-1/Chapter-02/Section-D/Q-06



- Answer:



$f^{-1}(y)=\frac{(4 y+3)}{6 y-4}$







> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $f\left(x_{1}\right)=f\left(x_{2}\right) \Rightarrow \frac{4 x_{1}+3}{6 x_{1}-4}=\frac{4 x_{2}+3}{6 x_{2}-4}$
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow\left(4 x_{1}+3\right)\left(6 x_{2}-4\right)=\left(6 x_{1}-4\right)\left(4 x_{2}+3\right) \\
& \Rightarrow 24 x_{1} x_{2}-16 x_{1}+18 x_{2}-12=24 x_{1} x_{2}+18 x_{1}-16 x_{2}-12 \\
& \Rightarrow 34 x_{1}=34 x_{2} \Rightarrow x_{1}=x_{2} .
\end{aligned}
$$
> 
> $\therefore \quad f$ is one-one.
> 
> Let $y$ be an arbitrary element of $A$. Then,
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
f(x)=y & \Rightarrow \frac{4 x+3}{6 x-4}=y \\
& \Rightarrow(6 x-4) y=4 x+3 \Rightarrow 6 x y-4 y=4 x+3 \\
& \Rightarrow 6 x y-4 x=4 y+3 \Rightarrow x(6 y-4)=(4 y+3) \\
& \Rightarrow x=\frac{4 y+3}{6 y-4}
\end{aligned}
$$
> 
> Thus, for each $y \in A$, there exists $x=\frac{4 y+3}{6 y-4}$ such that
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
f(x) & =f\left(\frac{4 y+3}{6 y-4}\right)=\frac{4 \cdot\left(\frac{4 y+3}{6 y-4}\right)+3}{6 \cdot\left(\frac{4 y+3}{6 y-4}\right)-4} \\
& =\frac{16 y+12+18 y-12}{24 y+18-24 y+16}=\frac{34 y}{34}=y .
\end{aligned}
$$
> 
> $\therefore f$ is onto.
> 
> Now, $f(x)=y \Rightarrow x=f^{-1}(y)$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad f^{-1}(y)=\frac{4 y+3}{6 y-4}$, for all $y \in A$.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 07



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-1/Chapter-02/Section-D/Q-07



- Answer:



$f^{-1}(y)=\frac{(4 y)}{(4-3 y)}$







> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $f\left(x_{1}\right)=f\left(x_{2}\right) \Rightarrow \frac{4 x_{1}}{3 x_{1}+4}=\frac{4 x_{2}}{3 x_{2}+4} \Rightarrow \frac{x_{1}}{3 x_{1}+4}=\frac{x_{2}}{3 x_{2}+4}$
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
& \Rightarrow x_{1}\left(3 x_{2}+4\right)=x_{2}\left(3 x_{1}+4\right) \\
& \Rightarrow 3 x_{1} x_{2}+4 x_{1}=3 x_{1} x_{2}+4 x_{2} \\
& \Rightarrow 4 x_{1}=4 x_{2} \Rightarrow x_{1}=x_{2}
\end{aligned}
$$
> 
> $\therefore \quad f$ is one-one.
> 
> Let $y$ be an arbitrary element of $A$. Then,
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
f(x)=y & \Rightarrow \frac{4 x}{3 x+4}=y \\
& \Rightarrow 3 x y+4 y=4 x \Rightarrow 4 x-3 x y=4 y \\
& \Rightarrow x(4-3 y)=4 y \Rightarrow x=\frac{4 y}{(4-3 y)}
\end{aligned}
$$
> 
> Thus, for each $y \in A$, there exists an $x \in A$ such that
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
f(x) & =f\left(\frac{4 y}{4-3 y}\right)=\frac{4 \cdot\left(\frac{4 y}{4-3 y}\right)}{3 \cdot\left(\frac{4 y}{4-3 y}\right)+4} \\
& =\frac{16 y}{12 y+16-12 y}=\frac{16 y}{16}=y
\end{aligned}
$$
> 
> $\therefore \quad f$ is onto.
> 
> Now, $f(x)=y \Rightarrow x=f^{-1}(y)$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad f^{-1}(y)=\frac{4 y}{4-3 y}$.
> 
>
