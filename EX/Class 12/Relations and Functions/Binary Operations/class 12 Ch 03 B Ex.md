### Answer to Question 04/i

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-1/Chapter-03/Section-B/Q-04/Q-i

- Answer:

0

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $e$ be the identity element. Then, for all $a \in Q-\{1\}$, we have
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> a * e=a & \Rightarrow a+e-a e=a \\
> & \Rightarrow e(1-a)=0 \Rightarrow e=0 \quad[\because a \neq 1] .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> $\therefore \quad 0$ is the identity element.
>

### Answer to Question 04/ii

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-1/Chapter-03/Section-B/Q-04/Q-ii

- Answer:

$a^{-1}=\frac{a}{(a-1)}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $a \in Q-\{1\}$ be an arbitrary element and let $b$ be its inverse.
> 
> Then, $a * b=0 \Rightarrow a+b-a b=0 \Rightarrow a b-b=a$
> 
> $$
> \Rightarrow \quad b(a-1)=a \Rightarrow \quad b=\frac{a}{(a-1)} .
> $$
> 
> Thus, each $a \in Q-\{1\}$ has $\frac{a}{(a-1)}$ as its inverse.
>

### Answer to Question 05/i

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-12/Unit-1/Chapter-03/Section-B/Q-05/Q-i

- Answer:



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> For all $a, b, c \in Q_{0}$, we have
> 
> $$
> a * b=\frac{a b}{4}=\frac{b a}{4}=b * a .
> $$
> 
> And, $(a * b) * c=\frac{a b}{4} * c=\frac{\frac{a b}{4} \cdot c}{4}=\frac{(a b) c}{16}$.
> 
> Also, $a *(b * c)=a * \frac{b c}{4}=\frac{a\left(\frac{b c}{4}\right)}{4}=\frac{a(b c)}{16}$.
> 
> But, $(a b) c=a(b c)$. Hence, $(a * b) * c=a *(b * c)$.
>
