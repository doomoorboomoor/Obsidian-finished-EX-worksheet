### Answer to Question 07

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-B/Q-07

- Answer:

$\frac{-5}{x^{6}}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $y=x^{-5} \Rightarrow(y+\delta y)(x+\delta x)^{-5}$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \Rightarrow \frac{\delta y}{\delta x} & =\frac{(x+\delta x)^{-5}-x^{-5}}{\delta x} \\
> \therefore \frac{d y}{d x} & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\delta y}{\delta x} \\
> & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{(x+\delta x)^{-5}-x^{-5}}{\delta x}=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{x^{-5}\left\{\left(1+\frac{\delta x}{x}\right)^{-5}-1\right\}}{\delta x} \\
> & =\frac{1}{x^{5}} \cdot \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\left\{\left(1+\frac{\delta x}{x}\right)^{-5}-1\right\}}{\delta x} \\
> & =\frac{1}{x^{5}} \cdot \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\left\{1+(-5) \cdot \frac{\delta x}{x}+\frac{(-5)(-6)}{2} \cdot\left(\frac{\delta x}{x}\right)^{2}+\ldots-1\right\}}{\delta x} \\
> & =\frac{1}{x^{5}} \cdot \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\left\{(-5) \cdot \frac{\delta x}{x}+15 \cdot\left(\frac{\delta x}{x}\right)^{2}+\ldots\right\}}{\delta x} \\
> & =\frac{1}{x^{6}} \cdot \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0}\left[(-5)+15\left(\frac{\delta x}{x^{2}}\right)+\ldots\right] \\
> & =\frac{-5}{x^{6}} \cdot
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Hence, $\frac{d}{d x}\left(x^{-5}\right)=\frac{-5}{x^{6}}$.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 09

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-B/Q-09

- Answer:

$\frac{5}{2 \sqrt{5 x-4}}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $y=\sqrt{5 x-4} \Rightarrow(y+\delta y)=\sqrt{5(x+\delta x)-4}$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \quad \Rightarrow \frac{\delta y}{\delta x}=\frac{\sqrt{5(x+\delta x)-4}-\sqrt{5 x-4}}{\delta x} \\
> & \therefore \frac{d y}{d x}=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\delta y}{\delta x} \\
> & \quad=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sqrt{(5(x+\delta x)-4}-\sqrt{5 x-4}}{\delta x} \\
> & \quad=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\{\sqrt{5(x+\delta x)-4}-\sqrt{5 x-4}\}}{\delta x} \times \frac{\{\sqrt{5(x+\delta x)-4}+\sqrt{5 x-4}\}}{\{\sqrt{5(x+\delta x)-4}+\sqrt{5 x-4}\}} \\
> & \quad=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\{5(x+\delta x)-4\}-\{5 x-4\}}{\delta x\{\sqrt{5(x+\delta x)-4}+\sqrt{5 x-4}\}} \\
> & \quad=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{5}{\{\sqrt{5(x+\delta x)-4}+\sqrt{5 x-4}\}}=\frac{5}{2 \sqrt{5 x-4}} .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Hence, $\frac{d}{d x}(\sqrt{5 x-4})=\frac{5}{2 \sqrt{5 x-4}}$.
>

$\frac{-1}{2(x+2)^{3 / 2}}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $y=\frac{1}{\sqrt{x+2}} \Rightarrow(y+\delta y)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{x+\delta x+2}}$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \quad \Rightarrow \frac{\delta y}{\delta x}=\left\{\frac{1}{\sqrt{x+\delta x+2}}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{x+2}}\right\} \cdot \frac{1}{\delta x} \\
> & \therefore \frac{d y}{d x}=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\delta y}{\delta x} \\
> & \quad=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{\delta x}\left\{\frac{1}{\sqrt{x+\delta x+2}}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{x+2}}\right\} \\
> & \quad=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{\delta x} \cdot \frac{(\sqrt{x+2}-\sqrt{x+\delta x+2})(\sqrt{x+2}+\sqrt{x+\delta x+2})}{(\sqrt{x+\delta x+2})(\sqrt{x+2})(\sqrt{x+2}+\sqrt{x+\delta x+2})} \\
> & \quad=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\{(x+2)-(x+\delta x+2)\}}{\delta x(\sqrt{x+\delta x+2})(\sqrt{x+2})} \cdot \frac{1}{(\sqrt{x+2}+\sqrt{x+\delta x+2})} \\
> & \quad=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{-1}{(\sqrt{x+\delta x+2})(\sqrt{x+2})} \cdot \frac{1}{(\sqrt{x+2}+\sqrt{x+\delta x+2})} \\
> & \quad=\frac{-1}{2(x+2)^{3 / 2}} \cdot
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Hence, $\frac{d}{d x}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{x+2}}\right)=\frac{-1}{2(x+2)^{3 / 2}}$.

- Answer:

$\frac{3}{2(2-3 x)^{3 / 2}}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $y=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2-3 x}} \Rightarrow(y+\delta y)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2-3(x+\delta x)}}$
> 
> $$
> \begin{array}{r}
> \Rightarrow \frac{\delta y}{\delta x}=\frac{1}{\delta x} \cdot\left\{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2-3(x+\delta x})}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2-3 x}}\right\} \\
> \therefore \quad \frac{d y}{d x}=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\delta y}{\delta x} \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{\delta x} \cdot\left\{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2-3(x+\delta x)}}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2-3 x}}\right\}
> \end{array}
> $$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{\delta x} \cdot \frac{\{\sqrt{2-3 x}-\sqrt{2-3(x+\delta x)}}{\{\sqrt{2-3(x+\delta x)}\}\{\sqrt{2-3 x}\}} \cdot \frac{\{\sqrt{2-3 x}+\sqrt{2-3(x+\delta x)}\}}{\{\sqrt{2-3 x}+\sqrt{2-3(x+\delta x)}\}} \\
> & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{[(2-3 x)-\{2-3(x+\delta x)\}]}{\{\sqrt{2-3(x+\delta x)}\}\{\sqrt{2-3 x}\}} \cdot \frac{1}{\{\sqrt{2-3 x}+\sqrt{2-3(x+\delta x)}\}} \cdot \frac{1}{\delta x} \\
> & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{3}{\{\sqrt{2-3(x+\delta x)}\}\{\sqrt{2-3 x}\}} \cdot \frac{1}{\{\sqrt{2-3 x}+\sqrt{2-3 x(x+\delta x)}\}} \\
> & =\frac{3}{2(2-3 x) \sqrt{2-3 x}}=\frac{3}{2(2-3 x)^{3 / 2}} \cdot
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Hence, $\frac{d}{d x}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2-3 x}}\right)=\frac{3}{2(2-3 x)^{3 / 2}}$.

### Answer to Question 17

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-B/Q-17

- Answer:

$\frac{-3}{2} \cdot \frac{\sin 3 x}{\sqrt{\cos 3 x}}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $y=\sqrt{\sec x} \Rightarrow y+\delta y=\sqrt{\sec (x+\delta x)}$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \Rightarrow \delta y=\sqrt{\sec (x+\delta x)}-\sqrt{\sec x} . \\
> \therefore \frac{d y}{d x}= & \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\delta y}{\delta x} \\
> = & \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sqrt{\sec (x+\delta x)}-\sqrt{\sec x}}{\delta x}=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\left\{\frac{1}{\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)}}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{\cos x}}\right)}{\delta x} \\
> = & \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0}\left\{\frac{[\sqrt{\cos x}-\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)}]}{\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)} \cdot \sqrt{\cos x}} \cdot \frac{[\sqrt{\cos x}+\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)}}{\sqrt{\cos x}+\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)}} \cdot \frac{1}{\delta x}\right\} \\
> = & \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0}\left[\frac{[\cos x-\cos (x+\delta x)]}{\delta x[\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)} \cdot \sqrt{\cos x}} \cdot \frac{1}{[\sqrt{\cos x}+\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)}]}\right] \\
> = & \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin \left(x+\frac{\delta x}{2}\right) \sin \frac{\delta x}{2}}{\delta x \cdot[\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)} \cdot \sqrt{\cos x}] \cdot[\sqrt{\cos x}+\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)}]}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \sin \left(x+\frac{\delta x}{2}\right) \cdot \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin \left(\frac{\delta x}{2}\right)}{\left(\frac{\delta x}{2}\right)} . \\
> & \quad \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{\{\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)} \cdot \sqrt{\cos x}\}\{\sqrt{\cos x}+\sqrt{\cos (x+\delta x)}]} \\
> & =\left[\sin x \times 1 \times \frac{1}{(\cos x) \cdot 2 \sqrt{\cos x}}\right]=\frac{1}{2} \tan x \sqrt{\sec x} .
> \end{aligned}
> $$

### Answer to Question 19

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-4/Chapter-28/Section-B/Q-19

- Answer:

$2 \tan x \sec ^{2} x$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $y=\tan ^{2} x \Rightarrow y+\delta y=\tan ^{2}(x+\delta x)$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \Rightarrow \delta y=\tan ^{2}(x+\delta x)-\tan ^{2} x \\
> & \Rightarrow \frac{\delta y}{\delta x}=\frac{\tan ^{2}(x+\delta x)-\tan ^{2} x}{\delta x} . \\
> & \therefore \frac{d y}{d x}=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\delta y}{\delta x} \\
> & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\left\{\frac{\sin ^{2}(x+\delta x)}{\cos ^{2}(x+\delta x)}-\frac{\sin ^{2} x}{\cos ^{2} x}\right\}}{\delta x} \\
> & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin ^{2}(x+\delta x) \cos ^{2} x-\sin ^{2} x \cos ^{2}(x+\delta x)}{\delta x\left[\cos ^{2}(x+\delta x) \cdot \cos ^{2} x\right]} \\
> & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{[\sin (x+\delta x) \cos x+\sin x \cos (x+\delta x)][\sin (x+\delta x) \cos x-\sin x \cos (x+\delta x)]}{\delta x\left[\cos ^{2}(x+\delta x) \cdot \cos ^{2} x\right]} \\
> & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin (x+\delta x+x) \cdot \sin (x+\delta x-x)}{\delta x\left[\cos ^{2}(x+\delta x) \cdot \cos ^{2} x\right]}=\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin (2 x+\delta x) \cdot \sin \delta x}{\delta x\left[\cos ^{2}(x+\delta x) \cdot \cos ^{2} x\right]} \\
> & =\lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin (2 x+\delta x)}{\cos ^{2}(x+\delta x) \cdot \cos ^{2} x} \cdot \lim _{\delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin \delta x}{\delta x} \\
> & =\left(\frac{\sin 2 x}{\cos ^{4} x} \times 1\right)=\frac{2 \sin x \cos x}{\cos ^{4} x}=2 \tan x \sec ^{2} x .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
>
