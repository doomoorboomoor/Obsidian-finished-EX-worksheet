### Answer to Question 09

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-21/Section-A/Q-09

- Answer:

$\left(x^{2}+y^{2}-12 y+11=0\right)$ or $\left(x^{2}+y^{2}+4 y-21=0\right)$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let the centre of the circle be $C(0, k)$.
> 
> It is given that the circle passes through $P(3,2)$.
> Then, $|C P|^{2}=5^{2} \Leftrightarrow(0-3)^{2}+(2-k)^{2}=5^{2}$
> $$\Leftrightarrow(2-k)= \pm 4 \Leftrightarrow k=6 \text{ or } k=-2 .$$
> $\therefore \quad$ the centre of the circle is $(0,6)$ or $(0,-2)$.
> Hence, the equation of the circle is
> $$\left[(x-0)^{2}+(y-6)^{2}=5^{2}\right] \text{ or } \left[(x-0)^{2}+(y+2)^{2}=5^{2}\right]$$
> 
>

### Answer to Question 12

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-21/Section-A/Q-12

- Answer:

$4 x^{2}+4 y^{2}-88 x-16 y+451=0$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> We know that the point of intersection of two diameters of a circle is the centre of the circle.
> On solving $x-y=9$ and $x-2 y=7$, we get the centre as $C(11,2)$.
> Now, $\pi r^{2}=\frac{77}{2} \Leftrightarrow\left|r^{2}\right|=\left(\frac{77}{2} \times \frac{7}{22}\right)=\frac{49}{4} \Leftrightarrow r=\frac{7}{2}$.
> $\therefore \quad$ the required equation is $(x-11)^{2}+(y-2)^{2}=\frac{49}{4}$.
> 
>
