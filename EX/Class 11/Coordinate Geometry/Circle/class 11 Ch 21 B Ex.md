### Answer to Question 14

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-21/Section-B/Q-14

- Answer:

$x^{2}+y^{2}-4 x-2 y+4=0, x^{2}+y^{2}-2 x-4 y+4=0$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let the centre of the circle be $C(h, k)$.
> 
> Then, $|C A|=1$ and $|C B|=1$
> 
> $\Rightarrow(h-1)^{2}+(k-1)^{2}=1^{2}$ and $(h-2)^{2}+(k-2)^{2}=1^{2}$
> 
> $$
\begin{equation*}
\Rightarrow h^{2}+k^{2}-2 h-2 k+1=0 \tag{i}
\end{equation*}
$$
> 
> and $h^{2}+k^{2}-4 h-4 k+7=0$.
> 
> Subtracting (ii) from (i), we get
> 
> $$
2 h+2 k-6=0 \Rightarrow h+k=3 \Rightarrow k=(3-h)
$$
> 
> Putting this value in (i), we get
> 
> $$
\begin{array}{ll} 
& h^{2}+(3-h)^{2}-2 h-2(3-h)+1=0 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & (h-2)(h-1)=0 \Rightarrow h=2 \text{ or } h=1 . \\
\therefore \quad & k=1 \text{ or } k=2 .
\end{array}
$$
> 
> Hence, the centres are $(2,1)$ and $(1,2)$.
> 
>
