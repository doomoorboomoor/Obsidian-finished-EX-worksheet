### Answer to Question 15

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-26/Section-B/Q-15

- Answer:



$\left(0, \frac{31}{16}, \frac{-3}{16}\right)$





> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Any point in the $y z$-plane is of the form $P(0, y, z)$.
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & |A P|=|B P|=|C P| \Leftrightarrow A P^{2}=B P^{2} \text{ and } B P^{2}=C P^{2} . \\
> & (0-3)^{2}+(y-2)^{2}+(z+1)^{2}=(0-1)^{2}+(y+1)^{2}+(z-0)^{2} \\
> & (0-1)^{2}+(y+1)^{2}+(z-0)^{2}=(0-2)^{2}+(y-1)^{2}+(z-2)^{2}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> $\Rightarrow 3 y-z-6=0$ and $4 y+4 z-7=0$.
> On solving, we get $y=\frac{31}{16}$ and $z=\frac{-3}{16}$.
> Hence, the required point is $\left(0, \frac{31}{16}, \frac{-3}{16}\right)$.
> 
>
