### Answer to Question 05



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-25/Section-A/Q-05



- Answer:



$2 \sqrt{6} \mathrm{~m}$







> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> The beam takes the shape of a parabola, whose equation is of the form $x^{2}=4 a y$. Since the point $P\left(6, \frac{3}{100}\right)$ lies on it, we have
> 
> $$
> \left(4 \times a \times \frac{3}{100}\right)=36 \Leftrightarrow a=300 \mathrm{~m} .
> $$
> 
> Let $A B$ be the deflection. Then,
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & A B=\frac{1}{100} \mathrm{~m} \text { and } B \text { is } B\left(x, \frac{2}{100}\right) . \\
> \therefore & x^{2}=4 \times 300 \times \frac{2}{100} \Leftrightarrow x=2 \sqrt{6} \mathrm{~m} .
> \end{aligned}
> $$ $\square$
> 
>
