### Answer to Question 21

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-3/Chapter-23/Section-A/Q-21

- Answer:

$16 x^{2}+7 y^{2}=688$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Since the centre of the ellipse lies at the origin and its foci lie on the $y$-axis, it is a vertical ellipse.
> Let its equation be $\frac{x^{2}}{b^{2}}+\frac{y^{2}}{a^{2}}=1$.
> Let $c^{2}=a^{2}-b^{2}$.
> Now, $e=\frac{3}{4} \Leftrightarrow \frac{c}{a}=\frac{3}{4} \Leftrightarrow c=\frac{3}{4} a$.
> $\therefore b^{2}=\left(a^{2}-c^{2}\right)=\left(a^{2}-\frac{9}{16} a^{2}\right)=\frac{7 a^{2}}{16}$.
> So, the equation of the ellipse is
> 
> $$
> \begin{equation*}
> \frac{x^{2}}{\frac{7 a^{2}}{16}}+\frac{y^{2}}{a^{2}}=1 \Leftrightarrow 16 x^{2}+7 y^{2}=7 a^{2} \tag{ii}
> \end{equation*}
> $$
> 
> Since it passes through $(6,4)$, putting $x=6$ and $y=4$ in (ii), we get
> 
> $$
> 7 a^{2}=688
> $$
> 
> Hence, the required equation is $16 x^{2}+7 y^{2}=688$.
>
