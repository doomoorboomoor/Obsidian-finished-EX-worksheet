### Answer to Question 09

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-09/Section-A/Q-09

- Answer:

$n=9, r=3$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{{ }^{n} C_{r-1}}{{ }^{n} C_{r}}=\frac{r}{n-r+1}$ and $\frac{{ }^{n} C_{r}}{{ }^{n} C_{r+1}}=\frac{r+1}{n-r}$.
> 
> $$
> \begin{equation*}
> \therefore \quad \frac{r}{n-r+1}=\frac{36}{84}=\frac{3}{7} \Rightarrow 3 n-3 r+3=7 r \Rightarrow 3 n-10 r=-3 \tag{i}
> \end{equation*}
> $$
> 
> and $\frac{r+1}{n-r}=\frac{84}{126}=\frac{2}{3} \Rightarrow 2 n-2 r=3 r+3 \Rightarrow 2 n-5 r=3$.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 10

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-09/Section-A/Q-10

- Answer:

$n=10, r=5$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Use $\frac{{ }^{n} C_{r}}{{ }^{n-1} C_{r-1}}=\frac{n}{r}$ and $\frac{{ }^{n+1} C_{r+1}}{{ }^{n} C_{r}}=\frac{n+1}{r+1}$.

> Solve $n-2 r=0$ and $6 n-11 r=5$.
