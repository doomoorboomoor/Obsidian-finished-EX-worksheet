- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-08/Section-C/Q-06

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{15!}{\{15-(r-1)\}!}: \frac{16!}{\{(16-(r-2)\}!}=3: 4 \Rightarrow \frac{15!}{(16-r)!}: \frac{16!}{(18-r)!}=3: 4$.
> $$
> \begin{array}{ll}
> \therefore & \frac{15!}{(16-r)!} \times \frac{(18-r)!}{16!}=\frac{3}{4} \Rightarrow \frac{15!}{(16-r)!} \times \frac{(18-r)(17-r) \cdot[(16-r)!]}{16 \times(15!)}=\frac{3}{4} . \\
> \therefore & (18-r)(17-r)=12 \Rightarrow r^{2}-35 r+294=0 \Rightarrow(r-14)(r-21)=0 \Rightarrow r=14
> \end{array}
> $$
> $[\because r \neq 21]$.

### Answer to Question 07

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-08/Section-C/Q-07

- Answer:

$n=10$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{(2 n-1)!}{(2 n-1-n)!}: \frac{(2 n+1)!}{\{(2 n+1)-(n-1)\}!}=\frac{22}{7}$
> $\Rightarrow \quad \frac{(2 n-1)!}{(n-1)!} \times \frac{(n+2)!}{(2 n+1)!}=\frac{22}{7} \Rightarrow \frac{(2 n-1)!}{(n-1)!} \times \frac{(n+2)(n+1) n \cdot[(n-1)!]}{(2 n+1)(2 n) \cdot[(2 n-1)!]}=\frac{22}{7}$
> $\Rightarrow 7(n+2)(n+1)=44(2 n+1) \Rightarrow 7 n^{2}-67 n-30=0 \Rightarrow(n-10)(7 n+3)=0$.
> Hence, $n=10$.
>
