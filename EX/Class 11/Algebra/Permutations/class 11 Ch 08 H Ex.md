- Answer:

$x=36$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{1}{4!}+\frac{1}{5 \times(4!)}=\frac{x}{(6 \times 5) \times(4!)} \Rightarrow \frac{x}{30}=\left(1+\frac{1}{5}\right)=\frac{6}{5} \Rightarrow x=\left(\frac{6}{5} \times 30\right)=36$.

### Answer to Question 05

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-08/Section-H/Q-05

- Answer:

25

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Given digits are $4,5,6,7,8$ and repetition of digits is allowed.
> 
> Clearly, the required number must have 4 at the thousand's place.
> 
> So, this place can be filled in 1 way.
> 
> Since each number is divisible by 5 , so we must have 5 at the unit's place. So, unit's place can be filled in 1 way.
> 
> Each of the hundred's and ten's places can be filled in 5 ways.
> 
> $\therefore \quad$ the required number of numbers $=(1 \times 1 \times 5 \times 5)=25$.
>
