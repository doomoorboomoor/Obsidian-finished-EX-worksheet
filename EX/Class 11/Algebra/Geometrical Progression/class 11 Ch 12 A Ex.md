### Answer to Question 14

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-12/Section-A/Q-14

- Answer:



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $A$ be the first term and $R$ be the common ratio of the given GP. Then,
> 
> $$
> \begin{align*}
> & \quad a=A R^{(p-1)} \Rightarrow \log a=\log A+(p-1) \log R  \tag{i}\\
> & \quad b=A R^{(q-1)} \Rightarrow \log b=\log A+(q-1) \log R  \tag{ii}\\
> & \quad c=A R^{(r-1)} \Rightarrow \log c=\log A+(r-1) \log R .  \tag{iii}\\
> & \therefore \quad(q-r) \log a+(r-p) \log b+(p-q) \log c \\
> & \quad=(q-r) \log \left[A R^{(p-1)}\right]+(r-p) \log \left\{A R^{(q-1)}\right]+(p-q) \log \left[A R^{(r-1)}\right] \\
> & \quad=(\log A)\{(q-r)+(r-p)+(p-q)\}+(\log R)\{(p-1)(q-r)+(q-1)(r-p) \\
> & \quad+(r-1)(p-q)\} \\
> & \quad=(\log A) \times 0+(\log R) \times 0=0 .
> \end{align*}
> $$
>

### Answer to Question 16

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-12/Section-A/Q-16

- Answer:



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $a$ be the first term, $r$ be the common ratio and $l$ be the last term of a GP containing $n$ terms. Then,
> 
> ( $p$ th term from the beginning) $\times(p$ th term from the end)
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & =(p \text { th term from the beginning }) \times\{(n-p+1) \text { th term from the beginning }\} \\
> & =T_{p} \times T_{(n-p+1)}=a r^{p-1} \times a r^{(n-p+1)-1}=a r^{(p-1)} \times a r^{(n-p)} \\
> & =a \times\left(a r^{n-1}\right)=\left(T_{1} \times T_{n}\right)=(\text { first term } \times \text { last term }) .
> \end{aligned}
> $$

### Answer to Question 17

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-12/Section-A/Q-17

- Answer:



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{a+b x}{a-b x}=\frac{b+c x}{b-c x} \Rightarrow \frac{(a+b x)+(a-b x)}{(a+b x)-(a-b x)}=\frac{(b+c x)+(b-c x)}{(b+c x)-(b-c x)} \Rightarrow \frac{b}{a}=\frac{c}{b}$.
> 
> Similarly, $\frac{b+c x}{b-c x}=\frac{c+d x}{c-d x} \Rightarrow \frac{c}{b}=\frac{d}{c}$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad \frac{b}{a}=\frac{c}{b}=\frac{d}{c} \Rightarrow a, b, c, d$ are in GP.
>

### Answer to Question 18

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-12/Section-A/Q-18

- Answer:



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $r$ be the common ratio of the given GP.
> 
> Then, $b=a r, c=a r^{2}$ and $d=a r^{3}$.
> 
> Also, $a+b=3, a b=p, c+d=12$ and $c d=q$.
> 
> Now, $a+b=3, c+d=12 \Rightarrow a(1+r)=3$ and $a r^{2}(1+r)=12$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \Rightarrow \frac{a r^{2}(1+r)}{a(1+r)}=4 \Rightarrow r^{2}=4 \Rightarrow r=2 \\
> & \Rightarrow a(1+2)=3 \Rightarrow a=1 .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> $\therefore \quad p=a b=a \times a r=a^{2} r=\left(1^{2} \times 2\right)=2$ and $q=c d=a r^{2} \times a r^{3}=a^{2} r^{5}=(1)^{2} \times 2^{5}=32$.
> 
> Hence, $\frac{q+p}{q-p}=\frac{32+2}{32-2}=\frac{34}{30}=\frac{17}{15}$.
> 
>
