### Answer to Question 09

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-06/Section-B/Q-09

- Answer:

$x \in(-\infty, 2) \cup(5, \infty)$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{3}{x-2}-1<0 \Rightarrow \frac{3-x+2}{x-2}<0 \Rightarrow \frac{5-x}{x-2}<0$.
> $\therefore \quad(5-x<0$ and $x-2>0)$ or $(5-x>0$ and $x-2<0)$
> $\Rightarrow \quad(x>5$ and $x>2)$ or $(x<5$ and $x<2)$
> $\Rightarrow \quad(x>5$ or $x<2) \Rightarrow x \in(-\infty, 2) \cup(5, \infty)$.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 10

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-06/Section-B/Q-10

- Answer:

$x \in(-\infty,-5) \cup(5, \infty)$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{x}{x-5}-\frac{1}{2}>0 \Rightarrow \frac{2 x-x+5}{2(x-5)}>0 \Rightarrow \frac{x+5}{x-5}>0$.
> $\therefore \quad(x+5<0$ and $x-5<0)$ or $(x+5>0$ and $x-5>0)$
> $\Rightarrow \quad(x<-5$ and $x<5)$ or $(x>-5$ and $x>5)$
> $\Rightarrow \quad(x<-5)$ or $(x>5) \Rightarrow x \in(-\infty,-5) \cup(5, \infty)$.
> 
>
