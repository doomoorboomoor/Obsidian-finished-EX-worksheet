### Answer to Question 22

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-11/Section-A/Q-22

- Answer:

$4,7,10$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $\theta_{2}-\theta_{1}=\theta_{3}-\theta_{2}=\theta_{4}-\theta_{3}=\ldots=\theta_{n}-\theta_{n-1}=d$.
> $\sec \theta_{1} \sec \theta_{2}+\sec \theta_{2} \sec \theta_{3}+\ldots+\sec \theta_{n-1} \sec \theta_{n}$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & =\frac{1}{\sin d}\left\{\frac{\left.\frac{\sin d}{\cos \theta_{1} \cos \theta_{2}}+\frac{\sin d}{\cos \theta_{2} \cos \theta_{3}}+\ldots+\frac{\sin d}{\cos \theta_{n-1} \cos \theta_{n}}\right\}}{=\frac{1}{\sin d} \cdot\left\{\frac{\sin \left(\theta_{2}-\theta_{1}\right)}{\cos \theta_{1} \cos \theta_{2}}+\frac{\sin \left(\theta_{3}-\theta_{2}\right)}{\cos \theta_{2} \cos \theta_{3}}+\ldots+\frac{\sin \left(\theta_{n}-\theta_{n-1}\right)}{\cos \theta_{n-1} \cos \theta_{n}}\right\}}\right. \\
> & =\frac{1}{\sin d} \cdot\left\{\frac{\sin \theta_{2} \cos \theta_{1}-\cos \theta_{2} \sin \theta_{1}}{\cos \theta_{1} \cos \theta_{2}}+\frac{\sin \theta_{3} \cos \theta_{2}-\cos \theta_{3} \sin \theta_{2}}{\cos \theta_{2} \cos \theta_{3}}+\right. \\
> & \left.  \ldots+\frac{\sin \theta_{n} \cos \theta_{n-1}-\cos \theta_{n} \sin \theta_{n-1}}{\cos \theta_{n-1} \cos \theta_{n}}\right\} \\
> & =\frac{1}{\sin d} \cdot\left\{\left(\tan \theta_{2}-\tan \theta_{1}\right)+\left(\tan \theta_{3}-\tan \theta_{2}\right)+\ldots+\left(\tan \theta_{n}-\tan \theta_{n-1}\right)\right\} \\
> & =\frac{1}{\sin d} \cdot\left(\tan \theta_{n}-\tan \theta_{1}\right)=\frac{\left(\tan \theta_{n}-\tan \theta_{1}\right)}{\sin d}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
>

### Answer to Question 29

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-11/Section-A/Q-29

- Answer:





> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> The sum of the interior angles of a triangle is $180^{\circ}$. A polygon of 4 sides can be divided into two triangles and therefore, the sum of its angles is $360^{\circ}$. A polygon of 5 sides can be divided into 3 triangles and therefore, the sum of its angles is $\left(3 \times 180^{\circ}\right)=540^{\circ}$ and so on. Thus, a polygon of 21 sides is divided into (21-2) = 19 triangles and therefore, the sum of its angles is $\left(19 \times 180^{\circ}\right)=3420^{\circ}$.
