### Answer to Question 17

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-11/Section-F/Q-17

- Answer:

$41: 59$

> [!INFO] Hint
>
> $\frac{\frac{n}{2} \cdot\left[2 a_{1}+(n-1) d_{1}\right]}{\frac{n}{2} \cdot\left[2 a_{2}+(n-1) d_{2}\right]}=\frac{2 n+3}{3 n+2} \Rightarrow \frac{2 a_{1}+(n-1) d_{1}}{2 a_{2}+(n-1) d_{2}}=\frac{2 n+3}{3 n+2}$.
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \therefore \quad \frac{a_{1}+\left(\frac{n-1}{2}\right) d_{1}}{a_{2}+\left(\frac{n-1}{2}\right) d_{2}}=\frac{2 n+3}{3 n+2} & \Rightarrow \frac{a_{1}+9 d_{1}}{a_{2}+9 d_{2}}=\frac{2 \times 19+3}{3 \times 19+2} \quad\left[\text{putting } \frac{n-1}{2}=9 \Rightarrow n=19\right] \\
> & \Rightarrow \frac{a_{1}+9 d_{1}}{a_{2}+9 d_{2}}=\frac{41}{59}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> Hence, the ratio of their 10 th terms is $41: 59$.
>
