### Answer to Question 01



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-11/Section-C/Q-01



- Answer:



3





> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let the number of sides of the polygon be $n$. Then, sum of its interior angles $=(2n-4) \times 90^{\circ}=(180n-360)^{\circ}$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad 52+60+68+\ldots$ to $n$ terms $=(180n-360)$
> 
> $\Rightarrow \quad \frac{n}{2} \times[2 \times 52+(n-1) \times 8]=180n-360$
> 
> $\Rightarrow n^{2}-33n+90=0 \Rightarrow(n-3)(n-30)=0 \Rightarrow n=3$ or $n=30$.
> 
> When $n=30$, then last angle $=[52+29 \times 8]^{\circ}=284^{\circ}$, which is a reflex angle. So, $n \neq 30$. Hence, $n=3$.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 04



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-11/Section-C/Q-04



- Answer:



25 hours





> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Suppose the second car overtakes the first car in $n$ hours.
> 
> Distance covered by the first car in $n$ hours $=(60n) \mathrm{km}$.
> 
> Distance covered by the second car in $n$ hours
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & =[48+49+50+\ldots \text{ to } n \text{ terms}] \\
> & =\frac{n}{2} \times[2 \times 48+(n-1) \times 1]=\frac{n}{2}(95+n) \\
> \therefore \quad & \frac{n}{2}(95+n)=60n \Rightarrow 95+n=120 \Rightarrow n=25 \text{ hours.}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
>
