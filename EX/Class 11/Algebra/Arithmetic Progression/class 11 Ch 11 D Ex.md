### Answer to Question 05

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-11/Section-D/Q-05

- Answer:

$n=5$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $A_{1}, A_{2}, \ldots, A_{n}$ be the $n$ arithmetic means between 9 and 27 .
> 
> Then, $9, A_{1}, A_{2}, \ldots, A_{n}, 27$ are in AP.
> 
> $$
\begin{aligned}
& \therefore \quad 9+(n+2-1) d=27 \Rightarrow d=\frac{18}{n+1} . \\
& \quad \frac{A_{n}}{A_{1}}=\frac{2}{1} \Rightarrow \frac{T_{n+1}}{T_{2}}=\frac{2}{1} \Rightarrow \frac{9+(n+1-1) d}{9+(2-1) d}=\frac{2}{1} \\
& \quad \Rightarrow 9+n d=18+2 d \Rightarrow d=\frac{9}{n-2} . \\
& \therefore \quad \frac{18}{n+1}=\frac{9}{n-2} \Rightarrow 18(n-2)=9(n+1) \Rightarrow n=5 .
\end{aligned}
$$
> 
>

- Answer:



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Sum of $m$ AMs between $a$ and $b$
> 
> $$
=m \times(\text { AMs between } a \text { and } b)=m \times \frac{(a+b)}{2} .
$$
> 
> Sum of $n$ AMs between $a$ and $b$
> 
> $$
=n \times(\text { AMs between } a \text { and } b)=n \times \frac{(a+b)}{2} .
$$
> 
> $\therefore \quad$ required ratio $=m: n$.
