### Answer to Question 17

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-2/Chapter-05/Section-E/Q-17

- Answer:

$\left\{\frac{2}{3} i,-3 i\right\}$



> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $a=3, b=7 i$ and $c=6$.
> $\therefore \quad\left(b^{2}-4 a c\right)=\left\{(7 i)^{2}-4 \times 3 \times 6\right\}=(-49-72)=(-121)<0$.
> So, the given equation has complex roots, given by
> 
> $$
> \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^{2}-4 a c}}{2 a}=\frac{-7 i \pm \sqrt{-121}}{2 \times 3}=\frac{-7 i \pm 11 i}{6} .
> $$
> 
> Solution set $=\left\{\frac{-7 i+11 i}{6}, \frac{-7 i-11 i}{6}\right\}=\left\{\frac{2}{3} i,-3 i\right\}$.
> 
>
