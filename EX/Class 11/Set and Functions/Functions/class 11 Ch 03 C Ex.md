### Answer to Question 10



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-03/Section-C/Q-10



- Answer:



$\operatorname{dom}(f)=(3,5]$, range $(f)=[0, \infty)$





> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $f(x)=\sqrt{\frac{x-5}{3-x}}$ is defined only when $\frac{x-5}{3-x} \geq 0$.
> 
> Now, $\frac{x-5}{3-x}=0 \Rightarrow x-5=0 \Rightarrow x=5$.
> And, $\frac{x-5}{3-x}>0 \Rightarrow(x-5<0 \text{ and } 3-x<0) \text{ or } (x-5>0 \text{ and } 3-x>0)$
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> & \Rightarrow(x<5 \text{ and } 3<x) \text{ or } (x>5 \text{ and } 3>x) \\
> & \Rightarrow(3<x \text{ and } x<5) \text{ or } (x>5 \text{ and } x<3) \\
> & \Rightarrow 3<x<5 \quad [\because x>5 \text{ and } x<3 \text{ cannot hold }] .
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Thus, $f(x)$ is defined when $3<x \leq 5$.
> $\therefore \quad \operatorname{dom}(f)=(3,5]$.
> Clearly, $f(x)=\sqrt{\frac{x-5}{3-x}} \geq 0$.
> Let $y=f(x)$. Then,
> 
> $$
> \begin{align*}
> y=\sqrt{\frac{x-5}{3-x}} & \Rightarrow y^{2}=\frac{x-5}{3-x} \Rightarrow 3 y^{2}-x y^{2}=x-5 \\
> & \Rightarrow x+x y^{2}=3 y^{2}+5 \Rightarrow x\left(1+y^{2}\right)=\left(3 y^{2}+5\right) \Rightarrow x=\frac{3 y^{2}+5}{1+y^{2}} \tag{i}
> \end{align*}
> $$
> 
> It follows from (i) that $x$ is defined for each real value of $y$.
> $\therefore \quad \operatorname{range}(f)=[0, \infty) \quad [\because f(x) \geq 0]$.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 11

- Answer:



$\operatorname{dom}(f)=(-\infty,-1) \cup(1, \infty)$, range $(f)=R-\{0\}$





> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $f(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{x^{2}-1}}$ is defined only when $x^{2}-1>0$.
> 
> Now, $x^{2}-1>0 \Rightarrow(x+1)(x-1)>0 \Rightarrow(x<-1) \text{ or } (x>1)$
> 
> $$
> \Rightarrow x \in(-\infty,-1) \text{ or } x \in(1, \infty) .
> $$
> 
> $\therefore \quad \operatorname{dom}(f)=(-\infty,-1) \cup(1, \infty)$.
> Let $y=f(x)$. Then,
> 
> $$
> y=\frac{1}{\sqrt{x^{2}-1}} \Rightarrow y^{2}=\frac{1}{\left(x^{2}-1\right)} \Rightarrow x^{2}-1=\frac{1}{y^{2}} \Rightarrow x=\sqrt{\frac{1}{y^{2}}+1}=\sqrt{\frac{1+y^{2}}{y^{2}}} .
> $$
> 
> Clearly, $x$ is not defined when $y=0$.
> $\therefore \quad \operatorname{range}(f)=R-\{0\}$.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 15



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-03/Section-C/Q-15



- Answer:



$\operatorname{dom}(f)=R$, range $(f)=\left[\frac{1}{3}, 1\right]$





> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Clearly, $-1 \leq-\sin 3 x \leq 1$ for all $x \in R$
> $\Rightarrow \quad 2+(-1) \leq 2+(-\sin 3 x) \leq 2+1$ for all $x \in R$
> $\Rightarrow \quad 1 \leq 2-\sin 3 x \leq 3$ for all $x \in R$
> $\Rightarrow \quad 2-\sin 3 x \neq 0$ for any $x \in R$.
> $\therefore \quad f(x)=\frac{1}{(2-\sin 3 x)}$ is defined for all $x \in R$.
> 
> We have, $2-\sin 3 x \geq 1$ and $2-\sin 3 x \leq 3$
> $\therefore \quad \frac{1}{2-\sin 3 x} \leq 1$ and $\frac{1}{2-\sin 3 x} \geq \frac{1}{3} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{3} \leq \frac{1}{2-\sin 3 x} \leq 1$.
> $\therefore \quad$ range $(f)=\left[\frac{1}{3}, 1\right]$.
> 
>
