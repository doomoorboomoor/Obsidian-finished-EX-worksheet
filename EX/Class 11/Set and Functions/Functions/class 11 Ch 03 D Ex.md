### Answer to Question 03



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-03/Section-D/Q-03



- Answer:



Let $f: R^{+} \cup\{0\} \rightarrow R: f(x)=\sqrt{x}$. Then,

$$
\operatorname{dom}(f)=[0, \infty) \text { and range }(f)=[0, \infty) .
$$

We have

$$
\sqrt{0}=0, \sqrt{1}=1, \sqrt{2.25}=1.5, \sqrt{4}=2, \sqrt{6.25}=2.5 \text { and } \sqrt{9}=3 \text { . }
$$

On a graph paper, we plot the points $O(0,0), A(1,1), B(2.25,1.5), C(4,2), D(6.25,2.5)$, and $E(9,3)$. Join these points freehand successively to get the required curve.

### Answer to Question 04



- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-03/Section-D/Q-04



- Answer:



Let $f: R \rightarrow R: f(x)=x^{1 / 3}$ for all $x \in R$.

Clearly, $\operatorname{dom}(f)=R$ and range $(f)=R$.

We have

$$
\sqrt[3]{8}=2, \sqrt[3]{5}=1.7, \sqrt[3]{3}=1.4, \sqrt[3]{1}=1, \sqrt[3]{0}=0, \sqrt[3]{-1}=-1, \sqrt[3]{-3}=-1.4, \sqrt[3]{-5}=-1.7,
$$

and $\sqrt[3]{-8}=-2$.

On a graph paper, take the horizontal line $X^{\prime} O X$ as the $x$-axis and the vertical line $Y O Y^{\prime}$ as the $y$-axis.

Take the scale: 5 small divisions $=1$ unit.

Now, plot the points $A(8,2), B(5,1.7), C(3,1.4), D(1,1), O(0,0), E(-1,-1), F(-3,-1.4)$, $G(-5,-1.7)$ and $H(-8,-2)$.

Join $A B C D O E F G H$ freehand to get the required curve.

Remember: $\sqrt[3]{3}=1.4$ and $\sqrt[3]{5}=1.7$.
