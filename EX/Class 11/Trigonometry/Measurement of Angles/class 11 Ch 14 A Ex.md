### Answer to Question 04

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-14/Section-A/Q-04

- Answer:

$\left(40^{\circ}, 60^{\circ}, 80^{\circ}\right),\left\{\left(\frac{2 \pi}{9}\right)^{c},\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right)^{c},\left(\frac{4 \pi}{9}\right)^{c}\right\}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let the required angles be $(a-d)^{\circ}, a^{\circ}$ and $(a+d)^{\circ}$.
> 
> Then, the sum of the angles of a triangle being $180^{\circ}$, we have
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> (a-d)+a+(a+d) & =180 \Leftrightarrow 3a=180 \Leftrightarrow a=60. \\
> \text{Also, } (a+d)=2(a-d) & \Leftrightarrow a=3d \\
> & \Leftrightarrow 3d=60 \quad [\because a=60] \\
> & \Leftrightarrow d=20.
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Hence, the angles in degrees are $40^{\circ}, 60^{\circ}, 80^{\circ}$.
> 
> These angles in radians are $\left(40 \times \frac{\pi}{180}\right)^{c},\left(60 \times \frac{\pi}{180}\right)^{c}$ and $\left(80 \times \frac{\pi}{180}\right)^{c}$, i.e., $\left(\frac{2 \pi}{9}\right)^{c},\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right)^{c}$ and $\left(\frac{4 \pi}{9}\right)^{c}$.
> 
>

### Answer to Question 05

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-14/Section-A/Q-05

- Answer:

$\left(63^{\circ}, 27^{\circ}\right),\left\{\left(\frac{7 \pi}{20}\right)^{c},\left(\frac{3 \pi}{20}\right)^{c}\right\}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let the acute angles of the given triangle be $x^{c}$ and $y^{c}$. Then,
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> x+y &= \frac{\pi}{2} \\
> x-y &= \frac{\pi}{5}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> 
> Solving the equations, we get $x=\frac{7 \pi}{20}$ and $y=\frac{3 \pi}{20}$.
> 
> Thus, the required angles are $\left(\frac{7 \pi}{20}\right)^{\mathrm{c}}$ and $\left(\frac{3 \pi}{20}\right)^{\mathrm{c}}$.
> 
> These angles in degrees are $\left(\frac{7 \pi}{20} \times \frac{180}{\pi}\right)^{\circ}$ and $\left(\frac{3 \pi}{20} \times \frac{180}{\pi}\right)^{\circ}$, i.e., $63^{\circ}$ and $27^{\circ}$.
>

### Answer to Question 10

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-14/Section-A/Q-10

- Answer:

15.71 cm

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Let $O$ be the centre of the given circle of radius 15 cm and let $A B$ be the chord of length 15 cm . Then, $O A=O B=A B=15 \mathrm{~cm}$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad \triangle A O B$ is an equilateral triangle.
> 
> And, therefore, $\theta=\angle A O B=60^{\circ}$
> 
> $$
> =\left(60 \times \frac{\pi}{180}\right)^{c}=\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right)^{c}
> $$
> 
> Let the minor $\operatorname{arc} A B=l \mathrm{~cm}$. Then,
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> \theta & =\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right)^{\mathrm{c}} \text{ and } r=15 \mathrm{~cm} \\
> \therefore \quad \theta & =\frac{l}{r} \Rightarrow l=r \theta=\left(15 \times \frac{\pi}{3}\right) \mathrm{cm} \\
> & =\left(5 \times \frac{22}{7}\right) \mathrm{cm}=\frac{110}{7} \mathrm{~cm}=15.71 \mathrm{~cm}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
>

### Answer to Question 11

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-14/Section-A/Q-11

- Answer:

$\left(\frac{11}{45}\right)^{\mathrm{c}}, 14^{\circ}$

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> Here, $r=45 \mathrm{~cm}$ and $l=11 \mathrm{~cm}$.
> 
> $\therefore \quad$ Let the angle be $\theta^{c}$. Then,
> 
> $$
> \begin{aligned}

> & \text{Now, } \pi^{c}=180^{\circ} \\
> & \Rightarrow \left(\frac{11}{45}\right)^{c} = \left(\frac{180}{\pi} \times \frac{11}{45}\right)^{\circ} \\
> & =\left(180 \times \frac{7}{22} \times \frac{11}{45}\right)^{\circ}=14^{\circ}
> \end{aligned}
> $$
