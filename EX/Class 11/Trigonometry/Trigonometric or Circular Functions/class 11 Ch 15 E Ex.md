### Answer to Question 06

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-15/Section-E/Q-06

- Answer:

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> LHS $=\frac{\cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}-\frac{\sin \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}{\cos \left(\frac{x}{2}\right)}=\frac{\left\{\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\right\}}{\sin \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2}}$
> 
> $$
> =\frac{2 \cos x}{\sin x}=2 \cot x=\mathrm{RHS} .
> $$
> 
>

### Answer to Question 07

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-15/Section-E/Q-07

- Answer:

> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\mathrm{RHS}=\left(\frac{1}{\cos x}+\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\right)=\frac{(1+\sin x)}{\cos x}=\frac{1-\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)}{\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}+x\right)}$
> $=\frac{2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{x}{2}\right)}{2 \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{x}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{x}{2}\right)}=\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{x}{2}\right)=$ LHS.
> 
>
