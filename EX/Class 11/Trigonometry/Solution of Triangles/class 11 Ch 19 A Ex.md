- Answer:





> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> LHS $=2[b c(1+\cos A)+c a(1+\cos B)+a b(1+\cos C)]$
>
> $$\begin{aligned} & =2[(b c+c a+a b)+b c \cdot \cos A+c a \cdot \cos B+a b \cdot \cos C] \\ & =2\left[(a b+b c+c a)+b c \cdot \frac{\left(b^{2}+c^{2}-a^{2}\right)}{2 b c}+c a \cdot \frac{\left(c^{2}+a^{2}-b^{2}\right)}{2 c a}+a b \cdot \frac{\left(a^{2}+b^{2}-c^{2}\right)}{2 a b}\right] \\ & =a^{2}+b^{2}+c^{2}+2(a b+b c+c a)=(a+b+c)^{2}=\text{RHS} \end{aligned}$$

### Answer to Question 07

- [ ] InBoX/Ans/Ex/Class-11/Unit-1/Chapter-19/Section-A/Q-07

- Answer:





> [!INFO] Hint
> 
> $\frac{a}{\sin A}=\frac{b}{\sin B}=\frac{c}{\sin C}=k$ (say)
>
> $$\begin{aligned} & \Rightarrow \quad a=k \sin A, b=k \sin B \text{ and } c=k \sin C. \\ & \therefore \quad \begin{aligned} \text{LHS} & =k \sin^{2} A-k \sin^{2} B \\ & =k\left(\sin^{2} A-\sin^{2} B\right)=k \sin (A+B) \cdot \sin (A-B) \\ & =k \sin (\pi-C) \sin (A-B)=k \sin C \sin (A-B) \\ & =c \sin (A-B)=\text{RHS} \end{aligned} \end{aligned}$$
>

> [!INFO] Hint
>

>
